The Samsung India Workers Union (CITU) issued a strike notice on March 13, demanding the reinstatement of 27 workers suspended on March 7, 2025, and the withdrawal of disciplinary actions against union members. A 14-day window was provided for resolution; failure to comply would result in industrial action. The Labour Commissioner, A. Yasmin Begum, directed Samsung India Electronics Pvt Ltd and the union to attend a conciliation meeting on March 16.

A funeral was held for Ali Shamkhani, Iran’s Defence Council Secretary, following his death in a February 28 strike. The event was followed by promotional content for CNBC TV18’s “Young Turks at 22,” highlighting the network’s two-decade coverage of India’s startup ecosystem.

Adani Power was awarded a 1,600 MW power supply agreement by Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Company Ltd, with a combined tariff of ₹5.30/kWh under a 25-year Power Supply Agreement effective from FY31. The power will be generated from an upcoming ultra-supercritical coal plant with secured coal linkage, ensuring fuel stability and reduced emissions. This award brings Adani’s total secured PSA capacity to 13.3 GW out of a 23.8 GW pipeline, supporting its target of 41.87 GW generation capacity by FY32.

Hindalco Industries reported that force majeure declarations by certain gas suppliers may impact its aluminium extrusion operations. The disruption affects less than 0.1% of total output, with primary aluminium and other downstream activities continuing unaffected due to captive power and alternative energy sources. Extrusion operations, located in Renukoot, Alupuram, Silvassa, and Kuppam, serve sectors including automotive, construction, defence, aerospace, maritime, renewables, and railways.

Automakers including Hyundai, Tata Motors, Mahindra & Mahindra, and JSW MG are offering substantial incentives—up to ₹5.05 lakh in cash discounts, exchange bonuses, corporate incentives, and extended warranties—to clear EV inventory ahead of FY25. These measures are driven by fuel price volatility, a 40% accelerated depreciation tax benefit for businesses, and the need to meet PLI sales targets. EV sales rose 44% year-on-year to 13,733 units in February, though demand remains inconsistent, with expectations of stronger uptake if fuel prices increase.

Manish Poddar, founder and CEO of Rare Rabbit, accepted an award, reflecting on the brand’s decade-long evolution into a full-stack fashion ecosystem across men’s, women’s, and children’s apparel and footwear. Over 1,200 points of sale and 200 premium stores were established, with growth attributed to design innovation, team support, and India’s market opportunities. Future expansion and potential IPO plans were noted.

Ongoing gas supply disruptions due to the West Asia conflict are affecting MSMEs in western India, particularly metal-processing, foundries, and aluminium extrusion units. Over 70% of affected firms have reduced output by 50–60%, unable to fulfill orders before the fiscal year-end. Attempts to switch to coal have been hindered by high costs and uncertainty. Industry bodies are seeking a 3–6 month loan moratorium and additional credit under the Credit Guarantee Scheme from the RBI. The situation has led to increased TMT bar prices, production cuts in the Morbi kiln cluster, and risks to real estate and infrastructure projects. Hindalco has paused extruded aluminium production. Relief measures similar to those during the Covid-19 pandemic are being urged.

At the CNBC TV18 India Business Leader Awards, Ferrari CEO Benedetto Vigna emphasized the brand’s fusion of heritage and innovation, citing India’s rising enthusiasm for Ferrari and cultural alignment with its “ITech” identity. His first visit to India in 1998 was recalled, along with admiration for the nation’s people and culture. Ferrari aims to deepen presence through partnerships and unique offerings, not solely sales targets. Hybrid, thermal, and electric models were discussed, including the fully electric “Ferrari Lucha,” named after Kashi. Vigna underscored leadership responsibility, respect for differing views, and his personal mantra: “Learn from everyone, everywhere,” expressed through the phrase “Oga, kaise, nahi.”

Lupin’s Managing Director Nilesh Gupta announced strategic investments in innovation, with plans to develop and launch new chemical entities (NCEs) in cardiology, diabetes, respiratory, and oncology for the Indian market. Lower development costs and faster time-to-market in India are expected to be leveraged. Global innovation initiatives will be pursued, and the first Day-1 generic of semaglutide will be introduced post-patent expiry, with anticipated price reductions. AI integration, partnerships retaining global rights, and scalability amid geopolitical and logistical challenges were highlighted.

The all-electric Mercedes-Benz CLA was reintroduced in India on the new MMA skateboard platform. It features an 85.5 kWh battery with a WLTP-rated range of 792 km and 800 V fast-charging (400 km in 20 minutes). A 200 kW rear-wheel-drive unit is paired with a two-speed gearbox, achieving 0–100 km/h in 6.7 seconds and a drag coefficient of 0.21.
