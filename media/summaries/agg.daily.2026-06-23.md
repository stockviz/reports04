### **India: Industry & Innovation**

#### **JSW Group’s Electric Bus Push**
* Building in-house e-bus tech: battery packs, vehicle control units, software at Pune R&D center.
* New 90-acre plant in Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar.
* Subsidiary JSW Greentech (led by Sumit Mittal, ex-Greencell) submitted aggressive bids for 6,230 central government buses.
* ₹3,000 cr investment plan; ₹15,000 cr borrowing cap to expand automotive ambitions.
* Goal: Reduce partner dependency, gain cost/innovation edge in competitive e-bus market (vs. Eka Mobility, PMI Electro Mobility).

#### **AI & Deep Tech Momentum**
* Info Edge: AI startup portfolio value doubled from ₹614 cr to ₹1,268 cr since 2020.
  - 28 AI + 45 consumer tech startups backed; half secured follow-on funding.
  - Focus: Sovereign AI models, deep-tech, consumer tech.
  - Strategic alignment with IndiaAI Mission (₹10,300 cr) and unicorns like Sarvam AI.
* Palo Alto Networks: Surge in demand from Indian banks, telcos, infrastructure firms due to AI-powered cyber threats (e.g., Anthropic’s Claude Mythos).
  - 5x increase in vulnerabilities discovered → shift to machine-level security.
  - Expanding focus to SMEs; leveraging India’s strong regulations and public-private partnerships.

#### **Auto & Manufacturing**
* Interface Auto (Gurgaon): In early talks to sell majority stake; valuation $250–300M (₹2,830 cr).
  - Products: Door modules, LED lighting, TPMS, battery sensors.
  - Driven by Make-in-India, PE funding, succession planning.
  - Comparable deals: Tessolve, Dhoot Transmission, Carlyle’s auto consolidation.

### **Geopolitics & Sanctions**
* **U.S.-Iran Deal**:

  - Sanctions lifted on Iranian assets → funds to benefit U.S. farmers.
  - Trump claims Iran’s navy/air force destroyed (disputed by media).
* **U.S. Quantum Push**:
  - Two executive orders: Build scientifically relevant quantum computer, advance quantum sensors, networking, and post-quantum cryptography.
  - DOE-led initiative; Google, IBM, Commerce Dept. involved.
  - Goal: Secure U.S. future computing infrastructure amid global AI race.

### **Healthcare & Pharma**
* **FDA Urges India to Help Fill U.S. Ifosfamide Shortage**:
  - Caused by Baxter technical disruption + West Asia war supply chain issues.
  - Indian firms under query: Cipla, Zydus Lifesciences, Alkem, GLS Pharma.
  - FDA open to non-registered facilities with strong compliance history.
  - Shortage expected to persist through 2026.
  - India is a major supplier of U.S. anti-cancer drugs; regulatory strength key.
