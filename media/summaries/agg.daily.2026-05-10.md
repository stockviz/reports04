Airbnb’s expansion in India is being driven by a structural shift toward domestic travel, with domestic bookings now constituting approximately 80% of reservations, a significant departure from pre-COVID patterns. Listings have increased from around 15,000 in 2017 to approximately 115,000, and host occupancy rates average 80%, with average nightly rates at ₹3,000. Growth is supported by product enhancements, the co-host program, and trust-and-safety features that have bolstered host confidence. Rising domestic tourism, projected at 4.13 billion trips by 2025, and the phenomenon of "revenge travel" are key demand drivers. Despite challenges such as regulatory resistance from housing societies, reduced international tourist inflows, and an underdeveloped supply base compared to mature markets, India’s young, digitally active population is seen as a long-term growth engine.
