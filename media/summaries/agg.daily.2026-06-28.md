Persistent Systems has initiated the acquisition of Nagarro, a German digital-engineering firm, for $1.3 billion, with the transaction expected to drive Persistent’s annual revenue to $5 billion by March 2031, positioning it as India’s seventh-largest IT services firm. The acquisition, justified by Nagarro’s strong client base and growth trajectory, is intended to scale AI transformation capabilities by integrating two engineering-first cultures. The deal is structured to expand Persistent’s European footprint, strengthen SAP and AI competencies, and unify service delivery under a shared strategic direction, with Manas Human, Nagarro’s CEO, remaining involved to ensure cultural alignment and operational continuity. The partnership is designed to deliver AI-driven engineering solutions without a shift toward sales-led growth, emphasizing trusted advisory in AI transformation.

Quess Corp has identified construction as a hyper-growth segment, having deployed 3,000–5,000 workers in infrastructure projects including airports, roads, industrial plants, and semiconductor facilities within two years of market entry. The expansion is supported by India’s infrastructure push and large-scale manufacturing investments, which are increasing demand for compliant, scalable labor. The company intends to replicate its established staffing model in the construction sector, which remains largely informal, while maintaining strong demand from banking, retail, and consumer sectors as formal employment expands into smaller cities.

ITC has transitioned from a consumer-brand-centric model to a capability-driven enterprise platform, as outlined in its FY26 report. Core capabilities in agriculture, tobacco science, and fiber technology are being leveraged across three platforms: ITC MAARS, a digital rural services ecosystem linking over 2.6 million farmers with AI advisory, finance, market access, and mechanisation, with a target of 10 million users by 2030; a life sciences venture with an integrated pharmaceutical-grade nicotine plant in Mysuru and expansion into botanical extracts, now EBITDA-positive; and sustainable materials development through paperboard innovations, including recyclable barrier boards and fibre-based packaging, supported by the planned Century Pulp & Paper acquisition and the formation of ITC Fibre Innovations. The strategic shift enables scalable, efficient expansion into adjacent high-value markets.

JK Maini Global Aerospace (JKMGAL) is growing at approximately 25% annually, driven by rising global demand for aerospace components, India’s “China+1” strategy, and a sustained aircraft backlog. The company’s order book is projected to generate over ₹2,350 crore in revenue over the next five years, with 75% of sales from engine parts, 15% from structural components, and 5% from systems. While 79% of revenue is export-oriented, a strategic shift is underway from a build-to-print model to a value-chain approach, involving design, IP development, and a ₹510 crore investment in a new manufacturing plant in Andhra Pradesh to produce sub-assemblies and assemblies. Defence activities remain in the prototype phase, with focus on component supply rather than direct contracts.

Tesla has formally raised concerns with the Delhi government regarding the draft EV Policy 2.0, which excludes vehicles priced above ₹30 lakh from tax and registration fee exemptions and a ₹1 lakh scrapping incentive. The Model Y (₹50.89 lakh) and Model Y L (₹61.99 lakh) are therefore ineligible for benefits, despite Tesla operating experience and service centers in Delhi-NCR and having sold 342 vehicles in FY26. The company warns that the price cap will impede the adoption of high-tech EVs in the region. The policy, still under public consultation, aims to provide 100% road-tax exemption for EVs below ₹30 lakh and a scrapping bonus for eligible buyers, but offers no incentives for higher-priced models.

Athulya Senior Care is in advanced negotiations to raise $30 million by FY27, following a prior ₹130 crore funding round. The capital will be used to expand assisted-living beds from 1,500 to 3,500 and establish a network of 10 geriatric hospitals (approximately 500 beds) across South India, complementing its newly opened dedicated geriatric hospital in Chennai. The company aims to address a significant market gap—India requires approximately 400,000 senior-care beds, compared to the current 20,000—while planning expansion into Pune, Ahmedabad, and a 100-bed facility in Mauritius, with an IPO anticipated in approximately four years.

SAEL Industries, through its subsidiary SAEL Solar P6, has inaugurated a 200-acre integrated solar cell and module plant in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh, with an investment of ₹8,200 crore. The facility will produce 5 GW of solar cells and 5 GW of modules, generating approximately 5,000 direct and 15,000 indirect jobs.
