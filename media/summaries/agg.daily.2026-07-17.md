### 📈 **2. Earnings & Stock Highlights**

#### ✅ **Reliance Industries (Q1 FY27) – Strong Recovery**
- **Revenue & EBITDA**: +4% QoQ each, **flat 15% margins**.
- **Net Profit**: ~**₹18,628 cr** (+9.8% YoY).
- **Key Drivers**:
  - **Oil-to-Chemicals (O2C)**: EBITDA up **20% YoY & QoQ** due to strong diesel/gasoline/ATF spreads.
  - **Digital**: +11% revenue, ARPU up 3%.
- **Headwinds**:
  - **Gas production down 7% YoY**, dragging upstream EBITDA to **₹3,943 cr** (-21% YoY).
- **Overall**: Chemicals offset oil & gas weakness → **earnings recovery** despite lower output.

> 💡 **Valuation**: Stock down **17% YTD**, trades at **~20.7x FY27 EPS** → **reasonable** given growth trajectory.

---

#### ✅ **Tech Mahindra – Outperforming Peers**
- **Revenue**: +2.6% CC (mostly one-off), but **Q1 growth driven by AI & cloud**.
- **Margins**: Improved to **14.6%** (up from sub-10% in prior quarters).
- **EPS Estimate Upgrade**: +3%.
- **Valuation**: Trades at **18x** vs. TCS (~12.5–13x) → premium justified by **AI execution & margin recovery**.
- **Analyst View (Thakkar)**: Bullish on **Tech Mahindra & TCS**, recommend buying on dips post-Infosys results.

> 📌 **Takeaway**: Tech Mahindra is **regaining momentum** after margin struggles; AI & fixed-price contracts are key levers.

---

#### ⚠️ **Wipro – Margin Pressure, But Turnaround in Sight**
- **Revenue**: -0.9% YoY (weakest in 15 quarters).
- **Margins**: **16%** (down 120 bps YoY), **lowest in 12+ quarters**.
- **Bookings**: $3.3B (driven by **large AI deals**).
- **Management Outlook**:
  - Focus on **AI-native unit**, fixed-price contracts, G&A efficiency.
  - Target: Restore margins to **17–17.5%** by FY27.
  - Q2: Expect **modest decline (-1.5% to +0.5%)**.
- **Headcount**: Hiring paced to demand; **no aggressive hiring**.

> 🔥 **Bullish Case**: AI-driven consulting (especially BFSI) can convert cost savings into **AI spend** → long-term margin recovery.

### 🏭 **3. Sectoral & Strategic Developments**

#### 🏭 **India’s Push for Indigenous AI Chips**
- **Goal**: Develop **Nvidia-style AI inference chip** by **2029–30**.
- **Lead Agency**: **C-DAC** (40-year-old govt. R&D body).
- **Partner**: **HCL Infosystems** (design validation).
- **Funding**: ₹1.27 trillion **Semicon 2.0 Mission**, **National Supercomputing Mission**.
- **Objectives**:
  - Run AI workloads on **domestic servers**.
  - Reduce reliance on **US-controlled tech**.
  - Build **indigenous semiconductor IP**.
- **Private Sector Role**: Encouraged to innovate; C-DAC to provide **design incentives & validation**.

> 🌐 **Strategic Implication**: India is building **AI self-reliance** – critical for national security, data sovereignty, and digital economy.
