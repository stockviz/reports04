### 📌 **Summary of Key Developments (India & Global Markets)**

#### 🔥 **Geopolitical Flashpoint: Strait of Hormuz & Iran–US Tensions**
- A **missile attack from Iran** struck Bahrain’s oil refinery, causing **extensive flames and damage**, underscoring the real risk to global energy infrastructure.
- The **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical chokepoint for ~20 million barrels/day of crude, remains under threat, fueling **extreme crude price volatility**.
- **Brent crude** spiked above $100, but **fell over 10%** following **President Trump’s claim** that the U.S.-Iran conflict is nearing an end — a signal that eased short-term panic.
- **Vandana Hari** and analysts stress: **G7 SPR releases are logistically limited** and cannot offset a full closure of the strait. **Brent is unlikely to fall below $90** until the strait reopens reliably.

> 💡 **Market Takeaway**: While oil prices have corrected, **geopolitical risk premium remains baked in**. Volatility is likely to persist.

#### 🏢 **India-Specific: LPG Crisis & Restaurant Sector in Peril**
- **March 5 order** by the Petroleum Ministry directed OMCs to **prioritize domestic LPG**, disrupting **commercial LPG supply**.
- **NRAI (National Restaurant Association of India)** has formally appealed to the government for urgent intervention.
- **Eateries in Bengaluru, TN, Maharashtra, Delhi** report **severe shortages**, with many facing **shutdowns**.
- **Food delivery platforms (Swiggy, Zomato)** at risk due to supply chain breakdowns.
- **Government response**: Ordered **higher LPG production** and set up a **panel to resolve commercial shortages**.

> ⚠️ **Economic Risk**: If not resolved, this could trigger **widespread job losses and inflation in food services**.
