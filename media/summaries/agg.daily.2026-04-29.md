- **Elon Musk’s $150B lawsuit** against OpenAI, Sam Altman, and Greg Brockman:
  - Alleges betrayal of nonprofit mission; OpenAI turned into a **profit-driven entity**.
  - Demands: Revert to nonprofit, remove Altman/Brockman, divert damages to charity.
- **OpenAI’s defense**: Musk pushed for for-profit model to attract talent and funding.
- **Trial implications**:
  - Could redefine **AI nonprofit governance**, **charity law**, and **corporate control**.
  - May impact **OpenAI’s IPO plans** and public trust in AI development.
- **Musk’s testimony**: Claimed he created OpenAI in 2015 as a **safety-focused, open-source alternative** to Google.

> ⚖️ **Broader significance**: A high-stakes legal battle over the soul of AI — **philanthropy vs. profit**, **transparency vs. control**.
