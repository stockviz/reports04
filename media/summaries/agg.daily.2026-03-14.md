Ferrari introduced the 10.37-crore Testarossa in Mumbai, highlighting India’s growing significance in the ultra-luxury performance vehicle market. The country’s ultra-high-net-worth individual base (> $30 million in assets) is projected to expand by 50% by 2028, supporting an annual supercar market of 1,000–1,300 units. Competitors including Lamborghini, Porsche, and Aston Martin are advancing hybrid-powered models for India, reflecting a broader industry shift toward electrification, supported by the EU-Free Trade Agreement. The Testarossa’s plug-in hybrid powertrain delivers 1,050 hp and achieves 0–100 km/h in approximately 2.25 seconds, with on-road pricing exceeding 12 crore. Ferrari’s presence in India, established through Mumbai and Delhi dealerships over 15 years, is being reinforced through this launch, signaling sustained growth in the nation’s high-end automotive segment.

New Era Cleantech Solutions Pvt. Ltd. commenced construction of a 20,000-crore greenfield coal gasification and carbon-capture plant in Bhadrawati, Chandrapur, spanning 1,650 acres. The facility will process over 5 million tonnes of domestic coal annually in Phase 1 to produce ammonia, ammonium nitrate, and monoethylene glycol, with future phases targeting dimethyl ether (DME), urea, sustainable aviation fuel, and other derivatives. The project, approved under India’s strategic energy and industrial development goals, aims to reduce reliance on imported chemicals and lower emissions through integrated carbon capture.

Hettich completed a ₹2,000-crore expansion of its Indore facility, including the commissioning of a new production line for 5 million drawer runners annually. The site now produces approximately 60 million hinges and enhances export capacity, local sourcing, and manufacturing efficiency. India has been positioned as a central node in Hettich’s global operations, with the company advancing its “Made in India, Made for the World” strategy.

JSW Steel initiated development of the Minas de Revubo (MdR) coking-coal mine in Mozambique’s Moatize basin, with an initial target of 2.4 million tonnes per annum (Mtpa) over the first 2.5 years. The mine holds 850 million tonnes of reserves and is strategically located near Tete and Indian ports. The project supports JSW’s backward integration strategy, ensuring a secure, cost-effective supply of premium coking coal for steel production, reducing exposure to price volatility and emissions.

The Adani Group announced a plan to invest 2 lakh crore annually over the next five years in greenfield projects across renewable energy, transmission, ports, logistics, airports, data centers, and related infrastructure. The strategy aims to enhance India’s energy self-reliance, strengthen logistics networks, and mitigate supply-chain vulnerabilities exposed by recent geopolitical disruptions. Targets include doubling port capacity to 1,200 MMT, expanding renewable power to 50 GW, and increasing thermal generation to 45 GW by 2030, with emphasis on cost-effective power, integrated logistics, materials, and defense sectors.

Tata Consumer Products Ltd. received a tax demand of ₹98.03 crore (including interest) for FY23 following a Section 143(3) assessment by the Kolkata tax office. The company has declared the demand unsustainable and intends to file an appeal, asserting it will not impact financial position or operations. Hindalco Industries invoked force majeure on extruded aluminium product sales due to supply chain disruptions caused by the Iran-Israel conflict, which has blocked shipping through the Strait of Hormuz and disrupted gas supplies essential for aluminium smelting. While Indian smelters remain operational, global aluminium markets are experiencing shortages and price spikes, with LME futures reaching four-year highs and spot premiums surging.

Fried, a Gurugram-based fintech, raised ₹60 crore in a funding round led by Avishkar Kapil, with participation from existing investors. The capital will be used to enhance technology, expand customer acquisition, and scale financial-literacy initiatives targeting Tier-2 and Tier-3 Indian markets. The platform uses AI-driven tools to negotiate debt settlements and restructure payments, operating on a performance-based fee model that charges borrowers only upon successful savings. With over 2 million counseled borrowers, 120,000 active accounts, and 19–20% monthly revenue growth, Fried aims to manage $1 billion in debt and achieve approximately ₹150 crore in revenue within 18 months.

Anand Deshpande, founder of Persistent Systems, emphasized AI’s transformative potential in solving previously intractable problems, creating new opportunities for Indian IT firms. He highlighted the current buoyant market environment compared to past downturns and stressed the importance of execution—expanding sales, securing new work, and delivering projects—amid rapid technological adoption. Deshpande advised IT services firms to diversify beyond traditional enterprise clients into healthcare, finance, and education, leveraging AI to drive productivity. He forecasted a resurgence in job creation as AI enhances development efficiency and recommended a balanced capital allocation strategy, combining dividends, buybacks, and investment in cost-effective AI R&D and platform development.
