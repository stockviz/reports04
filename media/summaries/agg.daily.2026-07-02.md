### 🔹 **Technology & AI: Rapid Innovation Amid Regulatory Scrutiny**
- **Meta’s New Cloud Infrastructure Play**: Meta surged 9% after announcing a new cloud-infrastructure business, signaling aggressive expansion beyond social media into AI-powered infrastructure—potentially competing with AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.
- **AI Hardware Breakthroughs**: New AI chips promise **10x efficiency gains**, driving a shift toward **on-device AI processing**, which could reduce data-center costs and energy use.
- **AI in Corporate Performance Management**: Major firms (Axis Bank, PwC, Reliance) are now tying **5–15% of senior leadership KPIs to AI implementation**, requiring leaders to dedicate ~20% time to AI projects with measurable outcomes (cost savings, revenue growth, productivity).
- **AI Inflation vs. Commodity Deflation**: Yardeni notes a paradox—**AI-driven costs** (expensive chips, agentic models) are pushing up prices, while broader **commodity deflation** (oil, metals) persists. This "AI-inflation" may challenge central banks.
- **Regulatory Pushback**: India’s government has **asked WhatsApp to pause its username feature** over fears of **cyber fraud, impersonation, and phishing**, citing IT Act compliance. WhatsApp defends with built-in verification tools—tension continues.

### 🔹 **Global Markets & Macroeconomics: Volatility Amid Policy Divergence**
- **US Markets**: Nasdaq fell 0.5%, S&P 500 dropped 0.2% amid **rotation out of semiconductors**. Fed Chair Kevin Walsh signaled **hawkish caution**, warning that tolerating inflation above 2% would disappoint markets—raising prospects of **further rate hikes**.
- **Gold Outlook**: Yardeni remains **bullish on gold**, forecasting **$10,000/ounce by 2035**, despite short-term volatility, driven by central bank buying and long-term de-dollarization trends.
- **Oil & Commodities**: Brent crude dipped to **$72/barrel**, WTI to **$69**—pre-war levels. **Gold and silver rose** on soft US jobs data; **base metals fell**, reflecting demand concerns.
- **India’s Market Resilience**: Nifty held above **23,800 support**, trading near **24,100–24,300 resistance**. Strong FMCG, auto, and real estate sectors outperformed; IT, pharma, and metals lagged due to global exposure. **FII net buying (~₹3,100 cr)** supported momentum.

### 🔹 **India-Specific Developments: Growth, Policy, and Corporate Strategy**
- **WhatsApp vs. Government**: Regulatory clash over **username feature** highlights India’s tightening grip on digital platforms. WhatsApp has **3 days to respond**—a critical test of tech sovereignty vs. innovation.
- **Auto Sector Momentum**: 
  - **Bajaj Auto** reported strong sales growth (13% revenue, 18% earnings CAGR forecast).
  - **TVS Motor** posted **₹2,000 cr post-tax losses** on Norton acquisition (2020–2026), but sees it as a **decade-long premium brand turnaround** strategy—targeting 20,000 units/year and 20.5% domestic market share by FY28.
- **Steel Expansion**: **Vedanta Iron & Steel** plans to **double Bokaro plant capacity (1.5 → 3 mtpa)** by end-2026 (ahead of schedule), aiming for **5 mtpa by FY29**, funded via internal cash and market debt—part of a post-demerger focus on high-margin steel.
- **Private Refiners React**: **Nayara Energy slashed petrol by ₹5/litre, diesel by ₹3**, pressuring state OMCs (IOCL, BPCL, HPCL) to follow—despite lagging inventory adjustments and 50–60-day supply chains.
- **Health Tech Funding**: **Age Care Labs raised ₹85 cr** (Series B), targeting expansion of at-home elderly care and emergency support—backed by Rainmatter, Pegasus Finvest, and Shrem Group.
