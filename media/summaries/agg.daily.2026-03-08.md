The Rameshwaram Café, originating in Bengaluru, opened a 6,500-sq-ft outlet in Mumbai’s historic Eros building. The space was designed to replicate a temple-style dining environment, featuring banana-leaf service. The menu included traditional South Indian dishes such as masala dosa, ghee podi idli, pongal, bisi bele bath, and fresh chutneys, alongside a unique “soft” filter-coffee affogato. Operations span from early morning to late night, with daily production capacity reaching up to 20,000 idlis and dosas. The outlet was established to preserve regional authenticity while accommodating Mumbai’s continuous lifestyle.

Krishna Institute of Medical Sciences (KIMS) launched two super-specialty hospitals in Bengaluru—Mahadevapura (450 beds) and Electronic City (350 beds). Each facility includes 30 advanced operation theatres, over 170 outpatient department (OPD) rooms, and more than 25 super-specialty centers covering cardiology, neurology, oncology, paediatrics, orthopaedics, ENT, OB-GYN, general surgery, internal medicine, critical care, and chronic disease management. The hospitals are designed to offer 24/7 primary care, advanced diagnostics, and comprehensive treatment, reinforcing KIMS’s strategy to expand its healthcare network across high-growth Indian markets.
