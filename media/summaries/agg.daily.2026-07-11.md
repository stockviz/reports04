Emcure’s Gennova Biopharmaceuticals has agreed to transfer its mRNA business, contributing 0.71% of its ₹911.58 crore revenue, to Immunoscript Life Science Private Ltd via a slump-sale for ₹139 crore. The transaction, scheduled for completion by 17 July 2026, is being executed under the BTA and not a Scheme of Arrangement. Immunoscript, promoted by former Gennova director Sanjay Singh, will focus on precision immunology, utilizing bio-engineering, artificial intelligence, and scalable manufacturing for next-generation vaccines and therapeutics.

Avenue Supermarts reported a 14.9% year-on-year revenue increase to ₹18,794.5 crore in Q1 FY27, with consolidated net profit rising 11.33% to ₹860.4 crore. Profit margins declined slightly to 4.6% from 4.7%. Same-store sales growth slowed to 5.5%, down from 7.1%, with flat performance in mature metro stores and continued expansion in smaller cities. The chain added three new outlets, reaching a total of 503. DMart Ready reduced its footprint to 11 cities, exiting seven underperforming markets. Revenue per square foot decreased by 2.4%, attributed to lower productivity in older stores amid intensified competition from quick-commerce platforms. The board approved up to ₹1,000 crore in NCDs, with the purpose of fund utilization unspecified.

Swiggy Instamart faced regulatory scrutiny after the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) issued a notice demanding detailed explanations, root-cause analyses, and corrective measures related to quality assurance, inventory management, hygiene, and consumer complaint handling. The directive followed concerns over food safety and operational practices. Additionally, Swiggy Instamart was required to address issues arising from its AI image-generation tool, Muse, which had previously been withdrawn due to privacy violations involving user data.

Meta’s Muse AI tool, which generated images using publicly posted Instagram photos without explicit consent, was discontinued following global backlash. The incident highlighted gaps in existing privacy legislation and underscored the need for transparent opt-in mechanisms, user control, and stricter regulatory oversight. Experts emphasized that public posting does not equate to unrestricted rights for AI training, calling for enforceable frameworks to govern data use in AI development.

Typhoon Bavi, with sustained winds of up to 162 km/h, caused widespread disruption across Ishigaki Island and Taiwan. Airport and port operations were suspended, over 40,000 flights were canceled, and emergency preparations were underway. Taiwan authorities implemented school and office closures, flight suspensions, and sandbagging efforts in low-lying areas of Taipei due to forecasted heavy rainfall of up to one meter, posing risks of flash floods and landslides.

HDFC Bank’s CEO Sashidhar Jagdishan described the abrupt resignation of former chairman Atanu Chakraborty in March as a governance challenge. The bank initiated an independent legal review involving Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati and Wadia Ghandy & Co., overseen by a special committee of independent directors. The review examined communications and board records from the two years preceding the resignation and concluded that Chakraborty’s claims of mis-selling Credit Suisse perpetual bonds and ethical conflict lacked factual basis. Chakraborty declined to cooperate, labeling the review a “superfluous exercise.” The bank affirmed its commitment to transparent corporate governance.

Analog AI, established in early 2024 in Abu Dhabi, formed a 50/50 joint venture with Hyderabad-based Megha Engineering (MEAL) to deploy AI-driven solutions in traffic management, smart cities, and public infrastructure across India. The partnership leverages Analog’s statistical intelligence models and MEAL’s execution capabilities. Pilot projects and R&D activities commenced in Hyderabad, with engagement underway with the Telangana government and private sector. The joint venture anticipates hundreds of millions in investment and plans to recruit top Indian talent within six months.

Vibhuti Arora transformed personal health challenges into a scalable wellness enterprise, founding India’s first face-yoga school and establishing a franchise network generating approximately ₹1.5 crore annually per center. The brand integrates yoga, Ayurveda, and non-invasive treatments such as lymphatic drainage and jawline tightening, targeting a market gap between salons and dermatological clinics. With over 1,200 certified instructors and a 3 million-strong social media following, House of Beauty plans to expand to 10–12 new centers in tier-1 and tier-2 cities, including Mumbai, Gurgaon, Noida, Bilaspur, Bangalore, and Kolkata.

Mercedes-Benz and BMW adopted divergent strategies in India’s premium automotive segment. Mercedes-Benz reported a 14% EV share and launched India’s first plug-in hybrid, emphasizing hybrid models to ease range anxiety among affluent customers. BMW achieved a 78% surge in EV sales, with EVs accounting for 26% of total sales, and dismissed hybrids as unnecessary and cost-inefficient. The contrasting approaches reflect differing market positioning: Mercedes prioritizing hybrid-enabled ultra-premium models, while BMW pursuing aggressive EV pricing to gain market share.

Venture-builder 100 into 100 launched its second $100 million fund targeting the creation of 50 climate-focused startups across India and Southeast Asia in clean energy, agriculture, logistics, and advanced materials. The hands-on venture-building model, spanning six months, emphasizes early validation of problem-solution fit, resulting in a 50% higher graduation rate compared to traditional venture capital. Initial investments of ₹5–8 lakh per startup are planned, with follow-on funding.
