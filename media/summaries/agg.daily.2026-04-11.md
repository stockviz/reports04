U.S.–Iran ceasefire negotiations were initiated in Islamabad, with Vice President JD Vance leading the U.S. delegation and Iranian officials, including the parliament speaker and foreign minister, participating. Iran has demanded inclusion of Lebanon in the ceasefire, sanctions relief, control over the Strait of Hormuz, and compensation for war damage. The U.S. requires the Strait of Hormuz to be cleared of mines and Iran to halt uranium enrichment before releasing frozen assets. Pakistan’s role is limited to hosting, with no significant influence on outcomes. The talks are described as tense and strategic, resembling Cold War–era negotiations in neutral venues. The outcome will determine regional stability and global market implications, with consequences for India’s Gulf diaspora, remittances, energy supplies, and economic stability.

A temporary ceasefire has been declared, but a durable resolution depends on resolving complex, interlinked issues through negotiation. The upcoming U.S.–Iran leadership meeting in Islamabad is considered pivotal, with Pakistan committed to ensuring its success due to its global stability implications.

Reliance Industries has sought Indian government approval to import Iranian crude on four U.S.-sanctioned tankers, aiming to comply with U.S. and Indian regulations as a key sanctions waiver nears expiration. The shipping ministry has cleared the tankers—Kaviz, Lenore, Felicity, and Hedy—for potential cargo. Reliance also holds a U.S. license for Venezuelan crude and is adjusting to reduced Russian oil volumes following EU restrictions.

Commercial electric air taxis, developed by Joby Aviation and integrated with Uber’s Elevate platform, are scheduled to launch in Dubai by the end of 2026. The e-VTOL aircraft feature six rotors with redundancy, carry a pilot and four passengers, achieve speeds up to 200 mph, and operate on a 100-mile electric range with noise levels of approximately 65 dB. Uber’s Elevate program, acquired by Joby in 2021, will initially use Blade’s helicopter routes before transitioning to electric taxis post-certification. Dubai’s independent flight approval authority and severe traffic congestion make it an ideal launch market, with competitors including Archer Aviation and China’s Ehang advancing similar services.

Burma Burma, a Burmese-cuisine restaurant chain, raised over ₹38 crore from Nijin Capital, Jorans Capital, and Koharan Wealth, achieving a valuation of ₹500 crore. The company reported ₹200 crore in annual recurring revenue, operates 21 outlets across seven cities, and maintains a 50% year-on-year growth rate. Expansion plans include opening stores every 5–6 km in current metropolitan areas and entering 15–20 new cities within 36 months, targeting 3,000 sq ft outlets with 90–100 seats. The brand maintains a vegetarian, alcohol-free menu prepared fresh without central kitchens, aiming to achieve 25% outlet profitability and 12–14% EBITDA margins. Input cost increases have been managed by switching most outlets to induction cooking, with no price adjustments.

A federal judge issued a restraining order at the CFTC’s request, halting Arizona’s 20-count indictment of prediction-market platform Kalshi. The CFTC argued that state prosecutions cannot override its federal authority over derivatives and prediction markets. A prior judge had allowed the state case to proceed, but the new order affirms federal preemption. The CFTC is also pursuing similar legal actions against Connecticut and Illinois.
