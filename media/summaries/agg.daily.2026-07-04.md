The Indian government, through the Ministry of Information and Broadcasting and the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology, has directed Telegram to implement platform-wide measures to prevent the distribution of pirated movies and OTT content within 15 days, requiring submission of a compliance report. The directive emphasizes the need to address repeat infringers, including channels, groups, bots, and administrators, and reflects a coordinated enforcement effort to protect intellectual property rights, support the creator economy, and mitigate revenue losses for the film industry, broadcasters, and OTT providers. The move follows previous actions against digital platforms hosting illegal content and underscores India’s commitment to digital compliance.

Tribe Amrapali, a jewellery retailer, plans to expand its physical footprint by opening 8–10 new stores, with a strategic focus on airport locations that contribute approximately half of its offline sales. The brand continues to emphasize in-person shopping experiences to drive online engagement and has observed a shift in consumer preference from gold to silver and fashion jewellery due to elevated gold prices. The company operates 39 Tribe Amrapali and 15 Amrapali stores, produces over 200,000 jewellery pieces monthly, and has the capacity to increase output by up to 50%. It collaborates with approximately 1,600 artisans and has expanded into lifestyle offerings through its Tribe Home division, which includes home décor and gifting items.

Automakers including Maruti Suzuki, Toyota Kirloskar, and Hyundai have been engaged in discussions with government officials regarding public concerns over the E20 ethanol-petrol blend. Testing conducted by ARAI, SIAM, and the manufacturers confirms that E20 is safe for vehicles manufactured before 2023, with no evidence of engine damage or increased insurance claims. A reduction in fuel efficiency of 3–4% is attributed to ethanol’s lower calorific value, consistent with engineering expectations. Maruti Suzuki and Toyota have affirmed the presence of a safety buffer in E20-compatible vehicles, while Hyundai reported no customer complaints. ARAI is currently evaluating E22 and E25 blends, with results expected by December. The E20 initiative, based on years of scientific research, aims to reduce crude oil imports and align with global ethanol adoption practices.

OpenAI is reportedly in preliminary discussions with U.S. government entities to offer a 5% ownership stake, potentially through Trump-linked accounts, to enable public financial participation in AI-driven economic growth. The proposal, which aligns with CEO Sam Altman’s vision for a public wealth fund, is intended to foster broader societal benefits from AI development and encourage similar arrangements by other technology firms. The initiative faces scrutiny over potential conflicts of interest and political motivations, though proponents cite the 9.9% Intel stake under the CHIPS Act as a precedent. The proposal remains non-binding and would require congressional approval.

A heatwave in New York City on July 2 raised temperatures to 100°F, prompting public use of cooling stations, shaded public spaces, and air-conditioned facilities such as Battery Park and Petco’s Union Square store. Tourists and residents adapted to the conditions, with pet owners prioritizing hydration and cooling gear for animals. Petco reported increased demand for pet cooling products. City authorities issued advisories urging hydration, avoidance of midday outdoor exposure, and monitoring of vulnerable individuals and pets.

Maruti Suzuki’s Rahul Bharti stated that E10-compatible vehicles, including those from 2010, show no mechanical degradation, corrosion, or increased insurance claims when operated on E20 fuel. Analysis of servicing data from over 2.5 crore vehicles supports this conclusion, with any mileage reduction attributed to ethanol’s lower energy content and limited to 3–3.5%. The company attributes widespread misinformation to fuel adulteration rather than the E20 specification and anticipates future improvements in fuel efficiency through higher-octane ethanol formulations and next-generation engine designs.

Toyota Kirloskar Motors’ Vikram Gulati confirmed that no customer complaints or insurance claims have been linked to E20 fuel, with isolated incidents attributed to general fuel contamination. The transition from E10 to E20 was based on independent scientific studies, and future flexibility is expected through the development of flex-fuel vehicles capable of handling variable ethanol blends.
