Rajesh Exports Ltd: Massive Accounting Irregularities Unveiled by SEBI

RAJESHEXPO

SEBI’s investigation reveals that Rajesh Exports Ltd (REL) fabricated approximately ₹15.4 lakh crore of revenue between FY 21‑25, inflating its FY 25 revenue to ₹4.2 lakh crore despite actual earnings likely near ₹3,000 crore. The group’s consolidated figures are driven almost entirely by the Swiss subsidiary Valcambi SA, whose holding company recorded gross gold purchases and refined‑gold sales as revenue, a treatment deemed untenable. Auditors BSD & Co relied on un‑audited subsidiary memoranda, while KPMG’s Swiss audit covered only Valcambi’s value‑addition books, leaving key subsidiaries, including REL‑Singapore, unaudited. Additional red flags include an unsubstantiated ₹1,000 crore African mine purchase, a 55‑fold surge in operating cash flow driven by a drastic drop in trade receivables and a spike in payables, and a massive rise in retail shareholding (1.6 % to >14 %) despite share price crashes. LIC still holds about 10.8 % of REL’s stock. The findings suggest systematic revenue inflation and severe lapses in audit oversight
