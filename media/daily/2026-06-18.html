Crude Oil Slides to 3‑Month Lows Amid Iran‑US MoU and Outlook for Oversupply

Crude oil prices have fallen to three‑month lows, dropping about 15% in the past week and 28% in June, retreating from $120 to around $78 per barrel. The decline follows the signing of an Iran‑US memorandum of understanding, which signals an end to the regional conflict and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, as well as the termination of the U.S. sanctions waiver on Iranian oil. Satellite data show two Iranian super‑tankers (Dona and Hero 2) have breached the blockade with 3.8 million barrels, and roughly 60 vessels—about 120 million barrels—remain in the strait, poised for market release. The International Energy Agency warns of a potential 5 million‑barrel‑per‑day surplus by 2027, prompting major banks to slash price forecasts: Goldman Sachs sees $80/barrel for Q4 2026 and $75 in 2027; Citi projects $75 in Q3 2026, $70 by Q4, and $65 in 2027; JP Morgan expects $60 in 2026; Barclays forecasts $100 this year and $80 next. Overall, analysts now expect Brent in the $60‑$80 range for the near term
