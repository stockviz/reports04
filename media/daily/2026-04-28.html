RBI Finalizes 3‑Stage ECL Provisioning Model, Hits PSU Banks Hard

CENTRALBK TV18BRDCST BANKINDIA PNB SBIN INDUSINDBK ICICIBANK AXISBANK

The Reserve Bank of India has issued final guidelines, effective April 1 2027, for a three‑stage asset classification and provisioning framework based on the Expected Credit Loss model. Stage 1 assets require 12‑month loss provisions (25‑40 bps, up to 1% for unsecured retail), while Stage 2 and Stage 3 require lifetime provisions, with a minimum floor of 5% for most exposures (1.5% for housing/GOI loans). No deadline extensions were granted, but banks get a transition relief to phase the impact into Tier‑1 capital over FY 28‑31. Public‑sector banks, with thinner capital buffers, face a one‑time net‑worth hit of 5‑10% and a 55‑130 bps drag on Tier‑1, whereas large private banks are largely insulated. Analysts estimate a one‑time profit reduction of ₹50‑60 billion for the banking system, but note improved risk governance and greater eligibility for foreign capital. Governance and supervisory scrutiny over the subjective provisioning methodology are expected to intensify
