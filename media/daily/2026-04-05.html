DABUR MARICO AWL

UBS Experts Discuss West Asia Conflict’s Impact on the Indian Rupee, Economy and Markets

TV18BRDCST

The UBS panel—Gautam Chachurya (global markets), Tanvi Gupta Jain (chief India economist) and Divya Nagarajan (head of India research)—assessed how the escalating war in West Asia could pressure India’s currency, current‑account deficit and growth. Tanvi warned that RBI measures offer only temporary relief; the external deficit may stay high and funding gaps could persist, keeping rupee depreciation likely. Divya noted that supply‑chain disruptions, especially in natural‑gas‑dependent sectors, are still early and their full impact is uncertain. Gautam highlighted that valuations remain stretched, AI‑driven inflows are missing India, and despite some contrarian buying, EM strategists stay underweight. The team outlined three oil‑price scenarios (short‑term de‑escalation, medium‑term disruption, prolonged shock) with corresponding GDP growth ranging from 7% to 5.5% and inflation potentially breaching 5%. They stressed the importance of currency stability, the need for RBI’s policy tools, and that any prolonged conflict could erode growth, raise inflation and weigh on equities, while short‑term opportunities may exist in tech and banks if the situation resolves quickly

Biofuel Circle’s Rural Biomass Bank Network Shows Profitability and Sustainable Growth

Over the past three years, Pune‑based Biofuel Circle has set up 70 biomass banks, each costing about ₹3 crore and generating ₹3‑4 crore annually. The first ten units are already profitable; the next 25 are on track, with typical units becoming EBITDA‑positive in 12‑18 months and achieving payback in 3‑4 years. These banks act as independent micro‑enterprises, creating local jobs and structured markets for agricultural residues. The company stresses that subsidies should accelerate investment rather than sustain operations, and it relies on a digital marketplace, GIS/GPS intelligence, and transparent pricing to build reliable supply chains. Biofuel Circle does not depend on carbon credits, viewing them only as additional incentives, and aims to reduce fossil fuel reliance through scalable farm‑to‑fuel‑to‑soil models integrated with other renewables

Greeley Meatpacking Strike Ends as Workers Push for Better Pay and Health Care

INDSWFTLTD

After a three‑week strike at JBS USA’s Swift Beef plant in Greeley, Colorado, workers agreed to return to work as the company pledged to resume negotiations. Union members, represented by UFCW Local 7, are demanding higher wages and improved health‑care benefits, arguing that recent offers fall short of inflation and Colorado’s higher cost of living. The halt marks the first U.S. slaughterhouse strike since 1985, and JBS has reiterated its final contract offer while preparing to ramp up operations
