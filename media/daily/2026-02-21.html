Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei on India Market, Partnerships, AI Impact, and Future Plans

TVVISION INFY

In an exclusive CNBC TV 18 interview at the India AI Impact Summit, Dario Amodei emphasizes Anthropic’s focus on enterprise collaborations rather than consumer products, highlighting India’s vibrant ecosystem of developers, startups, and large firms as key partners. He stresses that Anthropic aims to complement—not replace—the existing IT services industry, describing its technology as “Lego blocks” that enable joint value creation, citing a newly announced partnership with Infosys and indicating further collaborations are in discussion. Amodei acknowledges AI‑driven job disruption, predicting significant changes but also new roles such as “forward‑deployed engineers” to help customers adopt AI solutions, stressing the need for proactive reskilling. Regarding AI safety, he outlines extensive stress‑testing of models, likening it to automotive crash tests, and notes ongoing concerns about autonomous weaponry and mass surveillance, especially with U.S. national‑security applications. He discusses Anthropic’s financial outlook, noting that while each model is currently profitable, continued investment in next‑generation models requires substantial capital, and the firm has recently raised $30 billion. Finally, Amodei projects continued advances across domains—software engineering, finance, biomedicine, and cybersecurity—without a single breakthrough, and reaffirms Anthropic’s commitment to democratic values and further Indian partnerships

Insights from the FE India Awards 2026: Creativity, AI, and the Future of Advertising

CCHHL 3MINDIA NESTLEIND

At the 2026 FE India Awards, industry leaders reflected on how rapid disruption—driven by AI, changing consumer habits, and evolving media touchpoints—is reshaping advertising. They stressed that while AI is a powerful tool that accelerates ideation and execution, true creativity remains a uniquely human capability. The conversation highlighted the symbiotic relationship between award-winning work and business results, emphasizing that effectiveness sells and that awards, client growth, and creative excellence are interlinked. Talent retention was discussed, noting a shift from scarcity to abundance, with agencies focusing on culture and growth opportunities to keep top talent. Leaders also outlined their vision for a future‑focused Ad Club, stressing the need for inclusive, high‑energy platforms that spotlight great ideas. Overall, the panel underscored the importance of blending technology with human insight to drive impactful, effective campaigns in a dynamic market
