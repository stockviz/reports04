Oil Glut Persists Amid Middle East Tensions

RELIANCE IOC NDTV

Global oil markets remain oversupplied despite heightened geopolitical risks, with speculators driving price movements more than physical availability. Indian refiners are technically capable of handling heavy Venezuelan crude, but high shipping, financing, and blending costs limit its attractiveness versus cheaper Gulf or Russian supplies. Sanctions on Russia and Iran have left large inventories stranded at sea, reinforcing the supply glut. Consequently, Brent crude is expected to stay under pressure, and the surplus is unlikely to dissipate soon

FitPass: India’s “Uber for Gym Memberships” – How Siblings Akshay and Anushree Verma Disrupted the Fitness Market

FitPass, founded by brother‑sister duo Akshay and Anushree Verma, offers a single subscription (FitPass 360) that unlocks access to 12,000 gyms, studios and wellness services across 150 Indian cities for ₹2,999/month. Inspired by the fragmented, annual‑pay gym model they encountered during a sabbatical in New York, the siblings built a tech‑enabled platform that combines gym access, AI‑driven coaching, nutritionist support, tele‑medicine and habit‑building rewards. Their data‑driven approach has flipped industry retention from 25 % to 78 %, growing to 11 million active “FitStars” who work out on average eight times a month. FitPass also collaborates with governments on fitness‑related policy, influences GST changes, and partners with corporate wellness programs. While traditional gyms struggle with high capex and low flexibility, FitPass acts as an aggregator without owning assets, focusing on consumer choice and habit formation, even paying users cash rewards for consistent activity. The founders credit family support, rigorous research, and persistence through challenges like demonetisation, GST reforms, and COVID‑19 for their sustained growth
