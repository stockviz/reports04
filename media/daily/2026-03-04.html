Oil Price Spikes Amid Geopolitical Tensions: Implications for India

5PAISA HDFCBANK

Recent geopolitical flare‑ups have triggered sharp one‑day jumps in Brent crude (8‑12% in a session), echoing past crises such as the Gulf War and Ukraine‑Russia conflict. In India, each $10 rise in oil price typically widens the current‑account deficit by ~0.5% of GDP, lifts retail inflation by 35 bps, raises WPI by ~130 bps, and trims GDP growth by 15‑20 bps. Historically, oil shocks have also devalued the rupee (e.g., to ₹24.58 during the Gulf War, over ₹80 in the Ukraine war). India’s buffers include 25‑day crude stocks, 2‑3‑week LNG reserves, 25‑30‑day LPG supplies, and $723 billion in foreign‑exchange reserves. However, prolonged conflict could strain the current account, keep rupee pressure high, fuel inflation, and complicate RBI’s rate policy. Short‑term shocks are more manageable given the existing buffers
