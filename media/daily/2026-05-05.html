Retirement Corpus Planning & Portfolio Decluttering: Key Takeaways from MF Corner

3MINDIA FRACTAL

The episode emphasized that building a retirement corpus is only the first step; investors must adjust for inflation, health costs, and longevity to determine how long the corpus will last. A cash‑flow calculator helps gauge sustainability, while regular income streams (pension, rent, etc.) should be factored in. Realistic targets often run into crores, and the required corpus grows sharply with inflation‑adjusted expenses. Health planning includes porting group policies, adding top‑up or unlimited covers, and maintaining a dedicated health‑savings fund separate from emergency reserves.

On portfolio management, experts warned against “clutter” – holding too many mutual funds dilutes returns due to overlapping holdings and higher monitoring/tax costs. A practical rule is to keep the total number of funds under ten, ideally five‑six for a retirement portfolio, with one fund per major category (large‑cap, mid‑cap, flexi‑cap/small‑cap, gold, debt). Diversify across styles (active vs passive) and fund houses, but avoid multiple funds in the same category. To declutter a bloated portfolio, list each fund, its category, and allocation; consolidate overlapping funds, exit under‑performers, and consider tax implications. The goal is a clean, manageable portfolio that maximizes returns while staying aligned with risk tolerance and investment horizon
