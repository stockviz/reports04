Government Raises ONGC Chairman Age Limit and Sets Fixed Tenure

OIL ONGC BPCL

The government has lifted the maximum entry age for the ONGC chairmanship to 59 and introduced a three‑year term, extendable by up to two years after a performance review, widening the candidate pool. Applications close 21 July, with submissions due 30 July. The selection will be made by an oil‑ministry search‑cum‑selection committee rather than the usual PESB process. The incumbent, Arun Kumar Singh, will vacate the post on 7 December 2026 after an extended tenure that sees him serve beyond 60 on a contractual basis. Tata Steel Plans ₹20,000 cr FY27 Capex Focused on Indian Expansion and New Technologies

TATASTEEL

Tata Steel will raise FY27 capital spending to roughly ₹20,000 crore—about 38 % above FY26’s ₹14,559 crore—allocating 60 % to India. The plan emphasizes a mix of sustenance, downstream, infrastructure, and technology projects, notably expansions in tinplate, wire, the HRPGL at Tarapur, and new coke ovens at Jamshedpur, alongside continued mining, supply‑chain, and sustainability investments. With a current consolidated capacity of over 36 MTPA (27.35 MTPA in India), the firm targets >50 MTPA long‑term, adding >12 MTPA in India, including a 4.8 MTPA Phase‑I expansion at NINL and a phased 6 MTPA greenfield steel hub in Gadchiroli. The strategy aims to lift Indian steelmaking capacity to 40 MTPA, supported by low‑emission routes such as the newly commissioned 0.75 MT electric arc furnace in Punjab

Greaves Cotton’s Diversification Begins to Pay Off

SHAREINDIA CUMMINSIND CONTROLPR BCG GREAVESCOT KIRLOSBROS OLAELEC

Greaves Cotton Ltd has reduced its reliance on three‑wheeler diesel engines from about 80% a decade ago to just over 25%, shifting to a broader engineering and industrial solutions mix that now includes gensets, mechanical cables, electric motors and engine exports. The company reorganised into three verticals—mobility solutions, energy solutions and industrial solutions—driving an 18% revenue rise to ₹3,437 crore in FY26 and a 76% jump in EBITDA to ₹240 crore, with net profit nearly doubling to ₹107 crore. Mobility solutions now account for two‑thirds of revenue, energy solutions a quarter, and industrial solutions the remainder. Greaves is pursuing further acquisitions and joint ventures in defence, aerospace, auto components and energy, while its electric‑vehicle arm Ampere, though still loss‑making, is improving margins. The diversified portfolio is expected to deliver 16‑18% organic revenue growth and reduce vulnerability to regulatory shocks and market cycles. Shares are up about 17% in 2026 despite a recent dip
