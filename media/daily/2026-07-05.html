India’s Private‑Bank Leadership Shuffle: CFO Musical Chairs, Governance Overhaul & Growth Challenges

KOTAKBANK ICICIBANK AXISBANK IDFCFIRSTB RBLBANK BANDHANBNK HDFCBANK INDUSINDBK MINDACORP

India’s private banking sector is undergoing a wave of senior‑leadership changes, most notably a “musical chairs” rotation of CFOs—Axis Bank’s Puneet Sharma moving to HDFC Bank, Bandhan’s Rajiv Mantri likely heading to Axis, and several CEOs and chairpersons also exiting. The churn reflects three intertwined issues: tight governance demands, weak succession planning, and a scarce pool of seasoned banking CFOs who now need expertise across capital, liquidity, Basel compliance, investor relations, technology and M&A. HDFC Bank’s appointment of former finance bureaucrat Rajiv Kumar as part-time chairman underscores the preference for regulatory insiders amid recent governance lapses. Meanwhile, stock performance shows PSU banks vastly outpacing private banks over the past five years (PSU index CAGR ~37% vs private ~10%), driven by market‑share gains, improved credit costs, and higher ROA. However, the sustainability of PSU outperformance is debated due to reliance on SLR funding, treasury profits, and higher leverage. New private‑bank leaders will need to prioritize technology‑driven growth, deposit competition, fintech integration, robust risk management, and navigating increased FDI inflows and tighter regulatory scrutiny to revive profitability and shareholder value

India’s Outbound Travel Boom Defies West Asia Turmoil

RATEGAIN THOMASCOOK

Indian tourists continue to travel abroad in large numbers despite flight reroutes caused by the West Asia conflict. In 2025, 32.71 million Indians made overseas trips—a 5.9 % rise over 2024—and leisure accounted for about 43 % of departures. The Jan‑Mar 2026 quarter saw 19.07 million passenger movements, with the UAE, Thailand, Singapore, Vietnam and the US as top destinations. Thailand, Japan, South Korea, Australia, Singapore, South Africa and the US all reported record or rising Indian visitor numbers and spending, with India becoming a priority source market. Outbound tourism spend is projected to grow from $18.8 bn in 2024 to $55.4 bn by 2034 (CAGR 11.4 %). Drivers include rising disposable incomes, better connectivity, easier visas, and a shift toward experiential, wellness, culinary and adventure travel, with tourism boards expanding outreach beyond metros to tier‑I cities

Greaves Cotton’s Diversification Begins to Pay Off

Greaves Cotton Ltd has reduced its reliance on three‑wheeler diesel engines from about 80% a decade ago to just over 25%, shifting to a broader engineering and industrial solutions mix that now includes gensets, mechanical cables, electric motors and engine exports.
