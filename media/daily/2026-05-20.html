Bharti Airtel Introduces Priority Postpaid Service with 5G Network Slicing

AIRTELPP IDEA JIOFIN

Bharti Airtel is rolling out a “priority postpaid” offering that leverages standalone 5G network slicing to give high‑value postpaid subscribers a dedicated, faster, more reliable network experience. The move aims to boost customer stickiness, attract prepaid users to higher‑margin postpaid plans, and lure postpaid customers from rivals such as Vodafone Idea and Reliance Jio, whose offerings lack comparable dedicated‑network features. Although tariffs remain unchanged, the shift is expected to raise average revenue per user (ARPU) and reduce churn. Analysts see minimal regulatory risk, noting that network slicing differs from earlier net‑neutrality concerns, but the telecom regulator’s stance remains to be confirmed

Muthoot FinCorp’s IPO Push: From Gold‑Loan Giant to Digital‑Enabled Financial Services

MUTHOOTFIN MUTHOOTMF MOBIKWIK MANAPPURAM IIFL

Muthoot FinCorp is seeking to raise up to ₹4,000 cr through an IPO, positioning itself as a diversified, technology‑enabled financial services firm rather than just a gold‑loan NBFC. The company operates 3,800 branches, holds ₹56,185 cr in AUM and posted ₹1,640 cr net profit for FY‑26. Its fintech app, Muthoot FinCorp ONE, has 7 million downloads and 2 million monthly active users, with a 19% DAU‑to‑MAU ratio and a product‑per‑customer ratio of 1.93, indicating growing cross‑sell potential. While the loan book is now 87% secured (down from ~99% three years ago), the firm emphasizes maintaining credit discipline as it expands into unsecured, personal and marketplace lending. To diversify funding, Muthoot plans additional ₹8,000 cr via NCDs and a ₹30,000 cr commercial paper programme, cutting bank borrowings to ~60% of its debt mix. The success of the IPO will hinge on investor confidence in the company’s ability to scale digital reach without compromising its secured‑lending foundation

Oil India Targets ₹10,000 Cr Exploration Spend, 4 Mt Crude Output by FY27

ONGC BPCL OIL REGAAL

Oil India Ltd (OIL) plans to raise its exploration and production capex to over ₹10,000 crore in FY27, a >10% increase from FY26’s ₹8,900 crore. The company aims to lift crude output to about 4 million tonnes (up from 3.45 mt in FY26) and will drill more than 100 wells this fiscal, shifting to a 60:40 split of exploratory versus development wells. OIL has mobilised two charter‑hired rigs, added two more, and introduced a new long‑term rig‑deployment model to bring in newer equipment. It is in talks with global majors for joint bids on blocks secured under OALP‑IX and upcoming OALP‑X/XI rounds, and has signed a technology service agreement with TotalEnergies for deep‑water exploration. The $20 bn Mozambique LNG project, where OIL holds a 4% stake in a consortium with ONGC Videsh (16%) and BPCL (10%), has resumed construction and targets commissioning by late‑2028/early‑2029. OIL also reports recovery of over 91% of its $1 bn investment in Russian Vankorneft and Taas‑Yuryakh assets, expecting full recovery by FY27, and posted a 62% YoY rise in Q4FY26 net profit to ₹2,424 crore, with operating revenue up 4.4% to ₹10,013 crore

ARIHANTCAP HDFCAMC AVONMORE BFINVEST TCNSBRANDS IITL ORIENTBELL

Hedge fund Saba Capital Management, now holding nearly 30% of Impax Environmental Markets (IEM), is set to oust its board and likely wrest the investment mandate from Impax Asset Management. Saba plans to replace Impax, possibly run IEM itself, and may merge it with other discounted UK trusts it controls, following a similar play with Edinburgh Worldwide Investment Trust.
