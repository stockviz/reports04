Growth Of Large Cap IT Companies Modest, Midcaps Outperform | Editors’ Roundtable

OFSS TCS ALLDIGI HCL-INSYS BHARATFORG CALSOFT-RE TATACAP LTTS PERSISTENT

  • Large‑cap outlook:
    • TCS expected to deliver only modest growth (≈0.5% Q/Q, flat overall).
    • HCL Tech likely to outpace TCS thanks to its higher‑margin products business and seasonal strength.
  • Mid‑cap outlook:
    • Mid‑caps projected to outperform large caps, with an average Q/Q growth of about 3.5%.
    • Co‑forge and Persistent are the preferred mid‑caps, each delivering strong results.
  • AI revenue tracking:
    • TCS disclosed AI revenue of $1.5 bn and highlighted data‑investment plans.
    • HCL Tech reported $100 m AI revenue in Q2.
    • Currently only TCS and HCL Tech publish AI metrics; broader adoption by other IT firms is anticipated but not yet confirmed.
  • Recent market performance:
    • Nifty IT index down ~12% YoY.
    • Top mid‑cap performers (↑4% YoY): Persistent, Emphasis.
    • Mid‑caps moving in line with the index: LTTS, Tata LXE, Zensar, Co‑forge (post‑encore deal).
    • Under‑performers (down 20‑35% YoY): Billa Soft, Mastek, Scion, Oracle.
  • Potential contrarian play:
    • Consider the under‑performing stocks (e.g., Billa Soft, Mastek, Scion, Oracle) as possible contrarian bets, given their steep declines relative to the broader IT sector.

Q3FY26 Earnings Preview: Here’s What To Expect | Editors’ Roundtable

LTF IDEA ABB RELIANCE KOTAKBANK MARUTI BRITANNIA ETERNAL POLYCAB ULTRACEMCO

Q3 FY26 earnings are expected to be soft, mirroring the past six quarters, with single‑digit revenue and profit growth and modest margin expansion. Kotak projects a FY26 EPS of 1,072 and FY27 EPS of 1,267.

Sector outlook - Financials (lenders): Quiet quarter, no major surprises; credit‑card stress easing.
- NBFCs: Strong quarter; AUM growth of 4‑5% in vehicle‑finance and housing‑finance firms.
- Staples: Potential swing, demand up ~7% YoY (vs 4% in Q2).
- Retail: Generally subdued; jewellery and cable businesses expected to hold up.
- Auto: Volume growth strong, but higher commodity costs compress gross margins, offsetting operating‑leverage gains.
- IT: Flat, no fireworks, especially among large‑caps.
- Pharma: Domestic sales robust; US business flat; generic companies may post weak results.
- Cement: Weak EBITDA and contraction, except UltraTech and JK Cement.
- Energy: Mixed; Reliance OMC could benefit from higher GRMs; slight EBITDA improvement expected.
- Metals: Steel EBITDA likely to decline; non‑ferrous sector good except Novelis (plant fire).
- Industrials: EBITDA growth ~13% (vs 9% in Q2), the only sector showing clear growth.
- Internet platforms: Recent price drops, but potential earnings growth and notable capex discussion.

Citi’s surprise expectations - Positive surprises: Maruti, AGDFC Bank, Britannia, Polycab, Eternal.
- Negative surprises: ABB, Ambuja Cements, L&T, Mindtree, JSPL.

Overall market reaction is expected to be muted due to low expectations.
