HUL Q3FY26 Earnings Review

3MINDIA

India’s CPI Rebase Explained: Radhika Rao Breaks Down What Changes

AXISBANK

Senior economist Radhika Rao explains that India’s new CPI series involves a major methodological overhaul, expanding sample size, updating weights, reducing food’s weight by about 9 percentage points and raising core’s weight by roughly 10 points, making inflation more representative of household price pressures. She notes the upcoming GDP rebasing (around 27 Feb) may slightly boost growth figures. The new CPI will likely show a modest increase (≈40‑50 bps) versus the old series, but not enough to alter RBI policy, as the central bank focuses on forward‑looking inflation. Overall, the revised index offers a more realistic gauge of current consumption patterns, improving policy relevance without causing dramatic shifts in inflation readings or monetary decisions
