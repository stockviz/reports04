JSW Develops In‑House Tech to Compete for India’s Electric Bus Contracts

ARROWGREEN JSWCEMENT EICHERMOT HYUNDAI TATAMOTORS BCG JSWENERGY JBMA

JSW Group is building its own electric bus platform, vehicle control unit and battery packs at its Pune R&D center and through JSW Energy, aiming to reduce reliance on partners and offer more competitive bids for government tenders. The technology will be used in buses produced at a planned 90‑acre plant in Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar, Maharashtra. This move comes as the e‑bus market intensifies, with new entrants like Eka Mobility and PMI Electro Mobility outpacing legacy players. Experts say owning core tech—battery, power electronics, software, and controls—gives manufacturers cost and innovation advantages. JSW’s electric bus arm, JSW Greentech, has already submitted aggressive bids for a recent central government tender of 6,230 buses, undercutting established firms though it won no contracts this round. Led by former Greencell Mobility COO Sumit Mittal, the subsidiary is part of JSW Green Mobility, backed by Sajjan Jindal, and follows a broader ₹3,000 cr investment and a borrowing limit increase to ₹15,000 cr to expand the group’s automotive ambitions

India’s Top Corporates Boost AI‑Driven Cybersecurity Spending as Threats Rise

WIPRO AGCNET TATACOMM AIRTELPP TECHM

Palo Alto Networks sees heightened demand from India’s largest banks, telecoms and infrastructure firms amid a surge in AI‑powered cyber threats, especially following the release of Anthropic’s Claude Mythos model. President William “BJ” Jenkins highlighted a five‑fold increase in discovered vulnerabilities, prompting a shift toward machine‑level security solutions and expanding focus on small‑ and mid‑size businesses. Leveraging strong Indian tech regulations and public‑private partnerships, Palo Alto Networks aims to grow its India presence, partnering with local providers to meet rising compliance needs and counter emerging AI‑based attacks

Interface Auto explores majority stake sale at up to $300 m valuation

HILINFRA ADANITRANS PIXTRANS ATULAUTO

Interface Auto, a Gurgaon‑based maker of automotive electronic components, is in early talks to sell a majority stake that could value the company between $250 m and $300 m (≈2,830 cr). EY is advising the promoters, with interest expected from private‑equity and strategic investors. Founded in 1984, the firm offers a range of products such as door control modules, LED lighting, tire‑pressure sensors and battery sensors. The move reflects a broader trend of promoter‑led manufacturers seeking PE funding to boost growth, professionalize operations, and address succession planning amid India’s Make‑in‑India drive. Recent comparable deals include fundraises and PE investments in Tessolve, Dhoot Transmission, and Carlyle’s platform for auto component consolidation

US FDA Seeks Indian Suppliers to Alleviate Ifosfamide Shortage

ALKEM ZYDUSLIFE ABMINTLLTD INDIANB CIPLA AJANTPHARM

The US FDA has appealed to the Indian Drug Manufacturers Association (IDMA) for information on Indian companies capable of producing ifosfamide injection (1 g/3 g) to mitigate a domestic shortage caused by a technical disruption at Baxter’s contract site and supply‑chain impacts from the West Asia war. Indian firms such as Cipla, Zydus Lifesciences, Alkem Laboratories and GLS Pharma are being queried for manufacturing capabilities, FDA registration status, and market approvals. The FDA is willing to consider products from non‑FDA‑registered facilities with a strong compliance history. The shortage is expected to persist through 2026, and India’s large, FDA‑registered pharmaceutical sector, which accounts for a substantial share of US anti‑cancer drug imports, is positioned to help address the gap
