RBI’s New Upper‑Layer NBFC Classification Based on ₹1 Lakh Crore Asset Threshold

RECLTD 5PAISA IRFC PNBHOUSING NSIL PFC

RBI is moving to a simpler rule: any NBFC with assets above ₹1 lakh crore will be classified as an upper‑layer NBFC, dropping the earlier scoring‑model approach. The automatic inclusion of the top 10 NBFCs and the exemption for government NBFCs are being removed. Upper‑layer NBFCs will face tighter credit‑concentration norms (exposure limits cut to 20‑25% of Tier‑1 capital from 40%) and stricter capital, governance, and disclosure requirements. To ease the transition for government‑linked NBFCs, RBI will allow them to exclude state‑guarantee laws when calculating exposure limits, letting them maintain large exposures to state projects. Consequently, large government NBFCs such as REC, PFC, IRFC and HUDCO could enter the upper‑layer list, while smaller firms currently in the list (e.g., PNB Housing, Samman Capital) may be removed. No specific relief is mentioned for Tata Sons; its fate depends on RBI’s decision on its deregistration request

2026 Indian Monsoon Forecast Anticipation

The India Meteorological Department will release its 2026 monsoon rainfall outlook for June‑September, focusing solely on cumulative totals. Past monsoon data shows a 75% contribution to annual rainfall, vital for the 48% of India’s cultivable area lacking irrigation. In 2025, monsoon rainfall was 108% of the long‑period average (LPA), with IMD forecasts ranging 105‑106%. Rainfall 96‑104% of LPA is normal; 90‑95% is below normal; 105‑110% is above normal; >110% is excess; <90% is deficient. Private forecaster Skymet predicts a below‑normal 2026 monsoon at 94% of normal, citing a strengthening El Niño that could depress late‑season rains. Skymet expects June at 101% of normal, July 95%, August 92%, September 89%, with normal to above‑normal rainfall in the south and east‑northeast, but below‑normal in key central and north‑west grain zones.
