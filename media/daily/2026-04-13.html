2026 Indian Monsoon Forecast Anticipation

The India Meteorological Department will release its 2026 monsoon rainfall outlook for June‑September, focusing solely on cumulative totals. Past monsoon data shows a 75% contribution to annual rainfall, vital for the 48% of India’s cultivable area lacking irrigation. In 2025, monsoon rainfall was 108% of the long‑period average (LPA), with IMD forecasts ranging 105‑106%. Rainfall 96‑104% of LPA is normal; 90‑95% is below normal; 105‑110% is above normal; >110% is excess; <90% is deficient. Private forecaster Skymet predicts a below‑normal 2026 monsoon at 94% of normal, citing a strengthening El Niño that could depress late‑season rains. Skymet expects June at 101% of normal, July 95%, August 92%, September 89%, with normal to above‑normal rainfall in the south and east‑northeast, but below‑normal in key central and north‑west grain zones. Skymet’s past forecasts have been accurate, including 2023’s 94% rainfall prediction, which still yielded a rise in foodgrain production to 332.3 Mt

Dual‑Track IPO Strategy Gains Traction in India Amid Market Volatility

CLEANMAX FRACTAL AYE HEROMOTOCO IITL ADANIGREEN

Indian companies preparing for IPOs are increasingly adopting a dual‑track approach, simultaneously filing for a public listing while courting private equity or strategic buyers. The shift, driven by global uncertainty and a cooling IPO market, targets deals worth ₹500‑₹2,000 crore, offering a fallback liquidity option if valuations falter. Investment banks are leveraging private‑sale pipelines to maintain deal flow, though legal and regulatory complexities—such as engagement‑letter conflicts and information‑parity requirements—pose challenges. The trend reflects a broader move from prioritizing public listings to securing any viable exit, as many SEBI‑approved offerings risk expiry and delayed capital formation

Is A Covid-Style Moratorium Returning? Sanjay Agarwal On Why Banks Are Seeking Relief For MSMEs

BCG CARERATING

Title: RBI Tightens NBFC Oversight While MSME Moratorium Discussions Emerge

Summary: Senior Director Sanjay Agarwal (Care Edge Ratings) explained that the RBI’s recent actions focus on tighter regulation of the NBFC sector, targeting 15‑17 entities that represent roughly 78‑79% of NBFC assets. By classifying these as “upper‑layer” NBFCs, the regulator provides clear guidelines and tighter controls, enhancing oversight over a large asset base rather than increasing the number of entities under supervision. Regarding MSMEs, Agarwal noted that stressed pockets (e.g., due to export tariffs) now constitute less than 1.8% of the overall MSME loan book, reflecting significant improvement since the COVID‑19 peak. Nevertheless, banks are seeking a moratorium similar to the pandemic period to cushion emerging challenges, though such relief is expected to be short‑term. Overall, the regulatory clarity in NBFCs and a relatively healthy MSME portfolio underpin confidence in the financial system, while targeted support may be needed for specific MSME stress points.

Air India Introduces Performance‑Linked Stock Options for Employees

TCIEXP TENNIND BCG INDIGO NSIL SPICEJET

Air India will issue about 227.1 million performance‑linked stock options—0.25% of its share capital—under the PSOP 2026 to reward and retain staff, including pilots, engineers and senior managers. The options, granted at a price between the ₹4 face value and the fair market value on the grant date, will vest over one to five years, with eligibility and terms set by the board’s nomination and remuneration committee. The plan aims to drive profitability and align employee interests with the company’s growth, marking a shift from previous government‑era perks to merit‑based incentives. Singapore Airlines, a 25.10% shareholder, received pre‑emptive rights to maintain its stake. The move follows similar ESOP schemes at IndiGo, SpiceJet and Akasa Air, and comes as Air India seeks to improve its financial performance under Tata’s ownership

Tata Sons Likely Stays on RBI’s Upper‑Layer NBFC List

SHRIRAMFIN LICHSGFIN TATACAP NSIL HDBFS

The RBI’s draft circular, released on 10 April, sets a ₹1 trillion asset threshold for upper‑layer NBFCs, which Tata Sons exceeds with ₹1.75 trillion assets. Consequently, the holding company will probably remain subject to stricter oversight and mandatory listing unless RBI grants a case‑by‑case exemption or the firm deregisters as a core investment company (CIC). Tata Sons has already sought to become an unregistered CIC and eliminated public‑fund reliance, but the new rules limit RBI discretion, making exemption the only path to avoid listing. Experts note that while RBI can still exempt firms, the defined asset benchmark effectively locks Tata Sons into the upper layer

The End of an Era? Japan’s Department Stores Face Decline

GLOBAL DELTACORP AHLEAST EFCIL CHALET BCG

Seibu’s Shibuya flagship, a near‑60‑year‑old landmark, will close in September after Fortress Investment Group failed to renew its lease, symbolizing the broader struggles of Japanese department stores. Historically tied to railway operators and pivotal in shaping Shibuya’s youth culture, stores like Seibu, Sogo, and Takashimaya have seen sales fall to less than half their 1990s peak, relying heavily on tourist duty‑free purchases. The rise of e‑commerce giants such as Amazon, Rakuten, Temu, and Shein, alongside shifting consumer habits toward digital platforms, accelerates the decline. While some mixed‑use developments and experiential retail concepts aim to revive physical shopping, the sector’s future hinges on offering curated, human‑centered experiences that e‑commerce cannot replicate
