Audit Gaps and Regulatory Scrutiny in Rajesh Exports’ International Operations

TCNSBRANDS ACC RAJESHEXPO SENCO

Fresh audit concerns have emerged for Rajesh Exports Ltd after Mint’s review revealed that a limited‑purpose, non‑statutory opinion issued by KPMG Switzerland for its Swiss arm, Global Gold Refineries AG, was likely used in the group’s consolidated financial statements despite being marked “internal use only.” This raises questions about whether Indian and overseas auditors independently verified the accounts. SEBI’s June 3 interim order highlighted that over 98% of the company’s revenue could not be independently verified, citing opaque disclosures for subsidiaries in Singapore, the UAE, and Switzerland. KPMG’s engagement letter explicitly disclaimed a statutory audit, yet the opinion appears to have been relied upon for consolidation, shifting responsibility to the Indian group auditor under international standards. Other auditors—BSD & Co (India), Meridian Audit (Singapore), PV Ramana Reddy & Co (ACC Energy), and PwC/MSKA (Elest)—also faced issues: PV Ramana Reddy & Co resigned over an expired peer‑review certificate, and MSKA flagged insufficient audit evidence for Elest before resigning, with PwC later giving a clean report. Significant related‑party transfers (≈₹566 cr) between Rajesh Exports and its battery unit Elest, and back‑flows of ₹350 cr, attracted regulator scrutiny. SEBI has referred the matter to the National Financial Reporting Authority for possible action against the auditors, prompting calls to examine whether audit procedures and professional scepticism were adequate. All contacted auditors declined comment
