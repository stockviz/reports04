Why India’s Largest Caps Are Underperforming

KOTAKBANK ASIANPAINT IDEA INFY DABUR TCS WIPRO HDFCLIFE

A recent analysis of India’s 333 large‑cap stocks (market cap > ₹30,000 cr) shows that 32 have delivered negative returns and about 80 have lagged the benchmark over the past five years. The ten biggest losers—TCS, Infosys, Kotak Mahindra Bank, Asian Paints, Wipro, HDFC Life, Vodafone Idea, SRF, Shriram Cement and Dauber—have all underperformed despite being Nifty‑50 constituents. Key drivers include: IT firms suffering from a global tech‑spending slowdown and AI‑related concerns; consumer brands facing weak demand, fierce competition and inflated 2021 valuations; banks grappling with slower growth, higher valuations, leadership changes and RBI regulatory actions; paints and cement firms hit by margin pressure, new entrants and excess capacity; and insurers dealing with regulatory challenges and thin VNB margins. The findings highlight that simply holding blue‑chip large‑caps over the long term does not guarantee superior returns, underscoring the need for active investment approaches
