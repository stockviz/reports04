BFSI Q3FY26 Review | See Strong Earnings Growth For Banks In FY27e: Suresh Ganapathy, Macquarie

INDIANB FEDERALBNK SHRIRAMFIN HDFCLIFE ICICIBANK CUB HDFCBANK SBIN BAJFINANCE IOB AXISBANK KOTAKBANK 5PAISA BANKBARODA MAHABANK POLICYBZR

Key take‑aways from the Q3 FY‑26 review with Suresh Ganapathy (Macquarie Capital):

  • Earnings season focus: After the IT results, the market’s attention will shift to finance – insurance, NBFCs and banks – with a packed calendar (Bank of Maharashtra, IOB, Union Bank, Indian Bank, Federal Bank, ICICI, HDFC Bank, etc.) through mid‑January.

  • Insurance: Very bullish. Protection business shows strong volume growth and ultra‑high margins (80‑90 % vs. ~10 % for ULI‑type products). Even with a short‑term tax‑credit head‑wind, the sector’s fundamentals and upcoming risk‑based solvency reforms should drive margin expansion into FY‑27.

  • NBFCs: Prefer smaller‑mid‑cap players over the large ones. Large NBFCs (e.g., Bajaj Finance) face a trade‑off between high growth and sustainable ROA; valuation is stretched. Smaller NBFCs such as AB Capital and Shriram Finance offer comparable growth, stable ROA, and discount‑style multiples.

  • Public‑sector banks (PSUs): Strong upgrade, especially SBI (price target lifted from ~₹720 to ₹1,150). Drivers:

    1. Faster loan‑book market‑share gains vs. private banks.
    2. No LDR constraint under the new RBI regime.
    3. Very clean corporate‑credit quality – lower slippages than many private banks.
    4. Low credit‑cost environment, high leverage (≈14×), yielding ROA ~1 % that rivals private peers at cheaper valuations.

  • Private‑sector banks: Mixed view.

    • HDFC Bank: Earnings growth continues through FY‑27/28, but deposit‑growth is lagging; market will demand stronger deposits.
    • Axis Bank and SBI (still PSU) are seen as higher‑beta, cheaper‑valued bets with ~30 % FY‑27 EPS growth (benefiting from a weaker FY‑26 base).
    • ICICI Bank and Kotak Mahindra face near‑term uncertainty (e.g., CEO tenure, deposit‑growth concerns), prompting a tactical shift toward cheaper, higher‑beta banks.

  • FinTech / new‑age lenders: PTM FinTech and PB FinTech have upgraded outlooks thanks to unadjusted EBITDA growth and operating leverage. However, they trade at high multiples (≈50× FY‑28 EBITDA), so upside depends on sustaining leverage and margin expansion.

  • Mid‑cap private banks (e.g., Federal Bank, RBL, City Union, AU Finance): Expectations are high but structural challenges (governance, asset‑quality issues) limit upside. The analyst prefers proven models like City Union Bank, which has a secured‑asset base, SME focus, and a stable 1.5 % ROA.

  • Overall stance: The coming earnings season is likely to validate the bullish revisions for PSUs and insurance, while NBFC and private‑bank picks will hinge on growth‑versus‑valuation trade‑offs and the ability to sustain deposit or asset‑quality momentum.

METROPOLIS

    • Focuses on high‑complexity, high‑value indications such as oncology, neurology, rare genetic disorders, gut/colon cancer, strokes, and immunity‑related conditions.

  • Specialty Business & Margins
    • Specialty and super‑specialty tests already account for ~38 % of revenue.
    • Although gross margins on specialty tests are lower than routine tests, EBITDA per test/patient is higher, creating a strong moat and higher overall profitability.
    • The genomics portfolio is expected to further improve margins by adding high‑value, low‑volume services to existing patient flows.
  • Growth Outlook
    • Organic growth in Q3 FY‑26 was ~15 % YoY; specialty and “True Health” (preventive health) segments posted >30 % quarterly growth.
    • Management targets 13‑15 % organic growth annually for FY‑26/FY‑27, with continued margin expansion.
  • Acquisition Strategy
    • Recent bolt‑on acquisitions in Dehradun, Agra and Kolhapur were strategic, offering EBITDA arbitrage and scalability under the Metropolis brand.
    • No immediate acquisition plans in genomics; the company believes it already holds a leading talent and technology position.
    • A dedicated acquisition fund remains in place for future bolt‑on opportunities in other segments.
  • Diversification & Radiology
    • Metropolis already provides soft radiology services (ECG, X‑ray, sonography) across its network and is evaluating higher‑end imaging (CT, MRI).
    • No definitive plans announced for entering full‑scale diagnostic‑wellness centers like those launched by competitors.

Overall, Metropolis is leveraging its genomics launch and specialty test focus to build a higher‑margin, differentiated business, while maintaining a disciplined acquisition pipeline to fuel geographic and service expansion.
