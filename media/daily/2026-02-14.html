Globalization’s New Phase: Diversification, Emerging Markets, and Shifts in Trade Dynamics

MAXHEALTH

Didier Borowski argues that we are not witnessing deglobalization but rather the end of “great globalization” and the emergence of a more resilient, diversified trade system. While the US pursues protectionist measures, most countries are forging new bilateral agreements and expanding trade faster than global GDP, especially in critical sectors like rare‑earths, prompting investment in alternative supply chains across the EU, India, Brazil, Greenland, and other emerging economies. This strategic autonomy fuels opportunities in mining, processing, and broader infrastructure. Borowski sees the global economic centre of gravity shifting from the West to the East, with emerging markets set to capture a larger share of added value. Portfolios remain overly weighted toward US assets and AI, exposing concentration risk; investors should rebalance toward diversified emerging‑market exposure and consider gold as a hedge against a weakening dollar and geopolitical uncertainty. Despite “de‑dollarisation” pressures, the dollar will stay the primary reserve currency, though it is expected to weaken, benefitting commodities and EM equities. India, with its demographic dividend and near‑6 % growth potential, is a key beneficiary; although foreign inflows have lagged, a structural, long‑term shift toward greater investment in Indian assets is anticipated. Overall, the new globalization phase offers investors opportunities in diversified supply chains, emerging markets, and alternative asset classes amid a complex geopolitical landscape

India’s AI Surge: From Consumer to Global Superpower

India is rapidly transitioning from a major AI consumer to a leading AI creator, driven by strong policy support, indigenous model development (e.g., SERVUM), and massive investment in data centers and compute infrastructure. While challenges remain—global geopolitical influence, job displacement, and cyber‑risk—India is implementing rapid deep‑fake takedowns, upskilling programs, and fostering a vibrant AI startup ecosystem.
