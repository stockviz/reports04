Tata Motors joins Delhi-NCR fleet renewal scheme to replace old trucks and buses

TATAMOTORS

Tata Motors has signed an MoU with the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways to participate in the government’s ₹9,585‑crore Delhi‑NCR fleet renewal programme, joining Ashok Leyland and Switch Mobility. The scheme offers an 8% discount on eligible BS‑VI or electric commercial vehicles, plus a 5% interest subvention, fuel vouchers for five years, and state tax concessions. It targets replacement of BS‑IV or older trucks and buses, with stricter rules for Delhi (electric or CNG buses only) and broader eligibility in surrounding NCR districts. The participating OEMs together cover about 50% of India’s truck and bus market, aiming to accelerate fleet modernisation and cut emissions

PM E‑DRIVE Electric Ambulance Incentive Boosts EV Adoption, Force Motors Set to Lead

TATAMOTORS FORCEMOT M&M

The Ministry of Heavy Industries has launched a dedicated PM E‑DRIVE incentive for electric ambulances, allocating ₹500 crore to subsidise up to 3,811 vehicles in FY27‑28. A typical M2‑class ambulance with a 70 kWh battery (≈₹38 lakh ex‑factory) can now receive up to ₹13.3 lakh, lowering its net cost to ~₹24.7 lakh—near diesel parity. Operating costs drop to ₹2‑3 /km versus ₹9‑11 /km for diesel, with added benefits of lower noise, zero tail‑pipe emissions and better patient comfort. The scheme covers Type B, C and D ambulances, requires domestic production of key EV components (excluding medical equipment), and bans imported battery modules. Force Motors, whose Traveller platform dominates India’s ambulance market, is poised as the biggest beneficiary, followed by Tata Motors and Mahindra & Mahindra. The programme aims to modernise the ambulance fleet, cut healthcare transport emissions, and build a domestic electric‑ambulance manufacturing ecosystem
