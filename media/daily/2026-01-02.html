Govt To Hike Tax On Tobacco Products: How Does New Tax Structure Impact Margins Of Cigarette Cos?

DEVYANI SAPPHIRE ITC

The Indian government’s steep tax hike on tobacco products has negatively impacted cigarette companies, particularly ITC, leading to widespread brokerage downgrades and sharp target price cuts. Nomura downgraded ITC to “reduce” with a target of ₹340, citing a projected 40% cigarette tax hike leading to over 35% price increases, volume declines exceeding 15%, and a 14% drop in net sales and 17% fall in EBIT by FY27. Illicit trade risks and lower valuation multiples (cut from 25x to 16x) further weighed on sentiment. Other brokerages like JP Morgan and Nuvama also downgraded the stock, citing similar concerns, while maintaining “hold” or “neutral” stances due to ITC’s diversified business, dividend yield, and favorable raw material costs.

Analysts note this tax increase is unprecedented—potentially triggering 25–40% retail price hikes—and likely to cause significant volume erosion, with historical precedents showing 8–9% declines after prior tax hikes. Given the scale, double-digit volume drops are expected. While FMCG and paper segments may benefit from GST cuts and cost improvements, overall profit growth for ITC is likely muted. Structural concerns remain due to shifting government policy, which could depress P/E multiples further, discouraging institutional investors.

On another front, the long-expected merger between Devyani and Sapphire in the QSR sector was announced, seen as positive for Devyani due to cost synergies, minimal shareholder dilution, and revenue/EBITDA accretion. Analysts expect recovery in discretionary consumption and recommend stocks like Nestle, Britannia, Marico, Asian Paints, and Tata Consumer for the upcoming earnings season, despite rich valuations in some cases.

GST Impact Sparks Significant Uptick In These Auto Stocks

MARUTI HEROMOTOCO M&M HYUNDAI TVSMOTOR

Auto sales in December 2025 surged across major Indian automakers, driven largely by the lingering impact of GST cuts and post-JST (likely referring to tax reductions) momentum. TVS Motor reported over 50% sales growth, significantly surpassing estimates, with strong performance in two-wheelers and exports. Hero MotoCorp recorded a 40% year-on-year increase, selling 4.5 lakh units, exceeding expectations despite a slight dip in retail market share. Maruti Suzuki achieved its highest-ever monthly sales with 1.85 lakh vehicles sold and retail sales reaching 2.86 lakh units, backed by 1.75 lakh pending bookings; it is considering a price hike after tax incentives expired. Mahindra & Mahindra saw a 25% overall sales growth, with SUVs growing 23% (50,946 units) and LCVs up 34% (24,786 units), attributing the gains to September’s tax cuts and sustained industry momentum. In contrast, Hyundai’s 6.6% growth fell short of the expected 9%, marking a relative underperformance. Overall, nine out of ten auto companies posted positive numbers, signaling strong sector-wide growth heading into 2026.

Devyani & Sapphire Foods To Merge, Create A Single Unified Yum! India Franchise For KFC & Pizza Hut

DEVYANI SAPPHIRE

Sapphire Foods has approved its merger with Devyani International, creating a unified Yum! Brands franchise in India for KFC and Pizza Hut, excluding airport and railway station outlets. The swap ratio is set at 177 Devyani shares for every 100 Sapphire shares (1:1.77). The merger, effective April 1, 2026, is subject to regulatory, shareholder, and court approvals, after which Sapphire will cease to exist as a separate entity. As part of the deal, Sapphire Foods Mauritius will sell a 18.5% stake (around 5.95 crore shares) to Arctic International, a Devyani group company, which may later sell it to a financial investor. Post-merger, Sapphire’s promoters will become public shareholders in Devyani. A new agreement with Yum! India will update commercial terms for brand operations. The merger aims to consolidate India’s fast food sector, driving scale and cost efficiencies.
