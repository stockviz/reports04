2025: A Breakout Year For India’s Data Center Business | Data Center Business Boom In 2025 | N18V

TCS CUMMINSIND VOLTAS RELIANCE LTF

India’s data center sector is experiencing rapid growth, marking 2025 as a breakout year. Capacity has surged from 0.5 gigawatt in 2020 to 1.6 gigawatt in 2025, driven by a 30-fold increase in data traffic since 2017, 900 million internet users, and rising AI adoption, making India OpenAI’s second-largest market. Major investments include Reliance Industries’ Jamnagar data center and 1-gigawatt AI facility with Nvidia, TCS’s 1–1.2 gigawatt AI-focused hyper cloud, and OpenAI’s 1-gigawatt commitment aligned with DPDP Act mandates. Total investments announced in 2025 reached $63 billion, with $20 billion from Reliance. Hyperscalers added 97.9 megawatts in the first half of 2025, a 48% year-on-year increase, with occupancy rates at 97%. Mumbai dominates with 50% of capacity due to undersea cable connectivity.

India added a record 30 gigawatts of renewable energy in FY24–25, ranking third globally, supported by low-cost solar and government policies. Data centers require 5–6 times more power due to AI, and global power demand for them is projected to grow 17% annually until 2030—India’s growth is expected to reach 33%. Global execution delays due to power constraints and permitting issues are shifting focus to India.

Capex for data centers in India is $4–5 million per megawatt, with 45% spent on non-IT infrastructure (cooling, electricals, construction) and 55% on IT. This boom benefits companies like L&T (EPC), Cummins (backup power), Voltas (cooling), and others in electrical, real estate, and network infrastructure. India’s unified national grid supports expansion, although transmission is becoming a bottleneck. Capacity is projected to grow fivefold to eight gigawatts by 2030, requiring $30 billion in capex for 6.4 gigawatts of new capacity, creating opportunities across telecom, EPC, power, IT, and infrastructure sectors.
