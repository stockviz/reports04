Multi‑Asset Funds Outperform Equity Amid Market Volatility

AXISBANK

The interview with Access Mutual Fund’s equity fund manager Ashish Naik explains why multi‑asset funds have recently beaten pure equity funds. Multi‑asset funds must hold at least three asset classes—equity, debt, and commodities (typically gold or silver)—and aim for low‑correlation diversification to improve risk‑adjusted returns. Access’s fund maintains a gross equity exposure of 65‑80% (net 30‑80% using arbitrage) with the remainder in debt and 10‑25% in commodities, currently around 15% aligned with its benchmark. Equity allocation is adjusted based on valuations, earnings momentum, macro indicators, and geopolitical risks; the fund reduced equity to about 53‑54% during recent market stress and has since moved back toward 65% as fundamentals improve. Commodity exposure, especially gold, was high (up to 24%) during the previous year’s “anti‑dollar” sentiment but has been trimmed to about 11‑12% and now sits near the benchmark level. Naik stresses that multi‑asset funds are not meant to deliver pure equity returns but to provide steadier, long‑term performance by balancing assets, reducing volatility, and delivering superior risk‑adjusted returns over medium to long horizons. Investors should avoid short‑term expectations based on recent outperformance and view multi‑asset strategies as a “tortoise” approach—steady, less risky growth compared to the “hare” of pure equity investing
