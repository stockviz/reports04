India’s AI‑driven growth hinges on massive energy and data infrastructure

KOTAKBANK BCG

At the Kotak Institutional Equities Investor Conference, Adani Group CFO Jugeshinder “Robbie” Singh said AI will reshape industries over decades, but its long‑term impact is often underestimated. He stressed that AI’s scaling requires a parallel expansion of energy generation, storage, transmission, and data‑center capacity, positioning Adani’s core strategy around energy, logistics and digital infrastructure. Singh outlined a two‑pillar portfolio model—stable core businesses plus a high‑potential, incubator segment—to build capabilities India lacks, such as advanced tech and aerospace. He highlighted structural economic shifts—rising worker participation, SIP growth, metro rail expansion, and improved social indicators—that signal a non‑metro, aspirational growth wave. Over the next 25 years, India’s net‑worth is set to surge, though equity markets haven’t fully priced this. The group plans long‑term capex in renewables, baseload power, battery storage, and grid upgrades, while maintaining financial discipline via asset‑cash‑flow matching and credit‑rating preservation. Operationally, Adani is moving to a hybrid human‑robot workforce with a digital utility platform for autonomous generation, dispatch, and maintenance, already delivering efficiency gains. Singh cautioned against short‑term views on assets like airports, emphasizing long‑horizon investment, and warned that firms ignoring AI and foundational infrastructure risk losing competitiveness. Early, disciplined investors in this infrastructure stand to capture lasting value
