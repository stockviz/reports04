Key Takeaways on Motor Insurance Coverage and Add‑Ons

BAJAJ-AUTO

The discussion outlines how motor insurance is a flexible product with a mandatory base – Third‑Party Liability (TPD) – that covers injury or death to others. Optional layers include Own Damage (OD) for vehicle damage, Zero Depreciation (which eliminates part depreciation for newer cars, typically unavailable after five years), and specific add‑ons such as Water‑Logging/Engine Damage, Return‑to‑Invoice (pays the cost of a new equivalent car if stolen or total loss), tyre coverage, and key loss protection. Water‑damage claims are only payable if the relevant add‑on is purchased. Third‑party coverage does not pay for vehicle repairs; that is covered under OD, and claiming against another driver’s insurer requires filing an FIR and can be cumbersome. Premiums consist of a fixed TPD component plus variable rates based on the insurer’s claim experience, car model, city, and dealer servicing patterns. Choosing the right add‑ons and understanding claim processes helps owners stay fully protected, especially during monsoon‑related flooding
