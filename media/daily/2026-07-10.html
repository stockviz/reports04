Honda Eyes Expanded Premium Bike Line‑up and EV Push in India

GRINDWELL ATHERENERG HEROMOTOCO TATACAP BAJAJ-AUTO OLAELEC HONDAPOWER TVSELECT ROHLTD

Honda Motorcycle and Scooter India (HMSI) plans to bring more global premium motorcycles (above 125 cc) to India and localise production, contingent on sustained volume growth, especially among young consumers. Premium bike sales rose 15 % to 3.5 million units in FY‑26, while entry‑level sales were 3‑9.5 million. Honda’s current premium range includes Unicorn, CB350, SP160 and NX200. Rivals such as TVS, Hero, Bajaj and Royal Enfield are rapidly expanding premium offerings and exclusive retail networks. Honda logged 11 % scooter sales growth (3.16 million) and 4 % motorcycle growth (2.58 million) in FY‑26 and aims to increase its EV presence with at least one new model this year, despite infrastructure challenges. To support these moves, Honda will invest over ₹2,000 crore to boost Indian capacity by 1.2 million units annually, taking total capacity from roughly 6.2 million to over 7.4 million units

Investor Focus Shifts to Hotel Opening Timelines Amid India’s Branded Room Expansion

THELEELA ADVANIHOTR LEMONTREE ICRA HOTELRUGBY EIHOTEL INDHOTEL

India’s hospitality sector aims to add over 100,000 branded rooms by 2029, but analysts are now scrutinizing when pipelines will turn into operating hotels, as revenue growth from existing assets normalizes. Companies like IHCL, EIH, Leela and Lemon Tree are providing more granular opening schedules after delays—often six months or more—due to approvals, financing, construction and owner capabilities. Experts say the sector is maturing; timely openings shorten payback periods and match demand that outpaces supply. Faster greenfield builds (≈2.5 years) and conversions (9‑12 months) are being emphasized, with experienced multi‑asset owners preferred to reduce execution risk. The overall thrust is moving from aggressive signings to execution‑driven growth

AI‑driven consulting: higher fees persist as firms invest in tech and senior talent

CHEVIOT 3MINDIA

Indian companies hoped AI would cut consulting costs, but major advisory firms argue that savings from automation are offset by investments in upskilling, senior oversight, risk management, and AI infrastructure. Firms cite the need for human judgment, data‑privacy safeguards, and “hallucination” risks, leading to continued high billing rates. While AI speeds up research and expands service markets—especially in tier‑II/III cities—consultants are shifting to fewer junior analysts, more mid‑level staff, and value‑based pricing models, resulting in sustained or growing revenues despite client expectations for lower fees
