STT On Monthly Futures Rollovers In Arbitrage Funds Adds 24 Bps Annual Cost Due To Hike: EdelweissMF

  • The recent hike in Security Transaction Tax (STT) on futures‑and‑options (F&O) trades raised the cost from 2 bps to 5 bps.
  • Arbitrage funds roll over about 70‑75 % of their AUM in monthly futures positions. The higher STT adds roughly 3 bps per month, equating to about 24 bps annually for rollovers.
  • Additional churn and fresh deployments contribute another 6‑7 bps, so the total impact on fund returns is estimated at 30‑32 bps (≈0.30‑0.32 %).
  • Funds will try to pass part of this cost to investors by seeking slightly higher rollover yields (e.g., 51‑52 bps instead of 50 bps).
  • Net effect: arbitrage fund returns are expected to be 25‑30 bps lower, reducing the post‑tax advantage over liquid funds from ~120 bps to ~90 bps.
  • No impact will be felt until the April‑May rollover (positions are already rolled to April).
  • The STT hike also affects balance‑advantage, equity‑saving, and long‑short funds, though the impact there is smaller.
  • Arbitrage funds cannot use options as a substitute for futures; only other hybrid funds can partially offset the cost with options.
  • Overall, the STT increase will modestly compress arbitrage fund returns but the product should remain relatively more attractive than liquid funds.

Lot Of Growth Potential From The Current Levels: MedPlus Health | CNBC TV18

MEDPLUS

MedPlus Health posted a strong Q3, with healthy revenue and margins and double‑digit same‑store sales growth (≈10 %+) for stores operating over 12 months—the first such increase since FY‑24 Q4.   • On the positive side, he notes that the budget did include measures benefiting sectors he holds—defence, capital goods, power, and a specific “one‑ninety‑seven” investment—so his funds can still capitalize on those allocations.
  • Overall, Arora calls for the government to treat the equity market as a strategic sector, offering both moral acknowledgment and concrete policy tools to sustain FII inflows and domestic liquidity, especially given the current macro‑economic headwinds.
