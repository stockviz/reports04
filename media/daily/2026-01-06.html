Strong Q3 Biz Update For CSB Bank | Do Not See Any Material Risk In Any Specific Portfolio, Says Co

MUTHOOTFIN CSBBANK

  • CSB Bank’s Q3 results showed a sharp rally in its share price, driven by a 46% YoY jump in gold‑loan disbursements, now comprising about 51% of the overall loan book.
  • Deposits grew ~21% YoY; management reiterated the aim to lift net‑interest margin toward 3.9% and to keep credit costs low.
  • LTV on gold loans has fallen to below 60%, with an overall LTV cushion of 75% (i.e., 25% margin) and a 15% spread on the lower‑LTV segment, giving the bank a strong safety buffer.
  • Stress‑test scenarios (‑5% to ‑10% gold‑price moves) keep the portfolio well within the LTV limits; even a 20% price drop would leave sufficient collateral.
  • Gold‑loan tonnage growth is modest – low single‑digit percentages – but loan values are rising because ticket sizes have increased to INR 3‑4 lakhs, driven by SME working‑capital borrowers.
  • The bank does not track “top‑up” versus fresh gold loans separately; new regulatory rules will allow roll‑overs/top‑ups to be recorded as such from next year.
  • Management views gold as a tactical, high‑margin play, not a long‑term reliance point; the goal is to diversify into wholesale, SME, and retail franchises and complete core‑banking migration (52‑system integration, FC core).
  • Competitive pressure on gold‑loan yields is seen as limited; CSB Bank’s pricing remains lower than many peers, and higher ticket sizes should protect margins.
  • Long‑term vision: reduce gold‑loan share to under 20% of the book by around 2030 as the bank expands other segments.
  • Overall, no material risk is identified in the current gold‑loan portfolio, and risk‑mitigation frameworks (LTV discipline, shorter tenors, stress testing) are in place.

“Government Cannot Retain Invalid Tax”: SC Orders ₹500 Cr Refund to Adani Power’s Mundra Plant

ADANIPOWER

The Supreme Court ruled that Adari Power’s electricity sales from its Mundra SEZ plant are exempt from customs duty, declaring the levy “invalid tax.” The court overturned a 2019 Gujarat High Court decision and reaffirmed its 2015 stance that the duty lacked legal authority.
