Davos World Economic Forum 2026 | US Economy To Grow 2.8% In 2026: Bank Of America CEO Exclusive

BANKBARODA

Key Takeaways from the Davos 2026 interview with Bank of America CEO Brian Moynihan

  • Davos Atmosphere: The meeting was marked by high‑level political engagement (e.g., President Trump) and intense focus on AI, capitalism’s role, and the “global reset” in geopolitics and economics.

  • US Economic Outlook: Bank of America now projects US GDP growth of 2.8 % in 2026, above consensus, after previously forecasting ~1.5 % in mid‑2025. The forecast assumes Fed rate cuts later this year, inflation easing toward target by late 2027, and steady employment.

  • Consumer & Small‑Business Momentum:

    • Q4 consumer spending rose ~5 % YoY, adding about $1.5 trillion to the economy.
    • Early‑2026 data shows continued, though still nascent, growth.
    • Small‑business sales and payrolls are modestly up; floating‑rate credit costs have fallen with lower rates.

  • Tariffs & Trade: Current tariffs have not yet dented sentiment, but they are understood and businesses are adapting. Higher tariffs are tied to national‑security considerations; firms are finding ways to operate around them.

  • AI as a Structural Tailwind:

    • Massive, ongoing investment in AI infrastructure and applications (e.g., Erica virtual assistant, Microsoft 365 Copilot).
    • Hundreds of millions of dollars are being spent this year, with early signs of productivity gains and ROI measured against concrete process improvements rather than hype.
    • AI‑driven demand from enterprises and consumers is expected to continue for several years, bolstered by strong cash flows of large tech firms.

  • Fiscal Policy: The recent US tax bill provides higher refunds for many taxpayers and extends bonus depreciation for corporations, offering a modest stimulus without altering the corporate tax rate.

  • AI Valuations: Valuations reflect high demand for AI capabilities across licensing, subscription models, and embedded enterprise solutions. Moynihan emphasizes that market pricing will self‑correct if overvalued.

  • Monetary‑Policy Leadership: Potential Fed Chair turnover is being discussed publicly; candidates are being vetted, but independence and the dual mandate remain central.

  • M&A Outlook: Deal activity remains robust; 2025 was the second‑best fee‑earning year after the COVID‑recovery spike. Pipeline is full, with faster closings expected as regulatory reforms mature.

  • IPO Market 2026: A modest revival is underway, driven by private‑equity exits and improved market conditions.

  • India Focus:

    • Expecting ~6.5 % growth for India in 2026.
    • Ongoing investment in payments platforms, corporate banking, and a new country head to deepen the partnership.
    • Anticipation of a US‑India trade agreement in H1 2026, which would enhance market certainty and flow of goods/services.

  • Megatrends & Risks: Key themes include supply‑chain certainty, mineral (copper) availability for electrification, cyber‑risk, policy missteps, and credit‑card risk management. Affordability products (e.g., low‑interest cards, zero‑balance transfers) are being expanded.

  • Optimism & Resilience: Moynihan cites a well‑functioning capitalist system, declining rates, deregulation, and strong employment as drivers of confidence. The firm conducts regular stress tests, learns from past crises, and maintains a disciplined, risk‑aware growth strategy.

  • Boardroom Sentiment: Executives focus on controllable factors, preparing for “radical uncertainty” through scenario planning and resilient operational models. The priority is delivering solid, risk‑adjusted growth while supporting clients worldwide.
