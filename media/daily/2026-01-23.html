Srini Sriniwasan Explains: 5 Private Equity Demands To Boost Capital & Bonds | Union Budget 2026

KOTAKBANK TIGERLOGS

Key private‑equity/AIF demands for Union Budget 2026 (as outlined by Shrini Srinivasan, MD, Kotak Alternate Asset Manager):

  1. Tax‑certainty window – A clear rule that tax assessments for limited‑life AIFs must be completed within 12 months of fund closure, removing the current multi‑year delays that force managers to retain cash and erode returns.

  2. Tax‑rate parity – Reduce the disparity between capital‑gains tax on equity‑linked funds and the high marginal tax on interest income. The current gap drives savers toward equity‑styled products, starving the bond market and limiting capital for lower‑rated issuers.

  3. Boost private‑credit growth – Align taxation of private‑credit (Category‑II) funds with that of equity funds, so domestic investors (banks, family offices) are not penalised (25‑35 % tax) compared with foreign investors (≈15 %). A level‑playing field would attract more domestic capital to the credit market.

  4. Enable LLP structure for AIFs – Adopt the global best‑practice of running private‑equity funds as Limited Liability Partnerships, which simplifies borrowing, tax treatment and operational efficiency.

  5. Overall impact – These measures would provide certainty, improve capital allocation, deepen the bond market, and make India a more attractive destination for both domestic and foreign private‑equity and credit investment.
