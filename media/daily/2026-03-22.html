Five Warning Signs That the AI Rally May Be a Bubble

The AI boom shows a growing gap between massive spending on hardware, cloud credits and talent and the modest revenue these technologies currently generate. Companies pour billions into chips and data centers despite limited demand, often driven by a fear of falling behind. Circular financing—where firms fund customers who then spend the money back on the funder—creates phantom revenue without genuine market pull. “Multi‑tiered unicorn” funding splits rounds to inflate headline valuations, masking lower true values. The sector’s power needs are straining grids, prompting nuclear and massive generator projects. Finally, AI’s promised value remains unproven: demos often hallucinate or err, leading to project cancellations and a “show‑me‑the‑value” phase, with analysts warning that the hype may be outpacing real returns

Supreme Court Bars Post‑Fact Shareholder Ratification of Misused Funds

AARON BMWVENTLTD

The Supreme Court set aside the Securities Appellate Tribunal’s relief to Moryo Industries (now Terrascope Ventures) and reinstated SEBI penalties, ruling that companies cannot legitimize diversion of funds raised from investors by later shareholder approval. The court held that disclosures made at the time of raising capital are essential for investor decisions and market integrity, and that violations of securities laws affecting public interest cannot be cured by retrospective ratification. Consequently, listed companies must ensure accurate use of proceeds from preferential allotments, private placements, and IPOs from the outset, and investors gain stronger protection against post‑fact alterations of fund utilization
