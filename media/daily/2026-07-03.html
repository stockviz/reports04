India’s Nayara Energy fuels Russia amid shortages

ATHERENERG

Traders have sold gasoline from Nayara’s Vadinar refinery to Russia, which faces fuel shortages after Ukrainian attacks. Rosneft owns 49% of Nayara, which now relies on traders for imports and exports due to EU sanctions. Indian officials say Indian companies aren’t directly selling to Russia, though Russian purchases of Indian-origin fuel are possible. Around 60,000 metric tons of gasoline were dispatched, including two tankers carrying 30‑40 k tons each; one tanker, Agni, left Vadinar for Fujairah on June 20 and is now heading north through the Suez

Tata Agratas Boosts UK Loan to $1.15 bn and Extends Repayment Deadline

NSIL RUBICON BCG ADANIENSOL SIL PENINLAND

Tata’s UK battery arm Agratas Ltd has increased its syndicated loan from $750 m to $1.15 bn (≈14,000 cr) and pushed the repayment date from July 2026 to September 2027, with an option to extend to March 2028. Tech Mahindra’s Struggling Pininfarina Subsidiary Threatens Financial Health

M&M TECHM

Tech Mahindra’s 2015 acquisition of Ferrari‑design house Pininfarina has become a financial burden, with corporate guarantees soaring to ₹735 crore ($80 million) for FY25‑26. Pininfarina’s revenue grew only 25% since the deal, lagging Tech Mahindra’s 73% growth, and it posted a FY25 net loss of $7.6 million, its fourth loss in five years. Factors include automakers cutting external design spend, shifting to in‑house teams, weaker European car demand, and costly one‑off expenses such as severance and a lawsuit settlement. The firm is diversifying into residential, consumer, and even nuclear design, but recovery remains distant. Meanwhile, Tech Mahindra is focusing on an AI‑driven turnaround, boosting operating margins to 12.6% and targeting growth beyond its peers

Accelerating IPOs: Indian Tech Start‑ups Hit Public Markets in Record Time

LENSKART BLACKBUCK PINELABS NYKAA AWFIS FRACTAL

Indian tech start‑ups are now reaching IPOs in about eight years from their first funding round, a sharp drop from 14.5 years a year earlier. The speed is driven by attractive public‑market valuations, investor pressure for exits, improved corporate governance, and streamlined listing processes. Companies are preparing compliance, independent boards, and robust reporting early, while stronger domestic liquidity reduces reliance on foreign investors. This trend marks a faster path to public markets for new‑age technology firms
