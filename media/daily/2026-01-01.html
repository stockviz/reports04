‘$100 Increase In LME Copper Adds Rs 25 Crore To Profitability’: Hindustan Copper MD To NDTV Profit

HINDCOPPER

  • Hindustan Copper CMD Sanjeev Kumar Singh attributes surging copper prices (up 42% YoY) and strong stock performance (up 112% in 12 months) to a persistent supply-demand mismatch, driven by stagnant global production and rising demand from AI data centers, EVs, renewables, and infrastructure.

  • He expects the deficit to persist through FY26 due to constrained supply and increasing global demand, with limited new copper deposits and ongoing supply chain challenges.

  • For every $100 increase in LME copper prices, Hindustan Copper sees a ₹20–25 crore improvement in profitability.

  • The company plans to ramp up production from current levels (~3.47 million tons of ore) to 12.2 million tons by FY31, through expansion in Malanjkhand, revival of closed mines in Jharkhand (targeting 5 million tons), and development of K3 mines in Rajasthan (2.9 million tons).

  • Current domestic production meets only 4–5% of India’s copper needs; expansion aims to significantly reduce import dependence.

  • Capex of around ₹5,000 crore planned over 5–6 years: ₹2,000 crore for Malanjkhand, ₹1,000 crore each for K3 and Jharkhand. JSW will independently invest ₹2,400–2,500 crore in the Rakha mine under a revenue-sharing model.

  • Hindustan Copper is enhancing reserves (added 134 million tons in 3 years), boosting exploration spending from ₹35 crore to ₹50 crore annually, and collaborating with global major Codelco and institutions like IIT Dhanbad for R&D and deep exploration.

  • The company is exploring critical minerals in tailing dumps—a first in India—and has tied up multiple MoUs with Maharatna firms and state governments for new mining blocks.

  • No hedging is done; sales are based on spot LME prices, making revenues fully exposed to market volatility.

  • EBITDA margins have improved from 23% in FY21 to ~38% trailing, with the company targeting to sustain above 40% through operational efficiency and scale.

  • Copper prices are currently above $12,400/ton; outlook remains bullish due to structural deficits, though no formal price forecasting is provided.

  • Funding is secured via strong internal cash flows, QIP approval, and bond issuances; negligible debt (₹120 crore).

  • The company reaffirms commitment to national self-reliance in copper and long-term value creation amid favorable macro trends.

European Approval Now Expected By July-August For Zaynich: Wockhardt Chairman Habil Khorakiwala

WOCKPHARMA

Wockhardt Chairman Habil Khorakiwala expects European Medicines Agency (EMA) approval for Zyniquib (ZANIC/WCK-222) by July-August 2026, following an accelerated assessment. This comes alongside anticipated US FDA approval around the same time, after the NDA was accepted in December. The drug targets multi-drug resistant infections affecting ~200,000 patients in Europe, with peak European sales potential of $400–500 million and global potential of $1–2 billion, half from the US. Revenue contribution is expected to begin in FY28, growing slowly before accelerating after 18 months. Wockhardt plans to expand into other global markets within 3–6 months post-US/EU approvals.

The company expects McNiff sales in India to reach ₹80–100 crore in FY26/27, with regulatory filings underway in Saudi Arabia, Latin America, Brazil, and Mexico for emerging markets. Wockhardt is not pursuing McNiff in the US immediately.

Regarding the GLP-1 and obesity drug space, Wockhardt plans a delayed entry using cost-efficient recombinant technology, avoiding early competition dominated by semi-synthetic products. Insulin and diabetes treatments remain core, with no expected negative impact from rising GLP-1 usage.

Khorakiwala reaffirms a vision for sales to reach ₹6,000 crore by FY28 with 15–20% EBITDA margins, potentially doubling to ₹12,000 crore by FY31, driven by Zyniquib’s global rollout and improving operations.

The company has ₹1,700 crore debt but substantial cash reserves and expects cash-neutral operations by FY26, turning cash-positive thereafter. No near-term equity raise is planned; instead, Wockhardt is pursuing out-licensing deals in Europe, with updates expected during the year.

India Can Be An Outperformer If The AI Theme Outperforms: S Naren Of ICICI Prudential | Outlook 2026

ICICIAMC ENTERO AKUMS ASIANPAINT

S. Narain, ED and CIO at ICICI Prudential AMC, discusses market outlook for 2026, emphasizing that India can outperform if the AI theme continues to lead globally. He notes markets are in a mature bull phase with no broad cheap valuations, necessitating active stock picking rather than sector or market-cap bets. The focus is on well-researched, deeply corrected stocks across all sectors and sizes—especially big underperformers excluded from the AI rally—where risk-reward is favorable, targeting 15–20% returns rather than home runs.

Opportunities exist in unloved segments like select pharma, IT, and FMCG names, including past examples such as Asian Paints, where fundamentals may rebound after prolonged underperformance. There is no dominant sectoral theme today akin to past cycles (e.g., PSU or metals), and investors must rely on rigorous analysis using abundant public data.

While multi-asset allocation was previously attractive due to favorable Nifty-to-gold/silver ratios, those signals no longer support allocations to precious metals. Now, asset allocation without gold/silver is seen as safer, despite strong investor demand for metals. Exporters have benefited from currency tailwinds and potential trade resolutions, but valuations aren’t cheap enough broadly to be compelling, except possibly in isolated small-cap cases or block-sale situations.

Narain expresses concern about U.S. AI-driven tech valuations being overheated, though admits limited ability to judge precisely. While a correction could ripple into India, Indian equities are expected to outperform relatively. Heavy IPO supply in 2025–2026 remains a structural cap on market returns, as India’s buoyant IPO market offers high valuations across sectors—unlike global markets focused only on AI.

Lastly, he expects better earnings in 2026 due to government stimulus through rate cuts, tax reductions, and boosted consumption, though the extent to which this is priced in remains uncertain. Overall, moderate market returns are anticipated, demanding discipline, deep research, and diversified idea generation across market caps and sectors.

Vodafone Idea Gets Rs 5836 Cr Fund Infusion: What It Means For The Company? | Vodafone Share Price

IDEA IDEA

Vodafone Idea may receive ₹5,836 crore from its promoter, Vodafone PLC, as part of a pre-merger contingent liability adjustment mechanism established during the 2017 merger of Vodafone India and Idea Cellular. The amount includes ₹2,077 crore in cash to be paid over the next 12 months, while the remaining ₹3,529 crore depends on the market value of Vodafone Idea shares, as it will be raised through open-market sales of Vodafone PLC’s stake. This liability cap was previously ₹8,369 crore, reduced to ₹6,394 crore due to prior payments, and now further revised to ₹5,836 crore, valid until December 31, 2025. While the fund infusion is procedural and known to the market, it is seen positively amid financial stress. However, Vodafone Idea clarified it has not received official communication from the government regarding a potential moratorium on dues, casting uncertainty. A moratorium without an AGR waiver would likely be viewed negatively by investors, despite the parent’s financial support.
