Asian Markets Trade Mixed Despite Overnight Rally On Wall Street; Higher Start On D-Street? 2026-01-27 08:21

TV18BRDCST KOTAKBANK AXISBANK

Asian markets: Mixed trading after Wall Street’s overnight rally. Nifty hovering near its 200‑day MA (≈25,143); key support at the Jan 2021 low (~24,920). AFI sold ~₹4 trn in cash and ~₹2 trn in index futures; DII matched dollar‑for‑dollar, adding rupee pressure. Options activity suggests Nifty likely to stay in the 25,000‑25,250 range; put‑call ratio at 0.7 (oversold) may trigger a short‑term bounce, possibly on Feb‑series expiries.

India‑EU FTA: Talks concluded; announcement expected later today. Deal aims to boost Indian exports of marine products, textiles, leather and EU exports of EVs, cars, wines, and spirits. Issues to be ironed out include carbon tax, EU deforestation rules, IP, sanitary standards and sustainability across >20 chapters. Legal scrubbing and translation into 24 EU languages will continue; ratification targeted for 2027.

EU Trade Commissioner (Maros Sefkovic) remarks: Positive about fast ratification, acknowledges legal translation work, highlights complementary automotive strengths (India’s small‑car segment, EU’s premium vehicles) and potential new supply‑chain cooperation.

US markets: All three major indices closed higher (S&P 500 +0.5 %, Dow +300 pts, Nasdaq +0.43 %). Tech stocks rallied ahead of upcoming earnings. US Commerce Department took an equity stake in a critical‑minerals startup, lifting its shares ~5 %. EU opened a probe into Elon Musk’s X over alleged DS‑A violations.

Trump‑related updates:
- Announces raising South Korea tariffs from 15 % to 25 % on several product lines.
- Threatens 100 % tariff on Canada if it proceeds with a China trade deal.
- Praises release of the final Israeli hostage from Gaza.
- Commends Venezuela for rapidly freeing political prisoners.

Bank earnings:
- Axis Bank: Net interest income +5 %, PPOP +3 %, net profit +3 % (≈7 % above expectations). Provisions down 37 % QoQ, credit cost fell from 1.3 % to 0.8 %. NIM slipped 9 bps, in line with forecasts.
- Kotak Mahindra Bank: Profit up 7 % YoY but below expectations due to new labor code impact on PPOP. Credit cost fell 16 bps, slippage down 7 bps; NIM flat QoQ, expected to improve later with CRR cut. Shares trade at a premium, limiting upside.

Market start: Nifty implied open moved from +30 pts to +115 pts during the show, indicating a strong positive opening. South Korean markets remain pressured; Australian (ASX 200), Japanese (Nikkei), Hong Kong, and Taiwan indices showing gains.
