Oyo vs Zostel: 7% Stake Dispute Threatens IPO

PRSMJOHNSN

The Delhi High Court dismissed Zostel’s fresh plea to safeguard a claimed 7% stake in Oyo (worth up to ₹4,000 cr) because Zostel withdrew it. The dispute stems from a 2015 term sheet—Zostel asserts it’s binding, granting them the stake; Oyo argues it’s non‑binding. An arbitral tribunal in 2021 favored Zostel, but Oyo successfully appealed, and a 2025 Delhi High Court ruling overturned the award. Zostel’s current appeal before a two‑judge bench will decide if the term sheet is enforceable or contrary to public policy. If Zostel is awarded the stake, Oyo’s parent Prism may have to transfer shares or cash, posing a significant risk to Prism’s upcoming IPO. The matter is slated for hearing on August 12

Meta Pulls Muse AI Feature Amid Instagram Privacy Outcry

Meta’s Muse tool, which generated AI images using publicly posted Instagram photos without explicit user consent, sparked global backlash and led to its removal. Experts highlighted that public posts do not grant platforms unrestricted rights to repurpose content, stressing the need for clear opt‑in/opt‑out mechanisms and transparent user notifications. Existing privacy laws were deemed insufficient for AI‑driven data use, prompting calls for new, stricter regulations and robust enforcement, including penalties for violations. Responsibility was framed as a layered approach: users should control what they share, while governments set enforceable boundaries on how platforms may train and deploy AI models. The incident underscored the risk of eroding user trust in Meta and other tech firms, emphasizing that future AI rollouts must prioritize accountability, user education, and clear regulatory guidelines to avoid similar privacy breaches
