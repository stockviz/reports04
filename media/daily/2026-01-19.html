Revenue & EBITDA Of J&K Cement Rise In Q3; Exceptional Item Hit Of ₹47.8 Cr Due To New Labour Codes

MKPL

J&K Cement posted ~18% revenue growth in Q3, driven by 22% volume expansion in both grey and white cement. Trade sales fell to 60% of the mix, pressuring realizations, but margins and EBITDA improved sequentially thanks to cost control and operating leverage. A one‑off exceptional expense of ₹48 cr from new labour codes lowered PAT, and PAT fell despite higher earnings. Management aims to increase capacity from 28 Mt to 40 Mt, causing standalone net debt to rise by ~₹200 cr quarter‑on‑quarter. Overall, the company shows solid top‑line and margin momentum despite short‑term headwinds.

BCCL Debuts At 97% Premium, What Should Investors Keep in Mind Before Buying In

Bharat Kolkinkal Ltd (BCCL) listed at a 97% premium after an oversubscribed IPO, sparking strong market interest. The SWOT analysis highlights its strengths as India’s largest domestic coking coal producer (≈58.5% of national output) with 7.9 billion tons of reserves and solid backing from Coal India. Weaknesses include heavy reliance on a few customers (top 10 account for 88% of F‑525 revenue) and a product mix skewed toward raw coking coal, which faces declining demand. Opportunities lie in expanding capacity—doubling output to 54 Mt, targeting ₹20,000 crore revenue by FY 30‑31, and increasing coal‑washing capacity from 13.65 Mt to 27 Mt. Threats involve global decarbonisation pressures, price volatility, large contingent liabilities, and stricter environmental regulations. Analysts note that while the premium and subscription levels exceed expectations, growth projections (≈8% FY 30‑31 revenue rise) are modest, and the sector’s potential hinges on untapped Indian reserves and upcoming government initiatives on critical minerals.
