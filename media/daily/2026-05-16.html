Super El Nino 2026: A Major Climate Threat

Scientists warn that a strong or “super” El Nino could develop in 2026, with a 60‑90% chance of occurrence and a 50% chance of being strong. Warmer Pacific waters may amplify global heat, droughts, and erratic monsoons, potentially reducing Indian rainfall to about 92% of average, stressing water supplies, agriculture, and health. Regional impacts will vary, with rain‑fed areas like Maharashtra and Karnataka most vulnerable, while irrigated states may fare better. The Indian government is preparing with water‑management measures, reservoir monitoring, and agricultural support, but the event could still heighten fiscal pressures and global food‑price risks.
