Adani Power Forms Nuclear Subsidiary CMAEL

ADANIPOWER COASTCORP ADANIENSOL

Adani Power has created a wholly owned, step‑down subsidiary, Coastal‑Maha Atomic Energy Ltd (CMAEL), to handle generation, transmission and distribution of nuclear power. CMAEL was incorporated by Adani Atomic Energy Ltd (AAEL), itself a wholly owned subsidiary of Adani Power, on April 13, receiving its certificate on April 18, with an authorized capital of ₹5 lakh (50,000 shares of ₹10 each) fully subscribed in cash. No regulatory approvals were needed at this stage. This move follows Adani’s February 12 announcement of AAEL and aligns with the Indian government’s push to expand private sector participation in the nuclear power sector while maintaining strategic control over fuel and safety

Tata Trusts to Amend Trustee Eligibility Rules Amid Governance Dispute

NSIL

Tata Trusts affirmed confidence in CEO Siddharth Sharma while deciding to revise restrictive clauses that limit trustee eligibility to Zoroastrians at the Bai Hirabai Jamsetji Tata Navsari Charitable Institution. The move follows challenges by former trustee Mehli Mistry and accusations of bias after Sharma asked vice chairmen Venu Srinivasan and Vijay Singh to consider stepping down. The Trust will seek legal amendment of the deed, possibly via the Maharashtra Charity Commissioner or courts, to align with its inclusive, secular ethos

Germany Calls National Security Council Meeting on Energy Crisis

Chancellor Friedrich Merz announced that Germany will shortly convene its National Security Council to address the global energy crisis and its impact on the country’s economy. He stressed the need to guarantee supplies of gasoline, diesel and aviation fuel, noting current market tension but assured supply security. To alleviate rising fuel costs, the government has introduced a two‑month cut in automotive fuel taxes. Merz also met with Brazilian President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva to discuss strengthening strategic ties, including cooperation on raw materials, defense, digital transformation and climate, positioning Brazil as a key partner in reducing Germany’s energy dependence
