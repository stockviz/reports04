Arbitrage Funds Face Lower Returns Amid New Derivative Costs and Falling Interest Rates

DAMCAPITAL

The recent increase in securities transaction tax (STT) on futures and options adds 0.2‑0.3% to expenses for arbitrage funds, while declining interest rates have already shaved about 1% off their yields. Liquid funds, designed for a few weeks to months, invest in short‑term money‑market instruments and are best for emergency cash with minimal exit loads. Arbitrage funds exploit cash‑futures price gaps, offer low volatility, and receive equity‑style long‑term capital gains (12.5%) after 12 months, making them suitable for 3‑6 month horizons. Income‑plus‑arbitrage funds blend debt and arbitrage, requiring a 2‑year holding period for the same tax benefit. Hybrid Specialized Investment Funds (SIFs) combine arbitrage, fixed income, and selective derivative strategies, target a 2½‑3 year horizon, have a higher minimum (₹10 lakh) and limited track records, so investors may spread across multiple SIFs. Choosing the right vehicle depends on the intended use of funds, investment horizon, tax bracket, and risk tolerance
