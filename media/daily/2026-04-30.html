Brazil Prosecutes JBS and Cargill for Sourcing from Slave‑Labor Vendors

Brazil’s Public Prosecutors Office launched the “Chain Reaction” operation, revealing that JBS NV and Cargill Agrícola SA purchased billions of reais worth of goods from suppliers on the labor ministry’s “dirty list” of firms using slavery‑like conditions. Over 48 billion reais in transactions from 2024‑2026 were traced, prompting civil actions against more than 30 companies. While nine firms agreed to stricter compliance agreements, JBS and Cargill refused, facing potential fines and compensation for moral and collective damages. The case highlights intensified scrutiny of supply‑chain due diligence in Brazil’s agriculture and fuel sectors

Elon Musk Testifies, Accuses OpenAI of Betraying Nonprofit Promise

In a federal trial in Oakland, Elon Musk testified that OpenAI’s co‑founders Sam Altman and Greg Brockman misled him about the company’s nonprofit mission, later converting to a for‑profit structure in 2019 to enrich themselves. Musk claimed his $38 million donations were obtained under false pretenses, questioned tax benefits, and described Microsoft’s $10 billion investment as a “bribe.” OpenAI’s lawyer William Savitt countered Musk’s accusations, calling his questions “trick” attempts. Musk seeks $150 billion in damages, the removal of Altman and Brockman, and a reversion of OpenAI to a nonprofit, with any award directed to its charitable arm. The case underscores a deep rift between the tech moguls and highlights broader concerns over AI governance and corporate control
