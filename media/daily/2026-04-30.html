VilCart’s Rural‑First Expansion Targets ₹1,500 Cr Revenue by FY27

Bengaluru‑based VilCart has built a network serving over 100,000 kirana stores in 30,000 villages, countering the shutdown of 200,000 urban kiranas. Leveraging a hub‑and‑spoke model, it uses kiranas as aggregation nodes, sources directly from farmer producer organisations, and drives margins through private‑label products (projected to generate ₹375‑450 cr of its ₹1,500 cr FY27 topline). The company reports a 99% fulfillment rate, 3.8‑5 orders per store per month, and is piloting electric delivery vehicles. FY25 revenue was ₹1,120 cr with a –6.8% EBITDA; FY26 improves to a –4.5% margin as private labels scale. VilCart has raised $26 m to date and is seeking up to $30 m (≈₹2.5 bn) in new funding to deepen rural penetration and enter select new states, aiming to become the core distribution infrastructure for India’s rural market

India’s Oil Marketing Crisis: Why Petrol Prices Can’t Stay Low Forever

BPCL

The article explains that while Brent crude has hovered around $120/barrel—lower than the 2022 peak of $139—the rupee‑denominated price has reached a record ₹11,000/barrel due to INR depreciation and lack of discounted Russian oil. In 2022, OMCs absorbed high crude costs with government excise cuts, limiting consumer impact, but still posted losses of about ₹18,800 cr (₹11,000 cr for HPCL). Today, higher rupee‑priced crude imposes a ₹27,000 cr monthly burden, suggesting a potential price hike of ₹25‑28 per litre. However, OMCs’ large reserves could theoretically sustain the loss for up to ten months without raising prices.
