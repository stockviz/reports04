Deputy Finance Minister Resigns Amid PIC Turmoil

DELTACORP SIL BCG

South Africa’s deputy finance minister, David Masondo, stepped down as chair of the Public Investment Corp., which manages about $220 billion in pension assets. His resignation follows a week of board upheaval, including the suspension of PIC CEO Patrick Dlamini and the resignation of six non‑executive directors, after a governance and procurement probe. Finance Minister Enoch Godongwana, who was not consulted on the CEO’s suspension, scheduled a board meeting to consider removing Masondo and other directors. Masondo defended his actions as guided by integrity, fiduciary duty, and the need to protect the fund’s assets, which include sizable stakes in major South African firms such as Naspers, MTN, and the country’s leading banks
