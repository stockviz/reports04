EU Fines Google $1 Billion for DMA Violations

The European Union has imposed a record $1 billion fine on Google for breaching the Digital Markets Act. Regulators found Google gave preferential placement to its own services, such as Google Shopping and Hotels, in search results and blocked app developers from directing users to cheaper options outside the Play Store, violating anti‑steering rules. Google must now treat rival search services fairly and allow developers to promote external deals. While the fine is historic, EU officials say ongoing talks show progress toward compliance, potentially averting further penalties
