Wisconsin Beagle Facility Closes, Dogs Rescued by Big Dog Ranch Rescue

Ridglan Farms, a beagle breeding and research operation in Blue Mounds, Wisconsin, is permanently shutting down after years of protests and legal pressure. Big Dog Ranch Rescue will take in the remaining 475 beagles, moving hundreds in the coming days and the rest in August, spaying/neutering them and preparing them for adoption. The closure follows arrests during activist raids, a deal to surrender the state breeding license, and accusations of illegal eye procedures. The rescue group emphasizes ending breeding, testing, and sales, while urging activists to cease further demonstrations. The transition aims to provide the dogs with safe, permanent homes and highlights broader concerns about animal welfare and research practices

Judge Dismisses xAI’s Trade Secret Lawsuit Against OpenAI

U.S. District Judge Rita Lin in San Francisco dismissed xAI’s lawsuit alleging OpenAI stole trade secrets related to its Grok chatbot, ruling the claims unfounded and noting that asking job candidates about prior work is routine. The decision, with prejudice, follows a prior dismissal and adds to recent legal setbacks for Elon Musk’s AI ventures, including a jury verdict against his $150 billion suit accusing OpenAI of betraying its nonprofit mission. OpenAI called the lawsuit baseless, and the case underscores ongoing tensions between competing AI firms over talent and proprietary information

Global Markets React to Iran‑US Peace Deal; Oil Recovery Faces Long Road Ahead

ATHERENERG

The U.S.–Iran agreement ending three‑month hostilities in the Strait of Hormuz sparked a sharp drop in oil prices, but analysts expect a staggered return of supply. About 14 million barrels per day—roughly 14 % of global demand—were removed from the market, and restoring production could reach 70 % within three months and 90 % within six, though full recovery may take years. Rebuilding damaged facilities (e.g., Qatar Energy’s 17 % capacity loss) and re‑mobilizing the global shipping fleet will be time‑consuming, with Middle‑East repair spending projected at $46 billion, keeping oil stocks low and normalizing supply a prolonged process
