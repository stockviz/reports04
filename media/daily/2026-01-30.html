Here’s why the 2026 Mercedes-Benz S-Class is still the luxury king | Mercedes-Benz | Auto News |N18V

The video reports the launch of the 2026 Mercedes‑Benz S‑Class at the historic plant in Stuttgart.
Key updates include:

  • Exterior: New “second‑look” star pattern on the massive grille (optional plain version), standard illuminated grille and standing three‑pointed star, three‑pointed motifs in the digital headlights with up to 40 % better light spread, and a sportier AMG‑line with dual‑tone black‑chrome styling.

  • Driver‑assistance hardware: 10 cameras (including a new one between the mirror and fender), 5 radars and 12 sensors, all fed to a water‑cooled central computer that provides Level 2+ autonomous capability and future‑proof software updates.

  • Interior layout: The traditional vertical “hyperscreen” is replaced by a horizontal 16∶9 infotainment display, plus a digital instrument cluster, a passenger‑side screen, and two rear horizontal screens with integrated cameras for video‑conferencing.

  • MBOS platform: A new service‑oriented architecture (MBOS) replaces the previous domain‑based system, enabling faster response, deeper personalization and AI‑driven assistants with multi‑turn dialogue.

  • Climate & comfort: Electrically movable AC vents that auto‑aim, memory‑linked vent positions, digital vent control with a rapid‑refresh cabin filter, four‑seat “first‑class” layout with individual bucket seats, massage, heating, rear refrigerator, ambient lighting, window blinds, and a 4D Burmester audio system with seat‑borne sound and vibration.

  • Charging & connectivity: Two wireless‑charging pads (one with cooling), expanded native‑app shortcuts, and a configurable wallpaper that changes ambient lighting.

  • Powertrains (India focus): Likely a range of six‑cylinder petrol and diesel engines (S 350 d, S 450 d, S 450, S 500, S 580) all with 48 V mild‑hybrid systems and optional all‑wheel‑drive; a new 4.0 L V8 is also mentioned. Plug‑in hybrids are expected to be omitted for the Indian market.

  • Market timeline: The new S‑Class should arrive in India in 2027, with a preview test drive to be shown on the Overdrive channel.

Believe We Are Well Ahead Of The Curve To Achieve The 2030 Target: Hitachi Energy | CNBC TV18

POWERINDIA PREMIERENE

Hitachi Energy’s executives say they are ahead of schedule for their 2030 carbon‑neutral goal, having already powered all factories and project sites with renewable energy. They are investing ₹2,000 crore over the next 3‑4 years to expand greenfield and brownfield capacities, adopting a program‑based rather than project‑based approach to meet the rising, unpredictable loads from data centers and other new loads. The company is scaling up in India, aligning with the nation’s 500 GW renewable target and upcoming 100 GW pumped‑storage goal, while localizing technology to meet “Make in India” objectives. Challenges remain in getting MSME partners and the broader ecosystem to adopt sustainability, and they call for continued and stronger policy support—such as incentives and bottleneck removal—to sustain the energy transition. Despite macro‑economic uncertainties, Hitachi Energy sees strong growth prospects in India’s industrial and energy sectors.

Q3 Tends To Be Seasonal Due To A Technical Ramp-Down Following Diwali: Quess Corp | CNBC TV18

QUESS POWERINDIA

Questcorp’s Q3 was “almost perfect,” posting 3% revenue growth to ₹3,930 crore, a 4% rise in EBITDA to an all‑time high of ₹80 crore (28% YoY), and EPS of ₹4.1. Headcount was flat sequentially (≈500 up) because the quarter is seasonal: hiring spikes around Diwali and then tapers off. Additionally, the implementation of India’s new labor code on 21 Nov caused customers to pause hiring and shift sales to Q4. The company expects the deferred demand to rebound in Q4 and into early FY2027, with early signs of orders returning in Jan and stronger activity projected for Feb‑Mar. Sectorally, IT staffing delivered 12.5% EBITDA margin and 42% YoY growth; international staffing posted a 7% margin and 26% growth. Volume‑driven segments (consumer retail, telecom, banking) felt the slowdown, while value‑oriented lines (manufacturing, construction) continued to grow. Questcorp sees the labor‑code impact as a one‑off reset and anticipates “business as usual” thereafter. An interim dividend of ₹5 per share was recommended.

Do Not See Serious Challenge Due To More Competition In The Industry: Borosil Renewables | CNBC TV18

BORORENEW RELIANCE

Borosil Renewables reported strong Q3 results, with standalone EBITDA margins around 33% in India. Management highlighted steady pricing due to a government‑imposed minimum import price on Chinese glass, and expects modest margin improvement once captive power comes online in February, though exact gains are uncertain. The company sees ample domestic demand despite new entrants like Reliance, which will add up to 2,000 t/d of capacity; Borosil’s current capacity and an upcoming furnace in Indonesia should keep it well positioned. Regarding its German subsidiary Geosphere, the firm explained that the plant was built with a €4.3 million grant, but the European module market collapsed after policy changes, leading to insolvency and a €5.5 million claim. No write‑offs have been taken yet, and the situation is deemed a “false measure” beyond the company’s control.

Mixed Q3 For Canara Bank | If Rates Remain Stable, NIM Will Be In The Range Of 2.45-2.5%, Says Co

CANBK

Canara Bank’s MD & CEO Hardeep Singh‑Alawaliya said net interest margin (NIM) will stabilise between 2.45 %‑2.50 % if rates hold, as the yield on advances fell 45 bps while deposit costs fell only 8 bps after the December rate cut. About 49 % of advances are repo‑linked, so the bank has already passed on most of the rate benefit, and any further cuts could modestly compress NIM but it should stay range‑bound.

Credit growth is accelerating, with total advances up 13.59 % YoY (well above the 10‑11 % guidance). Rural‑agricultural‑micro (RAM) loans grew 18.70 % and now make up 59 % of the book. Retail RAM grew 31.37 % (housing 17 %, gold 30 %, MSME 13.74 %). The overall goal (agri‑plus‑retail) portfolio is ₹2.21 lakh crore, of which ₹73 000 crore is retail; it’s expanding at ~30 % with low slippages.

Corporate loan growth remains modest (~7 %) as the bank remains pricing‑conscious, but overall loan growth is expected to stay strong into Q4. Return on assets is 1.13 % after a recent uptick and is expected to hold. The bank’s guidance of 10‑11 % advance growth appears achievable, given the 13 % plus growth recorded in the first nine months.

Expect Growth To Continue Via A Mix Of Organic & Inorganic Strategies: SJS Enterprises | CNBC TV18

ADANIENT NEWGEN TV18BRDCST

HJS Enterprises’ Group CEO Sanjay Thapar highlighted the company’s strong performance and outlook. Since its 2021 IPO, revenue has compounded ~18% annually, with a 36.4% YoY increase in the latest quarter and margins around 30.5%, expected to stay in the high‑20s. Growth will be driven by a mix of organic expansion and acquisitions, notably:

  • Newgen products: now 23% of sales, projected to rise to 25‑27% over the next three years, adding a margin‑accretive boost.
  • BOE Technical License Agreement: a new plant in Hosur (near Bangalore) will receive equipment in FY27, with revenue expected from FY28 onward; target revenue of ₹400‑500 cr by FY30, aiming for 15‑20% of the ₹1,500 cr Indian market (potentially ₹6,000 cr).
  • Exports: rising, supported by an EU agreement that should add further tailwinds.
  • Consumer‑durable segment: currently contributes ~21‑22% of revenue and is expected to remain at that level long‑term.

Overall, Thapar is confident the company can sustain 20%‑plus revenue growth and maintain margins in the high‑20s through FY26 and beyond.
