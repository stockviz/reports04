Rajeev Thakkar vs S Naren: Two Value Investors, Two Completely Different Ways to Manage Money

ICICIAMC

Title: Value Investing Showdown: Rajeev Thakkar vs S Naren – Contrasting Cash‑Heavy Discipline with Aggressive Deployment

Summary: The NDTV Profit interview explores how two top Indian value fund managers—Rajeev Thakkar (PPFAS Mutual Fund) and S Naren (ICICI Prudential India Opportunities Fund)—manage nearly ₹4.5 lakh crore. Thakkar’s strategy leans heavily on holding large cash reserves (up to 20‑25 %) and selective international exposure, using cash as a defensive buffer and deploying it opportunistically during market lows, which yields low down‑capture ratios but can lag in strong rallies. Naren, by contrast, keeps cash under 8 %, maintains broader mid‑/small‑cap exposure, and makes macro‑driven sector bets that often capture upside, resulting in higher up‑capture ratios but greater volatility. Both have delivered strong five‑year returns—≈ 17 % for Thakkar’s fund and ≈ 21 % for Naren’s—demonstrating distinct paths to value‑focused alpha. The discussion concludes that investors can learn from both: Thakkar teaches the discipline of conviction and cash patience, while Naren exemplifies timely thematic positioning. A balanced portfolio that blends these complementary value approaches, alongside growth assets, can smooth returns across market cycles.

NMDC Achieves Record Iron Ore Production in 2025‑26

NMDC

NMDC Ltd, a central public sector enterprise, reported a record 50 million tonnes of iron ore output in FY 2025‑26, with an additional 2‑3 million tonnes expected from the remainder of the year. Production has surged from 10 Mt in 1978 to 44 Mt in 2024‑25 and rose by nearly two‑thirds since 2015, with one‑fifth of current capacity added in the last four years—the fastest expansion in its history. Chairman Amitava Mukherjee highlighted the importance of a stable domestic iron ore supply for India’s goal of 300 Mt steelmaking capacity by 2030, attributing the rapid growth to sharper execution, responsible mining, and alignment with national priorities

Fried Secures ₹60 Cr Funding to Expand India’s Debt‑Relief Platform

Investors view the venture as a high‑impact, scalable addition to India’s emerging debt‑resolution infrastructure

Zydus Lifesciences’ Desidustat approved in China for CKD‑related anemia

ZYDUSLIFE CMSINFO WEBELSOLAR

Zydus Lifesciences announced that its oral anemia drug Desidustat (Oxemia in India), a hypoxia‑inducible factor prolyl hydroxylase inhibitor, has received marketing approval from China’s National Medical Products Administration for non‑dialysis CKD patients. The drug will be commercialized by CMS International Development and Management Ltd., a subsidiary of China Medical System Holdings, which holds an exclusive licence since 2020. Phase III trials in China met primary efficacy endpoints, showing sustained hemoglobin control, reduced hepcidin, and good safety. The approval opens a large market, given China’s >120 million CKD patients and high anemia prevalence, especially among non‑dialysis patients, addressing a significant unmet need. Over 100,000 Indian CKD patients have been treated with Desidustat since its 2022 launch

Adani Group’s $260 Billion Greenfield Push to Bolster India’s Infrastructure

ADANIPORTS

The Adani Group, led by Karan Adani, plans to invest 2 lakh crore (≈$260 bn) annually in greenfield projects over the next five years across renewable energy, transmission, ports, logistics, airports, data centres and related infrastructure. The strategy aims to make India self‑reliant in energy, strengthen logistics networks, and reduce supply‑chain vulnerabilities highlighted by recent geopolitical shocks. The group targets doubling port capacity to 1,200 MMT, expanding renewable power to 50 GW, and increasing thermal generation to 45 GW by 2030, while also streamlining management and leveraging partnerships for faster execution. Emphasis is placed on cost‑effective power, integrated logistics, materials and defence, and a values‑driven, purpose‑focused leadership
