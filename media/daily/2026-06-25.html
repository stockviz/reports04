RBI’s Circuit‑Breaker Measures Stabilize the Rupee, Buying Time for Structural Reforms

In a recent interview, JP Morgan analyst Sajjid Chinoy explains that the RBI’s aggressive forward‑swap “circuit‑breaker” policy, alongside government actions, successfully halted a self‑fulfilling rupee depreciation spiral triggered by Middle‑East tensions, high oil prices, and drying capital flows. Generous swap terms and incentives (NRI deposits, PSU dollar borrowing, tax changes) reversed a sharp spike in forward hedging, restored market confidence, and kept the rupee steady even as the dollar surged. While these steps provide a six‑to‑twelve‑month “bridge” that should attract a significant capital influx and support the balance of payments, Chinoy warns that longer‑term reforms are still needed to sustainably draw foreign investment and revive private corporate capital cycles
