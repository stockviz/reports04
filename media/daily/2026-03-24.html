Blackstone Mulls $200‑$300 M Investment in IPL Franchises

WIPRO OFSS APOLLOHOSP BCG 5PAISA TCNSBRANDS

Blackstone’s private‑equity fund for wealthy investors is evaluating a $200‑$300 million stake in either the Royal Challengers Bengaluru or the Rajasthan Royals, both currently for sale at valuations of $1.3‑$2 billion. The deal would be made through the BXPE fund, allowing a longer holding period than typical drawdown funds. Other PE firms are also eyeing sports assets, with recent bids on RCB from EQT and Azim Premji’s family office and a consortium led by Ranjan Pai; Rajasthan Royals bidders are still assessing offers. New buyers must pay in full upfront, unlike earlier IPL sales. The move reflects growing interest in the lucrative IPL, whose media rights and high‑profile ownership make it an attractive platform in India’s expanding wealth market

Meta Faces New Mexico Trial Over Alleged Child‑Safety Misleading Practices

In a landmark New Mexico state court case, prosecutors allege Meta (owner of Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp) knowingly prioritized profit over child safety, violating state consumer protection laws by allowing algorithms to push harmful content, failing to enforce the minimum age limit, and concealing internal research showing problematic teen use. After six weeks of testimony from teachers, experts, whistleblowers, and Meta executives, the jury will decide on two Unfair Practices Act violations, which could result in billions of dollars in fines. Meta denies wrongdoing, claiming robust safety measures and accusing prosecutors of cherry‑picking evidence. The trial’s outcome could set precedent for numerous similar lawsuits nationwide
