Understanding ROE vs. ROCE: How Efficiently Companies Earn on Invested Capital

NESTLEIND ASIANPAINT

The script explains that Return on Equity (ROE) measures profit generated per rupee of shareholders’ equity but can be inflated by high debt, as it ignores debt risk. Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) addresses this by evaluating earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) against total capital used (assets minus current liabilities), reflecting the efficiency of the entire capital base, not just equity. Consistently high ROCE indicates strong, debt‑independent business performance, as seen in companies like Asian Paints and Nestle India, whereas capital‑intensive firms may have modest ROCE despite profit growth. Investors should focus on ROCE to assess true value creation and sustainable compounding
