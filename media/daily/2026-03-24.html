HDFC Bank Appoints External Law Firms to Review Chairman’s Resignation Letter

MANCREDIT HDFCBANK

HDFC Bank announced the appointment of independent domestic and international law firms to investigate the resignation letter of former part‑time chairman Atanu Chakraborty, who stepped down citing differences over values and ethics. The board approved the review on March 23, 2026, but did not disclose the firms or timeline. Chakraborty’s abrupt exit on March 18, 2026, sparked governance concerns and a sharp decline in the bank’s share price, which fell about 12% over three sessions. The move follows recent executive dismissals linked to alleged mis‑selling of Credit Suisse AT‑1 bonds, raising questions about the bank’s governance and stakeholder confidence

Accenture Enforces Mandatory Seat Bookings for Office Visits in India

TCS INFY RUBICON EVERESTIND

Accenture India has warned employees that walking into the office without a pre‑booked seat will trigger corrective action, as the firm monitors unreserved walk‑ins. Employees must reserve a desk at least a day in advance (by 12 pm the day before) to access office space and shuttle services. The policy aims to improve seat utilization and support a $1.5 billion global real‑estate consolidation effort that seeks to reduce office footprints and boost margins, which rose to 14.7% in FY25. While other Indian IT firms mandate fixed office days, Accenture follows a hybrid model with no set days. The move reflects concerns that the company has more staff than available seats, prompting tighter capacity management despite growth in headcount and plans to hire more entry‑level talent

Rapido Eyes Food‑Delivery Disruption with Ownly

3MINDIA ZOMATO OLAELEC SWIGGY

Rapido, the bike‑taxi leader, is launching Ownly, a subscription‑based food‑delivery platform aimed at challenging Zomato and Swiggy by offering zero‑commission, transparent pricing for restaurants. Starting in Bengaluru with ~20,000 partners (5,000 new to delivery apps), Ownly targets small, underserved eateries and plans a metro‑first expansion to tier‑II cities. The model relies on restaurant subscription fees rather than customer charges, hoping to curb the high sticker shock from fees and delivery costs that inflate orders by up to 50%. Despite the lucrative market (combined quarterly GOV of ~₹19,000 cr) and past failures of Uber and Ola, Rapido is seeking $500‑$600 million funding to scale its low‑cost logistics approach, betting that transparent pricing will win both restaurant and consumer loyalty

Blackstone Mulls $200‑$300 M Investment in IPL Franchises

WIPRO OFSS APOLLOHOSP BCG 5PAISA TCNSBRANDS

Blackstone’s private‑equity fund for wealthy investors is evaluating a $200‑$300 million stake in either the Royal Challengers Bengaluru or the Rajasthan Royals, both currently for sale at valuations of $1.3‑$2 billion. The deal would be made through the BXPE fund, allowing a longer holding period than typical drawdown funds. Other PE firms are also eyeing sports assets, with recent bids on RCB from EQT and Azim Premji’s family office and a consortium led by Ranjan Pai; Rajasthan Royals bidders are still assessing offers. New buyers must pay in full upfront, unlike earlier IPL sales. The move reflects growing interest in the lucrative IPL, whose media rights and high‑profile ownership make it an attractive platform in India’s expanding wealth market

Meta Faces New Mexico Trial Over Alleged Child‑Safety Misleading Practices

In a landmark New Mexico state court case, prosecutors allege Meta (owner of Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp) knowingly prioritized profit over child safety, violating state consumer protection laws by allowing algorithms to push harmful content, failing to enforce the minimum age limit, and concealing internal research showing problematic teen use. After six weeks of testimony from teachers, experts, whistleblowers, and Meta executives, the jury will decide on two Unfair Practices Act violations, which could result in billions of dollars in fines. Meta denies wrongdoing, claiming robust safety measures and accusing prosecutors of cherry‑picking evidence. The trial’s outcome could set precedent for numerous similar lawsuits nationwide
