It Seems Refiners In India Have Been Asked To Reduce Purchases: Energy Aspects | CNBC TV18

RELIANCE

The discussion centered on India’s refining sector and global oil‑gas dynamics. Refiners in India have been asked to curb purchases amid U.S.–India trade talks and tariff pressures, limiting access to cheaper Russian crude. Amrita Sen highlighted that, despite apparent oversupply, about 140 million barrels of Russian oil are “on water” and unavailable, creating a market discrepancy. Geopolitical tensions—particularly sanctions on Russia and Iran—remain the primary price driver, with the outlook hinging on whether sanctions ease. She forecast a modest pullback in crude prices in Q1, but cautioned that maintenance outages, a major U.S. winter storm (reducing output by ~9 million barrels), and tight gas markets could keep prices elevated. U.S. gas prices have spiked to $7/MMBtu, and the emerging global LNG market makes regional shocks more worldwide. India’s import mix is shifting: reduced Russian imports (down from ~2 mb/d to <1 mb/d), no Iranian purchases, and potential new supplies from Venezuela pending licensing. Overall, refining margins remain healthy, though future price trajectories depend on sanctions, production responses, and seasonal demand patterns.

Tweaks In Capital Taxes? | No Real, High Conviction Expectations From Budget 2026: Samir Arora

5PAISA TVVISION

Samir Arora, founder and fund manager at Helios Capital and CNBC TV 18 budget editor, says he has no concrete expectations for the upcoming Budget 2026. He notes the budget is unusually quiet and unlikely to contain market‑moving measures. While some speculate about a possible reduction in long‑term capital gains (LTCG) tax for foreign institutional investors (FIIs), Arora doubts any substantive change will occur, especially after recent tax hikes.

He discusses the broader issue of capital‑gain taxation, arguing that equity investors in India effectively pay higher taxes than debt investors because interest income benefits from tax offsets that capital gains lack. He suggests a uniform, lower tax—perhaps 7.5% temporarily—could boost market sentiment, but acknowledges political and fiscal constraints make such reforms unlikely.

Arora also touches on the impact of currency volatility on returns, the need for tax parity between resident and foreign investors, and the limited scope for budget‑driven market catalysts. Overall, he urges investors not to place high hopes on the budget and to focus on fundamentals and longer‑term trends.

India & EU Sign Landmark FTA | European Cars Can Become Cheaper By 7-8% Due To FTA: BNP Paribas

ASHOKLEY M&M MARUTI

  • India and the EU have formalised a free‑trade agreement; it will be signed later this year after legal vetting and ratification.
  • Under the deal, cars made in India will get duty‑free access to the EU, while EU‑built cars (CBU) imported into India will see tariffs cut from the current 70‑110 % to about 10 % over 5‑10 years – a potential price drop of ~20 % in the long run, with an interim reduction to 35‑40 % of the tariff.
  • Most European models sold in India are assembled locally as CKD units; for these the tariff cut translates to an estimated 7 % price reduction.
