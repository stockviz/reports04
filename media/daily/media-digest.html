Bangladesh’s Harnest Debuts Sustainable Trims Platform

BMWVENTLTD

Harnest has launched the Responsible Trims Collection, a manufacturing platform that helps brands incorporate recycled, next‑generation and biodegradable trims, threads and accessories into existing supply chains at industrial scale. Trims make up over 40 % of a garment’s material cost yet are rarely addressed in circular fashion. The collection partners with five material innovators—OceanSafe, Ambercycle, BlockTexx, Indorama Ventures and Jiaren—to provide textile‑to‑textile recycling, advanced polymer regeneration and biodegradable solutions. Overseas Acquisitions Drive Indian Pharma Shift Up the Value Chain

BIOCON TCNSBRANDS ZYDUSLIFE LUPIN NATCOPHARM APLLTD AUROPHARMA SUNPHARMA ANTHEM AHL

Indian drugmakers are increasingly pursuing outbound M&A to move beyond generics, targeting innovative and specialty assets abroad. While deals remain modest compared to global biotech megadeals, activity is rising, with Sun Pharma eyeing a $12 bn purchase of Organon and Zydus evaluating Ardelyx for $2.2‑2.5 bn. Recent mid‑size acquisitions include Intas’s $558 m Udenyca purchase, Natco’s $226 m stake in Adcock Ingram, and Lupin’s €190 m VISUfarma deal. Drivers are global uncertainties, generics price erosion, and a shift toward biologics, prompting firms to seek higher‑margin markets in the US, Europe, Latin America, and South Africa, and to explore licensing with China. Strong balance sheets provide financing capacity, but execution risk remains, as seen in mixed reactions to past large deals like Biocon‑Viatris. The next 4‑5 years could see $20 bn of outbound M&A, focusing on capability‑led, targeted acquisitions rather than large‑scale transformations
