Persistent Systems Acquires Nagarro: CEO Manas Discusses Growth, Synergies, and Future Outlook

PERSISTENT

Persistent Systems announced its acquisition of European digital engineering firm Nagarro, with Nagarro’s co‑founder and CEO Manas joining Persistent’s board. Manas explained that Nagarro’s modest 5.5% CAGR stemmed from a focus on digital build rather than large‑deal sales and not from AI disruption. He highlighted three reasons the deal makes sense: Persistent’s commercial savvy and CFO expertise will bring margin discipline; complementary geographies, service lines, and client relationships will unlock synergies; and Persistent can transform Nagarro’s engineering‑centric culture into a growth engine. The acquisition offers Nagarro access to deeper capital markets and a higher valuation (a 140% premium). Manas emphasized that people are the core asset, assuring employees of a people‑centric integration with minimal job losses. He affirmed his personal commitment to the combined entity’s success, while noting that future revenue and margin targets will be shaped by Persistent’s commercial discipline and the realized synergies. The market will watch how Persistent scales Nagarro’s capabilities and improves profitability

Audit Gaps and Regulatory Scrutiny in Rajesh Exports’ International Operations

TCNSBRANDS ACC RAJESHEXPO SENCO

Fresh audit concerns have emerged for Rajesh Exports Ltd after Mint’s review revealed that a limited‑purpose, non‑statutory opinion issued by KPMG Switzerland for its Swiss arm, Global Gold Refineries AG, was likely used in the group’s consolidated financial statements despite being marked “internal use only.” This raises questions about whether Indian and overseas auditors independently verified the accounts. SEBI’s June 3 interim order highlighted that over 98% of the company’s revenue could not be independently verified, citing opaque disclosures for subsidiaries in Singapore, the UAE, and Switzerland. KPMG’s engagement letter explicitly disclaimed a statutory audit, yet the opinion appears to have been relied upon for consolidation, shifting responsibility to the Indian group auditor under international standards. Other auditors—BSD & Co (India), Meridian Audit (Singapore), PV Ramana Reddy & Co (ACC Energy), and PwC/MSKA (Elest)—also faced issues: PV Ramana Reddy & Co resigned over an expired peer‑review certificate, and MSKA flagged insufficient audit evidence for Elest before resigning, with PwC later giving a clean report. Significant related‑party transfers (≈₹566 cr) between Rajesh Exports and its battery unit Elest, and back‑flows of ₹350 cr, attracted regulator scrutiny. SEBI has referred the matter to the National Financial Reporting Authority for possible action against the auditors, prompting calls to examine whether audit procedures and professional scepticism were adequate. All contacted auditors declined comment

Big Four Expand into Tier‑2/3 Indian Cities to Capture SME Growth

India’s leading audit and consulting firms—EY, KPMG, PwC, and Deloitte—are opening offices in non‑metro hubs such as Jaipur, Coimbatore, Mysore, Bhubaneswar, Jamshedpur, Kochi, Chandigarh, Trivandrum and Gandhinagar. They target fast‑growing promoter‑led SMEs and mid‑size firms that seek services like M&A, tax, succession planning, ERP implementation and digital transformation. The shift is driven by rising revenue of regional companies (₹200‑500 cr) aiming for 10‑fold growth, the proliferation of GCCs, and AI‑powered platforms that enable remote consulting. While 80 % of revenue still comes from metros, the Big Four anticipate a gradual rebalancing as tier‑2/3 markets expand. Talent pools in these cities are strong and willing to stay locally, with compensation comparable to metro levels, supporting the firms’ hiring spurt

US Judge’s Procedural Inquiry Unlikely to Block Dismissal of Adani Case

ADANIGREEN BCG

A senior US lawyer says the Brooklyn judge’s request for a detailed explanation from the DOJ under Rule 48(a) is a routine procedural step, not a sign the indictment will survive. Courts have virtually no authority to compel the Justice Department to pursue a case the executive has decided to drop. The DOJ is expected to comply by the July 13 deadline and dismiss the securities‑fraud and wire‑fraud charges within weeks. Adani’s lawyers argue the transactions fall outside U.S. securities law and highlight factual and legal weaknesses, reinforcing the DOJ’s decision to seek dismissal
