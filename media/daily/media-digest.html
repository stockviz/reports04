Rupee Opens At 96.37/$ | Rupee Depreciation Manageable, Should Avoid Vicious Cycle: Kotak

KEC KOTAKBANK ICICIBANK

Title: Managing the Rupee Depreciation Crisis: Short‑Term Firefighting and Long‑Term Strategies

Summary:
The panel discussed India’s recent rupee slide to 96.37 per $ and evaluated immediate “firefighting” actions—fuel price hikes, a $100 million net open‑market position for banks, tighter rupee‑derivative rules, a dollar facility for oil refiners, and increased duties on gold and silver imports. Samir Narang (ICICI) and Upasana Bharadwaj (Kaurigmandra) argued that further steps are needed: deeper compression of gold/silver imports to cut the current‑account deficit (aiming to reduce the import share from ~2 % to ~1.5 % of GDP), higher pump prices, and additional regulatory tweaks.

On exchange‑rate policy, they concurred that some rupee weakening is inevitable to address external imbalances, but it must be managed to avoid a vicious inflation‑devaluation cycle. Suggested capital‑account measures included tighter LRS caps, ODI restrictions, and selective quantitative controls, while cautioning that tax changes (e.g., on capital gains) would have limited impact amid global sentiment.

Regarding financing, they noted that issuing new debt in a high‑interest‑rate environment would be costly, so a mix of current‑account curbs and attracting sticky bond inflows is preferable. Growth forecasts were trimmed: Samir sees FY24 GDP at 6.5 % (down from 6.9 % if prices stay high), while Upasana projects 6.0‑6.3 %.

A brief aside highlighted labor shortages in the construction sector (e.g., KEC International operating at ~50 % of required workforce), which the panel viewed as a temporary, sector‑specific issue rather than a structural threat. The discussion concluded that coordinated, incremental measures—both demand‑side (fuel, import duties) and supply‑side (regulatory, capital‑account)—are essential to stabilise the rupee and restore balance‑of‑payments health.
