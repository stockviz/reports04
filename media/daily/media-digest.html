IFSCA Tightens GIFT City Credit Rating Rules, Introducing Issuer Fact‑Check and Enhanced Record‑Keeping

SPAL AGL

The International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA) has amended its Master Circular for Credit Rating Agencies operating in GIFT City. Effective immediately, agencies must give issuers a chance to review and correct any factual errors, omissions or misperceptions that could materially affect a rating before it is published, while preserving the agency’s analytical independence. The rule does not apply to unsolicited or private ratings. Additional changes include stricter record‑keeping requirements—maintaining detailed, reconstructible documentation of rating decisions and their rationale—and an expanded definition of credit ratings to cover issuers, credit‑quality and financial‑strength ratings. Private and unsolicited ratings are exempt from mandatory public disclosure. The amendments aim to align GIFT City practices with global standards and IOSCO guidelines

Should You Shift From EPF to NPS? The Truth Unveiled

ANANDRATHI

The episode explains why salaried employees should consider supplementing their EPF with the National Pension Scheme (NPS). NPS offers significant tax deductions (up to 14% of basic + DA as employer contribution and an additional ₹50,000 under Section 80CCD(1B)), forced long‑term savings, low fees, and the ability to allocate up to 75% in equities for higher returns. Tier‑1 NPS provides the tax benefits but locks money until age 60, allowing a 60% tax‑free withdrawal, 20% mandatory annuity, and the remaining 20% taxable. Tier‑2 is a flexible, non‑tax‑advantaged account. Investors can choose auto‑allocation (age‑based equity‑debt mix) or active allocation (selecting specific schemes, up to 75% equity, or a newer 100%‑equity option). Compared with hybrid mutual funds, NPS fees are ultra‑low (15‑30 bps). The key trade‑off is reduced liquidity and mandatory annuity versus higher pre‑tax returns and tax savings. For most employees, directing ~26% of basic + DA into EPF and NPS (while staying under the ₹7.5 L annual limit) maximizes retirement savings; the choice between EPF’s guaranteed ~8% and NPS’s market‑linked returns depends on risk appetite. At retirement, it’s advisable to withdraw the tax‑free portion, invest it for cash flow, and select an annuity that suits one’s survivor needs
