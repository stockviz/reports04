MSMEs Seek Six‑Month Loan Moratorium as Gas Shortage Halts Production

HINDALCO NDRAUTO

Ongoing gas supply disruptions from the West Asia war are crippling MSMEs in western India, especially metal‑processing, foundries, and aluminium extrusion firms. Over 70 % have cut output by 50‑60 % and cannot meet pending orders before the fiscal year‑end. Companies have tried switching to coal, but high costs and uncertainty persist. Industry bodies plan to lobby the RBI for a 3‑6‑month moratorium on existing loans and additional credit under the Credit Guarantee Scheme. The shortage has also driven up TMT bar prices, forced production cuts in the Morbi kiln cluster, and threatens real‑estate and infrastructure projects, while Hindalco has halted extruded aluminium production. The sector urges government relief akin to Covid‑era measures

Ferrari CEO Benedetto Vigna on India’s Potential and Leadership Vision

TV18BRDCST

At the CNBC TV18 India Business Leader Awards, Ferrari CEO Benedetto Vigna emphasized the blend of tradition and innovation that drives the brand, highlighting India’s growing passion for Ferrari and its cultural alignment with the company’s “ITech” identity. Mercedes‑Benz CLA Electric First‑Drive Review – Overdrive Highlights

OBEROIRLTY

The all‑electric CLA returns to India on Mercedes’ new MMA skateboard platform, offering an 85.5 kWh battery with a WLTP‑rated 792 km range and 800 V fast‑charging (≈400 km in 20 min). Power comes from a 200 kW rear‑wheel‑drive unit paired with an unusual two‑speed gearbox, delivering 0‑100 km/h in 6.7 s and a drag coefficient of 0.21. Inside, the MBOS system integrates infotainment, ADAS and powertrain via four Nvidia Orion domain computers (508 TOPS). The car features Level‑2 Gen‑6 ADAS, 360° cameras, six USB‑C ports, and a stylish digital interior, but cabin space is tighter for taller occupants. Overall, the CLA blends sporty dynamics, long‑range capability, and cutting‑edge tech, positioning it as an aspirational electric four‑door coupe for Indian buyers
