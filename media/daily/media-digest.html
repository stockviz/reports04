Cyient’s $218 M Tao Digital acquisition highlights mid‑size Indian IT firms’ buyout drive

COFORGE FSL AAATECH PERSISTENT HEXT INFY ASHIKA CYIENT SMCGLOBAL SASKEN PAISALO

Hyderabad‑based Cyient is buying AI‑focused data and product engineering firm Tao Digital Solutions for $218 million, its largest deal, to add $80 million of revenue and expand market access. The move reflects a broader trend of Indian mid‑size IT services companies (revenues under $2 bn) snapping up smaller firms to bolster AI capabilities, fill growth gaps, and counter pricing pressure on traditional services. Recent similar deals include Persistent’s €6 m purchase of Concise, Hexaware’s $120 m buy of SMC Squared, Firstsource’s $28 m acquisition of Pastdue Credit Solutions, and Sasken’s $40 m purchase of Borqs’s software unit, while Coforge’s $2.35 bn Encora deal remains the largest. These acquisitions have pushed the combined M&A spend of India’s top 15 IT firms to $5 bn since April 2023

Tata Sons Marks Down Tata Digital Valuation Amid Cash‑Burn Concerns

RELIANCE TATACAP TATAMOTORS NSIL TENNIND BEL PAISALO

Tata Sons injected just under ₹3,000 cr into Tata Digital in April at a $10.3 bn valuation, 5.5% lower than its February 2025 round, marking the first markdown. The infusion brings Tata Digital’s post‑money value down to ₹98,031 cr, with per‑share price at ₹47.75, down 8.35% from ₹52.10. The move follows scrutiny over the venture’s heavy cash burn (₹9,000 cr debt, ₹16,958 cr losses), weak consumer uptake of the Tata Neu super‑app, and multiple leadership changes. Tata Trusts chair Noel Tata has demanded a clear roadmap, linking future funding to operating milestones such as customer retention and profitability. The markdown signals a more disciplined capital‑allocation stance as Tata Sons evaluates the viability of its digital ecosystem, which includes BigBasket, 1mg, Tata CLiQ and other platforms, while questioning the super‑app strategy’s sustainability. The board’s upcoming meetings will address Tata Digital’s future and the tenure of Tata Sons chair N. Chandrasekaran

NVIDIA’s Aggressive H‑1B Hiring and High Salaries Amid AI Boom

NVIDIA is boosting its H‑1B hires, certifying about 1,200 positions in the first half of fiscal 2026—up from 1,000 a year earlier—while rivals cut theirs. Federal filings reveal base salaries ranging from roughly ₹1.03 cr to ₹4.67 cr (≈$108k‑$489k), with software engineers earning up to ₹3.74 cr, AI researchers and architects surpassing ₹4 cr, and directors/managers exceeding ₹4.6 cr. The chipmaker’s soaring stock enables generous equity awards, though the disclosed figures reflect only base pay. NVIDIA’s strategy underscores its commitment to attract top global talent for AI, chip design, and software development despite a tightening U.S. immigration climate

Wockhardt’s US‑Approved Antibiotic Zaynich Targets Commercial Success

WOCKPHARMA ORCHPHARMA SPARC

Wockhardt’s $800 million investment in antibiotic research paid off with US FDA approval for Zaynich, the second Indian‑origin new drug and the first without a foreign partner. The company plans a US launch in 6‑8 months, with parallel roll‑outs in India, Europe and the UK, aiming for peak global sales of $1.5 billion. Unlike generics, commercializing this new chemical entity will require a dedicated medical‑oriented organization, hospital outreach, and modest marketing spend due to its ICU focus. Wockhardt will build its own US and European teams, outsource salesforce functions, and expects lower costs than typical new‑drug launches. Zaynich, a cefepime‑zidebactam combo, showed 89 % efficacy versus 68.4 % for meropenem in Phase III trials. The firm’s pipeline includes six antibiotics, all with FDA QIDP and fast‑track designations, but Zaynich is the only one fully approved for US sales. Wockhardt sees no liquidity issues and will continue investing in R&D and pipeline development

India’s IPO slowdown pushes firms toward private deals, but deal appetite remains strong

JBCHEPHARM JSWENERGY JIOFIN TORNTPHARM MEDANTA NTPCGREEN ADANIPOWER TORNTPOWER SBILIFE NFL SERVOTECH

Global uncertainty and a softer IPO market are steering Indian companies to private equity, credit and strategic investors, though transactions are taking longer due to heightened valuation scrutiny. While some high‑profile IPOs (e.g., Flipkart, PhonePe) are delayed, large listings like SBI Funds Management and Manipal Health are still slated for mid‑year. Deal volume remains steady, but high‑value M&A fell sharply, with sectors such as healthcare, financial services, infrastructure and agri‑business retaining strong demand. Investors are more selective on pricing, preferring quality assets and longer closing timelines, but distress‑driven deals are not expected to dominate. Deloitte’s Rohit Berry notes the shift is in pace, not appetite, and the firm now focuses on transactions above $250 million

Alphabet Launches $80 Billion Capital Raise Backed by Berkshire Hathaway to Expand AI Infrastructure

STANLEY HINDWAREAP

Alphabet announced an $80 billion equity raise to fund a massive expansion of its AI compute capacity, citing record demand that outstrips current supply. Berkshire Hathaway will anchor the deal with a $10 billion private placement, buying $5 billion each of Class A and Class C shares. The remaining $70 billion will be raised via a $30 billion underwritten offering (including convertible preferred shares) and a $40 billion at‑the‑market program for Class A and C stock. Proceeds will support new data centers and related infrastructure as Alphabet lifts its cap‑ex outlook to $180‑$190 billion. The move occurs amid industry‑wide AI spending projected to exceed $700 billion this year, with analysts forecasting over $1 trillion in AI infrastructure outlays by 2027. Berkshire’s investment reflects a strategic shift toward technology assets under Greg Abel’s leadership
