MRF Steel Brace SP‑01 Review: A Homegrown Contender to Premium Imports

MRF MARUTI

Overdrive tests the new MRF Steel Brace SP‑01 on a Suzuki Hayabusa at MRF’s Trichy proving ground, noting its 0° steel‑radial construction delivers excellent high‑speed stability, cornering grip, and heat management even in 60 °C track temps. ONGC Rebrands as a “Gas‑and‑Oil” Company

ONGC

Chairman Arun Kumar Singh says ONGC’s production mix has tipped in favour of natural gas, which now exceeds crude oil and is set to drive 7‑8% annual growth. Flat oil output, rising domestic gas demand, market‑linked pricing reforms and new field developments (e.g., DUDP, DSF, offshore 98/2 wells) are boosting gas output, while offshore projects worth about ₹33,000 crore, a technical service partnership with BP, and overseas assets (Russia, Mozambique, Venezuela) support reserve replacement (>1.1). Gas is projected to become the dominant share of the portfolio, positioning ONGC as a gas‑heavy, diversified energy player

Mercedes‑AMG GLC 53 Reignites AMG DNA in Hamburg Test

BMWVENTLTD

The Overdrive episode reviews the new AMG‑tuned GLC 53, noting its 3.0‑litre inline‑six with a turbo, electric auxiliary compressor and 48 V hybrid system delivering 449 PS and up to 640 Nm on boost, enabling a 0‑100 km/h time of 4.2 seconds. Dalmia Bharat targets ₹4,000 cr fundraise to lift cement capacity to 110‑130 MTPA by FY31

JPASSOCIAT DALBHARAT AMBUJACEM ULTRACEMCO

Dalmia Bharat plans to raise up to ₹4,000 crore through equity, GDRs/ADRs, convertible bonds and other securities to fund acquisitions, greenfield and brownfield projects. It aims to boost capacity from 49.5 MTPA to 75 MTPA in the medium term and ultimately to 110‑130 MTPA by FY31, aligning with a projected 6‑7% CAGR in cement demand. The company has earmarked a ₹6,800 crore Phase II investment for 12 MTPA additions at Belgaum, Pune and Kadapa, is building a bulk terminal near Chennai, and has agreed to acquire Jaiprakash Associates’ assets for ₹2,850 crore. Proceeds will finance capex, debt repayment, working capital and corporate needs. FY26 saw record EBITDA of ₹3,083 crore, revenue of ₹14,804 crore and PAT of ₹1,157 crore, with capacity expected to reach 66.7 MTPA by FY28. The firm also plans to premium‑ise its product mix with specialised roof, column and foundation cements

India‑UK Free Trade Agreement Opens Duty‑Free EV Export Quotas for Indian Makers

MARUTI TMPV M&M

The India‑UK CETA, effective July 15, grants Indian passenger‑vehicle manufacturers (Maruti Suzuki, Mahindra & Mahindra, Tata Motors) duty‑free access to the UK electric, hybrid and hydrogen car market from the sixth year of the pact. Quotas are tiered by price: under £20,000, £20‑40 k and £40‑80 k, with 6,800 units for each of the first two tiers and 4,000 for the top tier in year 6, rising to a peak of 88,000 units (34,000, 34,000, 20,000) from year 15 onward. Cars above £80 k remain excluded. Executives see the agreement as an opportunity to expand EV exports, complementing existing sales (e.g., Maruti’s eVITARA) and supporting “Make in India for the world.” The FTA aims to double bilateral trade to $100 bn by 2030
