War‑Driven Market Outlook: Staggered Buying, Caution on Metals, Focus on Banks and Pharma

CHALET DAMCAPITAL

Jyoti Vartan Jayapuriya of Valentes Advisors notes that wars historically cause about a 7% market drawdown, with recovery typically within 20‑25 trading days, making the current dip a buying opportunity for investors with a 1‑2‑year horizon—provided they stagger entries rather than invest all at once. He warns that India feels the war most through rising oil and especially gas prices, which could tighten raw‑material supplies and logistics costs. While metals have spiked due to geopolitical tension, he recommends holding existing positions but avoiding new purchases at elevated levels. Chemicals face dual pressure from raw‑material volatility and higher freight/insurance costs, likely causing short‑term weakness; however, they could be bought once conditions normalise. Remittance flows to the Middle East are expected to dip briefly but not collapse, as reconstruction will drive demand later. In the near term, he favours sectors less exposed to war‑related disruptions: domestic banks (still cheap), select capital‑intensive names, and pharmaceuticals. Overall, the strategy is to deploy cash gradually, target undervalued domestic stocks, and steer clear of firms heavily dependent on war‑sensitive logistics or raw‑material inputs

Genesys International Launches India’s First Advanced GPR System for 3D Subsurface Mapping

GENESYS

Mumbai‑based Genesys International Corp introduced the country’s first advanced Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) system, sourced from Italy’s IDS GeoRadar, featuring patented Equalised Scrambling Technology and Wide/Multi‑Array Antennas for high‑resolution 3D imaging of underground utilities. Integrated with Genesys’s digital mapping and 3D geospatial platforms, the solution creates subsurface digital twins for smart city, metro, highway, water infrastructure, and utility modernisation projects, aiming to reduce excavation damage and project delays. Chairman Sajid Malik highlighted the critical need for better mapping of buried water networks. Following the launch, Genesys shares rose 4.21% to ₹283.15 on the NSE

Vertiv’s Vision for India’s AI‑Driven Data Center Future

In a conversation at the AI Summit New Delhi, Vertiv CEO Zodano Albatazi highlighted India’s pivotal role in the company’s global AI strategy, emphasizing the country’s growing importance as both a domestic market and an export hub for AI‑focused data centers, or “AI factories.” He discussed the shift from traditional data centers to high‑density, GPU‑driven AI facilities that demand novel power and cooling solutions, larger scales (gigawatt‑level), and faster, prefabricated construction to reduce field complexity and time‑to‑token. Vertiv is expanding its Indian manufacturing footprint, leveraging local suppliers and engineering talent to create a self‑sufficient ecosystem that serves global demand. The company benefits from India’s abundant power, supportive policies, and demographic advantages, though it views incentives as market‑driven rather than direct drivers for Vertiv. Subhashish Majumdar added that India’s robust supply chain flexibility enhances Vertiv’s ability to meet regional and global demand, mitigating worldwide supply challenges. On energy, Vertiv is moving toward 800 V DC architectures, battery storage, and multi‑source power integration to support AI workloads sustainably. Regarding AI’s broader impact, Albatazi stressed AI as an augmenting technology that will reshape jobs, rewarding those who adopt and integrate AI while presenting overall optimism for humanity’s future. The summit’s enthusiasm and networking were seen as catalysts for accelerating India’s AI infrastructure growth

Deloitte’s GenW.AI: A Unified Low‑Code Platform to Scale Enterprise AI
