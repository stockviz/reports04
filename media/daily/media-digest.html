Infosys Chairman Nandan Nilekani on Automation and AI’s Role in IT Services

TCS INFY

Infosys chairman Nandan Nilekani dismissed concerns that AI‑driven coding automation will make tech services obsolete, arguing that the rise of generative AI has actually heightened the firm’s relevance. In his May 22 shareholder address he highlighted Infosys’s 4.6% revenue growth to $20.16 billion, a 5% increase in headcount, and AI‑related revenue now accounting for 5.5% of quarterly sales. Nilekani emphasized that AI creates new opportunities across the software development lifecycle—testing, validation, cybersecurity, and modernising legacy systems—requiring upskilled talent, new mental models, and a focus on trust, security, and data sovereignty. He noted that blending new AI models with existing robust transaction systems will generate further business, and that Infosys is positioning itself with its Topaz AI Next platform and integrated EdgeVerve products to capture this demand, forecasting 1.5‑3.5% revenue growth in FY27

Audit Firms Block Access to Satirical “Cockroach Janta Party” Sites on Intranets

Employees at PwC, KPMG and Deloitte in India discovered that the CJP’s websites (cockroachjantaparty.org and cjpparty.com) were blocked on their corporate intranets, citing information‑security risk assessments. The satire, born from Chief Justice Surya Kant’s “cockroach” comment, quickly went viral, prompting a government block of the party’s X (Twitter) account. While other private firms allow access, the audit firms’ strict whitelist policies prevent employees from viewing the sites, raising questions about corporate internet‑filtering authority, potential overreach under India’s IT Act, and employees’ rights to information and expression

Google Positions Gemini 3.5 Flash as Cost‑Focused AI Solution Amid Surging Corporate Token Bills

OFSS

Companies are facing unsustainable AI token costs as usage spikes, prompting Google to promote its Gemini 3.5 Flash model that delivers frontier‑level output at significantly lower expense. Leveraging its own TPU hardware and data‑center infrastructure, Google claims it can compute 50‑75 % cheaper than rivals, offering a path for firms—especially large Google Cloud customers—to cut AI spend by up to a billion dollars annually. This strategy mirrors Google’s historic focus on speed and cost in search, shifting the industry debate from raw performance to financial viability

Reliance Retail’s Profit Boost Driven by Zero‑Coupon Debt Conversion

RELIANCE METROBRAND RCOM RETAIL

In FY 2025‑26, Reliance Retail Ltd (RRL) posted a profit of ₹13,476 crore, up ₹3,503 crore from the prior year, mainly due to a debt restructuring with its parent, Reliance Retail Ventures Ltd (RRVL). RRVL converted about ₹40,000 crore of interest‑bearing inter‑company loans into zero‑coupon optionally fully convertible debentures (OFCDs), eliminating most of RRL’s finance costs (down from ₹4,725 crore to ₹1,510 crore). While RRVL’s interest income fell correspondingly, the consolidation shifted profit to the operating retail company, improving its financial health ahead of a potential retail listing. Additional restructuring saw long‑term loans of ₹5,656 crore repaid and short‑term loans reduced to ₹11,300 crore, with further OFCD issuances of ₹19,000 crore in mid‑2025. The move, described as a deleveraging exercise, lifted RRL’s profitability and underscored the retail segment’s growing contribution to Reliance Industries’ overall earnings

Amazon shuts down employee‑built AI token leaderboard amid “tokenmaxxing” concerns

Amazon has deprecated “KiroRank,” an internal dashboard that ranked staff by AI token usage, after senior VP Dave Treadwell warned against using AI merely to boost scores. The company says token consumption will still be tracked for cost management, but emphasizes outcome‑driven AI adoption rather than volume metrics. The move reflects a broader industry shift, with firms like Uber reevaluating AI spending as productivity gains lag behind rising token usage

Codelco Shifts Focus to Profitability Over Production Growth

New chairman Bernardo Fontaine has signaled a strategic pivot for Chile’s state‑owned copper giant, prioritizing safe, efficient and profitable operations over sheer output growth. The board approved measures to boost state contributions, curb debt, and enhance governance, including hiring a forensic auditor to verify production figures after a recent over‑counting scandal. While first‑quarter output fell 8% year‑on‑year due to accidents, lower ore grades and plant issues, realized copper prices drove a 94% increase in payments to the state. The company will also form a committee to monitor the El Teniente collapse and explore public‑private partnerships and management reforms
