India’s Market Outlook: Resilient Midcaps, Industrial Capex Super‑Cycle, and Policy Challenges

GEPIL CGPOWER ABB SIEMENS CUMMINSIND POWERINDIA

The Sensex and Nifty slipped almost 1% this week after a sharp Friday sell‑off, while mid‑cap indices and Nifty Bank showed relative strength. In a round‑table discussion, CIO Ravi Dharmishi highlighted that global uncertainty—particularly the ongoing conflict affecting oil prices—has capped headline indices, but a “stealth bull market” is thriving in industrials and manufacturing, driven by a multi‑year global capex super‑cycle focused on supply‑chain resilience, energy security, and strategic autonomy. He noted that a handful of firms in sectors such as high‑end transformers, data‑center infrastructure, and renewable energy (solar, hydrogen, wind, batteries) stand to benefit from oligopolistic structures and massive investment pipelines extending to 2032.

Dharmishi warned that while valuations appear high, the length and scale of the capex cycle justify a longer‑term view, and that India’s domestic renewable push—backed by policies like ALMM, PLI, and upcoming ALMM2—aims to reduce dependence on China and create integrated supply chains.

The panel also debated fiscal measures: long‑term capital‑gain tax cuts are unlikely, but targeted incentives (e.g., tax holidays for data centers) could attract foreign capital needed to stabilize the rupee and balance‑of‑payments. Defense spending was identified as another growing theme, shifting from a PSU‑dominated model to private‑sector participation, though large defense stocks may only be modest compounders. Overall, the consensus is that while headline indices appear stagnant, selective exposure to industrial, renewable, and defense themes aligned with the global capex surge could deliver strong returns over the next decade

The panel debated India’s lag in the AI value chain, noting that while global AI capex is dominated by US giants (Microsoft, Amazon, Google) and hardware by Asian firms (TSMC, Samsung, SK‑Hynix), India has no trillion‑dollar AI players and its listed AI exposure is modest.
