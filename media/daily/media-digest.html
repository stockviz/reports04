India’s Consumption Recovery: Q1 2024‑2026 Overview

TRENT DMART JUBLFOOD FCONSUMER MARICO KALYANKJIL TITAN DABUR RADICO V2RETAIL

The first‑quarter data shows a clear turn in Indian consumer sentiment, driven by strong price‑led growth and volume gains across staples, discretionary, jewelry and retail. Staples firms (Marico, Goodricke Consumer, Dabur) posted double‑digit revenue growth, with margins stabilising as raw‑material costs fall. Retailers like Avenue Supermarts and Trent saw 15‑19% sales growth, while value chains (V‑Mart, Bazaar) posted 23‑58% growth on strong same‑store sales and new store roll‑outs. Jewelry benefitted from stable gold prices, with brands such as Titan, Kalyan and Senko posting 38‑60% growth. Discretionary players (Jubilant Foodworks, Nike) recorded mid‑single‑digit to high‑double‑digit gains, especially in premium segments. Rural markets are outpacing urban ones, with premiumisation and newer D‑to‑C brands reshaping urban demand. Channel shifts show modern trade and quick commerce expanding rapidly, especially in tier‑2/3 towns. Margin pressures are viewed as transitory, expected to improve in H2 as price hikes recede and commodity inflation eases. Investment outlook favours market‑leaders with pricing power and strong online presence—e.g., Nike, Jubilant Foodworks, and premium FMCG players—while caution remains around inflation‑driven price‑reversal risks and fuel/commodity cost volatility. Overall, Q1 signals an inflection point toward sustained consumption growth, pending validation in the monsoon‑driven second half

Quick Commerce Boosts Horticulture Farmers by Bypassing Middlemen

ZOMATO

Partnerships between quick commerce platforms (e.g., Blinkit, Zepto) and farmer producer organisations (FPOs) are narrowing the price gap for horticultural produce. The Sambhal Swarnim Potato FPO’s turnover rose to ₹15 crore, with members receiving ₹2‑2.5 kg⁻¹ above mandi rates and payments within five days. Platforms procure directly from FPOs, buying grade‑A potatoes at higher prices (₹13‑15 kg⁻¹) than local mandis (₹6‑12 kg⁻¹) but sell at competitive retail rates by eliminating intermediaries, using return‑to‑vendor clauses, and maintaining strict quality standards. Vertical integration—farmer networks, collection centres, packaging, cold storage, and logistics—reduces leakage, handling costs, and waste, allowing higher farmer realisation while keeping platform margins at break‑even. This model is scaling across multiple FPOs and regions, benefitting both farmers and quick‑commerce firms

AI‑Driven Memory Crunch: RAM Prices Set to Stay High Until 2028

RUBICON
