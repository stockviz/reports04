AI‑Driven Deflation Overstated, Kotak Sees Value in Indian IT Services

KOTAKBANK

The Nifty IT index has tumbled 20% in February—the worst monthly drop since 2008—prompting concerns that AI will sharply deflate IT services revenues. Kotak Institutional analyst Kavaljit Seluja argues the sell‑off is excessive, noting that AI‑related deflation is modest (about 3% over three years) and that the sector can still achieve modest growth, with FY27 earnings forecasts remaining solid. Current multiples have compressed to 16‑18× FY27 estimates, reflecting heightened cost‑of‑equity due to uncertainty. While rapid AI model releases outpace enterprise readiness, service firms can act as a bridge, mitigating some deflationary pressure. Recent news such as Claude’s COBOL‑modernization tool and IBM’s Watson X illustrate both a potential revenue squeeze and new opportunities for legacy modernization projects. Seluja believes that, over a 3‑5‑year horizon, the net effect of AI could be neutral to positive as savings from automation are redeployed into new programs, but investors remain jittery about timing and magnitude of these changes
