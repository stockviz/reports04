Ola Electric’s Home Inverter Setback Highlights Preference for LFP Batteries

OLAELEC ARE&M KABRAEXTRU

Ola Electric’s home inverter division, Ola Shakti, fell far short of its revenue target, generating only ₹4 crore versus the projected ₹100 crore for Q1 FY26. The shortfall is attributed to the company’s initial use of nickel‑manganese‑cobalt (NMC) batteries, which are costlier and less suited for stationary storage than lithium‑iron‑phosphate (LFP) chemistry. While the firm plans to switch to LFP in the next quarter to improve safety, lifespan, and cost (LFP being 10‑25% cheaper), the delayed transition slowed ramp‑up. Industry experts concur that LFP is the preferred chemistry for home energy storage in India due to its thermal stability, longer cycle life, and lower price, making it the mass‑market choice for stationary applications
