US Supreme Court Declines TCS Appeal in $168 Million Trade‑Secret Verdict

TCS

The US Supreme Court denied Tata Consultancy Services’ request to review a Fifth Circuit judgment, leaving in place a $168 million damages award to DXC Technology (formerly CSC) for alleged trade‑secret theft. TCS has already recorded $150 million of the liability and will provision an additional $70 million as a one‑time exceptional expense in Q1 FY2027, bringing total expense to about $220 million. While this represents a low single‑digit percentage hit to TCS’s FY26 profit of ₹49,454 crore, the cash outflow is material in absolute terms. The case stems from a 2024 lawsuit in which DXC claimed TCS misappropriated its trade secrets to secure a $2.6 billion contract with Transamerica and develop its BaNCS platform, resulting in $56 million compensatory, $112 million exemplary damages and $25 million prejudgment interest

Monsoon Outlook 2026: Stalled Western Front and Growing Deficit

DHANI

In an interview, Skymet’s G.P. Sharma outlines the current monsoon situation: the Arabian Sea branch has been stalled over the Konkan coast since June 8, while the Bay of Bengal system is making slow, uneven progress over West Bengal, Bihar and Jharkhand. All‑India rainfall is already 32 % below normal for June, and could reach a 40 % deficit within a week if conditions persist. Key rain‑fed regions—Gujarat, Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Bihar, Jharkhand and Chhattisgarh—remain under‑served, whereas Kerala, coastal Karnataka and parts of Andhra Pradesh are receiving adequate rain. The deficit is driven by weak monsoon dynamics and a developing El Niño, with other climate factors (IOD, MBO) currently unfavorable. Although June contributes only about 15 % of the total monsoon rainfall, a recovery in July‑August is crucial; a late‑June or early‑July system could still mitigate the shortfall, but the outlook remains uncertain
