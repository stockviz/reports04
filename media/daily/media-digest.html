Kearney’s Century of Insight: Rethinking Globalisation, Supply Chains & Investment in a Turbulent World

Celebrating its 100‑year milestone, Kearney’s global managing partner Bob Willen and India head Siddharth Jain discuss how unprecedented geopolitical volatility, supply‑chain shocks and the war in the Middle East have forced CEOs to embed resilience through scenario planning and diversification. While many firms pause capital spending, Indian businesses remain relatively buoyant, earmarking new investments in renewable‑energy value chains, data‑center and electronics manufacturing, resource self‑sufficiency (mining, rare‑earths) and cross‑border M&A. AI is seen as both a major opportunity and a driver of workforce transformation, prompting Kearney to build AI‑enabled platforms and upskill staff in data fluency; however, adoption timelines remain longer than hype suggests. The Indian IT services sector faces a crossroads: firms must shift from low‑margin, labor‑intensive models to consultative, AI‑focused offerings, with likely consolidation among mid‑tier players and increased cross‑border acquisitions. On reshoring, Kearney’s reshoring index shows U.S. manufacturing capacity is rising but jobs have not yet returned; global supply chains stay complex, and India cannot rely on tariffs alone to attract FDI—innovation, productivity and export diversification are essential. Bilateral trade deals (US, EU, UK) signal that globalization is evolving, not ending, moving toward multi‑regional, “global‑and‑local” networks. CEOs are asking how to build resilience, leverage AI, and adapt to a shrinking corporate lifecycle. Will Willen’s top three decade‑defining trends are: (1) harnessing AI, (2) accelerating innovation across technology and science, and (3) cultivating adaptive leadership

Studds Accessories posts 6% Q4 profit rise, eyes premium growth and capacity expansion

STUDDS

Helmet maker Studds Accessories reported a 6.1% rise in Q4 FY26 net profit to ₹21.1 cr, with revenue up 11% to ₹167.5 cr. The firm plans to launch Alpinestars private‑label helmets, expand its European footprint (especially Italy) by Q2FY27, and add 3 million helmets of annual capacity (30% increase) by mid‑FY27, targeting further premium‑led growth

India’s cement sector eyes 7‑8% FY27 growth despite West Asia fuel cost headwinds

NUVOCO SHREECEM DALBHARAT AMBUJACEM BCG ULTRACEMCO

Leading cement makers (UltraTech, Ambuja, Shree, Dalmia, Nuvoco) project 7‑8% annual volume growth in FY27, driven by government infrastructure spend, housing demand and urbanisation. They plan to boost earnings through premiumisation, improved trade mix and higher sales realisations, even as fuel, freight and import‑linked costs rise due to West Asia tensions. Capex remains strong: UltraTech targets ₹8,000‑₹10,000 cr annually, Dalmia ₹3,200‑₹3,400 cr, Nuvoco ₹900‑₹960 cr, while Ambuja keeps FY27 spend moderate at ₹6,000‑₹6,500 cr. Executives acknowledge near‑term cost pressures and macro risks (inflation, monsoon, currency volatility) but remain confident in long‑term demand fundamentals

Coal India Targets Syngas Hubs Near Power, DRI, and Fertiliser Plants to Offset Gas Shortages

COALINDIA

State‑owned Coal India Ltd plans to develop coal‑to‑syngas facilities at pit‑head sites or adjacent to gas‑based power plants, direct‑reduced iron (DRI) units, and fertiliser complexes, using BOO or BOM models to cut logistics costs and ensure reliable syngas supply. The firm has issued expressions of interest for bidders and off‑takers, leveraging its joint ventures Bharat Coal Gasification & Chemicals Ltd and Coal Gasification India Ltd to diversify into coal gasification amid tightening gas supplies from the West Asia conflict

OpenAI hires researcher to tackle AI self‑improvement risks

OpenAI posted a high‑paying role ($295k‑$445k, ~2.5‑3.7 crore) on its Preparedness safety team to study recursive self‑improvement—AI systems that can redesign and enhance themselves without human input. The job involves defending models from data poisoning, building interpretability tools, measuring AI‑driven automation within the company, and assessing safety implications of self‑improving agents. The move reflects growing industry concern, echoed by Anthropic and experts, as AI coding tools accelerate, with OpenAI aiming for an automated AI researcher by 2028 and emphasizing the urgent, societal impact of such capabilities
