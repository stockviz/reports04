India Tightens Silver Imports with Licensing Requirements

The Indian government has introduced new restrictions on the import of high‑purity silver bars, mandating that importers obtain licenses and approvals. This measure follows a recent hike in customs duties on precious metals—from 6% to 15%—and adds to the existing 9% arbitrage fee. The policy targets over 90% of the silver entering the country, effectively curbing free movement of the metal. It aligns with earlier steps to tighten monitoring of bullion imports by the Gem & Jewellery Export Promotion Council (GJEPC). Since India imports about 80% of its silver needs, primarily from the UAE and China, the new rules are expected to impact import volumes, market dynamics, and pricing for traders
