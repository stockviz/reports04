Amagi Media Cloud FY27 Outlook: Targeting 25‑30% Revenue CAGR Over the Next Three Years

AMAGI

Amagi Media Cloud, a cloud‑based broadcast‑tech SaaS firm that recently listed (initially below issue price but now up ~43% YTD), serves global content creators by migrating broadcast operations to the cloud, providing streaming‑infrastructure and monetization tools, and offering free‑ad TV solutions. CEO Bhaskar Subramani explained that the company virtualizes traditional MCR/PCR hardware with a subscription‑based software platform, delivering multi‑year, recurring revenue (≈60% from modernization services, ≈55% from streaming unification, rest from monetization). Their platform powers major live events such as the Olympics, FIFA and the Super Bowl, handling low‑latency, high‑reliability video processing. AI is seen as a growth catalyst rather than a threat. Amagi has turned profitable in FY5.26, and historically has grown 25‑30% CAGR; the firm expects to sustain that pace through FY27, leveraging operating leverage and long‑term contracts

NSE IPO Highlights: Risks, Shareholder Structure, and Market Outlook

LICI SBIN GICHSGFIN BANKBARODA

The National Stock Exchange (NSE) has filed a 693‑page DRHP for a pure offer‑for‑sale IPO, with 14.9 crore shares being offered and SBI emerging as the largest selling shareholder (2.4 crore shares, 16%). Major investors include Canada Pension Fund, Bank of Baroda, and Singapore’s GIC; LIC is not participating.
