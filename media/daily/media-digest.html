Matt Orton’s Selective Outlook for Indian Markets: Why He’s Cautious on IT & Which Stocks Shine

ADANIPOWER RAYMOND ADANIPORTS ADANIGREEN

Matt Orton, chief market strategist at Raymond James, says the rupee’s depreciation and higher oil prices are weighing on foreign investor sentiment and earnings growth in India. Global Markets Rally Amid Rising Yields and Fed Uncertainty

BANKBARODA

In a CNBC TV 8 interview, Aditya Bhavai, Head of US Economics at BofA Global Research, explains that higher global bond yields are driven by a temporary energy shock, persistent inflation concerns, and widening deficits in developed markets. He notes that long‑term US yields are expected to hover around 4.25% through 2026, with upside risks linked to a possible upward revision of the neutral rate. Bhavai anticipates the Fed will remain passive until data forces action, given the mix of supply‑side shocks (Middle‑East conflict, labor supply, tariffs, AI productivity gains) and demand‑side stimulus (fiscal spending, AI investment). The incoming Fed chair, previously hawkish but now more dovish, may advocate rate cuts and balance‑sheet reductions, though the committee appears reluctant. His first speech is expected to move markets significantly as participants gauge the Fed’s stance amid ongoing geopolitical tensions

Ola Electric’s Home Inverter Setback Highlights Preference for LFP Batteries

OLAELEC ARE&M KABRAEXTRU

Ola Electric’s home inverter division, Ola Shakti, fell far short of its revenue target, generating only ₹4 crore versus the projected ₹100 crore for Q1 FY26. The shortfall is attributed to the company’s initial use of nickel‑manganese‑cobalt (NMC) batteries, which are costlier and less suited for stationary storage than lithium‑iron‑phosphate (LFP) chemistry. While the firm plans to switch to LFP in the next quarter to improve safety, lifespan, and cost (LFP being 10‑25% cheaper), the delayed transition slowed ramp‑up. Industry experts concur that LFP is the preferred chemistry for home energy storage in India due to its thermal stability, longer cycle life, and lower price, making it the mass‑market choice for stationary applications

IBM Shares Soar on US Funding for $2 B Quantum Push

DLINKINDIA DELTACORP S&SPOWER

The Trump administration will award $1 billion to IBM to build a quantum chip foundry, with IBM matching it by investing $1 billion in a new processor company, Anderon. The news lifted IBM’s shares 11% and spurred gains for other quantum firms—GlobalFoundries (+14%), D‑Wave (+29%), Rigetti (+28%) and Infleqtion (+44%). The awards are part of a broader $2 billion quantum‑computing package funded by the Commerce Department under the 2022 Chips and Science Act, which also grants the government minority equity stakes in the companies. Recipients include GlobalFoundries ($375 M), D‑Wave, Rigetti, Infleqtion, PsiQuantum, Atom Computing, Quantinuum and Diraq, each slated for up to $100 M (Diraq $38 M). The initiative aims to bolster U.S. leadership in quantum technology, with potential impacts on drug discovery, finance and national security
