Metals Including Silver, Gold, Palladium & Copper Off Highs Ahead Of Index Rebalancing For 2026

GODIGIT TDPOWERSYS AXISBANK

Metals such as silver, gold, palladium and copper are pulling back after a strong 2025 rally, with declines of 3‑6% driven by profit‑taking and the upcoming Bloomberg Commodity Index (BCI) rebalancing. The BCI adjusts annually in the second week of January (Jan 9‑15) to cap any single commodity at 15% of the index; currently gold sits at 20% and silver at 14%, so the rebalancing will force sales in these and other over‑weight metals (copper, platinum, palladium, aluminum).

Analysts expect $5‑7 bn of outflows from gold and silver futures, translating to roughly 2‑3 mn oz of gold and a 10‑15% price dip in silver, with a 3‑4% correction in gold. Bank estimates:

  • Citi: $6.8 bn gold outflows by Jan 16
  • SocGen: $5 bn each in gold and silver
  • TD Securities: 13% of COMEX silver open interest to be sold in 10 days
  • Deutsche Bank: 2.4 mn oz gold sell‑off (≈3‑4% price drop)
  • Goldman Sachs: $5.5 bn gold, $5 bn silver sell‑off Jan 9‑16

Saxo Bank argues the sell‑off is a buying opportunity because fundamentals remain intact.

Additional market drivers: rising net short positions on COMEX base metals, potential U.S. Section 232 tariff probe that could add silver to the critical‑minerals list (supporting prices later in January), and upcoming U.S. non‑farm payroll data. The next few days are therefore pivotal for commodities.
