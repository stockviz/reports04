Meta Launches Free Five‑Week Trades Training with Guaranteed Data‑Centre Jobs

SRM TANLA

Meta’s $115 million “America’s Workforce Academy” partners with CBRE and the Associated Builders and Contractors to offer free five‑week training in electricians, HVAC, welding, plumbing and fibre‑optic work in Louisiana, Ohio, Indiana and Texas. Luxury Carmaker JLR Boosts Tata Group’s IT Arm Amid Sector Slowdown

TATATECH PENINLAND TCS TATAELXSI

Tata’s three technology firms—Tata Technologies, Tata Elxsi and TCS—are increasing reliance on Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) as a key client to offset a broader slowdown in IT spending. Tata Technologies plans to raise JLR‑related revenue to about ₹1,750 cr, roughly a third of its FY‑26 earnings, while Tata Elxsi and TCS also target roughly ₹6,000 cr combined from the carmaker. The push follows JLR’s recent cyber‑attack and a sharp dip in its own earnings, prompting the IT firms to deepen ties through backend, cybersecurity, cloud and infotainment services. Analysts note the benefit of related‑party business in a weak market but warn of concentration risk if JLR’s fortunes falter. Despite overall revenue pressure—TCS posted its first decline since 2004 and Tata Elxsi saw its slowest growth in five years—the expanded JLR contracts provide a crucial runway for the Tata tech units

Mehli Mistry accuses Tata Trust trustees of taking improper commissions and seeks their return

PENINLAND BCG TVSMOTOR NSIL GRINDWELL TVSHLTD

OpenAI Files Confidential IPO Amid Rival Anthropic’s Move, But Says Going Public May Take Time

STANLEY

OpenAI submitted a confidential S‑1 filing, marking its first formal step toward a public listing that could exceed a $1 trillion valuation, shortly after competitor Anthropic did the same. The filing, reviewed privately by regulators, typically precedes a listing by 6‑9 months, but OpenAI says the timing is undecided and may be delayed to handle private‑company tasks, though it retains the option for an earlier debut. Valued at $852 billion, OpenAI plans a tender offer for employee share liquidity and is reportedly discussing a potential government stake with the Trump administration. CEO Sam Altman downplays an IPO race, emphasizing focus on technology and business growth. The filing follows recent developments, including a legal dispute with Elon Musk, a shift toward enterprise and coding products, and continued massive cash burn despite 900 million weekly ChatGPT users. Both OpenAI and Anthropic aim for trillion‑plus market caps, positioning a potential IPO as one of the most consequential tech listings

Pentagon Expands “Chinese Military Companies” Blacklist to Include Top Tech and EV Firms

The U.S. Department of Defense updated its 1260H list, adding Alibaba, Baidu, and BYD—alongside Tencent and other firms—citing links to China’s Ministry of Industry and Information Technology and alleged support for the nation’s defense industrial base under its “military‑civil fusion” policy. While the designation does not impose immediate sanctions, it bars listed companies from U.S. defense contracts, federal research funding, and triggers heightened regulatory and investor scrutiny, signaling potential future trade or investment restrictions. The move, announced shortly after a Trump‑Xi summit, reflects ongoing U.S. concerns over Chinese involvement in advanced technologies such as AI and electric vehicles, and has prompted denials from the companies and criticism from Beijing
