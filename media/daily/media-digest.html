DoT Keeps Backhaul Spectrum Charges Unchanged, Defying TRAI’s Cut Proposal

JIOFIN IDEA AIRTELPP

The Department of Telecommunications (DoT) has drafted rules to retain backhaul spectrum usage charges at current levels (0.15% of Adjusted Gross Revenue for the first carrier, up to 3.95% for the 11th carrier), contrary to the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India’s (TRAI) recommendation to slash fees by up to 55% and move to a flat 0.1% per carrier. Backhaul spectrum, which links towers to core networks, does not generate revenue, and TRAI argued the existing tiered pricing inflates costs. Operators such as Airtel, Vodafone Idea and Reliance Jio paid ₹4,068 crore in SUC in FY25, 75% of which was for backhaul; TRAI’s proposal could have saved them about ₹1,545 crore, boosting EBITDA by under 1% for Jio and Airtel and about 1.5% for Vodafone Idea. The draft rules invite stakeholder feedback and will take effect within 30 days, while also leaving commercial satellite internet services out of the non‑auction spectrum framework, delaying roll‑outs by OneWeb, Starlink, Jio Satellite and Amazon Leo
