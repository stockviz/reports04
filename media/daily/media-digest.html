Impact of LNG Supply Disruption on Indian Fertiliser Sector – Outlook and Stock Picks

COROMANDEL GNFC RCF CHAMBLFERT

The LNG shutdown in Qatar has raised concerns about feedstock costs for Indian urea producers, but ample urea and phosphatic fertilizer inventories (4.1 Mt closing stock) and overseas JV supply mitigate immediate shortages. Seasonal demand is likely to be met as farmers pre‑buy, with only minor logistical hiccups expected. IT Stocks Slide in February as Leverage Surges

INFY MINDTREE HCL-INSYS TECHM PERSISTENT

February was the worst month for Indian IT equities since September 2008, with the Nifty IT index falling almost 20% and every constituent posting double‑digit losses. Stocks such as Koforge, LTI, Mindtree, Tech Mahindra, Persistent, and Infosys each dropped over 20%. At the same time, leveraged positions on these names jumped sharply according to NSC MTF data—TCAS rose from ~₹850 cr on Feb 1 to ~₹1,200 cr by Mar 4, Infosys from ₹232 cr to ₹900 cr, and similar spikes for HCL Tech, Persistent, and others. Foreign Institutional Investors sold roughly ₹17,000 cr of IT shares in February, pushing net FII holdings in the sector to a four‑year low of just over ₹4  lakh cr, down 22% from January. Consequently, while IT stocks fell, leveraged exposure to them expanded dramatically, making TCAS one of the most leveraged MTF stocks by early March
