Indian firms seek US tariff refunds amid pending legal challenge

CEATLTD 3MINDIA BALKRISIND GOKEX

Indian exporters, including tyre makers Ceat and Balkrishna Industries, tractor producer CNH India, and apparel exporter Gokaldas Exports, are applying for refunds of the inflated US tariffs imposed under the Trump administration after the US Supreme Court declared them illegal. The US Customs and Border Protection launched an online portal in April 2026 to process refunds for duties paid between April 2025 and February 2026, when tariffs rose from around 3‑15% to 50% before being reduced to a flat 10% post‑ruling. Companies face uncertainty over eligibility, timelines, and whether refunds will be passed on to customers or suppliers. The Trump administration has announced an appeal against the refund order, adding further ambiguity. Legal experts say the right to refunds is clear, but practical delays and the outcome of the appeal could affect the speed and mechanics of reimbursements

Infosys Workforce Ages as Young Talent Share Hits 15‑Year Low

TCS INFY

Infosys’ employee base is now half over 30, the highest proportion in 15 years, with only 50.7% under 30 in FY26—a decline from 53% the prior year. The drop is offset by growth in the 31‑50 age group, now 45.7% of staff. Industry analysts cite competition from global capability centres offering better pay and a shift by IT firms toward hiring fewer freshers with specialized skills, especially in AI, data science, and cybersecurity. Similar ageing trends are observed at TCS. Despite Infosys’ claim of hiring over 20,000 freshers in FY26, the proportion of young employees continues to fall, reflecting broader challenges in attracting new talent to Indian IT services

Berkshire Hathaway’s First Post‑Buffett Deal: $8.5 Billion Acquisition of Taylor Morrison

Berkshire Hathaway, under new CEO Greg Abel, agreed to buy homebuilder Taylor Morrison for $72.50 per share—an all‑cash $8.5 billion deal (≈2 % of its $397 billion cash reserve). The acquisition expands Berkshire’s residential construction footprint beyond its Clayton Homes unit, adds site‑built homebuilding across 21 markets in 12 states, and retains Taylor Morrison’s management. The transaction, valued at $6.8 billion in equity and $8.5 billion enterprise, is slated to close in H2 2026 pending regulatory and shareholder approval. Berkshire’s shares slipped after the announcement, while Taylor Morrison’s stock modestly rose

Reliance’s Hiring Slows Sharply as AI and Upskilling Take Center Stage

TANLA RELIANCE

Reliance Industries trimmed its new hires by roughly 90,000 in FY‑26, dropping from over 1.9 lakh in FY‑25 to just “1 lakh+”. The decline reflects a broader shift toward AI‑driven upskilling, micro‑entrepreneur models, and leaner organizational structures, with reductions especially in Jio Platforms, media‑entertainment, and oil‑to‑chemicals units, while retail hiring rose 17 %. Overall headcount still grew to 4.19 lakh, aided by lower attrition. Experts say AI adoption, global uncertainties, and a move away from middle‑layer staffing are prompting conglomerates to hire fewer fresh talent and repurpose existing roles
