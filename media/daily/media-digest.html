Nifty At 27,000–28,000? Rahul’s Cautious Outlook & Why FPIs Might Still Stay Away | India-US Deal

TITAN RELAXO BATAINDIA INDIGO MCDOWELL-N ITC SBIN M&M

Title: Rahul’s Cautious Outlook: Trade Deal Boosts FDI, Not FPI, and Markets Likely to Consolidate Around 27‑28k

Summary:
Rahul, CEO of Ashikar Institutional Equities, says the India‑US trade deal is more likely to attract foreign direct investment than foreign portfolio investment, as FPIs remain wary of currency risk and modest arbitrage opportunities versus US Treasury yields. He expects earnings growth of 12‑13% next year, but with a modest rupee depreciation the upside for FPIs is limited. Market valuations have reset, and a consolidation phase is probable, with a realistic Nifty ceiling around 27,000‑28,000 for the year, translating to roughly 10% annual returns.

Key sector views:
- Consumer discretionary (liquor, hotels, aviation) shows strength.
- PSU banks (e.g., SBI) appear undervalued relative to private banks.
- Defense and auto stocks offer value after recent corrections.
- Export‑linked sectors such as jewelry and leather have potential but need careful selection.

Overall, Rahul remains cautiously optimistic, emphasizing that future market moves will depend more on earnings, monsoon outlook, and macro‑asset‑class competition than on further trade‑deal optimism.

India‑US Interim Trade Deal: Progress, H‑1B Outlook & Next Steps

Minister Piyush Goyal highlighted that the Section 232 probe on pharma is ongoing, with assurances of zero‑duty tariffs for generic medicines and ingredients. He said the next phase involves finalising legal paperwork and further negotiations to ease tariffs and boost bilateral trade. Regarding H‑1B restrictions, Goyal noted Indian firms aren’t worried, expecting continued access and even potential benefits as fringe applications drop. He emphasized the rise of Global Capability Centers (≈1,800 in India) which keep talent and earnings domestic. No concessions for US e‑commerce inventory storage were discussed. On non‑tariff barriers, both sides aim to address issues like GPU and technology import restrictions as part of a broader trade agreement. Goyal declined to set a deadline for the full Bilateral Trade Agreement but affirmed strong political and people‑to‑people ties and a desire to conclude it swiftly. He praised US Ambassador Sergio Gore for his helpful role in the negotiations, while keeping detailed behind‑the‑scenes discussions confidential.
