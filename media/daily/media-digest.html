Airbnb’s Long‑Term Bet on India Begins to Pay Off

THOMASCOOK

Airbnb’s focus on domestic travelers is turning India into a key growth market. After years of building supply, listings have risen from ~15,000 pre‑2017 to ~115,000, and occupancy for hosts like Rooma Sinha now averages 80 % with average nightly rates of ₹3,000. Domestic bookings now make up about 80 % of Indian reservations, a structural shift from the pre‑COVID era when most Indian users booked overseas stays. The company’s extensive product updates, co‑host program, and trust‑and‑safety features have spurred host confidence, while a new generation of Indian travelers seeks experience‑focused, affordable stays. Growth is driven by “revenge travel,” a shortage of hotel rooms, and rising domestic tourism (4.13 billion trips in 2025). However, challenges remain: regulatory pushback from housing societies, lower international tourist inflow, and a supply gap compared with mature markets like France. Despite these hurdles, experts see India’s young, mobile population as a decade‑long growth engine for Airbnb

Soju’s Surge in India Driven by K‑Culture and Gen Z

GLOBUSSPR BANG

Indian demand for Korean culture has turned soju into a coveted, aspirational drink among Gen Z and young millennials. Brands are responding: Nuvola Spirits launched Seoulmate Soju (premium rice‑distillate) across Karnataka, Goa, Delhi and plans for more states; Monika Alcobev partnered with Jinro to introduce Chamisul Fresh and flavored variants (green grape, plum, strawberry, peach) in major metros; Penguin Overseas introduced locally‑produced So Good Soju (peach, green apple, yuzu lime, K‑Bomb) with further flavors slated. Consumers favor lower‑ABV, flavorful, experience‑driven beverages that pair with pan‑Asian cuisine, creating a niche beyond India’s whisky‑centric market. Success hinges on tailored products, state‑specific regulatory navigation, and a dual‑channel push through retail wine stores, modern trade, and on‑trade venues (restaurants, bars). Early-stage growth mirrors Southeast Asian markets, but localized strategies are essential for scaling nationwide

Nalco’s Alumina Exports to West Asia Hit by Geopolitical Tensions

NATIONALUM

National Aluminium Company (Nalco) says the West Asian market, accounting for 40‑50% of its alumina shipments, has been disrupted by regional tensions, leading to reduced smelter capacity and lower spot prices (now $305‑310/tonne). The company is shifting sales to markets like Indonesia, but overall revenues fell to ₹5,012.82 crore and net profit dropped 16.6% to ₹1,722.44 crore in Q4 FY26, reflecting lower export volumes (13.08 lakh tonnes out of 23 lakh tonnes produced) and higher expenses. The broader aluminium value chain faces divergent impacts: oversupplied alumina markets see price declines, while refined aluminium prices rise due to production shutdowns and shipping disruptions

NTPC readies first nuclear feasibility study for DAE approval
