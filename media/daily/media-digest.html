India Fixed Income Summit – Key Takeaways on Municipal Bonds, Infrastructure Funding and Market Evolution

TV18BRDCST HDFCAMC

The summit underscored that India’s infrastructure ambitions now outstrip traditional bank financing, making a deep, diversified bond market essential. Panelists highlighted the rapid rise of municipal bonds—1,700 issuances last year and over 5,000 this year—and stressed that robust governance, trust and clear repayment mechanisms are vital for scaling them. Institutional investors, especially insurance and pension funds, hold about 30 % of savings and could channel 4–5 lakh crore annually into corporate bonds if market gaps are bridged.

NBFCs are turning to bonds for liability diversification, longer tenors and cheaper funding, learning from past liquidity squeezes. REITs were praised for filling the equity‑debt gap, delivering moderate risk‑adjusted returns and attracting both debt and equity capital. Retail participation remains limited due to rating concerns and information asymmetry, but improved awareness and credit‑enhancement tools could boost demand.

Fixed‑income perception is shifting: investors are being educated about tax sensitivity, duration management, and the role of alternatives such as listed investment trusts and private credit, which blend equity‑like growth with debt‑like stability. Household savings are gradually moving from fixed deposits to bond‑linked mutual funds, driven by higher post‑tax yields.

Overall, speakers called for coordinated execution of reforms—strengthening municipal financing, unlocking institutional capital, expanding retail access, and leveraging digital tools—to build a resilient bond market capable of financing India’s $30 trillion economy target by 2047
