Monsoon 2026 Outlook: El Niño Influence and Regional Impacts

Founder Jatin Singh of Skaimit discussed the 2026 monsoon forecast, noting a 6% below‑normal rainfall expectation with a 70% probability of below‑normal or normal conditions. He linked the outlook to El Niño events, explaining that while El Niño generally weakens the southwest monsoon winds, its strength does not directly dictate drought severity. Historical data shows a 60% chance of drought and 80% chance of below‑normal rains during El Niño years. Singh projected that the monsoon onset may be normal, but June could be weak, and July–August—the critical months contributing ~31% and ~19% of total rainfall—are likely to face persistent deficits. Regionally, north‑west India (Uttar Pradesh, Haryana, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka) appears most vulnerable, while Rajasthan may fare better. He also highlighted broader Asian effects: weakened South‑west monsoon flow may shift precipitation toward the South China Sea, increasing typhoon activity in China, Japan, Taiwan, and the Philippines, while South Asia could see reduced rainfall. Monsoon onset and withdrawal dates remain variable, with deviations common across years
