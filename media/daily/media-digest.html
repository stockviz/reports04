NCLAT Rejects Vedanta’s Challenge to Adani’s Jaiprakash Resolution Plan

ADANIENPP1 VEDL JPASSOCIAT

New Mexico Seeks Sweeping Child Safety Restrictions on Meta in Trial’s Second Phase

State prosecutors in New Mexico are asking a judge to order Meta to overhaul its platforms—Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp—to protect children. In the second phase of a landmark nuisance trial, they demand changes such as redesigning recommendation algorithms, banning infinite scroll and push notifications, strengthening age‑verification, default privacy settings to curb sexual exploitation, and requiring parent‑linked child accounts with court‑supervised safety monitoring. This follows a first‑phase jury verdict that imposed $375 million in penalties, finding Meta knowingly harmed children’s mental health and concealed sexual abuse. Meta plans to appeal, arguing the mandates are impractical, violate free speech and parental rights, and would force it to exit the New Mexico market. The case is the first state‑level trial among over 40 lawsuits targeting Meta’s impact on youth mental health
