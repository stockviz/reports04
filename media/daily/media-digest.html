W Health Ventures Aims for Bold, Ground‑Up Healthcare Bets with Fund II

NH MEDANTA

Early‑stage VC firm W Health Ventures has closed its Fund II at ₹550 crore (target ₹630 crore) and is focusing on building companies from scratch in oncology, longevity, chronic pain and AI‑enabled B2B health services. Its first portfolio company, Everhope Oncology—partnered with Narayana Health—uses AI to improve cancer care and plans to serve 30,000 patients through new centers in Maharashtra, Gujarat and Delhi. The firm targets 8‑10 new ventures over four years, investing ₹30‑50 crore each, and aims to scale from 25 million patients served by Fund I to a billion lives in the next two decades. It emphasizes a ground‑up approach, leveraging global insights to create single‑specialty health firms and cross‑border AI health solutions

Fundamentum Shifts Focus to Fintech in Third Fund

3MINDIA

Infosys co‑founder Nandan Nilekani’s VC firm Fundamentum is gearing up to increase its fintech exposure in its upcoming third fund, leveraging India’s robust digital public infrastructure and large financial‑inclusion gap. Fintech lead Mayank Kachhwaha says the firm targets “India‑first” tech‑driven companies across MSME credit, consumer credit, wealthtech, and banking infrastructure, typically investing $10‑15 million post‑product‑market fit and following on with larger checks. Recent fintech bets include Olyv, Flexiloans, Stable Money and TransBnk, bringing fintech to nearly half of the 11 deals made from Fund II’s $227 million corpus. With a $30 trillion credit gap for MSMEs and rapid growth in SIP and fixed‑deposit investments, Fundamentum sees ample opportunity for credit and wealth‑tech startups, while also eyeing insurance‑tech as the underlying infrastructure matures. The firm evaluates 5‑10 startups weekly and builds long‑term relationships before committing capital
