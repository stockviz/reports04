Delhi EV Policy Implications for Auto Makers and Stock Outlook

M&M AETHER BAJAJ-AUTO LANDSMILL JBMA

The Delhi government’s EV scheme, pending LG approval, runs until 31 March 2038 with a four‑year outlay of ₹7,000 crore for direct consumer benefits and ₹8,000 crore for charging infrastructure. There is no cap on the number of vehicles eligible for subsidies, but benefits focus on commercial and two‑/three‑wheeler segments—electric three‑wheelers must be registered from 2027, electric two‑wheelers from 2028, and at least 30 % of school buses must be electric. Consequently, firms with strong EV two‑/three‑wheeler portfolios such as Bajaj Auto, Mahindra & Mahindra, JBM Auto, Electra, and GreenTech are poised to gain, while traditional ICE car makers see limited direct advantage. The policy also includes upgrades and subventions for old trucks and buses, further boosting commercial EV demand. Overall, the scheme favours EV manufacturers—especially in the two‑wheeler space—and is likely to drive stock outperformance for the identified winners while putting pressure on ICE‑dominant players

EPC Stocks Face a Tough Year: Six Factors Behind the Decline

DBL

EPC companies have shifted from high‑growth favorites to underperformers over the past year, posting negative returns across the board. Key reasons include: (1) execution delays despite strong order books, leading to lower earnings conversion; (2) land‑acquisition and clearance hurdles slowing project timelines and transmission targets; (3) cash‑flow strain from delayed government payments and rising working capital; (4) budget cuts affecting water‑infrastructure EPCs; (5) valuation de‑rating as expectations from the infrastructure boom faded and earnings misses prompted sharp price corrections; and (6) a broader market de‑rating of infra‑related stocks. Looking ahead, urbanisation and the energy transition could revive demand and order inflows from FY27, but investors should temper expectations, as EPC firms typically trade at modest 15‑16× earnings multiples
