India’s Energy Outlook: LNG, Peak Oil, Coal Wild Card

ATHERENERG

The interview with Per Magnus of Rystad Energy highlights that global LNG supply will be dominated by the U.S. while demand surges in Asia, potentially doubling over 25‑30 years. Short‑term U.S. gas prices are high due to winter storms, but long‑term LNG is expected to settle around $10/MMBtu, reflecting new project costs. The energy transition will move from “molecules” (fossil fuels) to “electrons” (electricity), with hydrogen playing a later supporting role. Oil demand is projected to peak within the next decade and flatten, leading to possible $80‑barrel prices ten years out, though debate remains on demand levels. Security dominates market strategy, with affordability lagging. India is diversifying its crude imports (now from 47 countries) and may rely more on U.S. oil, but sanctions on Russia and Iran keep Middle‑East supplies significant. Coal remains a wild‑card for India’s net‑zero 2070 goal, while renewable costs have fallen dramatically yet remain volatile due to mineral price swings. Storage challenges and the need for flexible power solutions are identified as critical for the coming years
