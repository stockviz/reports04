India‑UK Free Trade Agreement Opens Duty‑Free EV Export Quotas for Indian Makers

MARUTI TMPV M&M

The India‑UK CETA, effective July 15, grants Indian passenger‑vehicle manufacturers (Maruti Suzuki, Mahindra & Mahindra, Tata Motors) duty‑free access to the UK electric, hybrid and hydrogen car market from the sixth year of the pact. Quotas are tiered by price: under £20,000, £20‑40 k and £40‑80 k, with 6,800 units for each of the first two tiers and 4,000 for the top tier in year 6, rising to a peak of 88,000 units (34,000, 34,000, 20,000) from year 15 onward. Cars above £80 k remain excluded. Executives see the agreement as an opportunity to expand EV exports, complementing existing sales (e.g., Maruti’s eVITARA) and supporting “Make in India for the world.” The FTA aims to double bilateral trade to $100 bn by 2030
