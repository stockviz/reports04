What Quick Commerce Isn’t Telling You | Expert Breaks Down The Major Market Shift 2026-01-14 10:13

SGMART

The speaker explains that while D‑Mart can eventually scale, its growth will be slow, and competition comes from both platform players and local “quick‑commerce” services that promise sub‑hour deliveries. Traditional “started‑levy” grocery models (e.g., Amazon Fresh, BigBasket) never gained traction, whereas quick commerce (Q‑Com) – initially focused on food & groceries – has expanded to include small white goods, accessories, apparel, beauty, stationery, toys, etc. The core competitive edge remains ultra‑fast delivery (10‑20 minutes); extending to 30‑60 minutes puts Q‑Com firms on par with larger retailers like Amazon, Flipkart, and Myntra. Regulations do not restrict ultra‑fast delivery, so maintaining sub‑30‑minute times is crucial. Ultimately, success hinges on improving customer experience while achieving profitability and avoiding cash burn; firms that preserve speed will outpace complacent rivals.
