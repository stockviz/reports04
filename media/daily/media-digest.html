CDMO Industry Outlook: Strategic Shifts, Cyclical Revenue, and Long‑Term Growth Drivers

ONEPOINT ALIVUS

The CDMO market’s performance is tied to the mix of commercial and clinical‑phase molecules, making quarterly results volatile and emphasizing the need to assess annual figures. While inventory de‑stocking creates short‑term headwinds, the core issue is product concentration; firms with broader, diversified pipelines can smooth out swings. Indian CDMOs are transitioning from tactical, single‑molecule suppliers to strategic partners offering a wide technology and capacity portfolio, a shift that requires sustained investment and multi‑year growth cycles. Long‑term drivers—geopolitical diversification, competition from China, and the push for resilient supply chains—remain robust, rendering the U.S. Biosecure Act largely irrelevant now. RFP volume alone is a poor predictor of future business; genuine strategic relationships drive meaningful contracts. Overall, the industry faces short‑term volatility but is poised for steady expansion over the next five to ten years as companies build depth, diversify customers, and cement strategic partnerships
