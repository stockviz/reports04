HD Hyundai to Launch ₹40,000‑Crore Greenfield Shipyard in Thoothukudi

COCHINSHIP HYUNDAI ABAN TNPL

HD Korea Shipbuilding & Offshore Engineering (HD KSOE), a unit of South Korea’s HD Hyundai Group, is set to begin groundwork next month on India’s first large‑scale greenfield shipyard at the Thoothukudi shipbuilding cluster, following a forthcoming definitive investment agreement with the Indian central and Tamil Nadu governments. The ₹40,000 crore (≈$4.8 billion) project will be built in phases, targeting an annual capacity of 3.5 million GT and constructing vessels such as VLCCs and VLGCs. While HD KSOE will largely operate through a wholly‑owned Indian subsidiary, SIPCOT and the Centre’s Maritime Development Fund may join as minority equity partners for land and funding. The development aligns with India’s Maritime Amrit Kaal Vision 2047, which seeks to lift the nation into the top‑five global shipbuilding nations by 2047, and is part of a broader push to attract ₹75,000 crore of investment across four shipbuilding clusters, supported by the Maritime Development Fund and revamped Shipbuilding Financial Assistance scheme. The shipyard is expected to catalyze a multiplier effect, spurring logistics, housing, and urban infrastructure growth in the coastal region
