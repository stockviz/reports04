TV18BRDCST

The guests stress faith in India’s growth story, disciplined investing, and avoiding panic or greed

Impact of the Strait of Hormuz Disruption on Global Trade and Logistics

In a CNBC TV18 segment, Anisha Gupta interviews Soham Choksi, co‑founder and CEO of Shipsey, an AI‑driven logistics platform, about the fallout from the West Asia conflict centered on the Strait of Hormuz. The strait, handling over 20 million barrels of oil and a similar share of LNG daily—about 80 % destined for Asia—has seen vessel traffic plunge by roughly 85 % in two weeks, leaving around 700 ships stranded. This bottleneck cuts India’s crude imports (≈2.6 million bpd) and hampers exports such as Basmati rice, driving up commodity prices (e.g., polypropylene up 70 % since January). Freight costs have surged: air cargo rates are three times higher, ocean reroutes via the Cape of Good Hope add 20‑25 days, container surcharges reach $1,500‑$2,000, and war‑risk insurance premiums have tripled. Companies are scrambling to diversify supply sources (shifting from the Middle East to Russia), build inventory buffers, and adopt multi‑modal logistics, while Shipsey’s AI tools provide early‑warning signals and dynamic rerouting. If the blockage persists beyond a few weeks, higher logistics costs will likely filter down to consumers, prompting longer‑term shifts in sourcing, port usage (e.g., Salalah), and contractual arrangements for alternative transport modes
