STT Hikes Stump D-St | No ‘Offset’ From Capital Gains Tax & STT Hike Soured The Mood: Samir Arora 2026-02-02 09:01

M&M 5PAISA

Samir Arora, founder and fund manager at Helios Capital, joined the post‑budget discussion to explain why the recent rise in securities transaction tax (STT) is hurting market sentiment and liquidity. He argues that the equity market should be treated as an independent sector, not merely a by‑product of sector‑specific incentives, and that the government needs to acknowledge and offset the added cost—by cutting capital‑gains tax, reducing cash‑transaction STT, or offering other relief—especially when the market is already underperforming and investors are losing in foreign‑currency terms.

Arora stresses that high STT and other taxes increase the equity risk premium, discouraging foreign institutional investors (FIIs) and making Indian equities less attractive compared to global markets. He calls for verbal support and, if needed, concrete policy actions (e.g., temporary loan facilities, duty reductions) to sustain liquidity and confidence. While he remains long‑only and has continued buying selectively, he cautions that without supportive measures the market may stay weak, and he expects FIIs to stay cautious or sell.

In short, Arora wants the budget to pair the STT hike with compensatory tax relief or other incentives, to protect market liquidity, reduce the equity risk premium, and signal that the Indian stock market is a priority for economic growth.

RECLTD IDBI PAYTM

Asian markets: Mixed – Japan up, Korea, Taiwan and Shanghai down; overall red‑side bias.

India – pre‑market outlook: Nifty up ~25 pts after the budget‑day sell‑off; modest K‑PEX growth (9 %); defence capex up 21 % (to ₹5.9 lakh cr) and modernisation up 24 %; capex budget raised 9 % to ₹12.2 lakh cr. The Finance Minister projects 10 % nominal GDP growth next fiscal, acknowledging a 3 % shortfall on the prior‑year target.

Key budget measures

  • Securities Transaction Tax (STT) hike on equity futures & options – aimed at curbing speculation, not revenue‑raising; expected to fetch ~₹15,500 cr if volumes hold.
  • No changes to personal income‑tax slabs; compliance‑ease measures (longer return‑revision window, reduced litigation).
  • 20‑year tax holiday for data‑centre investments to lure global cloud providers.
  • Strategic dis‑investment of IDBI Bank moved to Stage 3 (technical & financial bids invited); broader public‑sector stake‑reduction to 26 % under discussion.
  • Defence, roads, railways, power, finance (PSE/REC restructuring), pharma, bio‑pharma, and UPI incentives (₹2 kcr for FI‑27) flagged as priority sectors.
  • Positive commentary from Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal that market panic is “momentary” and that US‑India trade talks are progressing.

Market reaction: Sensex and Nifty recorded their biggest budget‑day fall in six years; Nifty Bank down ~1,500 pts, PSU banks lagging. FIIs were absent (holiday) and sold modestly; domestic institutions (DIIs) net‑sold ≈₹700 cr. Futures activity showed heavy short‑selling (≈₹1,600 cr) and an aggressive call‑writing bias (≈6 calls per put). Put‑call ratio fell to 0.59 ahead of tomorrow’s expiry. Key technical support: ~24,450 – 25,100; breach of 24,572 could open a slide to 24,337 (Nifty) or 58,000 (Nifty Bank).

Brokerage sector outlook

  • IT: Favoured – buy‑backs now taxed as capital gains (12.5‑20 % for non‑promoters, 22‑30 % for promoters).
  • Defence, roads, railways: Positive – budget outlays up ~14‑18 %.
  • Power & financials: Anticipated benefit from PSE/REC restructuring.
  • Pharma/Bio‑pharma: Boost from dedicated scheme (≈₹10,000 cr).
  • Payments (PhonePe, Paytm): UPI incentive uplift (₹2,000 cr FY‑27 vs. ₹440 cr FY‑26).

Commodities: Gold and silver highly volatile; both down 3‑5 % (gold ≈ $4,730/oz, silver ≈ $83/oz) with some buying at lower levels. Base metals (copper, nickel, tin, zinc) down 4‑12 % from all‑time highs; crude oil down ~2 % after easing Iran‑related tension.

Take‑away: The budget mixes growth‑driving outlays with a speculative‑deterrent STT hike, triggering a sharp but potentially short‑lived market sell‑off. Watch for FII re‑entry, support levels around 24,450‑25,100, and sector‑specific rallies in IT, defence, infrastructure, power, pharma and payments as the market digests the reforms.
