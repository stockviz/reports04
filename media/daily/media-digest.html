Rahul Chaddha on IT Sector Valuations, AI Impact, and Better Investment Opportunities

Rahul Chaddha, CIO of Chikara Investment Management, explains that while IT services face valuation pressure and AI-driven disruption, the sector’s multiples may fall to 10‑15 × earnings, reflecting modest 3‑4 % growth and limited upside. He sees a “stay‑at‑home vs. reopening” analogy with AI infrastructure winners (foundries, chip makers) versus SAS and consumer‑internet losers. Chaddha remains underweight IT, preferring growth elsewhere—financials, manufacturing exports, and consumer‑internet platforms in China and ASEAN. He highlights improving macro conditions in India: proactive policy reforms, GST and labor changes, rising credit growth, and industrial opportunities driven by PLI and defense‑aerospace demand. He expects a shift from AI‑centric stocks to firms that can leverage AI for consumer engagement, offering better entry points in the coming months. Regarding the potential US‑Russia dollar trade, he notes a weakening dollar due to US fiscal deficits and policy uncertainty, which could affect gold and broader sentiment. Overall, Chaddha advocates a diversified portfolio with limited IT exposure, focusing on sectors with stronger growth prospects

Aniruddh Sarkar on Indian IT Sell‑off, AI Risks, Valuation Reset & Outlook for FMCG

HINDUNILVR INDIANB

Aniruddh Sarkar, CIO of Equinova Investment, warns that Indian IT firms are lagging in AI adoption, leading to a sharp valuation correction. He notes that large head‑counts and shrinking project sizes erode pricing power, but mid‑ and small‑cap IT stocks are becoming attractive on a historical basis—though still not compelling on a PEG ratio. Sarkar suggests waiting for firms that can quickly embed AI into existing offerings before scaling positions. Turning to HUL, he stresses that volume growth is the critical metric; without clear recovery, he remains averse to FMCG stocks, preferring exposure through autos, new‑age businesses, and hotels where earnings growth is stronger. Overall, he sees the worst of the downside in IT behind us, expects a sideways correction, and is selective about sector bets based on real‑growth drivers

Hindalco Q3 Results: Impact of One‑Time Loss and Debt Rise

CANHLIFE CTE HINDALCO

Hindalco’s Q3 earnings showed a modest 0.7% revenue rise but a 56% profit drop to ₹2,000 cr, driven by a one‑time ₹2,600 cr loss from the Oswego plant fire. EBITDA fell ~10% despite a 6% margin improvement, and net debt surged by ₹18,000 cr, pushing the net‑debt‑to‑EBITDA ratio to 1.73. India operations remain resilient, offsetting global volatility, but higher working‑capital needs and pending insurance recoveries raise leverage concerns. Analysts are split: CTE downgraded to neutral citing leverage and cash‑flow worries, while HSBC remains a buy, cutting its target price but urging investors to look through the one‑time event
