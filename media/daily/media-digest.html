Amazon Worker Dies Unattended While Colleagues Keep Working

An Amazon employee at the PDX9 warehouse in Troutdale, Oregon collapsed and lay on the floor for over an hour on April 6, 2024, while coworkers continued to pick, tote, and load packages. Supervisors reportedly instructed staff to “turn around and not look” and keep working. Paramedics arrived later and the area was closed. Employees expressed anxiety, sleep issues, and concern over heat and ventilation in the facility, noting a history of injuries. Amazon confirmed the death, expressed condolences, and said grief counselors and support were provided, but the exact cause of death remains unknown. The incident has sparked debate over workplace safety in large warehouses

Chartered Speed Targets ₹1,900 crore Revenue on 3,000‑Bus Electric Order Book

Ahmedabad‑based Chartered Speed plans to more than double its revenue to ₹1,800‑₹1,900 crore within two years, backed by contracts for 3,000 electric buses under the PM e‑Bus Seva and PM E‑DRIVE schemes. The company has filed a DRHP to raise ₹855 crore (₹655 crore fresh issue, ₹200 crore promoter sell‑off) to fund electric‑bus procurement (~₹98 crore) and repay ₹396.4 crore debt. Its gross‑cost‑contract model secures fixed per‑km fees, delivering predictable revenue but requiring high upfront capital. Current contracts are expected to generate ₹1,100‑₹1,300 crore annual revenue, with ₹400‑₹500 crore from PM e‑Bus Seva and ₹700‑₹800 crore from PM E‑DRIVE. Having grown from a family trucking firm in 1993 to over 2,000 buses across multiple states, Chartered Speed aims for FY26 revenues of ~₹750 crore and EBITDA >₹200 crore, positioning itself as one of the few profitable private bus operators. The sector remains fragmented, with only 17,000 of 52,000 sanctioned buses tendered, leaving a large pipeline. In‑house operations and the government’s Payment Security Mechanism mitigate payment risk, though depot electrification delays and OEM supply backlogs pose execution challenges. The lack of formal industry recognition inflates capital costs, prompting calls for lending norms that reflect sector realities

Just Dial Q4 FY26 Results Show Revenue Growth Amid Profit Decline

JUSTDIAL

In Q4 FY26, Just Dial’s net profit fell 36% to ₹100 cr, while revenue rose 6.2% YoY to ₹307.2 cr. EBITDA improved 3.2% to ₹88.8 cr (28.9% margin) and PBT grew 7.3% to ₹76.1 cr. Unique visitors hit 182.4 million, active listings rose 12.1% to 54.7 million (41 million geocoded), and images and reviews increased modestly. Cash and investments stood at ₹5,852.2 cr. The firm highlighted AI-driven platform enhancements and plans to expand these capabilities in FY27

GRB Dairy Expands Beyond Ghee into Value‑Added Dairy

DODLA

After four decades of building a ₹1,000‑crore FMCG empire around ghee, Bengaluru‑based GRB Dairy Foods is diversifying into paneer and other dairy categories by year‑end, while still growing its snacks, sweets and spices portfolio. The move reflects the pressure on legacy single‑product brands to broaden their offerings without weakening brand recall, prompting GRB to launch new categories under entirely different brand names

JSW Group’s Auto Venture Gains Momentum as India‑China Ties Warm

ARROWGREEN JSWCEMENT M&M TATACAP BCG HYUNDAI

Improved India‑China relations and relaxed investment rules (Press Note 3) have eased technology transfer and supply‑chain links for JSW. The group, led by Parth Jindal, is preparing to launch its first car in Q4 2026 via two ventures: JSW MG Motor India (a JV with SAIC) and JSW Green Mobility (own brand). Partnerships with Chinese firms, notably SAIC and likely Chery, are being deepened, with a $300 million capex plan. The regulatory easing also supports plans for a 10 GWh lithium‑ion battery plant, with talks ongoing with Chinese battery specialists. These developments position JSW as the third Indian conglomerate entering the auto market, following Tata and Mahindra

Justin Sun Accuses Trump‑Backed Crypto Firm of Secret Freeze Tool

LIBERTSHOE

Investor Justin Sun alleges that World Liberty Financial, a Trump‑linked crypto venture, embedded a “backdoor blacklisting function” allowing it to unilaterally freeze token holders’ WLFI tokens, claiming he was the first victim when his holdings were frozen in September. World Liberty refuted the claim on X, saying it has the contracts and evidence and will take the matter to court. The article outlines Sun’s investment history, the firm’s risk disclosures that permit freezing for illicit activity, and notes that similar powers exist at other crypto issuers like Tether, while regulatory oversight remains unclear

D1 Capital Partners’ March Losses Linked to Poor Stock Picks

DELTACORP HGM 5PAISA NUVAMA GLOBAL AARON

D1 Capital Partners’ equities book dropped 6% in March, marking one of the worst monthly performances among stock‑pickers. All six of its biggest stock positions lost money, led by Flowserve Corp. (‑17%) and James Hardie Industries Plc (‑22%). Despite a 2.7% gain for the year and raising $2.7 billion for a new private‑equity‑focused fund with an extra $300 million for co‑investments, the firm’s stock‑selection missteps drove the decline amid broader market weakness and geopolitical tensions
