Eli Lilly’s One‑Time Gene‑Editing Therapy for Cholesterol

Dr. Rajneesh Kapoor explains that the new therapy targets the PCSK9 gene in the liver to permanently lower LDL cholesterol, potentially reducing levels by about 60 % after a single infusion. While early trial results are promising and the FDA has fast‑tracked the study, safety and long‑term durability remain under investigation. Risks include off‑target DNA edits that could cause other diseases or tumors, so extensive studies are needed. The treatment may be especially useful for familial hyperlipidaemia, severely obese patients, and high‑risk individuals, possibly requiring re‑infusion every 5–10 years. Costs are expected to start high (≈ $50 k) but may decrease with wider use. Kapoor favors the gene‑editing approach over daily drugs for its compliance benefits, though lifestyle changes remain essential
