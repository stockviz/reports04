Vedanta’s Four‑Way Demerger Hits the Market

HINDALCO VEDL

Four de‑merged entities—Aluminum, Steel & Iron, Power, and Oil & Gas—are listed today, bringing the Vedanta group to five listed companies. UK Banks See Surge in APP Fraud After New Refund Rules Take Effect

UK banks reported a 19% rise in authorized push payment (APP) fraud losses to £576.4 million in 2024, the steepest increase since the pandemic. The spike coincides with October 2024 rules requiring banks and payment firms to reimburse victims up to £85,000, making the UK the only country with such a mandate. Banks refunded £354.3 million, but losses not covered by the scheme also rose. Officials warn fraudsters are using sophisticated social engineering aided by AI, often via tech and telecom platforms, and call for stronger responsibilities on these sectors. Fintech lobbyists urge technology firms, especially Meta, to help cover reimbursements and tighten seller verification, after internal documents revealed significant ad revenue from scam‑related content. Investment, purchase and romance scams all hit record levels, prompting an independent review of the rules by Frontier Economics

US‑Iran Peace Deal Sparks Macro Relief for India and Emerging Markets

Ed Yardini and Arvind Sanga discuss how the anticipated US‑Iran peace agreement will lift oil‑price pressures, benefiting oil‑importing economies like India, Japan, South Korea and Taiwan. They note that while the deal restores pre‑war conditions, India’s underperformance and structural challenges mean any rally will be modest. Emerging markets will gain from resumed oil exports, but U.S. valuation excesses and the hype around AI and SpaceX suggest investors may find better risk‑adjusted returns in cheaper markets such as Europe and India, provided foreign inflows stabilize
