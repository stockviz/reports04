U.S. Lawmakers Push Aggressive Reforms to H‑1B Visa Program

TCS KISSHT

Since early 2024, a wave of Republican-sponsored bills aims to severely curtail, pause, or end the H‑1B visa program, which allows foreign specialists to work in the U.S. Proposals include cutting the maximum stay from six to two years, requiring proof of intent to return home, limiting visas to high‑salary roles, raising wage requirements, restricting use by large IT outsourcers, and even suspending the program entirely. Notable bills are Chip Roy’s “American White‑Collar Worker Jobs Act” (2026), Marjorie Taylor Greene’s “End H‑1B Now Act,” Greg Steube’s EXILE Act, and Eli Crane’s “End H‑1B Visa Abuse Act.” The measures would also give U.S. workers a tort cause of action against employers who displace them with non‑immigrants. Indian IT firms, which rely heavily on H‑1B talent, claim they are increasing domestic hiring and reducing visa dependence. Simultaneously, the Biden administration has tightened green‑card rules, further heightening uncertainty for H‑1B holders, most of whom are Indian nationals. Critics warn the proposals could harm the U.S. economy and face resistance from industry and bipartisan lawmakers

Ukraine’s Long‑Range Drone Strikes Hit Putin’s Hometown, Targeting Naval Base and Energy Infrastructure

Ukrainian drones flew over 1,000 km to strike St. Petersburg’s Kronstadt Naval Base, oil depots and terminals, marking a new “logistical lockdown” strategy that brings the war directly to Russia’s elite cities. The Kremlin’s tightened information controls and residents’ growing resentment underscore a shift toward a more volatile phase of the conflict, as both sides trade accusations over nuclear safety while diplomatic overtures remain stalled

Panel pauses trials of TB vaccine, seeks more data

KIMS AJOONI

India’s Subject Expert Committee has temporarily halted the Phase‑III trials of Bharat Biotech’s TB vaccine MTBVAC, citing gaps in Phase‑II immunogenicity data and a serious adverse event. The panel asked for age‑stratified analyses, raw data verification, genomic testing of the adverse case, and a revised Phase‑III protocol with clear efficacy endpoints. Bharat Biotech’s executive chairman called the review routine and pledged to submit the requested information. The pause could delay the launch of what is seen as a promising successor to the BCG vaccine, amid India’s push to eliminate tuberculosis ahead of global targets

Ather taps government’s ₹1‑trillion RDI fund to offset PLI exclusion

TVSMOTOR OLAELEC ATHERENERG BAJAJ-AUTO

Ather Energy is finalising a low‑cost, long‑tenure loan under the government’s ₹1 trillion research, development and innovation (RDI) scheme, which will fund up to 50 % of approved EV projects at 2‑3 % interest. The move aims to fill a financing gap after the company’s inclusion in the production‑linked incentive (PLI) auto scheme remains stalled due to strict eligibility criteria. The funding will support Ather’s development of new electric platforms and core technologies such as batteries, power electronics, and software, providing patient capital for R&D. While rivals like TVS, Bajaj and Ola benefit from the PLI, Ather hopes the RDI support will boost its innovation capacity and competitiveness in India’s electric two‑wheeler market

Rajesh Exports’ Cross‑Border Financial Disclosure Dispute

THANGAMAYL RAJESHEXPO SENCO

Rajesh Exports Ltd and its Singapore subsidiary have given conflicting statements to Indian and Singapore regulators about the consolidation of step‑down subsidiaries’ financials, which generate about 98% of the group’s reported revenue. SEBI’s interim order alleges misreporting and revenue inflation, noting that the Singapore unit, exempt from filing consolidated statements, only filed standalone accounts while claiming consolidation occurs in India. The company contends the discrepancy stems from procedural choices, not fraud, and is addressing SEBI’s concerns. The controversy has led to a sharp decline in the company’s share price and substantial loss of public investor wealth

China Reassures Offshore Investors Amid Crackdown on Illegal Outbound Trading

TIGERLOGS GANGESSECU

The China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) clarified that its recent crackdown on “illegal” cross‑border securities trading will not result in forced closure of mainland investors’ offshore accounts or liquidation of assets, which total about $54 billion. Existing accounts remain intact, and investors can still sell assets and move money out, though brokers must stop offering illicit services on the mainland within two years and cannot open new onshore accounts or accept fresh funds from mid‑June. The move aims to “purify” capital markets and curb illegal capital outflows while preserving the safety of investors’ offshore holdings

Amazon India Accelerates Quick‑Commerce Push with Amazon Now Expansion

3MINDIA SABEVENTS SWIGGY ISEC

Amazon India head Samir Kumar admits the company was initially slow in the rapid‑growth quick‑commerce space, but now plans to scale its Amazon Now service to 100 cities via over 1,000 micro‑fulfilment centres, adding 2‑3 facilities daily. Quick‑commerce in India is led by Blinkit (47% share), Zepto (24%) and Swiggy Instamart (22%); Amazon Now holds about 7%. Amazon plans to double its Prime base by 2026, targeting non‑metro cities, and has pledged $35 billion in India by 2030, including a ₹2,800 crore investment in fulfillment and delivery infrastructure. Kumar stresses that growth and profitability can coexist and views AI as a key enabler for sellers and shoppers, especially in smaller cities

Hong Kong’s Private Banking Faces Uncertain Future Amid China’s Crackdown

China’s recent crackdown on cross‑border stock trading, targeting online brokers and tightening controls on capital outflows, threatens the lucrative private banking sector in Hong Kong. Regulators are demanding stricter client onboarding, monitoring dormant accounts, and may require banks to prove offshore fund origins. Potential extensions of these rules could limit mainland high‑net‑worth individuals from holding offshore accounts, increase compliance burdens, and erode the city’s position as the world’s leading offshore wealth hub. While the full impact remains unclear, the crackdown poses a significant risk to Hong Kong’s private wealth management industry
