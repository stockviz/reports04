RBI Raises LTV Limits for Mutual Fund Loans: Implications for Investors

DOLAT TV18BRDCST

The RBI has increased loan‑to‑value (LTV) caps for loans against securities: listed shares up to 60%, equity mutual funds to 75%, and debt mutual funds to 85%. Varun Fatehpur explains that LTV determines how much can be borrowed against a portfolio—e.g., a ₹10  lakh equity fund can now fetch a ₹7.5  lakh loan. While higher limits improve liquidity options, such loans carry steep interest (10‑12% vs. 7‑7.5% for home loans) and are best used for short‑term cash needs, not as a return‑enhancement tool. Borrowers must monitor LTV continuously; a drop in fund value below the loan amount triggers a margin call that must be remedied within a week, so keeping a buffer (e.g., borrowing only 65‑70% of fund value) is advisable. For conservative investors, debt mutual funds—offering stable 6‑7% distributions—are preferable over equity funds or REITs for pledging against loans
