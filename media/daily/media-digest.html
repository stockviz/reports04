In this episode of “Kiska Brand Bajiga,” Chef Vikas Khanna reflects on his humble beginnings in Amritsar—watching his grandmother cook, coping with a club foot, and learning the spiritual importance of food through Langar. He recounts his early hardships in New York, from washing dishes to opening multiple eateries, and the pivotal moment when a fire destroyed his Indian restaurant, prompting a move to the U.S. Khanna credits his family’s resilience, his grandmother’s faith, and a deep sense of bhakti (devotion) for driving him to earn a Michelin star in 2011. He emphasizes storytelling in cuisine, weaving cultural memories and Sufi poetry into every dish, and highlights the role of Indian festivals in creating an inclusive dining home. The chef shares memorable encounters—serving Shahrukh Khan, Presidents, and spiritual leaders—and underscores that true success stems from perseverance, humility, and honoring one’s roots. Looking ahead, he remains hungry for new creative projects, including films and an OTT series, while continuing to inspire the next generation of chefs to find their own truth and devotion
