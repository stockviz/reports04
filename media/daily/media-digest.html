Indian Monsoon Outlook: Crop Prospects & Commodity Price Implications

TV18BRDCST AGL

The segment opened with a market recap—U.S. equities closed higher, Brent crude rose to $79/barrel, while futures slipped amid fresh U.S.–Iran tensions. In an interview excerpt with Adnaud Pettit, Executive Director of the International Grains Council, the IGC highlighted that 2026‑27 global grain stocks remain ample, with record‑level harvests expected. However, weather risks loom: the U.S. and Europe face heat stress during maize flowering, and an emerging El Niño could disrupt Southern Hemisphere crops, potentially reducing Australian wheat/maize, lowering Indian monsoon rains, and affecting rice yields. India’s rice outlook stays cautious but positive, with no immediate downgrade to export capacity, while corn demand may rise as biofuel production expands, linking feed and fuel markets. Oilseeds continue strong trade growth, especially Brazil‑China flows, though drought‑related risks persist for soybeans. Overall grain trade is expected to contract short‑term due to lower demand, but volumes should grow ~2 % annually over the next five years. Fertilizer markets face lingering price pressure, limiting application rates in the EU and possibly the U.S. Maritime logistics have rebounded, yet freight rates are up 20‑25 % and longer routes remain a bottleneck. Finally, raw commodity prices are low, reducing direct inflation risk, but processing energy and labor costs could still drive consumer price pressures

MOSPI’s New Services Production Index (ISP): Key Highlights and Future Plans

MOTILALOFS

The Ministry of Statistics (MOSPI) has introduced a Services Production Index (ISP) that tracks output volumes for 19 service sectors, with 14 sectors showing double‑digit growth in April 2026 (e.g., retail trade +30.8%, accommodation & food +37%). Dr. Saurabh Garg explains that the ISP measures top‑line output (volume) using hard administrative data—primarily GST returns and other transaction records—whereas GDP reflects value‑added (EBITDA‑type) figures. Consequently, the ISP’s high growth rates can exceed GDP growth, especially because it covers only about 60 % of the services sector (≈30 % of the economy) and relies on aggregate, often urban‑centric data. MOSPI plans to publish an overall ISP composite number after a few months of data stabilization, aiming for coverage over 80 % by adding health and dwelling‑ownership categories. A back‑series for GDP (covering the past four years) is slated for release by year‑end, and a regular annual CAPEX intentions survey will continue, with efforts to provide more frequent gross fixed capital formation data. The PPI has already been used in the industrial index and is expected to be incorporated into GDP deflation by the first or second quarter of the next fiscal year

Draft CAF‑III Norms: New Fuel‑Efficiency Targets, Credits and Penalties

M&M TATAMOTORS HYUNDAI MARUTI

The Power Ministry’s draft CAF‑III norms, set to start 1 April 2027 for five years, replace the current CAF‑II rules. Compliance will be evaluated in a three‑year phase followed by a two‑year phase, with progressively stricter fuel‑efficiency targets through 2031‑32. The proposal introduces incentives for cleaner tech, carbon‑neutral benefits for ethanol, bio‑fuels and compressed biogas, and “super‑credits” for EVs, hybrids and flex‑fuel vehicles. Automakers will operate a credit‑debit system, with compliance credits priced at ₹2,500 each, rising by ₹500 annually. Manufacturers missing targets face penalties, though firms selling under 1,000 passenger cars per year remain exempt. The draft is pending public release for comment
