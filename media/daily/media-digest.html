Tax Expert Breaks Down Supreme Court Tiger Global Verdict | Why TRC Is Not Enough

5PAISA TIGERLOGS

Supreme Court Verdict on Tiger Global (Flipkart) – Key Takeaways

  • Core Ruling: A Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) or tax‑resiliency certificate issued by a foreign country is not conclusive proof of treaty‑benefit eligibility. The Indian tax authority can still examine whether the claimant has real substance and genuine residency in that jurisdiction.

  • Impact on the Tiger Global case: The Court held that Tiger Global lacked substantive presence in Mauritius; therefore, the 0 % capital‑gains rate under the India‑Mauritius treaty does not apply.

  • Effect on existing and future foreign investment:

    • Pre‑April 2017 investments (grandfathered under the old Mauritius treaty) remain unaffected for now, but any exit related to those legacy deals may face heightened scrutiny.
    • Investments made after April 2017 are already taxable in India, so the ruling does not change their tax treatment.
    • Current FDI/FII flows are not expected to be deterred; the decision primarily targets past transactions that relied solely on a TRC.

  • Broader precedent:

    • Any past litigation that treated a TRC (or equivalent certificate of residency, e.g., CUR) as a definitive proof of treaty benefits—across jurisdictions such as Singapore, the Netherlands, the U.S., etc.—could be revisited.
    • This includes other Flipkart exits (e‑Bay, etc.) and unrelated deals where TRCs were used to claim reduced dividend or capital‑gains tax rates.

  • What investors should do:

    • Ensure substantial economic presence in the treaty‑partner country (office, staff, board, real activities).
      – Be prepared for case‑by‑case examination by the Indian tax department, providing documentary and factual evidence of residency beyond the certificate.

Market Note – Flipkart Stock Movement

  • Recent catalyst: Nalanda Capital’s purchase of a 1.8 % stake lifted Flipkart shares about 8.5 % in trade.
  • Positive triggers: Attractive valuation metrics (high P/E, solid CAGR), strong management outlook, and confidence from a reputable private‑equity investor.
  • Negative triggers: Slower growth in the “silver‑tier” customer segment and technical chart weakness, which could temper upside.

Overall, the Supreme Court decision sharpens compliance scrutiny for legacy treaty‑based tax benefits, while the current market enthusiasm around Flipkart is driven by fresh institutional backing despite some operational headwinds.

Strong Q3 For Union Bank Of India | Focussing On Improving Average CASA & Deposits, Says Company

UNIONBANK

Union Bank of India posted a Q3 earnings beat, with profit up 9% YoY and both net interest margin (NIM) and net interest income rising ~1%. The bank shed bulk deposits of ₹40‑45 bn, boosted CASA by about ₹14 bn, and converted ₹40 bn of bulk deposits into retail term deposits, lowering its cost of funds. Treasury funding fell by ₹15 bn, while on the asset side IBPC fell from ₹35 bn to ~₹3 bn and short‑term liabilities (STL) of ₹10 bn were shifted to longer tenor. Asset quality remained strong, with a credit cost of 0.09% and a slippage ratio of 0.79%, translating to roughly ₹5 bn in slippages for the nine‑month period.

The bank has quantified its ECL implementation gap at around ₹4,500 bn and is comfortable with the transition. No near‑term equity raise is planned; a ₹6,000 bn capital plan is already approved, and funding can be sourced via inexpensive instruments such as infrastructure or green bonds.

Margin improvement persisted despite the RBI’s 25‑bp rate cut, and the bank aims to defend its current NIM while seeking incremental gains through further bulk‑deposit shedding and continued CASA growth. LDR sits at about 81%, deemed acceptable, with LCR and NSFR also in comfortable zones.

Credit growth slowed to ~8% YoY, below peers, but retail advances surged 21.7% QoQ, MSME grew ~6%, agriculture ~4%, and corporate pipelines include ₹24 bn of undisbursed loans. The bank expects double‑digit growth in the combined retail‑corporate book for the year.

Asset quality remains high (≈84% A‑and‑above rating), and advanced analytics/ML are being used to monitor risk. The bank projects credit cost and total slippages to stay in line with current levels, and recoveries of over ₹3 bn in the first nine months are expected to improve further in Q4.
