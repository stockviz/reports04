Honda to Add 3,800 Jobs in India with 80‑Lakh‑Unit Two‑Wheeler Capacity by 2028

HONDAPOWER

Honda Motorcycle & Scooter India (HMSI) will expand its annual production capacity to about 8 million two‑wheelers by 2028, adding a new third line at Tapukara (Rs 1,500 cr, 670,000 units) and a fourth line at Vithalapur (Rs 920 cr, 650,000 units). The upgrades will create over 3,800 jobs. HMSI’s total capacity will rise from 60 lakh to 80 lakh units, supporting both domestic demand and exports to 65 countries, with export volumes projected to grow from 0.36 million units in FY24 to 0.62 million in FY26. While emphasizing a balanced strategy of ICE, electric, and alternative‑fuel models, Honda welcomes India’s upcoming Delhi EV mandate but notes that market demand will dictate the pace of electrification

India’s Beer Sector Fuels Near Double‑Digit Top‑Line Growth Amid Cost Pressures

UBL

United Breweries MD Vivek Gupta says the Indian beer market is enjoying strong sales, driven by state reforms, favorable weather and a shift among younger consumers toward beer, with volume growth in the high single‑digit range and modest price gains. However, profitability is squeezed by rising input costs—glass bottles, aluminium cans and imported materials—all inflated by the war‑related supply shock and a weak rupee. Gupta expects margin recovery as the industry works to mitigate these cost pressures, noting that balanced monsoon patterns matter more than short‑term heat spikes for sustained demand
