Debunking Indian Market Myths: SIP Discipline, Global Diversification & Long‑Term Investing

TITAN LUPIN

In this conversation Devina Mehra dispels alarmist narratives—India being “too expensive,” the end of its demographic dividend, and the notion that SIPs guarantee riches. She explains that valuation multiples, short‑term sentiment and media hype often mislead investors, and that past winners frequently underperform later. Mehra emphasizes a sensible, diversified asset allocation: avoid 100% equity, incorporate global exposure (30‑40% of equity at least), and use risk‑adjusted strategies rather than chasing recent winners. She cites Rakesh Ranbala’s career to illustrate that a few stocks drive returns and that equity returns are lumpy, underscoring the need for long‑term, untouchable capital. Sentiment is a contrarian indicator—when fear spikes, staying invested is prudent. Gold, while a traditional hedge, is volatile and should not dominate portfolios. Overall, disciplined SIP investing, prudent risk management, and global diversification are the keys to navigating market cycles and achieving sustainable wealth growth

Former iLearningEngines Executives Charged with Massive Fraud

Former CEO Puthugramam Chidambaran and former CFO Sayyed Farhan Ali Naqvi of the AI-driven education company iLearningEngines have been indicted on ten counts, including securities and wire fraud, for fabricating nearly all of the company’s customer relationships and revenue. Prosecutors allege they used forged contracts and “round‑trip” fund transfers to create $421 million in fake 2023 revenue, misleading investors and lenders. The company, which went public in April 2024 and briefly reached a $1.5 billion market value, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in December 2024 and entered liquidation in March 2025. The criminal enterprise charge carries a potential life sentence
