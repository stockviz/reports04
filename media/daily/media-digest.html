Sagar Cements Announces ₹1,000 Crore ESG Drive Targeting 50% Green Power by 2030

SAGCEM

Sagar Cements will invest over ₹1,000 crore in a three‑year ESG transformation, outlined in its Business Plan 2030. The plan focuses on renewable energy, technology upgrades, energy efficiency, alternative fuels, and community development. By 2030, the firm aims to raise green electricity use to 50% of total power and cut specific electric consumption to 65 kWh/ton of cement (down from 73 kWh/ton in FY 2025‑26). Current green energy capacity totals 36 MW (13.58 MW solar, 14.1 MW waste‑heat recovery, 8.3 MW hydel), with additional solar and WHRS projects slated for the next year. Middle East Conflict Drives India’s Highest LPG Prices Since Aug 2023

IOC

India’s domestic 14.2 kg LPG cylinder price rose by about ₹60, the steepest increase in a year, reflecting import reliance (85‑90% from the Middle East) and geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz.
