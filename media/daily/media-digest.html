Reliance SEZ Export Duty Uncertainty Amid India’s Fuel Tax Overhaul

RELIANCE

India has reintroduced export duties of ₹21.50 per litre on diesel and ₹29.50 per litre on aviation turbine fuel while cutting excise duty on petrol and diesel by ₹10 per litre. The key question is whether Reliance’s SEZ refinery exports will retain the exemption they enjoyed under the 2022 windfall‑tax regime. If exempt, Reliance’s margins stay insulated; if not, diesel and ATF export margins could be sharply compressed. Analysts note that OMCs benefit from the excise cut, reducing marketing losses and raising their breakeven crude price to about $80 a barrel. Export duties translate to roughly $36 per barrel for diesel and $50 per barrel for jet fuel, yet strong product cracks still allow export margins of $15‑25 per barrel. The fiscal impact hinges on the SEZ exemption, with potential revenue loss ranging from ₹80,000 crore to ₹1.5 lakh crore annually. Clarity on the SEZ treatment will be a near‑term catalyst for Reliance’s earnings outlook and government revenue forecasts
