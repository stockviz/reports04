A Decade of Netflix India: Growth, Challenges, and the Future of Storytelling

Netflix entered India in 2016 with a premium price, quickly adjusted pricing and invested in local content, producing landmark series like Sacred Games that proved Indian audiences craved homegrown, cinematic storytelling. Over ten years the platform has become a cultural mainstay, shaping viewing habits, expanding the star system, and giving women creators and leaders significant roles in content development. Milestones include Oscar‑winning “The Elephant Whisperers” and numerous international awards, underscoring its creative impact. The company acknowledges increasing difficulty in producing bold narratives that touch politics or religion, balancing creative freedom with regulatory compliance. It stresses the importance of strong writing over star power, and views streaming as complementary to theatres—offering a second life to films and broadening audience taste. Looking ahead, Netflix India aims to push even bolder stories, leveraging its talented teams while navigating economic pressures and metric‑driven decisions, positioning the next decade as its most ambitious yet

Rocket seeks AI‑native talent who use generative tools fluently, evaluating candidates via project‑based assessments that gauge creativity, tool mastery, and problem‑solving judgment. Both speakers underscored that AI removes technical barriers, empowers individuals to focus on high‑value decision‑making, and heralds a transformative era where the world is rebuilt around AI‑native workflows
