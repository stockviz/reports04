APJ Surinder Park Hotels’ Expansion, ARR Growth, and Diversification Outlook

PARKHOTELS LEMONTREE CHALET

The hotel chain plans to add 43 hotels and roughly 4,000 keys over the next four years, boosting its portfolio from 42 hotels/2,677 keys to a larger asset‑light model with about 12 management‑only properties. Occupancy remains above 90%, and the company targets double‑digit average room rate (ARR) growth to drive FY27 top‑line expansion, aiming for a 12‑15% increase despite recent geopolitical disruptions. Recent acquisitions include a ₹232 cr Mumbai property and a ₹20 cr Kerala hotel, while its mixed‑use residential project in Kolkata is expected to generate ₹600‑650 cr in sales over four years, contributing ~₹70 cr cash flow this FY. Margin improvement of 2‑3 percentage points is anticipated as labor costs normalize and residential cash flows rise. Non‑hotel revenue, chiefly the Flurries restaurant chain, will expand from ~110 to 140‑150 outlets, with a focus on Delhi, Mumbai, and achieving 100 outlets in West Bengal. Revenue diversification beyond Kolkata is planned through new hotel openings, especially the Mumbai asset slated for FY27‑28, and broader geographic expansion of both hotel and F&B operations

Toddle Targets $50‑100 Million Fundraise Amid Edtech Revival

Bangalore‑based B2B edtech firm Toddle has hired US bank BGL to manage a $50‑100 million primary and secondary fundraising round, aiming to become one of India’s largest edtech raises since the sector’s downturn. The company, founded in 2019, offers curriculum and classroom management software for K‑12 schools, especially IB programs, and now generates about a third of revenue from the US. Toddle’s ARR is around $25 million with near‑breakeven status, reporting 60% YoY revenue growth to INR 114.4 crore in FY25 and narrowing losses. The last valuation was $136 million after a $17 million Series A in Jan 2023. The raise reflects a broader shift toward B2B SaaS edtech investments as the Indian edtech market shows early signs of recovery, with $178 million raised H1 2026
