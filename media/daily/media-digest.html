Asian Market Outlook Amid Geopolitical Tensions: Risks, Outflows, and Investment Strategies

CANHLIFE

Harold Wonder Lynn, HSBC’s head of Asia equity strategy, says the Russia‑Ukraine conflict is prompting a classic “risk‑off” shift, with roughly $60 bn of outflows from Asian equities since late February—double the March outflow seen during COVID. Investors are fleeing to cash, especially in Hong Kong where rates have fallen. While the outflows hit Korea, Taiwan, Japan and India hard, Lynn stresses that Asia’s banking system remains sound and a regional collapse is unlikely. He advises a three‑step approach: (1) assess risk tolerance and move to cash if needed; (2) tilt equity exposure toward defensive sectors such as consumer staples, utilities, telecoms and themes like defence, renewable energy and infrastructure; (3) focus on pockets of resilient growth, with India still relatively pricey but offering opportunities. Should the conflict de‑escalate, equities could rebound, but the path to pre‑conflict levels will be gradual due to real‑economy impacts. Country selection must consider oil import dependence and domestic capital bases—China and India are less flow‑dependent, while Korea, Japan and Taiwan face greater exposure to oil price shocks. Overall, safety and sector‑specific resilience drive the current investment playbook in Asia
