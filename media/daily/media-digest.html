2026 Republic Day Parade: CRPF And SSB Motorcycle Daredevils Showcase Skill; Rafale Jets Stun 2026-01-26 13:31

LAXMIMACH

The Republic Day Parade’s finale featured spectacular motorcycle and aerial displays. The CRPF and SSB daredevil teams, led respectively by Assistant Commandant Seema Nag (CRPF) and Assistant Commandant Naveen Kumari (SSB), performed intricate one‑wheel and multi‑bike formations—including Sarvatra Suraksha, Garuda, butterfly, and a 42‑woman CRPF pyramid—showcasing balance, coordination, and courage.

The Indian Air Force fly‑past followed with three key formations: an “Urgent” formation of a C‑130J Super Hercules and two C‑295 transport aircraft; the “Vaduna” formation combining a P‑HI maritime reconnaissance aircraft and two Sukhoi‑30 MKI fighters; and the “Bajarang” formation of six Rafale jets led by Group Captain Arun Kumar, capped by a solo Rafale stunt at 900 km/h. The fly‑past highlighted India’s modern air power and precision.

The ceremony concluded with the President’s bodyguard arrival, the national anthem, and Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s interaction with the crowds. Foreign dignitaries from the EU were present, and the event was framed as a celebration of national unity, pride, and the armed forces’ achievements, including recent anti‑Naxal successes.
