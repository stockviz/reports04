  • India’s GDP outlook:
    • MOSP projects 8% nominal growth for 2026, with expectations of 10% growth next year amid higher inflation.
    • Sectoral growth: agriculture down to 3.1% (from 4.6%), manufacturing up to 7% (from 4.5%), services rising, trade‑hotels‑transport‑communication at 7.5%, financial services 9.9%, public administration ~10%.
    • Consumption growth forecast at 7% (slightly below last year’s 7.2%); investment (capital formation) projected at 7.8%, driven mainly by private capex despite government accounting for only ~10‑12% of total investment.
  • Wall Street recap:
    • Both the Dow and S&P 500 hit intraday record highs before pulling back; the Dow fell about 470 points, the S&P cooled off, while the Nasdaq closed in positive territory.
    • Market reaction linked to President Trump’s comments on home‑builder and defense stocks.
  • President Trump’s recent statements (Truth Social):
    • Criticized NATO’s relevance without U.S. support, claimed boosting NATO defense spending would have prevented the Ukraine war.
    • Discussed talks with Colombian President Petro on drugs; announced Venezuela’s purchase of U.S.‑made products under a new oil deal.
    • Supported bipartisan Russia‑sanctions bill, warning that countries buying cheap Russian oil (including India) would face penalties.
    • Proposed increasing the 2027 U.S. military budget from $1 trillion to $1.5 trillion, citing tariff revenues and debt reduction.
    • Called out defense contractors (e.g., RYTHON) for poor delivery, demanding upfront investment, bans on stock buybacks, and threatening contract losses.
  • U.S. naval actions:
    • Seized a Venezuela‑linked tanker (“Sofia”) near Iceland and a Russian‑flagged tanker (“Marinera”) in the North Sea, citing enforcement of sanctions on Venezuelan oil.
    • Russia condemned the seizure as piracy and a breach of maritime law, raising tension between the two nations.
  • Commodities update (Manisha Gupta):
    • Precious metals fell sharply (gold >1%, silver 4%, platinum 5.5%, palladium 3.5%) due to profit‑taking ahead of the 2026 Metals Index rebalancing (Jan 9‑15).
    • Crude oil rose on two factors: U.S. announcement to release stored Venezuelan oil and continued sanctions/seizures of Venezuela‑linked tankers, plus two weeks of declining U.S. crude inventories (down 3.8 million barrels).
  • Corporate note:
    • Angel1’s client base grew 2% month‑on‑month (21% YoY); daily orders also rose, supported by an upcoming stock split and dividend considerations.
