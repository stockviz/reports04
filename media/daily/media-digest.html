ABD’s FY27 Growth Plan: Premiumisation, International Expansion, and ICONiQ Boost

ABDL

Allied Blenders & Distillers posted a 9.1% rise in operating income and a 21.2% jump in EBITDA in Q4 FY26, but PAT fell 52% due to a one‑off tax provision and interest expense. Management attributes the profit dip to these provisions, not operational weakness. The company is pursuing premiumisation, backward integration (Kion Blenders acquisition) and capex to expand gross margins, with P&A volume up >20% and ICONiQ driving volume growth (12‑13 M cases MRR). Short‑term cost pressure from the West‑Asia conflict affects glass, caps and PET, but margins are expected to normalize once the conflict eases. State‑level policy changes (price hikes, excise reforms) should lift margins, especially in Karnataka and Telangana. ABD plans to re‑enter Tamil Nadu, expanding headroom. Internationally, presence grew from 23 to 36 countries, focusing on GCC, Africa, and Western markets, with a 15%+ YoY rise in overseas revenue. The Kion acquisition secures raw‑material supply and bottling capacity in Andhra Pradesh, expected to double sales there and improve margins. No further major margin pressures aside from forex risk, which is hedged. FY27 outlook: double‑digit volume growth, mid‑double‑digit value growth, stable gross‑EBITDA margins, and a revised FY28 margin target of 18% after a projected 200‑bp boost from the India‑UK FTA. ICONiQ’s market expansion and CSD approvals are key growth drivers

JIOFIN IDEA RUBICON

Following TRAI’s directive against device‑specific tariffs, Reliance Jio has removed the restriction that its ultra‑cheap 4G plans were only for JioPhone/JioBharat devices, now allowing users of other 4G feature phones (e.g., Nokia, Lava, itel) to access the same ₹123/28‑day pack (0.5 GB daily data, unlimited calls, 300 SMS). Sherritt Moves to Dissolve Cuban Mining Joint Venture Amid U.S. Sanctions

DELTACORP ICICIGI

Sherritt International, a Canadian metals producer, announced plans to dissolve its 50‑50 joint venture with Cuba’s General Nickel Company at the Moa nickel mine and to surrender its stake in the Cuban energy business Energas due to recent U.S. sanctions targeting non‑U.S. entities operating in Cuba. The company proposes swapping its mining interest for full ownership of its Alberta refinery and seeks a C$277 million equalization payment, citing higher value of mining assets. The breakup could take months or years, prompting Sherritt to request a court order to expedite the process. The sanctions have caused significant turmoil, including board resignations, a CFO departure, a sharp share‑price decline, and delayed financial reporting
