External Disruptions Threaten Indian Firms’ Talent Landscape

3MINDIA

A new Adecco India report, based on 1,044 employers across major metros, finds 97 % of Indian businesses now face constant external shocks—climate, infrastructure, health crises—leading to lower productivity, higher absenteeism and rising hiring strains. Nearly half say these disruptions hamper talent attraction and retention, with 25 % experiencing severe hiring impacts, especially in Delhi‑NCR’s services and IT sectors. While 95 % prioritize business continuity, many lack insight into the most vulnerable workforce segments, prompting calls for better workforce intelligence, mental‑health support, flexible work models and wellness infrastructure. Meanwhile, reverse migration is accelerating, with 30‑35 % of remote workers moving to tier II/III cities, driven largely by younger professionals seeking quality of life over higher salaries

Rajesh Mehta’s Elest Valuation Surge Amid Controversial Mauritius Fund Investments

RAJESHEXPO ACC 21STCENMGM

In three years, three Mauritius‑based funds (Geraldton Finance, Aviator Global, Knightsbridge) invested about ₹1,250 cr in Elest Pvt Ltd, a private company owned by Rajesh Mehta that holds land, technology and machinery for the battery arm of listed Rajesh Exports. Their investments, based on inconsistent valuation reports from Finshore Management Services, drove Elest’s equity value from ₹3,000 cr to nearly ₹50,000 cr despite having no operating revenue or a running plant. The funds hold a combined 4% stake, while Mehta owns 90% and Rajesh Exports 6%. The structure—Elest leasing critical assets to ACC Energy (a Rajesh Exports subsidiary) and returning them after 30 years—has attracted SEBI scrutiny over transparency, related‑party transactions, and whether value is being siphoned from the listed company. SEBI’s interim order questions the verifiability of over 98% of Rajesh Exports’ revenue, and the government may withdraw the company from a ₹18,100 cr production‑linked incentive scheme. Legal experts warn that such arrangements could breach fiduciary duties to minority shareholders

Kedaara Capital Tightens Investment Pace Amid Valuation Gaps

LENSKART 5PAISA

Kedaara Capital is slowing new investments and raising underwriting standards as private market valuations diverge from public market corrections. Founder Manish Kejriwal says the firm now approves fewer deals, focusing on lower‑risk opportunities and intensifying due diligence. Despite a weak IPO market, Kedaara maintains exit momentum through strategic sales, sponsor‑to‑sponsor deals and continuation vehicles, emphasizing operational readiness over market timing. Recent exits include Lenskart and Aavas Financiers; 2025 investments span Impetus, JusPay, Axtria, and a 5% stake in Axis Finance. In 2026, Kedaara plans a majority stake purchase in Tynor Orthotics. Managing about $6.5 bn, the firm sticks to core sectors—financial services, consumer, healthcare, and tech—while avoiding infrastructure. It views financial services as a key lever for India’s long‑term growth and competes with sovereign wealth funds and pension boards, often co‑investing. AI adoption is handled case‑by‑case by deal teams rather than a centralized mandate. The firm’s long‑term 10‑12‑year fund cycle underpins confidence despite short‑term market volatility

Kerala Real Estate Faces Mixed Impacts from West Asia Conflict

BRIGADE KOTAKBANK SOBHA CARBORUNIV TTKPRESTIG CANFINHOME

The war in West Asia has sparked a surge in property enquiries from Keralite NRIs, especially those reconsidering investments after layoffs and regional uncertainty, boosting demand for both luxury and mid‑income homes. However, construction costs have spiked due to oil price volatility and disrupted supply chains, with materials rising 30‑35%, while labor shortages persist as migrant workers delay return. Developers remain optimistic, launching new projects and targeting the heightened NRI interest, but warn that cost inflation and supply‑chain pressures could temper the demand growth if unresolved

JSW Infra Wins Kolkata Port Terminal Contract, Expands Capacity to 1.8 M TEUs

BCG JSWINFRA

JSW Infrastructure secured a 30‑year DBFOT concession to develop two container berths at Syama Prasad Mookerjee Port and five berths at Netaji Subhas Dock, adding 0.93 M TEU capacity in Phase I. Combined with its ongoing Berths 7‑8 project (0.45 M TEU), the total capacity at Kolkata Dock System will rise to about 1.4 M TEUs, boosting operational efficiency and supporting regional trade growth

QIP Pipeline Swells as Listed Firms Target Over ₹30,000 Crore Fundraise

CGPOWER POWERINDIA STLTECH PREMIER ACMESOLAR BIOCON SBIN JSWENERGY JSWINFRA WAAREEENER OLAELEC SWIGGY AUBANK POONAWALLA MFSL

India’s primary market shows early signs of recovery as a wave of Qualified Institutional Placements (QIPs) is underway. Companies such as Premier Energy, Waaree Energies, Max Financial Services, JSW Infrastructure, AU Small Finance Bank and Sterlite Technologies have secured board approvals or are in talks to raise between ₹2,000 crore and ₹10,000 crore each, collectively exceeding ₹30,000 crore. The surge follows a subdued IPO period caused by geopolitical tensions, rupee pressure and foreign outflows. While QIPs won’t fully offset the weak IPO market, strong institutional demand for existing listed stocks is viewed as a positive indicator. Recent QIPs by Poonawalla Fincorp, JSW Energy, Biocon and others have added to the momentum, though total fundraising remains below 2025 levels. Market participants expect continued support from foreign and domestic investors, with expectations that QIP activity will help revive IPO pipelines in the second half of 2026

Salesforce Announces AI‑Focused Layoffs with Generous Severance Packages

OFSS

Salesforce is cutting 86 jobs across its Agentforce AI product, Mulesoft integration platform, and Marketing Cloud divisions, marking a second round of cuts after January’s layoffs. A California WARN filing confirms the roles span sales, admin, and tech functions. Affected U.S. employees may receive up to 30 weeks of base pay in severance—13 weeks for senior directors, nine weeks for senior managers, plus additional weeks for tenure and for workers aged 60+, plus six months (or 12 months for those 60+) of COBRA coverage. This package exceeds recent offers from Oracle, Block, and Amazon. The cuts come as Salesforce’s stock has fallen over 30% amid investor worries that AI advances could diminish demand for its core CRM software, even as Agentforce revenue crosses $1 billion annually

Stadium Workers Secure Tentative Deal Ahead of World Cup

GLOBAL

Unionized staff at SoFi Stadium in Inglewood have reached a tentative contract with food‑service provider Legends Global, averting a strike before the U.S. men’s soccer World Cup opener. The agreement includes wage hikes—cooks could earn up to $40 an hour within two years—job‑security protections against subcontracting, and a unique clause preserving the right to strike in response to ICE raids. The contract runs through April 2028, covering the upcoming Olympics, and will be voted on by workers Wednesday. Legends Global welcomed the pact, citing plans for a high‑quality hospitality experience during the tournament
