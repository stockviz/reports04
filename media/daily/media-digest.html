The Pant Project: From Personal Frustration to a 100 Crore Custom‑Fit Pants Brand

SADBHIN

The Pant Project was founded by brothers Odit and Dhruv Toshniwal to solve the chronic fit problem of Indian men’s pants. Leveraging a D2C model, they combined traditional tailoring with modern tech to create fully customized, stretch‑enhanced garments, offering a simple online sizing process and an in‑store AI body‑scan for instant measurements. After 1,000+ trial fittings, they validated market demand, grew to over 3  lakh online customers, and scaled from 2 to 15 stores, reaching ₹100 crore in revenue within 1.5 years. Their unit economics improved as gross margins rose from 50 % to 60‑65 %, while marketing spend fell from ~50 % of sales to under 20 % through efficient digital ads and strong repeat‑purchase rates (40 %+ within six months). Operations are driven by an ERP‑linked checklist that ensures end‑to‑end quality control, minimal waste, and an asset‑light, made‑to‑order inventory model. Future initiatives include AI‑powered demand forecasting and expanding the product line while maintaining a “fit‑first” brand promise. The company emphasizes disciplined scaling, brand trust, and continuous innovation in fabric and technology to become the default choice for Indian men’s pants
