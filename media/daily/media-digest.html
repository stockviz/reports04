Impact of Deficient Monsoon and Global Turmoil on Insecticides India

INSECTICID

Managing Director Rajesh Agrawal explained that regional monsoon variability will affect crop patterns differently across India. In North India, groundwater and solar pumps cushion paddy growers from rain deficits, though electricity costs may rise. Central states (MP, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka) rely heavily on rainfall; gaps could force re‑sowing and boost pest pressure, affecting herbicide demand. South India benefits from both southwest and northeast monsoons, limiting disruption. Across the country, tight supply chains, rising input costs (solvents, emulsifiers, packaging) and El Niño risk push raw‑material prices up 10‑12 % (some items up 15‑20 %). These costs are being passed to customers, who accept higher prices due to scarcity. Q1 sales are insulated, but Q2 will reveal the true impact as inventories deplete. Despite the challenges, Insecticides India remains optimistic, citing full dams, diversified product mix (herbicides, insecticides, fungicides for paddy, cotton, soy, pulses) and expects strong FY‑26/27 growth, with no downgrade in outlook
