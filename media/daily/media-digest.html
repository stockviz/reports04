Swimwear Market Booms in India Amid Fitness Trends and Domestic Travel Surge

PAGEIND GROBTEA SWIGGY

India’s swimwear market has doubled to $8.5 bn in five years, driven by year‑round fitness swimming and a shift toward domestic beach resorts due to geopolitical disruptions. D2C brands such as Cava Athleisure and Cuckoo Swimwear are filling a niche between high‑performance gear and luxury resort wear, offering trend‑forward, affordable designs that appeal to younger consumers. Speedo, holding about 60% of the organized market, is responding by expanding omnichannel and quick‑commerce presence, but faces growing competition from online‑focused entrants. Prices from newcomers start around ₹2,500, undercutting Speedo’s ₹3,500 entry‑level offerings. Partnerships with pools, fitness platforms, and e‑commerce giants aim to retain leadership as the category’s growth—projected at 35%‑plus annually—continues. Domestic resorts in India, Thailand and Vietnam are becoming key drivers of swimwear demand as Indian travelers pivot from overseas trips

TRAI orders Reliance Jio to end discriminatory tariff practices

AIRTELPP JIOFIN

The Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) has directed Reliance Jio to stop tariff practices it deems non‑transparent and discriminatory. Jio must, by 14 April, publish all tariff offerings—including the Rs 249, Rs 199 and Rs 209 special tariff vouchers—on every sales channel (retail stores, website, app, etc.) and restructure its Jio Bharat and Jio Phone plans so they are available to users of any device, not just Jio‑specific hardware. TRAI’s probe found that these offers were previously limited to Jio stores or the MyJio app and that device‑specific tariffs restricted consumer choice and mobile number portability. The regulator warned that failure to comply could attract fines up to ₹1 lakh for a first offence and ₹2 lakh for subsequent offences, with daily penalties for continued non‑compliance
