Heatwave Safeguards for India’s Gig Delivery Workforce

ETERNAL SWIGGY ZOMATO TVSELECT

As temperatures soar above 40 °C across India, major e‑commerce and food‑delivery platforms are rolling out measures to protect gig workers from dehydration, heat exhaustion and heatstroke. Amazon’s “Project Ashray” will install 100 air‑conditioned rest stops with seating, washrooms, water and first‑aid, open to all delivery partners, alongside health‑check camps. Zomato, TVS and Shell are creating 5,000 shaded, water‑supply rest points; Zomato and Blinkit offer broader health‑insurance cover. Bigbasket is adding ventilated rest areas and electrolyte supplies. Zepto is retrofitting dark‑store hubs with coolers, reflective roofs and indoor rest spaces with water and insurance for heat‑related illnesses. Companies such as Eternal (Zomato/Blinkit) and Swiggy provide cooling vests and evaporative jackets that can lower body temperature by 6‑7 °C. Additional initiatives include summer kits with ORS and electrolytes, “Doctor on Call” services (Flipkart, Blinkit), ambulance helplines (Amazon) and targeted heat‑risk training for frontline staff
