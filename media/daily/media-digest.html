How Indian Investors Can Tap Into SpaceX’s $75 Billion IPO

The upcoming SpaceX IPO, slated for June 12, will be one of the world’s largest listings. Indian investors cannot subscribe directly like they do for domestic IPOs unless they hold an international brokerage account that allows pre‑IPO allocations. Even then, such private‑placement shares typically come with a 9‑12 month lock‑up. Post‑listing, Indians can gain exposure through U.S.‑listed ETFs or index funds that include SpaceX, or by buying fractional shares on U.S. exchanges. The pricing is expected around $135‑$150 per share, but fractional buying lowers the entry cost. While the company’s ambitious projects (space data centers, open platforms) are attractive, investors are urged to assess risks, consider currency depreciation effects, and allocate only a portion of their portfolio after thorough research

The Science Behind an A380’s Thrust and Lift

TV18BRDCST

The video explains how the Airbus A380’s four turbofan engines generate thrust: air is drawn in, compressed, mixed with fuel, ignited, and expelled at high speed, producing forward force. Most thrust comes from the large front fan moving massive volumes of air. This thrust accelerates the aircraft down the runway, allowing air to flow over and under the wings, creating lift that overcomes the plane’s weight. Together, thrust and lift enable the 570‑ton A380 to take off and fly
