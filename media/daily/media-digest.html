CESC BFUTILITIE CONCOR TATAPOWER

EPF Contribution Choices: Continue, Cap, or Shift to NPS & Other Investments

The discussion outlines three paths for salaried employees regarding their EPF: (1) Continue full EPF contributions – offers a stable 8.25% government‑backed return, forced savings discipline, and eliminates behavioral gaps, but limits equity exposure, may see rates fall, and withdrawals are cumbersome and inflexible. (2) Cap EPF contributions – frees up take‑home pay, which can be redirected to higher‑growth or more liquid options, but requires disciplined personal investing. Alternatives include: - NPS via employer – low expense ratios (0.03‑0.09%), potential for higher returns, tax‑efficient contributions (up to 14% of basic + DA), increased withdrawal flexibility (80% lump‑sum, 60% tax‑free), and lifecycle asset allocation choices. - PPF – similar tax benefits but essentially replicates EPF’s fixed‑income nature, offering little diversification advantage. - Mutual Fund SIPs – allow equity exposure for long‑term growth, tailored to risk profile. - Build an emergency fund – improves liquidity and reduces reliance on high‑cost short‑term credit. The hosts recommend evaluating age, risk tolerance, and financial goals before deciding, and suggest a future deep‑dive into NPS sub‑options
