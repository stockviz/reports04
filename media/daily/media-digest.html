Outlook for Crude Oil Prices Amid Prolonged Hormuz Closure and Supply Constraints

DLINKINDIA BBTC

In an interview, PIMCO’s Greg Sharenow explains that if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed through May, triple‑digit crude prices could persist for months due to limited transit capacity, trapped tankers, and low global inventories—the lowest in a decade. Even with a full reopening by the end of May, price normalization will be slow; Brent may fall to the $80s, but a return to $70‑level oil is unlikely for at least a year, possibly longer. Reduced flow (≈20% of normal) would still remove 3‑4 million barrels per day, pressuring prices upward. Refinery damage in the Gulf and ongoing attacks on Russian plants further tighten refined‑product markets. Gas markets face similar strain: Qatar’s output loss, low inventories in Europe, and delayed LNG projects keep LNG prices elevated, with modest relief only after demand rebuilds for winter. Overall, limited supply‑side CAPEX and heightened commodity demand from AI, defense, and energy transition suggest sustained price pressure across oil and gas for the foreseeable future

Sharp Expansion of Welfare Spending Strains State Fiscal Space in 2026 Elections

INFY

Indian states are dramatically increasing cash transfers and subsidy-led welfare programs, with unconditional cash transfers for women rising from 0.01% to 0.5% of GDP between FY21 and FY26. Major states allocate large portions of revenue expenditure to welfare—Jharkhand >12%, Karnataka ~9%, West Bengal ~9%, Tamil Nadu ~4%—leaving limited fiscal flexibility as 75‑77% of Tamil Nadu and Kerala’s revenue receipts are already committed. Overall, social sector spending accounts for 46% of state outlays, while capital spending is only 3% of GDP. State gross fiscal deficits have risen to 3.3% of GDP and debt to 29%, with 76% of deficits funded by market borrowing, constraining RBI rate cuts. The macro risk is that continued welfare expansion will crowd out productive investment unless financed by higher borrowing or cuts elsewhere, a key focus for markets during the election cycle
