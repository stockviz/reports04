Crude Oil Prices Slide Amid Restored Hormuz Flows and Rising Global Output

TV18BRDCST ATHERENERG AXISBANK ICICIBANK

Crude oil has fallen over 3% this week, staying below $72 per barrel—about 43% below the April wartime peak of $120. The dip is driven by renewed traffic through the Strait of Hormuz following the June 17 U.S.–Iran peace deal, higher production from OPEC‑plus (an extra 188,000 bpd in July, cumulative +940,000 bpd since the war), UAE’s post‑OPEC output of 3.7 million bpd, record Russian exports, and increased shipments from Iraq, Saudi Arabia, Iran, Canada, Kenya and the U.S. Vessel counts through the strait have risen from 223 (June 15‑21) to 343 (June 22‑28), though still well below pre‑war levels (130‑150 per day). Analysts now forecast further declines: Citi sees $85/bbl in 2026 and $75 in 2027; Goldman Sachs similar; UBS $83/$75; ICICI $79/$65‑75; JPMorgan $60/$57; Saxo Bank $75; and Crystal Energy sub‑$60, with City projecting $60‑65 by year‑end, implying another ~10% drop over the next six months. The August 2 OPEC ministerial meeting, especially Iraq’s OPEC status, will be closely watched
