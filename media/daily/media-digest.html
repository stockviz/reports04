India orders Tata Power’s Gujarat imported‑coal plant to run at full capacity

TATAPOWER

The government has directed Tata Power’s 4 GW imported‑coal plant in Gujarat to operate at full output from April 1 to June 30 to meet rising summer demand and avert power shortages as peak demand may hit 270 GW. The order may be extended to other imported‑coal plants if needed, follows a revised power purchase agreement with the state, and a committee will set benchmark rates for the plant’s electricity. The plant, idle for six months due to high coal costs and lack of a supply contract, will now be required to run at full capacity

HDBFS HDFCBANK

HDFC Bank’s part‑time chairman Atanu Chakraborty unexpectedly quit, citing “practices not in line with his values.” RBI quickly cleared the bank of material governance issues and appointed former CEO Keki Mistry as interim chairman for three months. Tata Semiconductor Secures $735 Million Loan, Pledges Zero‑Valued Dholera Land

EMIL IDFCFIRSTB CANHLIFE BAJAJINDEF NSIL 5PAISA PAISALO TENNIND BEL

Tata Semiconductor Manufacturing Pvt Ltd obtained a 6,835 crore (≈$735 m) loan from five foreign banks to fund its 91,000‑crore chip fab in Gujarat, with conditions that Tata retain a 51% stake, keep the Tata brand and maintain a 30:70 equity‑to‑debt ratio. The banks’ collateral is a 163.5‑acre plot in Dholera valued at zero, pledged as a legal mortgage. The loan, to be repaid by 2031, is split among HSBC, MUFG, FAB, DBS and ANZ via GIFT City branches, allowing lower interest rates. Tata Electronics has already invested 690 crore in the subsidiary and must inject more capital to meet the equity requirement. The fab, partnered with Taiwan’s Powerchip, targets mature‑node chips (28‑110 nm) with trial production slated for early 2027, while a parallel OSAT unit is planned in Assam. The central government will fund 50% of the projects, with Gujarat and Assam contributing 20% each. Analysts note the loan’s unconventional terms reflect reliance on Tata’s credit profile and the strategic push for domestic semiconductor manufacturing

Temple Ceremony Bridges Rift Between Tata Sons and Shapoorji Pallonji Group

CAPTRUST NSIL TATACAP BCG

After the 2016 fallout that saw Cyrus Mistry ousted as Tata Sons chairman, relations between Tata Sons and the Shapoorji Pallonji (SP) Group have remained strained. The week‑long celebrations begin on 2 April, with the Kanchi Kamakoti Peetam’s Sankaracharya performing the rites. While both companies declined comment, a Tata Trusts executive noted the unusual collaboration. The event coincides with ongoing discussions—prompted by a July 2025 Tata Trusts resolution—about providing an exit for the SP Group, which holds an 18.37 % stake in Tata Sons, and addressing its demand for an IPO of Tata Sons. The joint participation may signal a thaw in the long‑standing feud between the two Parsi‑led conglomerates

Saudi Aramco CEO Cancels CERAWeek Appearance Amid Iran Conflict

KICL SPAL BPCL

Saudi Aramco chief Amin Nasser has withdrawn from the CERAWeek energy conference in Houston, staying in Saudi Arabia because of the escalating Iran‑Saudi conflict that has closed the Strait of Hormuz and threatened Gulf energy infrastructure. The move underscores the severe challenges Aramco faces, its biggest since COVID‑19 and the 2019 attacks on its facilities, including output cuts and rerouting of crude. Kuwait’s KPC CEO will attend virtually, while Abu Dhabi’s Mubadala is unlikely to send representatives, and ADNOC’s attendance remains uncertain. The conflict has already caused significant disruptions, including attacks on Aramco’s joint‑venture refinery and a temporary halt at the Yanbu loading port
