Inside Bandra’s Mirai: Bringing Authentic Japanese Cuisine to India

The interview with Mirai’s founders, Sam and Rinshin, explores their journey from importing Japanese ingredients to opening a restaurant in Bandra that blends authentic Japanese flavors with Indian palate preferences. Rinshin, a Ladakh native, began importing Japanese food in 2007, learning product quality directly from Japanese exporters. Sam, a hospitality veteran, partnered with him after recognizing a gap in authentic Japanese dining in Mumbai. Together they focus on genuine umami, sourcing true Japanese ingredients, and training staff, while offering a slightly saucier menu for Indian diners and a dedicated “authentic” menu for Japanese guests, including dishes like chawan mushi and tamago yaki. Mirai aims to deliver an immersive Japanese cultural experience through its food, ambiance, and attention to detail

Google Expands AI Data Centre Plans in India Amid Growing Enterprise Demand

MCLOUD

Google Cloud CEO Thomas Kurian announced that, beyond the $15 billion Visakhapatnam AI data centre launched in October 2025, Google will invest further in additional AI‑ready facilities across India over several years. He highlighted India as Google’s largest Asian market, driven by enterprise adoption of AI services such as Gemini Enterprise, with customers like HDFC Bank, ICICI, and Apollo Hospitals leading the way. The move aims to boost cloud revenue (which hit $46 billion globally in FY24‑25) and compete with rivals Microsoft and AWS, leveraging Google’s full‑stack AI advantage (chips, models, cloud, and applications). Analysts note that while Google’s suite strengths are clear, persuading existing AWS/Azure customers to switch remains a challenge, though the broader digital‑transformation wave in India presents a sizable growth opportunity

Rapido’s Rapid Rise to India’s Top Consumer‑Internet Platform

OLAELEC SWIGGY ZOMATO

Rapido has surpassed Uber, Ola, Swiggy, Zomato, Blinkit and Zepto in monthly active users, reaching 82 million (Mar‑May) and leading ride volumes with 6.5 million daily rides. Its growth stems from a subscription‑based, flat‑fee model that boosts driver loyalty and lowers acquisition costs, alongside aggressive expansion into cabs, e‑rickshaws, food delivery (Ownly), financial services (Shyogsamart), and car‑pooling (Hopr). Backed by $798 million in funding (latest $240 million round valuing it at $3 billion), Rapido targets tier‑II+ markets where affordable two‑wheel mobility remains under‑served, positioning itself as a broad consumer‑internet ecosystem rather than just a ride‑hailing player

GIC Re Chief Hitesh Joshi Charts AI‑Driven Resilience and Growth Strategy

PRUDMOULI GICHSGFIN AIRTELPP LICHSGFIN EVERESTIND ICICIAMC

Hitesh Joshi, newly appointed chairman‑MD of General Insurance Corporation of India, prioritises short‑term stability and long‑term expansion through a “resilience” agenda. He has overhauled underwriting, exposure management and portfolio rebalancing—shifting from traditional catastrophe lines to a mix of property, casualty, specialty and emerging risks like data‑centres and green assets. The reinsurer will boost its cadre of catastrophe modelers from 7 to 24 to better price climate‑driven perils. Central to the plan is “Ragini,” an in‑house Retrieval‑Augmented Generation AI assistant that mines internal risk analyses, preserves proprietary data, and offers rapid, insight‑rich queries for underwriters and leadership. Keeping the AI platform internal mitigates data‑leak risks, reinforced by a ban on public AI tools. Attrition stays low (~1.9% annually) and talent loss is offset by overseas assignments and AI‑enabled knowledge retention. GIC Re aims to raise overseas business from 25% to 40% within 5‑7 years, gradually re‑enter markets such as Japan, and lift life‑reinsurance from under 5% toward global benchmarks. The government still holds 2.4% of shares after an oversubscribed sale, and competition from new GIFT‑City entrants is noted but deemed manageable in a price‑driven, capital‑constrained market
