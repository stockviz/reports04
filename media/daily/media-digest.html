India’s Economic Inflection Point: Trade Deals, Manufacturing, MSMEs, Startups & AI

MARUTI

The panel discusses how recent US, EU and UK trade agreements remove tariff uncertainties, opening lucrative markets for Indian manufacturers, especially auto components, and boosting export potential. They highlight government initiatives—budget allocations for semiconductors, electronics, rare‑earths, and capital goods—that strengthen self‑reliance and new‑age manufacturing. MSMEs, contributing ~30% of manufacturing and ~45% of exports, are poised for growth with enhanced credit access via NBFCs, supported by RBI and fiscal policies. The startup ecosystem sees the trade deals as a catalyst for global expansion, increased VC interest, and job creation, while AI is viewed as a commoditising technology whose cost decline will drive wider adoption across sectors. Industry leaders cite examples of tech‑enabled manufacturing at Maruti Suzuki and the importance of ecosystem collaboration with startups to sustain India’s rise as a global manufacturing and innovation hub.

SBI Records Record Quarterly Profit and Outlines Growth Strategy for FY27

SBIN

State Bank of India posted its highest-ever quarterly net profit, driven by a 9% rise in net interest income, strong retail deposit growth, and low credit costs (0.29%). Chairman C.S. Setty highlighted holistic growth across retail, agriculture, MSME, and corporate segments, with credit growth exceeding 15% and corporate credit up 13.5%. The bank raised its credit growth guidance for FY27 to 13‑15% (from 12‑14%) and aims for 9‑10% deposit growth, focusing on low‑cost retail and CASA balances. SBI expects continued double‑digit corporate lending, 18‑20% MSME growth, stable NIM around 3%, and credit costs around 50 bps, signaling confidence in sustaining momentum despite macro‑economic challenges.

Cement Sector Poised for Strong Q4FY26 Performance

GMBREW

The editor recaps the previous quarter’s solid volume growth (single‑digit to early double‑digit) and higher blended EBIT YoY, despite a sequential dip due to price dynamics. Companies reported cost‑saving initiatives (₹150‑₹200 per tonne) and robust demand, aided by seasonal factors. Prices have rebounded after an eight‑month dip, with a 2% hike already absorbed and further increases of ₹10‑₹15 per bag anticipated, pushing bag prices to around ₹340. Demand is expected to sustain low double‑digit growth into January and beyond. Analysts forecast a ₹250 per tonne improvement in gross margins from Q3 to Q4, suggesting a potential earnings surprise. Historically, small‑mid caps benefit more from sector upturns, while large caps lead in broader weeks, prompting a look at which segment will lead the cement rally in Q4.

JIOFIN

RBI Holds Repo Rate Steady, Signals Caution Amid Uncertainty on Growth, Inflation and Liquidity

SBIN

The Monetary Policy Committee kept the repo rate unchanged at 5.25% and maintained a neutral stance, offering no guidance on the rupee or short‑term liquidity despite excess cash in the system. Experts highlighted a likely “long pause” on rate moves, but emphasized wide uncertainty because of pending revisions to the CPI and GDP series and global headwinds. Bond yields rose as markets expected more open‑market operations or VRR measures that were not announced. RBI’s pre‑emptive liquidity support was noted, though core durable liquidity remains high, limiting further OMOs. Currency outlook hinges on the impact of trade deals and a projected balance‑of‑payments surplus, with limited forward guidance to avoid market distortion. Bankers discussed the strain on LCR/LDR ratios, the absence of a variable‑rate reverse repo, and the need for retail deposit mobilisation. The RBI’s new consumer‑protection scheme for fraud losses up to ₹25,000 was welcomed as a cost‑neutral safeguard. Overall, the policy was described as cautious, status‑quo‑ist, and focused on maintaining low rates while monitoring liquidity, growth, and inflation dynamics.
