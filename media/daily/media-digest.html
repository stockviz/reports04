TAJGVK CHALET

Live Nation’s Antitrust Trial Nears Verdict

BARBEQUE

In a Manhattan federal court, attorneys for 34 states argue that Live Nation and its ticketing arm Ticketmaster monopolize the concert market, citing 86% control of concert tickets and 73% including sports events, and accuse them of inflating prices. Live Nation’s counsel counters that the company faces robust competition, that its size reflects success, not illegality, and that any internal misconduct was isolated. The federal government had previously settled a related case, granting concessions on ticket sales at Live Nation venues. The jury will deliberate after closing arguments, needing only a preponderance of evidence to find antitrust violations

UBS Refuses to Release Privileged Nazi-Account Files After Court Ruling

BCG MANCREDIT

UBS Group AG announced it will not provide the privileged documents related to Credit Suisse’s handling of Nazi‑linked accounts to investigator Neil Barofsky, following a New York judge’s decision that the bank could not be shielded from future lawsuits. The bank, which inherited the case after acquiring Credit Suisse in 2023, cites the 1999 settlement that protected Swiss banks from additional liability. While UBS has allowed access to 16.5 million documents, it is withholding less than 0.1% of the files, arguing they remain protected. The move has drawn criticism from U.S. senators and could shift the dispute from the courts to the public and political arena
