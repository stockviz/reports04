Airlines Warn of Collapse Over Soaring ATF Costs

SPICEJET TENNIND INDIGO

Indian carriers, led by the Federation of Indian Airlines, have issued an SOS to the government citing unsustainable jet fuel (ATF) pricing. They highlight a 73 ₹/L increase for international flights, with ATF now accounting for 55‑60% of operating costs, up from 30‑40%. The spike is attributed to a surge in crack spreads—from the usual $11‑$18 to over $130 per barrel—despite Brent crude rising only 64%. Airlines demand reinstating the crack‑band mechanism (capping spreads at $12‑$22) and a temporary suspension of the 11% ATF excise duty. They warn that continued pricing pressure could force groundings, flight cancellations, and broader disruption to Indian aviation
