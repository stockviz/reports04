CHEMPLASTS

TVS Supply Chain Settles ZTE Debt Dispute, Withdraws Insolvency Appeal

SHYAMTEL TVSSCS

TVS Supply Chain Solutions resolved its Rs 4.27 crore debt dispute with ZTE Telecom India, leading its counsel to inform the NCLAT of the settlement and request withdrawal of its appeal against the NCLAT’s order rejecting insolvency proceedings. The NCLAT bench approved the withdrawal. The dispute, originating from invoices under two Master Service Agreements dating from 2012‑2019, involved alleged non‑payment, partial payments, and disputed invoice amounts, with ZTE raising audit queries and claiming discrepancies. The NCLT had earlier dismissed TVS’s insolvency petition, noting the pre‑existing reconciliation and audit objections, and that the debt was never admitted by ZTE. The settlement ends the litigation

HDFC, ICICI & Yes Bank Q4 Earnings: Strong Profits, Stable Performance, and Strategic Outlook

ICICIBANK YESBANK HDFCBANK

ICICI Bank reported an 8.5% year-on-year rise in net profit to ₹12,949 crore, driven by an 89% drop in provisions and improved asset quality, with gross NPA falling to 1.40%. HDFC Bank’s results were in line with expectations, posting a 9.1% profit growth and 3.2% rise in net interest income (NII) to ₹33,738 crore, supported by strong deposit and loan growth. Asset quality remained robust, with gross NPA at 1.15%, and a final dividend of ₹13 per share declared. Yes Bank delivered a standout performance with a 45% surge in quarterly profits, 16% NII growth, and improved margins at 2.7%, guided by cost optimization and better asset quality (GNPA at 1.3%). The bank, now led by new MD & CEO Vinay Tonse, targets NIMs of 3.25–3.5% in 2–3 years with SMBC collaboration and aims for double-digit retail growth. Market cap increased by over ₹14 lakh crore as sectoral indices rose. HDFC Bank’s leadership emphasized resilience, technology investments—including AI and digital security—and stable return on assets at 1.9%, despite margin pressure (3.38%). Management clarified governance issues, including ongoing legal reviews linked to the Dubai branch and former chairman’s exit, but affirmed strong balance sheets and growth momentum. Loan-deposit ratios are no longer a constraint, and HDFC aims for industry-leading deposit and credit growth. Sashidhar Jagdishan made a rare appearance on the earnings call, affirming confidence in sustainable, quality growth and future return enhancement

Renault Rolls Out Dual-Platform Strategy in India for CNG, Hybrids and EVs

BCG

Renault India will use two new platforms—RGEP for sub‑10 lakh vehicles with options like factory‑fitted CNG and upgraded Kiger/Triber models, and RGMP for larger (4.0‑4.7 m) vehicles offering multi‑energy flexibility, advanced digital architecture, and full electrification. The Duster SUV is the first RGMP product; a sub‑4‑m SUV called Bridger will follow, launching with a 1.2‑L turbo engine and later a fully electric version. Renault targets a 5% market share and a seven‑model portfolio by 2030, with India’s engineering centre becoming a hub for software and continuous model upgrades

Doctor‑led AI platform iLive Connect launches in Chennai for 24/7 remote health monitoring

APOLLOHOSP KOVAI AHCL NGIL

A doctor‑led, AI‑enabled ecosystem called iLive Connect was launched in Chennai, offering continuous real‑time monitoring of vital signs via a wireless biosensor patch and wristband, with data reviewed 24 × 7 by a remote team of around 40 physicians. Aimed at preventing escalation of chronic conditions and sudden cardiac events, the platform provides multi‑day cardiac screening, post‑discharge surveillance, and management of diabetes, hypertension, COPD, asthma, and geriatric care. After Chennai, it will roll out across other southern Indian states, leveraging continuous data to enable early risk detection and reduce readmissions

OpenAI’s Executive Shake‑up and Science Division Shutdown

In a single day on 17 April 2026, OpenAI saw three senior leaders depart: former CPO‑turned‑science head Kevin Weil, CTO for enterprise applications Srinivas Narayanan, and Sora product lead Bill Peebles. Their exits coincided with the dissolution of “OpenAI for Science,” a high‑profile initiative that had launched the Prism research workspace and showcased GPT‑5.2’s strong scientific benchmark performance. The Prism team and its resources have been folded into Codex, OpenAI’s coding platform, as the company pivots to make Codex an “everything app.” This consolidation reflects a broader strategy to simplify product offerings ahead of a planned 2026 IPO and to focus on commercially viable tools. The departures add to a recent pattern of turnover among top executives, raising questions about continuity for researchers and developers relying on OpenAI’s APIs
