AI Disruption in Indian IT: Risks, Opportunities, and Outlook

INFY OFSS COFORGE TCS

The Indian IT sector, valued at about $280 bn, is split into services (≈50%), R&D (≈20%), BPO (≈20%) and hardware/platforms (≈10%). AI threatens both IT services and BPO through higher automation‑driven deflation, raising typical renewal discounts from 15‑20% to 20‑30% and shortening contract cycles, potentially pushing annual revenue deflation to 6‑7%. However, AI also creates new work in implementing, managing, and integrating AI‑driven SaaS bots and platforms, shifting spend from maintenance (≈70%) toward innovation (potentially rising to 40‑45%). Analysts outline three growth scenarios:
- Bear: Near‑zero or negative growth, EPS flat/declining, IT stocks trading at 10‑12 × earnings.
- Base: Recovery to 5‑8 % revenue growth, multiples returning to early‑2020s levels.
- Bull: IT firms evolve into platform/value‑creator models, achieving 30‑× multiples.

Valuation gaps with US peers like Cognizant have narrowed (now ~15‑20% premium versus ~30‑40% earlier) due to higher dividend and cash‑flow yields (~5%). Top stock picks: large‑cap Infosys and Cognizant; mid‑cap Coforge; small‑cap ECL (a BPO firm with low voice exposure). While AI may reduce the cost‑advantage of GCCs, they remain strategic for scale talent and AI implementation. Overall, the sector is likely to be an AI beneficiary in the medium‑to‑long term, despite near‑term revenue pressure
