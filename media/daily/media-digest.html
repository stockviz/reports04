Quality Walls India IPO: Growth, Distribution, and Margin Outlook

KWIL QPOWER

Deputy MD Chitrang Goyal and CFO Prashant Premarashka highlighted a three‑pillar growth strategy: expanding cabinet‑based distribution beyond metros into tier‑2/3/4 cities, launching bold, price‑segmented innovations, and investing in cold‑chain infrastructure (cabinets, cold rooms, factories). They note India’s ice‑cream penetration is low (≈0.5‑0.6 L per capita vs 5‑13 L in western markets) and the product is present in only ~1 million of 13 million FMCG outlets, leaving huge headroom.

The company aims to improve ROC by leveraging operating leverage as volume scales, expecting double‑digit revenue growth and EBITDA margin expansion through cost‑down initiatives (procurement synergies, supply‑chain efficiencies). GST cuts (18%→5%) should boost affordability. Capital intensity remains high; capex will be deployed to reach critical mass in distribution and production, with free cash flow tied to the pace of investment.

Commodity volatility will be managed via long‑term contracts, hedging, and a broad pricing ladder (₹10‑₹100). Competition is seen as an opportunity to grow the overall category rather than just share, focusing on access, repeat purchase, and innovation (e.g., Magnum, Coneto). Post‑de‑merger operational support from HOS will continue for 18‑24 months under a PSA, after which an independent board will drive decisions.

Key metrics for investors: top‑line volume growth, expansion of cabinet footprint, EBITDA margin trajectory, and ROC improvement, with the primary focus on sustained growth followed by profitability
