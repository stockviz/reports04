India’s GLP‑1 Pen Manufacturing Hub: Process, Capacity, and Market Outlook

DRREDDY ONESOURCE

The video tours OneSource’s sterile facility that fills, inspects, and assembles semaglutide (GLP‑1) cartridges into injectable pens. Workers wear PPE and operate isolator‑based, fully automated filling lines that can produce 150‑200 cartridges per minute, up to 55 000 per day (potentially 120‑130 000 with larger batches). OneSource serves major pharma customers (e.g., Dr. Reddy’s) and prepares for launches in emerging markets as patents expire and generics increase demand, anticipating a huge global patient pool (1.2‑1.5 billion) for GLP‑1 therapies

Lufthansa Crew Strike Causes Massive Flight Cancellations

A one‑day strike by the UFO union of Lufthansa and Lufthansa CityLine cabin crew on Friday is set to cancel over 520 flights, affecting about 90,000 passengers returning from Easter holidays. Lufthansa plans to operate roughly a third of its scheduled flights and mitigate disruptions with other group airlines and larger aircraft, aiming to resume normal operations by Saturday. The strike follows stalled collective bargaining talks, with unions accusing Lufthansa of failing to present a negotiable offer. Concurrently, Lufthansa CityLine reached a separate agreement with Ver.di covering employment terms through 2029, including salary hikes of 20‑35% for crew. The labor unrest adds to broader challenges for the airline, including volatile fuel prices and market competition

Oracle’s AI‑Driven Expansion Triggers Cash Crunch and Massive Layoffs

BCG OFSS

Oracle’s aggressive push into AI infrastructure—spurred by a $300 billion OpenAI deal and massive new data‑center builds—has ballooned capital expenditures from $6.9 billion in FY24 to a projected $50 billion in FY26. The company now carries $105‑124 billion of debt, $248 billion of off‑balance‑sheet data‑center leases, and negative free cash flow of $24.7 billion. To curb the resulting liquidity strain, Oracle is raising its restructuring budget to $2.1 billion and cutting up to 30,000 jobs (≈19% of its workforce), including 10‑12 k roles in India. While OCI revenue is soaring (84% YoY to $4.9 billion) and contracted revenue hit $553 billion, most of it is deferred, margins are lower than peers, and the heavy reliance on a single, unprofitable customer—OpenAI—creates a mismatch between long‑term lease commitments and five‑year contract horizons, amplifying financial risk
