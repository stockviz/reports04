Power Grid’s Equity Cap Raised to Boost Transmission & Renewable Goals

POWERGRID

The cabinet approved increasing Power Grid Corp.’s equity investment ceiling from Rs 5,000 cr to Rs 7,500 cr. This higher limit lets the company bid for larger transmission projects, accelerate grid expansion, and fund high‑voltage DC lines, thereby speeding renewable power evacuation and supporting India’s 500 GW non‑fossil energy target while mitigating risk to public funds

AI Disruption and Indian IT: Insights from Emkay Global’s Nirav Sheth

EMKAY

Nirav Sheth argues that AI will fundamentally reshape the Indian IT services sector, shifting business models from OPEX to CAPEX and compressing existing profit pools as companies share revenue with large LLM providers. He believes the sector is being underestimated and expects a gradual, non‑linear disruption across multiple industries (legal, banking, education, travel, etc.) over the next decade. While cautious about exact timing and valuation, Sheth sees no immediate sharp pullback, citing cyclical buying opportunities and the need to monitor sector‑specific impacts, including tariff dynamics affecting exports and competition. He emphasizes that AI’s profit pool will largely flow to global tech giants, leaving Indian firms to adapt or risk losing market share

Macquarie Keeps Bearish Outlook on Swiggy and Zomato Valuations

SWIGGY ZOMATO

Macquarie maintains an underperform rating on Swiggy and Zomato, arguing that their food‑delivery businesses are over‑valued by 30‑35% compared with consensus estimates. It points to modest net take‑rates (Swiggy trailing Zomato by ~150 bps) and thin margins of 5‑6% as insufficient to justify current market caps of $12‑14 bn. With no sign of take‑rate deflation in the duopoly, Macquarie expects continued pressure on the stocks and a lower fair‑value target

Indian Govt to Offload 4% IRFC Stake via OFS

IRFC

The government will sell about 4% of its 86.36% holding in Indian Railway Finance Corporation through an Offer for Sale—2% as a base issue and 2% via a greenshoe. The OFS opens Wednesday for non‑retail investors and Thursday for retail, with a floor price set at 104% of the previous close (≈4.5% discount). The sale aims to meet minimum public shareholding norms and raise roughly ₹5,600–6,000 crore. Analysts note the stock trades at a premium to its ₹43 book value (₹109 price) and suggest other NBFCs may offer more attractive returns
