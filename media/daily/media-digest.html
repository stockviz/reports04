Impact of CERC Draft Market‑Coupling Norms on Indian Power Exchanges

5PAISA IONEXCHANG IEX

The CERC’s draft power‑market coupling framework, open for comments until 16 May, aims to introduce uniform price discovery across day‑ahead and real‑time markets by FY 27, eliminating arbitrage that currently concentrates liquidity at IEX. Under the new regime, the Grid Controller of India will act as the market‑coupling operator, setting a single price for all three exchanges (IEX, PXI, SPX), while the exchanges become bid aggregators. This shift could compress IEX’s fee‑based revenue, especially as its market‑share advantage may erode and the regulator proposes cutting the transaction‑fee cap from 2 paisa to 1.5 paisa. Analysts note that earnings per share could become volatile, depending on how market share and pricing settle, but long‑term fundamentals remain positive given India’s growing power demand, renewable integration, and storage expansion. Exchanges anticipate defending their fee structures in the public‑feedback process and expect the broader market growth to offset some near‑term revenue pressures
