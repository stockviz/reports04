NCLAT Upholds Separate Insolvency Processes for Videocon Industries and Videocon Oil Ventures

BMWVENTLTD AHL BLUESTARCO BPCL ATHERENERG SBIN OIL AARON CHEVIOT

The National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) set aside an earlier NCLT order that had sought to club the insolvency cases of Videocon Industries Ltd (VIL) and Videocon Oil Ventures Ltd (VOVL). It affirmed that the creditors deliberately pursued independent CIRPs because VIL (consumer electronics) and VOVL (oil‑related) operate in distinct sectors requiring specialised buyers. The tribunal noted that a single entity could not effectively revive both businesses and that the Committee of Creditors’ decision should not be interfered with. Consequently, BPRL exercised its right of first refusal to acquire VOVL, a transaction approved by the NCLT, while VIL’s CIRP remains pending. The order also rejected former chairman V.N. Dhoot’s attempts to consolidate the cases or exclude foreign oil‑gas assets, emphasizing the commercial wisdom of keeping the processes separate

Venezuela Releases Draft Oil Law Regulations to Attract Foreign Investment

The Venezuelan government has circulated a 63‑page draft outlining technical, operational, fiscal and control rules for private companies in oil and gas, ending PDVSA’s monopoly on refining, upgrading and trading. The draft, part of a new hydrocarbons law enacted in January, replaces the 1943 oil law and 1969 regulations, and introduces clauses on domestic utilization, unitization, data reversion, greenhouse‑gas effects, and mandatory enhanced recovery. A model contract has also been shared with interested firms. The move follows U.S. sanctions relief and aims to open the sector to foreign investment and integrate Venezuela’s economy into the global financial system
