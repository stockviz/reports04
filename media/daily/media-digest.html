Aditya Birla Health Leverages Wellness Incentives to Cut Claims Ratios and Boost Growth

ABCAPITAL

The insurer is positioning itself as a health‑behaviour influencer, rewarding policyholders for active, healthy lifestyles through its Active Health app and wellness‑first products. By nudging low‑risk customers to stay healthy and intervening with high‑risk ones, it has reduced loss ratios (94% for moderately active, 75% for highly active versus 100% for inactive) and improved persistence by over 11%. This strategy, coupled with GST removal on policies, has driven a 39% YoY rise in gross written premium to ₹6,855 cr, a market‑share gain to 13.7%, and a 13% rise in net profit to ₹3,764 cr in FY26. Around 40% of policyholders now engage with the insurer on health, and 11% receive rewards for good health, up from 5‑6% two years ago. The wellness model is also being extended to the group‑health segment, which accounts for 45% of business, with flexible, inflation‑proof plans like Activ Yuva offering premium refunds and comprehensive benefits. The company asserts that the investment in wellness incentives will continue to lower claims and sustain profitability

Legal Challenge Over 1980s Share Transfer Fuels Tension Ahead of Tata Trusts Meeting

NSIL

A third legal notice has been sent to the Sir Ratan Tata Trust (SRTT) alleging that a 1989 transfer of 833 Tata Sons shares—originally donated by SRTT to the Navajbai Ratan Tata Trust and then to the late Naval H. Tata—was performed at an undervalue, violating fiduciary duties, the Companies Act, and the trust’s objectives. The notice, filed by Sunil Patilkhede through lawyer Katyayani Agrawal, accuses the six SRTT trustees (including Noel Tata, Jimmy Tata, Jehangir H. C. Jehnagir, and others) of a void transaction and gives them 15 days to remedy the alleged loss. This follows earlier complaints about trustee composition and a court rejection of an adjournment request. The dispute coincides with a postponed Tata Trusts meeting on 16 May, where the reappointment of TVS chairman emeritus Venu Srinivasan as a Tata Trust nominee on the Tata Sons board will be decided. The case underscores growing internal tensions within the philanthropic entities that control a majority stake in Tata Sons

Ventive Hospitality Targets ₹2,000 cr Luxury Expansion Over Five Years

5PAISA JINDWORLD CHALET HILTON VENTIVE ABMINTLLTD EMAMIREAL

Blackstone‑backed Ventive plans to add 2,000 rooms (≈2,000 keys) through greenfield projects and acquisitions, focusing on luxury branded residences and villas. The firm, which operates 13 assets (mostly luxury) across India and the Maldives, aims to develop new properties like a Soho House in Delhi, Ritz‑Carlton Bangalore, AC Hotels by Marriott Bengaluru, multiple Moxy hotels, and a Marriott in Varanasi, while evaluating leisure acquisitions in Goa, Udaipur and Sri Lanka. Backed by Blackstone and Panchshil Realty, Ventive expects to fund growth largely from internal cash flows, targeting >12% yield on capital and generating ~₹500 cr annual cash flow. It projects 1,100 keys already under development, including a dual‑branded JW Marriott‑Moxy project in Navi Mumbai, and sees strong demand outpacing supply, especially in large cities with land constraints. The company also eyes overseas luxury markets, notably Sri Lanka and the Maldives, where its assets deliver high EBITDA multiples. Despite geopolitical and economic headwinds, Ventive remains confident of a prolonged upcycle in India’s luxury hospitality sector

Rural Tractor and Entry‑Level Motorcycle Sales Face Slowdown Amid Sub‑Normal Monsoon

VSTTILLERS HEROMOTOCO BAJAJ-AUTO M&M ESCORTS MARUTI HYUNDAI

India’s major tractor manufacturers Mahindra & Mahindra and Escorts Kubota project muted or declining FY27 sales, citing a weaker rural economy due to a below‑average monsoon, high base effects, reduced subsidies and rising vehicle prices. While Mahindra sees pockets of growth in states like Uttar Pradesh, overall growth will trail the 20% rise seen last year. Entry‑level two‑wheelers at Bajaj Auto also show slower growth, reflecting broader rural demand weakness, though other automakers such as Hero, Maruti Suzuki and Hyundai have not flagged stress. Analysts expect the slowdown to hit tractor makers hardest and could spill over into other rural‑driven sectors

Actis Restarts Sale of Nxt‑Infra Trust, Hires Ambit for Broader Buyer Outreach

HILINFRA JPASSOCIAT SIL ATHERENERG WELENT CAPTRUST GAYAHWS HDFCAMC

Actis LLP is reviving its mandate to sell all or part of its 86% stake in the listed Indian road‑asset InvIT Nxt‑Infra Trust after a six‑month lull, appointing advisory firm Ambit to widen the buyer pool beyond traditional private‑equity and pension investors. The InvIT, launched in July 2024 with a market cap of ~₹2,800 cr, holds six SPVs covering 2,000+ lane‑km of toll and annuity roads across northern and western India. While the exact valuation Actis seeks is undisclosed, the process is moving into due diligence, with the final stake retained by Actis to depend on investor interest and pricing. The sale reflects strong demand for Indian road assets, buoyed by SEBI’s regulatory enhancements that have made InvITs a preferred vehicle for monetising operational infrastructure and recycling capital into new projects. Recent comparable deals include Macquarie’s $1.7 bn sale to Vinci and KKR’s consolidation under Vertis. Actis is also navigating other stake sales, such as its renewable platform BluPine Energy
