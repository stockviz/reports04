PwC India Considers Global Consulting Standardisation

3MINDIA ABMINTLLTD TCNSBRANDS

PwC India is evaluating a major overhaul of its consulting operations to align with a worldwide initiative aimed at reducing the fragmented, country‑specific approach that has characterized the Big Four’s consulting arms. Senior partners say early talks focus on delivering uniform services, methodologies, and technology platforms across regions, enabling faster cross‑border collaboration and specialist access. The move follows PwC UK’s merger of its risk and consulting businesses and seeks to create a Global Integrated Consulting Model (GICM) while keeping audit functions separate due to regulatory constraints. The restructuring comes amid a slowdown in deal activity in India and growing AI pressures on traditional consulting roles, with implementation dependent on the firm’s roughly 1,000 partners

Overseas Acquisitions Drive Indian Pharma Shift Up the Value Chain

BIOCON TCNSBRANDS ZYDUSLIFE LUPIN NATCOPHARM APLLTD AUROPHARMA SUNPHARMA ANTHEM AHL

Indian drugmakers are increasingly pursuing outbound M&A to move beyond generics, targeting innovative and specialty assets abroad. While deals remain modest compared to global biotech megadeals, activity is rising, with Sun Pharma eyeing a $12 bn purchase of Organon and Zydus evaluating Ardelyx for $2.2‑2.5 bn. Recent mid‑size acquisitions include Intas’s $558 m Udenyca purchase, Natco’s $226 m stake in Adcock Ingram, and Lupin’s €190 m VISUfarma deal. Drivers are global uncertainties, generics price erosion, and a shift toward biologics, prompting firms to seek higher‑margin markets in the US, Europe, Latin America, and South Africa, and to explore licensing with China. Strong balance sheets provide financing capacity, but execution risk remains, as seen in mixed reactions to past large deals like Biocon‑Viatris. The next 4‑5 years could see $20 bn of outbound M&A, focusing on capability‑led, targeted acquisitions rather than large‑scale transformations

Bira 91 Founder Ankur Jain to Exit as Investors Inject Fresh Capital

5PAISA TIGERLOGS VBL AXISBANK DEVYANI AHL

B9 Beverages, the maker of Bira 91, is undergoing a leadership overhaul with founder‑CEO Ankur Jain expected to resign this week amid severe cash crunch, salary delays and looming insolvency. Investors and lenders are preparing a rescue plan that includes a ₹400‑crore infusion at a steep valuation cut (potentially another ₹100 crore later) to settle liabilities, restart production and bring in a professional management team. The company, once valued at $625 million, has seen revenue drop to ₹638.5 crore in FY24 and losses widen to ₹748.8 crore, with operations halted since September 2024 and mounting tax and vendor pressures. Existing shareholders, including Kirin (20.3% stake) and Jain’s family (17.8%), are part of the restructuring, while an extraordinary general meeting is being planned to formalize the changes

EU Approval Delays Threaten Timeline of Tata Motors’ $4.4 bn Iveco Acquisition

TATAMOTORS

Tata Motors’ €4.4 billion purchase of Iveco is lagging behind schedule as approvals from the European Central Bank and the European Commission remain pending, risking a push beyond the original April‑June closing target. While most regulatory clearances have been obtained, the remaining ones are being actively pursued. The deal, announced in July 2025, would create a global commercial‑vehicle group with >540,000 units sold and $25 bn revenue, making Europe its largest market. Analysts note the timing coincides with concerns over Iveco’s weakened cash flow, but many still view the acquisition as value‑accretive and beneficial for Tata Motors’ global expansion

Luggage makers pivot to smaller, lower‑priced items as muted travel drags suitcase sales

VIPCLOTHNG SAFARI

Travel‑related war, rising fuel‑linked material costs and higher airfares have softened India’s seasonal surge in suitcase demand, prompting brands to lean on backpacks, accessories and other small‑ticket products. While the April‑June quarter normally drives 40% of annual sales, domestic air traffic growth has stalled and raw‑material inflation squeezes margins. Companies such as Swiss Military, Assembly Luggage and Nasher Miles are absorbing costs, trimming inventory and expanding into high‑frequency categories like backpacks, packing cubes and passport covers, which now account for a growing share of the market. The sector still sees long‑term growth potential (projected to reach ₹267 bn by 2028), but short‑term outlook remains challenged by geopolitical tensions, higher travel expenses and price‑sensitive consumers

Thoma Bravo Nears Deal to Hand Medallia to Creditors

DELTACORP 5PAISA APOLLOPIPE

Private equity firm Thoma Bravo is close to an agreement to transfer customer‑experience software company Medallia to its lenders, wiping out roughly $5.1 billion in equity for Thoma Bravo and co‑investors. Medallia, acquired for $6.4 billion in 2021, is weighed down by about $3 billion of debt owed to Blackstone, KKR, Apollo and Antares Capital.
