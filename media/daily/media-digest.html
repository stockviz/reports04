Auto PLI disbursements lag behind targets as 2028 deadline looms

TATAMOTORS OLAELEC M&M EICHERMOT ATHERENERG KIRLOSBROS

India’s ₹25,938‑crore auto Production‑Linked Incentive scheme has released just ₹2,321 crore—under 10 % of the allocation—leaving more than ₹23,000 crore unclaimed with less than two years to the March 2028 deadline. Recent payouts were driven by a ₹2,000 crore release for FY‑2024/25 to boost EV and advanced tech manufacturing, but companies struggle to meet the 50 % domestic value‑addition (DVA) rule, especially for imported battery cells, motors and power electronics. Only four OEMs claimed ₹322 crore in the first year; participation has risen (eight champion OEMs and ten component makers in FY‑25), yet disbursements remain low. Start‑ups such as Euler Motors and River Mobility argue that eligibility thresholds (₹10,000 crore global revenue, ₹3,000 crore fixed‑asset investment) favour large incumbents and exclude growth‑stage EV firms. A parliamentary panel warns of a “2028 cliff” where firms must shift to high‑volume, localized production or forfeit incentives. The FY‑26 allocation was cut to ₹2,091.26 crore from ₹2,818.85 crore, reflecting revised claim forecasts tied to FY‑25 DVA‑certified sales
