Varaha Secures $45 M Series B to Accelerate Multi‑Path Carbon Removal Projects

5PAISA

India’s AI Surge: From Consumer to Global Superpower

India is rapidly transitioning from a major AI consumer to a leading AI creator, driven by strong policy support, indigenous model development (e.g., SERVUM), and massive investment in data centers and compute infrastructure. The country aims for AI sovereignty, targeting 10‑15% of the projected $20 trillion global AI value addition over the next decade. Initiatives include tax holidays for foreign firms using Indian data centers, expanded GPU capacity, and incentives for semiconductor production. Indigenous large‑language models trained on Indian languages and cultural contexts are addressing bias and delivering sector‑specific solutions in finance, healthcare, agriculture, wildlife conservation, and child protection. While challenges remain—global geopolitical influence, job displacement, and cyber‑risk—India is implementing rapid deep‑fake takedowns, upskilling programs, and fostering a vibrant AI startup ecosystem. Collectively, these efforts position India as an emerging AI middle power poised to rival the US and China in the coming years

AI Threatens Indian IT Firms, Market Reacts with Sharp Sell‑off

COFORGE PERSISTENT TCS INFY

Indian IT stocks plunged as fears of AI‑driven disruption sparked a major sell‑off, with the Nifty IT index falling over 8% in a week—the steepest drop in nine months. Analysts noted that unlike past tech cycles, incumbents like TCS and Infosys now face higher “incumbency risk” due to their size, and AI is targeting their core operations and business models rather than just infrastructure. While Citi warns that AI could erode legacy revenues, JPMorgan sees deep‑value buying opportunities if free cash flow and dividend yields remain attractive, outlining three growth scenarios ranging from modest 2‑3% growth to a zero‑growth bearish case that could shave more than 30% off valuations. The debate centers on whether the current downturn is a buying chance or a sign of a fundamentally altered industry landscape

OmniCom’s Bold Re‑Build: Scaling Up in India While Preserving Talent and Innovation

PRESSMN

In a candid Storyboard 18 interview, Aditya Kanthi, President & MD of Omnicom Advertising India, explains the group’s strategy of expanding to a 3,000‑person operation that balances scale, complexity, and talent retention amid industry consolidation. He stresses the importance of preserving iconic agency brands (TBWA, Lintas, BBDO, McCann, Mudra, etc.) and leveraging a “Connected Capability Hub” that lets clients plug in specialized services while maintaining flexibility. Kanthi outlines recent restructuring focused on non‑client functions, the aim to protect client‑facing teams, and the limited future of layoffs. He discusses AI as an enabler that will automate lower‑funnel tasks but won’t replace creative talent, and previews the upcoming Omni AI platform that combines data, cultural insights, and commerce capabilities. Finally, he envisions the integration’s success as teams delivering standout work within six months, marking a shift from agency shrinkage to renewed growth in the Indian market
