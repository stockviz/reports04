Cleartrip Targets Operational Break‑Even by FY27 as It Slashes Discount Spending

EIFFL BCG

Cleartrip, owned by Flipkart Group, aims to become operationally neutral by the end of FY27, moving away from heavy discount reliance. In FY25, it spent ₹608 cr on discounts versus ₹169 cr revenue, alongside ₹102 cr on advertising and ₹129 cr on commissions, resulting in a ₹651 cr net loss. The company is confident it can meet the new profitability goal

Suven Life Sciences’ High‑Stakes CNS Drug Push

APLLTD ADVENTHTL NOVARTIND SUVEN PRSMJOHNSN

Suven Life Sciences, after raising ₹858 crore and setting up a Singapore subsidiary, is betting on its CNS pipeline—lead candidate Masupirdine for Alzheimer’s agitation in Phase 3, plus narcolepsy and other Phase 2 candidates—to deliver a breakthrough by early 2027‑28. Founder‑CEO Venkat Jasti, who sold the CDMO arm for ₹6,313 crore to fund discovery, emphasizes the high risk (≈0.5% success) of CNS trials and the need to meet or exceed existing benchmarks to attract big‑pharma partners. Despite a 117% stock surge, the firm still faces heavy R&D losses and limited marketing expertise, hoping one success will shift investor sentiment toward Indian biotech

Turtlemint aims for FY27 profitability despite seasonal dips

Turtlemint Fintech Solutions Ltd posted its first quarterly profit—adjusted EBITDA of ₹2.9 cr and PAT of ₹3 cr—for the March quarter, after a FY‑wide loss of ₹184 cr. CEO Dhirendra Mahyavanshi expects FY27 to be profit‑after‑tax positive, acknowledging that some quarters may still show losses due to insurance seasonality. Revenue rose 42% YoY to ₹357 cr, and corporate overheads fell from ~50% of revenue in FY23 to ~23% now, with a target of 6‑7% by 2030. Operating leverage, stable acquisition costs, and high free‑cash‑flow conversion (97‑98% of EBITDA) underpin the outlook. The company’s cash fell to ₹127.6 cr, but management claims an “infinite runway.” Turtlemint, backed by several venture funds, raised ~$190 mn to date and recently listed at a >10% discount, planning an IPO of ₹883 cr at roughly half its 2022 valuation

Skyroot’s Patient Path to Success: A Private Indian Rocket’s First Orbital Launch

HAL GICHSGFIN BMWVENTLTD UNIMECH

Skyroot Aerospace, India’s only space unicorn, finally achieved a successful orbital launch of its lightweight Vikram‑1 rocket after delaying its timeline by two years to focus on reliability over speed. Co‑founder and CEO Pawan Kumar Chandana emphasized a “fixated on not failing” approach, with the team working long hours and learning from unknowns. The launch placed five satellites into a 450 km low‑Earth orbit, marking the first private orbital launch from Indian soil. The company plans a second Vikram‑1 flight by year‑end and a Vikram‑2 launch with liquid propulsion by next year, while targeting multiple commercial missions. Skyroot has raised about $160 million, with major investors including Singapore’s GIC and Greenko, but has no immediate fundraising plans. The success boosts confidence in India’s private space sector, attracting global investors and opening opportunities for government contracts and international launch services, though competition from ISRO’s SSLV and rivals like Agnikul Cosmos remains fierce

Toyota Mobility Foundation Supports Varanasi Mobility Challenge

The Toyota Mobility Foundation (TMF) launched a global challenge to address urban mobility issues, selecting Detroit, Venice, and Varanasi as finalists. TMF allocated $9 million total, with $3 million for Varanasi, funding innovators at each stage and awarding winners enough resources to pilot solutions for at least a year. After beta testing, two winners and two honorable mentions were chosen, and TMF emphasized sustainable, business‑model‑driven approaches that can continue beyond the funding period, with ongoing guidance but ultimately self‑sufficiency. TMF’s involvement reflects Toyota’s broader goal of enhancing quality of life, not merely a CSR initiative
