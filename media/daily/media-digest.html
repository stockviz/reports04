Nalco’s Alumina Exports to West Asia Hit by Geopolitical Tensions

NATIONALUM

National Aluminium Company (Nalco) says the West Asian market, accounting for 40‑50% of its alumina shipments, has been disrupted by regional tensions, leading to reduced smelter capacity and lower spot prices (now $305‑310/tonne). The company is shifting sales to markets like Indonesia, but overall revenues fell to ₹5,012.82 crore and net profit dropped 16.6% to ₹1,722.44 crore in Q4 FY26, reflecting lower export volumes (13.08 lakh tonnes out of 23 lakh tonnes produced) and higher expenses. The broader aluminium value chain faces divergent impacts: oversupplied alumina markets see price declines, while refined aluminium prices rise due to production shutdowns and shipping disruptions

Aramco CEO Warns Lost Oil Will Delay Market Recovery

Saudi Aramco’s chief, Amin Nasser, said that about 1 billion barrels of oil have been lost in the past two months due to Iran’s blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, slowing the oil market’s rebound even if shipping resumes. He emphasized keeping energy flowing, noting the company’s use of its East‑West pipeline to bypass Hormuz as a “critical lifeline.” Nasser highlighted that underinvestment and low inventories exacerbate the strain, and reaffirmed Asia’s importance as a demand hub

NTPC readies first nuclear feasibility study for DAE approval

NTPC

Power giant NTPC is set to submit its inaugural nuclear feasibility study to the Department of Atomic Energy, aiming to secure approval for its first standalone nuclear project and achieve a target of at least 2 GW by 2032. The company has completed a study in one state, is finalizing studies in two others, and has received Bihar’s nod to assess a project in Banka district. NTPC plans to develop up to 30 GW of nuclear capacity across 14 states, involving roughly Rs 15,000‑20,000 crore per GW, with typical requirements of 5 L water per unit and a 1 km exclusion zone. It already co‑develops a 4×700 MW plant in Rajasthan with NPCIL, where it holds a 49 % stake

Banks Push to Ban Stablecoin Rewards as Senators Seek Compromise on CLARITY Act

Banking lobby groups have proposed language that would prohibit stablecoin issuers from offering any rewards, countering a bipartisan Senate compromise that allows limited, usage‑based incentives. Crypto advocates argue the change would stifle competition and kill yield‑bearing products, while the senators maintain their proposal still permits other customer rewards and aims to pass the CLARITY Act for clear digital‑asset regulation. The Senate Banking Committee is set to markup the bill next week
