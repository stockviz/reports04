Xbox Faces Major Layoffs Amid Declining Sales and Content Shortages

Microsoft’s Xbox division plans its first significant layoffs under CEO Asha Sharma as console sales slump and a dearth of blockbuster titles strain profitability. Despite spending over $20 billion on content, platforms, and hardware subsidies in five years, revenue fell by nearly $500 million, prompting cuts to staff, marketing, and a reassessment of its platform and portfolio. The layoffs are expected shortly after the fiscal year ends on June 30

India Inc. Turns to Expanded Allowances to Shield Take‑Home Pay Amid New Labour Code

INFY SPARC AXISBANK INDIGO HCLTECH TCS HDFCBANK MARUTI

Companies are redesigning compensation packages to comply with India’s new labour code, which mandates that at least 50 % of remuneration be classified as wages, raising statutory costs such as gratuity and PF. To protect employees’ take‑home salaries, firms are shifting a larger share of the cost‑to‑company into tax‑efficient allowances—meal vouchers, fuel/driver reimbursements, internet, professional development, children’s education and hostel, uniform, car lease, etc.—and leveraging the higher tax‑exempt limits under the revised income‑tax regime. Many are also increasing variable pay and moving performance incentives to quarterly or semi‑annual payouts, which are excluded from “wages” for statutory contributions. This strategy helps firms manage compliance and cost while retaining talent despite muted salary hikes, a sluggish job market, and broader economic pressures

Zepto’s IPO filing reveals the ongoing struggle to make quick‑commerce profitable

5PAISA FCONSUMER 3MINDIA

Zepto’s draft prospectus shows that while the company has aggressively expanded its network of riders and dark stores—boosting order volume and slightly improving loss per order—the unit economics remain challenging. Delivery costs per order stayed high, and despite higher utilization of dark stores, the firm’s losses widened as revenue grew, reflecting heavy spending on infrastructure, lease expenses, and marketing. The filing’s transparency offers a rare glimpse into the cost structure of quick‑commerce at scale, highlighting that scaling speed and reach does not yet translate into sustainable profitability

Manam Chocolate Secures $9 Million to Expand in India’s Premium Chocolate Market

Hyderabad‑based Manam Chocolate raised $9 million (≈86 cr) in a Series A led by Omnivore VC to fund a four‑year retail rollout across NCR, Mumbai and Bengaluru, strengthen its team and build institutional capabilities. Targeting the ₹5,000 cr premium chocolate segment—currently dominated by imports—the company plans to grow revenue from ₹53 cr (2025‑26) to ₹125 cr (2026‑27) and reach ₹260 cr the following year, aiming for operating break‑even within a year. Manam differentiates itself from niche bean‑to‑bar brands by focusing on gifting, confectionery, desserts, beverages, and a farm‑to‑cacao model involving 250+ farms in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana and Kerala, with 5 % of business from exporting premium Indian cacao beans. The funding reflects rising investor interest in Indian premium food brands

Air India’s Post‑Crash Overhaul: Progress and Persistent Gaps

NSIL TCIEXP INDIGO TENNIND BBL

A year after the June 2025 AI‑171 Dreamliner disaster, Air India has bolstered safety by hiring Singapore Airlines veterans, grounding its 787 fleet for inspections, and tightening maintenance oversight with regulator and partner support. Despite these steps, recurring technical defects in a majority of its aircraft, lapses like operating without valid airworthiness certificates, and operational missteps persist, eroding corporate travel business and passenger confidence. Ongoing regulator scrutiny and the upcoming CEO transition underscore the need for deeper engineering discipline and systematic defect management to fully restore trust

Thomson Reuters Shareholder Vote Rejects ICE‑Contract Human‑Rights Resolution

A shareholder proposal urging Thomson Reuters to examine the human‑rights impact of its contracts with U.S. immigration authorities, including a $22.8 million ICE license‑plate‑reader deal, garnered only about 3% support at the annual meeting. The board and major investors, including Norway’s sovereign wealth fund, voted against it, saying the company shows no significant sustainability gaps and that shareholders lack appetite for confronting government policy

SpaceX’s IPO Marginalizes Junior Banks

BCG

Junior banks such as Societe Generale and Mizuho have been assigned low‑profile co‑manager roles in SpaceX’s $75 billion IPO, earning modest fees under 0.75% and lacking the prestige of lead bookrunner positions. While they handle retail and institutional allocations in various regions, they are frustrated by the limited credit and smaller fee share compared to the lead bulge‑bracket banks, yet they remain eager to maintain the relationship with SpaceX
