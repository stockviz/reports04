Quick‑Commerce Price War Looms as Amazon Joins Blinkit, Zepto, Swiggy

ETERNAL SWIGGY

The quick‑commerce sector has scaled rapidly, with players like Blinkit targeting 3,000 stores, Swiggy holding 1,100 and focusing on utilization, and Zepto planning 1,000‑1,500 new stores post‑IPO. Amazon’s entry, leveraging its massive financial muscle, is offering the deepest discounts (≈20% on a staple basket) compared with Instamart (18%), Blinkit (16%) and Zepto (15%). While Blinkit has recently turned modestly positive, Swiggy and Zepto remain loss‑making; intensified discounting could erode margins further, putting pressure on their financial performance and sustainability

Bloom Energy CEO KR Sridhar on Powering the AI Boom

ATHERENERG OFSS

In the interview, KR Sridhar explains how the AI surge is creating an unprecedented electricity demand that traditional grids can’t meet. He describes Bloom’s “digital electricity” – clean, on‑site power generated directly from a molecule (natural gas or hydrogen) via fuel‑cell technology, eliminating the inefficiencies of heat‑to‑mechanical‑to‑electrical conversion and long transmission lines. Sridhar visualizes the demand: a single data‑center rack now needs the power of 300 homes, and future GPU units could require the equivalent of 2,000–3,000 homes. To supply this, Bloom offers modular, plug‑and‑play power blocks that can be delivered in weeks, not months, and scaled like LEGO pieces. The solution is water‑free, zero‑emission (with carbon capture in development), quiet, and far more efficient than natural‑gas turbines or grid‑fed AC systems. While renewable sources remain essential for the broader grid, Sridhar argues they cannot alone provide the reliable, high‑density power needed for AI workloads, especially in regions like India with limited land, water, and copper for transmission. He emphasizes the need for a diverse energy mix—including natural gas, hydrogen, nuclear, solar, and wind—combined with on‑site clean power to avoid grid instability and rising costs. Bloom’s rapid execution (delivering a 55 MW project in 55 days) and its vision of abundant, affordable electricity position it as a critical player in the AI‑driven energy landscape, despite market volatility and policy challenges

US‑Iran Tensions Heighten Geopolitical Risk for Oil, Tech and Emerging Markets

HALDYNGL BEL

Senior analyst Seth R. Freeman discusses the escalating US‑Iran conflict over the Strait of Hormuz, warning that while a temporary flare‑up is likely, the risk of a prolonged crisis remains. He notes that 20‑25% of global oil passes through the strait, making its continued openness crucial for oil, fertilizer and food prices. Investors should stay the course, avoiding attempts to time military actions, and watch for further shipping attacks as key triggers. Freeman also comments on broader market stress: a tech sell‑off affecting the Nasdaq, Asian equities (especially Korean chips) and a potential unwind of Japan’s yen carry trade. He sees high demand for AI‑related chips persisting, with supply catching up in two‑to‑three years. Regarding emerging markets, he expects continued foreign‑investor outflows but believes India’s long‑term growth, especially in health‑care and autos, remains attractive for patient investors

RBL Bank’s Post‑ENBD Deal Outlook: Governance, Funding Cost Benefits, Growth Targets and Strategic Plans

YESBANK RBLBANK PNB IOB

RBL Bank has completed a 60 % stake sale to Emirates NBD (ENBD), injecting ₹26,860 crore. The board now has 13 members – five nominated by ENBD, seven non‑independent and six independent directors – with no immediate senior‑management changes, as the three small ENBD branches will be merged. The capital boost and a recent rating upgrade have cut the bank’s funding costs by roughly 40‑50 bps, allowing deposit rates to fall from 7.25 % to around 6 % and projected further to 4.8‑4.9 %. RBL expects advances to grow 23‑25 % YoY, deposits at a similar pace, and aims for a 1.5‑2 billion USD FCNR inflow. Growth will be driven by Gift City, mid‑corporate and SME lending, and an expanded branch network targeting 200 high‑growth locations (130 already opened). Technology upgrades include a refreshed core platform, digital UI/UX enhancements and AI for internal automation, fraud detection and analytics. Asset quality remains stable with Special Mention Accounts low; the bank anticipates ROA of 0.55 % rising toward 1‑2 % and ROE improving accordingly. Management stresses a people‑first culture, disciplined deployment of the new capital, and resilience to potential macro shocks such as a CRR hike
