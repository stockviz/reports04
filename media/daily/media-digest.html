Should You Invest In Steel Stocks Despite Market Volatility? Global Investor Weighs In

JSWSTEEL TATASTEEL SAIL

  • Morgan Stanley upgraded JSPL to overweight (target ₹1,250) and raised target prices for JSW Steel (₹1,330) and Tata Steel (₹215), keeping an outperform rating on both.
  • Sail was downgraded to underweight (target ₹140) mainly due to high valuation, though the broader steel sector is viewed as still having upside.
  • The brokerage expects continued steel spread expansion and believes domestic steel prices can rise further; current prices are still about 6 % below historical peaks despite a recent 13 % rally.
  • Macro tailwinds cited:
    • Medium‑term benefit from China’s anti‑involution measures.
    • Potential Fed rate cuts and a weakening US dollar supporting steel demand.
  • Myuresh Joshi (Marketsmith India) highlighted:
    • Base metals (silver, gold, copper, zinc, aluminium) have largely completed their rally; the next leg is expected for steel.
    • The safeguard duty on HRC, CRC, and coated steel (price floor ≈ $675/mt) should add ₹4.5‑5 k per tonne pricing headroom, reduce imports by 30‑50 %, and lift utilization to > 80‑85 %.
    • Higher utilization should drive margin expansion; integrated players like JSW and Tata could see 3‑5 % profit growth per ₹1,000 rise in steel prices.
    • Forward EV/EBITDA multiples are projected to move from ~6× to the mid‑cycle range of 7.75×, signaling a more attractive valuation.
  • The rupee is at a record low (~₹91.2/USD), adding another layer of macro‑economic context for investors.

  • Industry Viewpoints:

    • TCS: Emphasises a five‑layer “infrastructure‑to‑intelligence” approach; value lies in contextualising models for specific business needs rather than building generic LLMs.

    • Start‑ups (Jeetu Bippal, etc.): See India’s AI‑savvy youth as a competitive advantage; aim to become global AI‑agent providers.

  • Government Role:

    • Acts as a demand generator for public‑good AI use‑cases (agriculture, weather, health, preventive care).
    • Plans large‑scale data‑center infrastructure, backed by nuclear/SMR projects to meet rising power needs.
    • Seeks industry help to design AI curricula and training programs.

  • Challenges Highlighted:

    • Energy: AI data‑centers will add substantial load to a grid already growing ~6 %/yr; need new substations, connectivity, and possibly micro‑nuclear reactors.
    • Employment: AI‑driven productivity (e.g., 50 % gains in manufacturing) could displace workers; solution proposed is upskilling youth for AI solution‑provider roles.

  • Key Take‑aways for Investors: India already leads in talent, application layers and is building a strong presence in model, chip and infrastructure layers. Continued public‑private partnership, scalable demand creation, and energy‑secure data‑center rollout are seen as critical enablers for sustained AI growth.
