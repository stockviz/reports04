Neysa Secures $1.2 Billion Debt‑Equity Funding from Blackstone and Partners

360ONE TVSELECT HALDER AQYLON

AI cloud platform Neysa, founded in 2023, raised $600 million in equity and is seeking $600 million in debt led by Blackstone, giving the firm a majority stake. Investors also include Teachers Venture Growth, TVS Capital, 360 ONE Assets and Nexus Ventures. The capital will fund expansion, including deployment of 20,000 GPUs in India, and build capabilities in orchestration, observability and security. Neysa aims to serve Indian and regional AI workloads, leveraging tax holidays and Blackstone’s global data‑center assets (AirTrunk, QTS). Management projects revenues to more than triple next year, positioning Neysa as a key infrastructure player in India’s AI mission

India’s Auto Parts Industry Ramps Up Capacity to Match Car Makers’ Expansion

M&M TATACAP HYUNDAI MARUTI HEROMOTOCO BELRISE BOSCHLTD TATAMOTORS UNOMINDA

Indian auto component makers such as Tata AutoComp, Bosch, Uno Minda, Belrise and others are accelerating brownfield expansions and modest debottlenecking to add 5‑10% capacity, aiming for utilization levels of 75‑85% as demand surges after a GST cut. OEMs like Maruti Suzuki, Mahindra, Hyundai and Tata Motors plan to add over 2 million vehicle units in four years, while two‑wheelers and cars saw record sales in Q4. Suppliers are responding with new plants and upgrades—Uno Minda’s alloy wheel facilities, Bosch’s brake and EV component capacity reviews, Tata AutoComp’s seating and thermal units, and Belrise’s new Haridwar plant—to address noted constraints in casting, suspension, and EV components and support growing EV, export, and scooter segments. Share prices of key suppliers have risen 33‑39% over the past year, outpacing broader market indices

Dhruva Space seeks $20‑25 million in new funding round

AWFIS

Indian space‑tech startup Dhruva Space is aiming to raise up to $50 million, likely targeting $20‑25 million, after closing $10.2 million in earlier rounds ($6 million in November and $4.2 million in February) from existing investors such as Blue Ashva Capital and Indian Angel Network. The company, founded in 2012 and offering full‑stack satellite services, lost payloads on ISRO’s PSLV‑C62 mission and is now looking for additional investors amid a bullish but cautious deep‑tech space investment climate, where recent sector funding reached $276 million across 33 deals. Despite challenges, Dhruva Space’s satellite expertise positions it well for future payload and launch services
