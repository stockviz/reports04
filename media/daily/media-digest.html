Loyalty Points Go Mainstream as E‑Retailers Expand Rewards to Everyday Essentials

3MINDIA NYKAA

Indian e‑retailers are broadening loyalty programs beyond travel and luxury to cover food, groceries, and daily needs, driving higher redemption frequency and embedding points as a budgeting tool at checkout. Platforms like TWID, POP, Flipkart, Swiggy, and others report significant savings for users and growing redemption volumes, while partnerships with airlines and hotels add cross‑category flexibility. Although this expansion boosts consumer engagement and lifetime value, it also raises cost pressures on thin‑margin categories, prompting a shift toward more liquid, instantly usable rewards and richer tiered benefits
