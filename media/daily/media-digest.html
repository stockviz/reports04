Textile Sector Poised for a Turnaround as Tariffs Ease and Yarn Spreads Surge

GOKULAGRO ARVIND KMSUGAR VTL 3MINDIA PGIL

The Indian textile industry may be at an inflection point driven by the reduction of US import tariffs from ~50% to ~10%, pending FTAs with the UK and Europe, and a weaker rupee that benefits exporters. Bangladesh’s energy constraints could give India a competitive edge. Cotton yarn spreads have jumped 20‑25% to around ₹130‑135 per kg, signalling higher margins. Companies with strong US exposure such as Arvind, Poonawalla (Pearl Global), and Gokaldas, as well as home‑textile and spin‑off players like Welspun, Indocot, KPR Mill (with 45% revenue from government and UK/Europe), and Vardhman Textiles, are highlighted as potential beneficiaries. The sector, traditionally under‑owned, may see renewed investor interest and a rebound in valuations

Netflix Co‑Founder Reed Hastings Plans a Planned Exit

OFSS

Reed Hastings announced he will step down as Netflix chairman later this year to focus on philanthropy, marking a smooth, self‑scripted departure after a two‑decade tenure. The transition follows a gradual handoff to co‑CEOs Ted Sarandos and Greg Peters, who have been running day‑to‑day operations since 2020. Analysts view the succession as successful, contrasting it with less orderly founder exits at other firms. The article also compares Hastings’ exit to Bill Gates’ board departure from Microsoft and Larry Ellison’s continued involvement at Oracle, noting a broader trend of founders managing their own exits, often aided by dual‑class share structures

Lilly CEO Predicts Weight‑Loss Drugs Will Reach About Half of Potential Users

Eli Lilly’s David Ricks said GLP‑1 weight‑loss drugs will likely be used by only 40‑50 % of the eligible overweight and obese population due to healthcare system complexities and cost barriers. He noted that today only 1 in 10 such individuals use GLP‑1s, comparing the ceiling to statin uptake. Launches of Lilly’s oral pill Foundayo and Novo’s Wegovy show modest early prescription numbers, and scaling production to meet a 50 % market share—roughly 500 million people—will be costly and slow. Ricks emphasized the need to make these drugs more affordable, citing the moral and economic imperative to address obesity, especially among low‑income groups

Federal Judge Blocks Nexstar‑Tegna $6.2 Billion TV Merger Over Antitrust Concerns

BCG

The deal, which would create a company owning 265 stations in 44 states, faces claims it would raise consumer prices, diminish local journalism, and give Nexstar excessive leverage over broadcasters and distributors. Former iLearningEngines Executives Charged with Massive Fraud

Former CEO Puthugramam Chidambaran and former CFO Sayyed Farhan Ali Naqvi of the AI-driven education company iLearningEngines have been indicted on ten counts, including securities and wire fraud, for fabricating nearly all of the company’s customer relationships and revenue. Prosecutors allege they used forged contracts and “round‑trip” fund transfers to create $421 million in fake 2023 revenue, misleading investors and lenders. The company, which went public in April 2024 and briefly reached a $1.5 billion market value, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in December 2024 and entered liquidation in March 2025. The criminal enterprise charge carries a potential life sentence
