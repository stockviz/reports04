Tech‑Focused Outlook: Why India May Outpace Peers as AI Hype Cools

DHUNINV CLEANMAX RELIANCE INFY BFINVEST

In a CNBC TV18 interview, Rahul Chaddha (Shikara Investment) and Prashant Perota (Allspring Global Investments) note that Indian equities have held up better than many peers despite global volatility and a recent 40,000‑crore FII outflow. They attribute this resilience to a breather in the AI‑driven IPO frenzy that has siphoned capital from markets like Korea, Taiwan and China, and to supportive fundamentals such as low oil prices, stable rupee, and growing data‑center capacity. They expect the index to outperform over the next few months, with the AI trade’s slowdown allowing capital to rotate back to India, provided Indian IT firms can integrate AI and sustain top‑line growth. The conversation ends with a reminder that long‑term premiums will depend on addressing these deeper structural issues

Reliance‑Meta Partner to Build AI‑Enabled Data Centre in Gujarat

RELIANCE

Reliance Industries and Meta have agreed to construct a 168 MW AI‑focused data centre in Jamnagar, Gujarat, slated for completion within two years with scalability options. Meta will lease capacity to expand its global AI infrastructure, while Reliance will handle design, utilities, renewable power, connectivity, and operations. The facility will run on renewable energy and use desalinated seawater for cooling, leveraging proximity to submarine cable landing stations and Jio’s fiber network. The project positions Reliance as a one‑stop provider for hyperscale AI infrastructure in India and underscores both companies’ commitment to sustainability and to establishing India as a global AI hub

External Disruptions Threaten Indian Firms’ Talent Landscape

3MINDIA

A new Adecco India report, based on 1,044 employers across major metros, finds 97 % of Indian businesses now face constant external shocks—climate, infrastructure, health crises—leading to lower productivity, higher absenteeism and rising hiring strains. Nearly half say these disruptions hamper talent attraction and retention, with 25 % experiencing severe hiring impacts, especially in Delhi‑NCR’s services and IT sectors. While 95 % prioritize business continuity, many lack insight into the most vulnerable workforce segments, prompting calls for better workforce intelligence, mental‑health support, flexible work models and wellness infrastructure. Meanwhile, reverse migration is accelerating, with 30‑35 % of remote workers moving to tier II/III cities, driven largely by younger professionals seeking quality of life over higher salaries

Rajesh Mehta’s Elest Valuation Surge Amid Controversial Mauritius Fund Investments

RAJESHEXPO ACC 21STCENMGM

In three years, three Mauritius‑based funds (Geraldton Finance, Aviator Global, Knightsbridge) invested about ₹1,250 cr in Elest Pvt Ltd, a private company owned by Rajesh Mehta that holds land, technology and machinery for the battery arm of listed Rajesh Exports. Their investments, based on inconsistent valuation reports from Finshore Management Services, drove Elest’s equity value from ₹3,000 cr to nearly ₹50,000 cr despite having no operating revenue or a running plant. The funds hold a combined 4% stake, while Mehta owns 90% and Rajesh Exports 6%. The structure—Elest leasing critical assets to ACC Energy (a Rajesh Exports subsidiary) and returning them after 30 years—has attracted SEBI scrutiny over transparency, related‑party transactions, and whether value is being siphoned from the listed company. SEBI’s interim order questions the verifiability of over 98% of Rajesh Exports’ revenue, and the government may withdraw the company from a ₹18,100 cr production‑linked incentive scheme. Legal experts warn that such arrangements could breach fiduciary duties to minority shareholders
