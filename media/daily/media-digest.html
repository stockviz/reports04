IEX Faces Pressure from CERC’s Draft Market‑Coupling Rules

IEX

The Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (CERC) has issued draft rules to introduce market coupling across India’s power markets, shifting price determination from individual exchanges to a single central body that will aggregate bids from all exchanges. This new framework, applicable to day‑ahead, real‑time, and other market segments, could reduce IEX’s pricing power and revenue, as it currently dominates the spot power market with ~90‑99% share. While the rules are still in draft form and open for public comment until May 2026, their implementation within six months could erode IEX’s monopoly, shift volumes to rival platforms, and pressure margins, potentially impacting the stock negatively though a drastic drop isn’t expected
