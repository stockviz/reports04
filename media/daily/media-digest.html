PSU Banks’ Staffing Stagnation Amid Private‑Sector Hiring Surge

BANKBARODA SBIN

BETRA’s latest data shows that while public‑sector banks (PSUs) handle about 61% of India’s banking business, their employee headcount has barely moved over the past five years, contrasting with a 32% rise in private‑sector bank staff. Veterans S.S. Mundra (ex‑Bank of Baroda) and Dinesh Khara (ex‑SBI) explain that consolidation and rationalisation of PSU branches, higher cost‑to‑income ratios, reliance on higher‑paid staff, and recruitment constraints (national exams, compulsory transfers) have limited hiring. PSUs have shifted many functions to subsidiaries and digitisation to control costs, but the lower staffing levels raise concerns about service capacity, especially for labour‑intensive schemes like Jan‑Dhan accounts. Private banks, meanwhile, have expanded back‑office and sales centres, driving higher profitability despite smaller networks. Both experts agree the trend warrants deeper analysis, particularly as AI and technology adoption could either offset staffing gaps or expose new challenges for PSUs. The discussion concludes that while PSUs remain crucial for financial inclusion, monitoring headcount trends and skill acquisition will be vital to prevent market share erosion
