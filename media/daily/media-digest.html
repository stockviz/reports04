Lemon Tree Splits After Warburg Pincus Invests In Fleur | What Investors Need To Know

LEMONTREE

Warburg Pincus will invest in the split of Lemon Tree Hotels, acquiring about 41.09 % of the company from APG for roughly ₹960 crore. The business will be demerged into an asset‑light arm (Lemon Tree) and an asset‑heavy arm (Fleur). Fifteen Lemon Tree hotels will be transferred out, and about 400 % of Lemon Tree’s subsidies will be merged into Fleur. Existing shareholders will receive roughly 74 % of Fleur on a 2‑to‑31 swap ratio (2 Fleur shares for every 31 Lemon Tree shares). Post‑split, Lemon Tree and Fleur will have separate management teams, with Mr Keswani as executive chairman of both, Mr Nilinder Singh as MD/CEO and Mr Kapil as ED/CFO of Lemon Tree, and Mr Saurabh as MD/CEO and Mayank Sharma as CFO of Fleur. Brokerage houses maintain a “Buy” rating with a target price of ₹187, citing the unlocked value in the high‑growth, high‑margin, asset‑light business.
