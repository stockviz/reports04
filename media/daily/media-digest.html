Anthropic’s Hiring Playbook: Generalists, Humility, and Empiricism

At a Fortune Brainstorm Tech conference, Claude Code architect Boris Cherny explained that Anthropic prioritizes three traits over pure technical brilliance when hiring: intellectual range (a generalist mindset that spans engineering, design, product, and data science), low ego (humility that encourages safe, collaborative experimentation), and empiricism (a data‑driven, reality‑anchored willingness to discard ideas when evidence shows they’re wrong). These qualities reflect the company’s fast‑moving AI environment where interdisciplinary collaboration, teamwork, and evidence‑based decision‑making are essential for success

Google Android Security Director Resigns Over Pentagon AI Deal

Senior Android security director Ren Mayrhofer quit Google after the company signed a contract giving the U.S. Department of Defense access to its AI systems for classified military work, citing a breach of Google’s 2018 AI ethics principles that barred weaponization and surveillance. In his farewell letter, Mayrhofer, a pacifist and tenured European academic, condemned the secretive reversal of climate and warfare commitments, warned the deal could target European citizens, and highlighted growing internal dissent over the company’s shift toward military collaboration. He will serve his notice period but will disengage from any AI work related to the Pentagon contract

Swiggy’s Toing App Sparks Restaurant Backlash Over Unauthorised Sign‑Ups and Price Tweaks

ZOMATO SWIGGY

Several restaurants across Delhi, Mumbai, Pune and other Indian cities allege that Swiggy has enrolled them on its budget‑focused Toing app without consent and altered menu prices without their approval, hurting margins. Toing lacks a merchant dashboard for price changes, forcing restaurants to rely on Swiggy contacts for adjustments, sometimes causing delays. Merchants cannot opt out of Toing without disabling their entire Swiggy presence. While some owners praise Toing for driving up to 70% of orders, many complain of hidden discounts and inability to suspend service. The controversy arises as Swiggy pushes Toing to capture value‑price customers, expanding rapidly after its 2025 launch, while competition intensifies with new entrants like Rapido. Swiggy has not commented, and the issue remains unaddressed by the National Restaurant Association of India

SpaceX IPO Risks Retail Investors

The upcoming SpaceX IPO, valued at $75 billion, unusually allocates about 30 % of shares to retail investors, exposing them to high volatility and potential losses. Brokers have lowered entry thresholds, making participation easier. Historical data on large tech IPOs show an average 55 % decline within a year, with many experiencing double‑digit drops. Aggressive revenue forecasts (e.g., $474 billion by 2030, $3.4 trillion by 2040) are seen as overly optimistic, and the waiving of the six‑month lock‑up for insiders raises concerns they may dump shares early. While short‑term gains are possible, longer‑term performance often turns negative, and analysts warn the IPO frenzy could signal a market peak, putting “mom‑and‑pop” investors at risk of significant drawdowns

Investigation Uncovers Exploitative Pay for Foreign Workers on U.S. Consulate Build in Milan

BIGBLOC

Italian prosecutors have opened a probe into Montgomery‑based Caddell Construction after workers—mostly from India and Kenya—reported being paid less than €2 an hour, far below Milan’s construction minimum wage, for a $350 million consulate project. Employees said promised salaries of €2,300‑€25,000 were replaced with monthly earnings of €500‑€800 after illegal deductions for room and board, excessive 10‑hour days, and six‑day weeks. Two Caddell managers were arrested, and the company, along with the U.S. State Department, says it is cooperating with authorities. Union representatives are seeking damages, while workers face homelessness and fear retaliation. The case highlights broader concerns over labor abuses in high‑profile diplomatic construction contracts

Four state officials challenge Nasdaq and FTSE Russell over fast‑tracking SpaceX

Investment leaders from New York, Illinois, Maryland and Oregon have urged Nasdaq and FTSE Russell to pause recent rule changes that ease entry criteria for megacap IPOs like SpaceX, demanding a formal risk analysis. They warn that the $75 billion debut’s valuation, governance and volatility could misprice shares and burden passive funds that must buy the stock when it joins indices. Nasdaq says the updates reflect market shifts, while S&P Dow Jones kept traditional rules. The letters request transparency on the rule‑making process and any influence from the company or its advisers

Brooklyn Preschool Director Pleads Guilty to $2.8 Million Tuition Theft

Murielle Misczak, a 43‑year‑old Swiss national and former director of a Park Slope preschool, admitted to stealing over $2.75 million in tuition payments. She used the funds for luxury goods, first‑class travel, and $350,000 worth of professional wrestling tickets, among other personal expenses. Prosecutors say she altered accounting records to conceal the theft. Misczak faces a maximum 20‑year prison term and must repay the full amount, while the school she managed, KinderHaus, pursues restitution
