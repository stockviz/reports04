RBI’s New Collateral Rules Threaten Indian Prop Trading

AKCAPIT HDFCBANK TV18BRDCST ICICIBANK SBIN

On July 1, the RBI mandated that guarantees for proprietary trading must be fully collateralized, with at least 50% in cash or fixed deposits, effectively ending the practice of using low‑cash bank guarantees. According to industry expert Rajesh Baheti, this move will cripple Indian prop desks and HFT firms, which currently account for roughly 30‑35% of exchange turnover, reducing their viable trading volumes by about 10‑12%. India’s Cruise Market Sets Sail with Young Travelers and Wider Access

MUFTI ROHLTD THOMASCOOK

India’s cruise industry is transitioning from a niche luxury offering to a mainstream vacation choice, driven by younger travelers, multigenerational families, and customers from tier‑II/III cities. Domestic operator Cordelia Cruises went public to fund new ships, while travel agencies like Thomas Cook India and Tirun Travel Marketing report rising demand across segments. Cruise penetration remains low (8‑10% of international, 15‑18% of domestic travelers), leaving significant growth potential. Short regional itineraries in Southeast and West Asia attract first‑time cruisers, whereas repeat travelers are opting for longer trips to the Mediterranean, Caribbean, Alaska, and Asia. Companies are bundling flights, visas, insurance, and hotels to offer end‑to‑end packages. Domestic river cruises (Antara) and short coastal cruises (Cordelia) are also gaining traction, with affordable pricing and minimal visa requirements lowering barriers. Industry experts project 10‑12% annual growth, noting that about 13% of India’s population could afford cruising, making it one of the country’s most affordable holiday options
