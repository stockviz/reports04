Spirit Airlines Ceases Operations Amid Fuel Price Surge and Failed Bailout

MCDOWELL-N

Spirit Airlines has shut down after being unable to secure credit for a proposed $500 million U.S. government rescue. The airline, which once handled 5 % of U.S. flights, was hit by a rapid doubling of jet‑fuel costs triggered by the Iran‑Israel conflict, exposing its financial fragility. Despite efforts by President Trump’s administration and the Transportation Department, the bailout fell through, leading to staff layoffs and customer disruptions. Rival carriers are positioning to absorb displaced workers and offer reduced‑fare options to affected travelers

Google Poised to Overtake Nvidia as World’s Most Valuable Company

Nvidia currently leads with a market cap of about $4.85 trillion, but its share gains have stalled, while Alphabet’s Google has surged 140% over the past year, lifting its valuation to roughly $4.65 trillion. Accelerated AI integration across search, YouTube, and Google Cloud, massive capex, and a record‑high cloud order backlog are fueling Google’s rise. With Nvidia’s earnings due on May 20 and its upcoming Vera Rubin chips, the margin is razor‑thin; analysts predict Google could surpass Nvidia within weeks, potentially becoming the first $6‑trillion‑valued public company

Greg Abel’s First Year as Berkshire CEO: Reassuring Shareholders and Managing Record Cash

At Berkshire Hathaway’s annual meeting, new CEO Greg Abel pledged to maintain the conglomerate’s decentralized, low‑bureaucracy culture, avoid break‑ups, and prudently deploy its historic $380 billion cash pile. He emphasized long‑term value creation, disciplined capital allocation, and willingness to acquire assets while saying “no” to unsuitable deals. Abel highlighted strong Q1 results—an 18% rise in operating profit to $11.35 billion—and a favorable court ruling reducing wildfire liability for PacifiCorp.
