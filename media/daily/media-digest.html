April 2026 Tax Changes: Impact on Salary, Allowances & Take‑Home Pay

3MINDIA

The interview with EY India tax partner Amarpal Chadda explains the key aspects of India’s 2026 tax reforms. New rules adjust perquisite valuations (e.g., higher taxable values for company cars and drivers) while raising limits for benefits such as meals, medical loans, and education reimbursements. Under the old tax regime, HRA exemptions remain, now extended to four additional cities, but these benefits are unavailable under the new regime, which many employees are favoring for its lower slabs and simplicity. Salary structure typically allocates ~40 % of CTC to basic pay, with HRA, retirements (EPF/NPS) and other allowances filling the rest; bonuses are paid over and above CTC. Companies will need to update payroll systems to reflect revised limits, but the reforms do not mandate a shift in basic pay proportion. Employees should discuss the changes with their employers before the new financial year begins

NFOs vs SIPs vs Gold ETFs: Investor Shifts and What It Means

DHANI

In January, Indian mutual funds saw a massive ₹24,000 cr inflow into gold ETFs, while new fund offers (NFOs) attracted only about ₹800 cr, with total NFO inflows falling from roughly ₹118 cr in 2024 to around ₹68 cr in 2025 despite a higher number of schemes. The dip reflects waning appetite for equity‑focused thematic funds after a strong 2024 rally, as investors gravitate toward safer assets like gold. Santosh Joseph explains that NFOs are merely new schemes from existing AMCs, offering no inherent cost or performance advantage over existing funds; expense ratios depend on fund size, and many new thematic funds duplicate categories already available. Investors should compare a new scheme against established alternatives, consider the fund’s manager, track record, and size, and avoid the “IPO‑style” lure of a low ₹10 NAV. Timing matters—entry during market corrections can boost returns—so staggered investments or waiting for post‑launch performance data is prudent. Meanwhile, systematic investment plans (SIPs) remain robust, growing from ₹24,000 cr in January to nearly ₹30,000 cr by December 2025, indicating a shift toward long‑term, disciplined investing despite market volatility. The overall trend shows smart money moving out of crowded equity NFOs toward gold ETFs and consistent SIP contributions
