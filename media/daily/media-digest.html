India’s Electric Cycle Market Accelerates as Start‑ups and Legacy Brands Compete for Growth

OLAELEC HEROMOTOCO

India’s e‑cycle sector is entering a rapid growth phase, with revenues projected to reach $720 million in FY26 and over $850 million by FY27. Low operating costs (≈₹0.07 km⁻¹ vs ₹2.50 km⁻¹ for petrol two‑wheelers) and the ability to charge from a standard home socket are driving adoption among commuters and delivery riders. Growth is focused on the ₹30,000‑₹55,000 segment, especially commercial use. Start‑ups like Aoki Mobility and EMotorad lead product innovation and fleet‑focused engineering, targeting monthly volumes of 2,500‑8,000 units and investing in in‑house battery and motor capabilities. Incumbents such as Hero Lectro, Stryder (Tata International) leverage brand trust, distribution networks, and scale, with Hero holding a 30‑35 % organized‑segment share and selling 12,000‑15,000 units monthly. Current combined monthly sales of the top players are 25,000‑32,000 units, leaving a large gap to the projected 75,000‑unit run‑rate by FY27. Policy support—including Delhi’s draft EV policy restricting ICE two‑wheelers in delivery fleets, exemption from registration/licensing for e‑cycles under 250 W, and a lower 5 % GST—boosts demand. Experts anticipate FY27 as a tipping point, where execution, scale, affordability and real‑world utility will determine market winners

Brands Pivot to Retention and Brand‑Building as Digital Ad Costs Surge

EIFFL CELLO BCG HTMEDIA

Consumer brands are reworking e‑commerce strategies to counter rising digital ad expenses and diminishing incremental reach. Rather than relying on costly marketplace ads and aggressive discounting, they are allocating spend to owned websites, brand‑building channels (YouTube, Instagram), and customer retention. Companies are testing campaigns, scaling only what works, and emphasizing channel quality, cohort behavior, and long‑term value over pure acquisition metrics. Efficiency is key: acquisition costs have risen 10‑15%, prompting disciplined budget allocation, first‑party data use, AI‑led optimization, and exploration of quick commerce. The shift reflects a broader move from scale‑at‑all‑costs to smarter, execution‑focused growth
