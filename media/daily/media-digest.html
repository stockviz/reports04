ONGC Expands India’s Strategic Crude Reserves by 1.75 Million Tonnes in Mangaluru

NFL MRPL BPCL ONGC

ONGC has approved a 1.75 Mt capacity addition to India’s strategic crude oil reserves at Mangaluru, Karnataka, marking the first time a state‑owned oil company invests its own funds in such stockpiles. The new underground caverns will augment the Indian Strategic Petroleum Reserves, which currently hold 5.33 Mt across three sites and are expanding with two more sites for an additional 6.5 Mt. The move aims to boost energy resilience after the Iran war disrupted the Strait of Hormuz, aligning with government goals for a month’s domestic demand coverage and leveraging public‑private partnerships. The facility may be used alongside MRPL’s 300,000‑bpd refinery, and space has already been leased by Abu Dhabi National Oil Co
