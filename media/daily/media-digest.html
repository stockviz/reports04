FICCI urges removal of three‑year audit cooling‑off period

BBL

The Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI) has asked the government to scrap a proposed amendment to the Companies Act that would bar auditors from providing non‑audit services to a client for three years after their audit term ends. FICCI argues the rule would create operational inefficiencies, raise costs, and force large corporate groups to turn to smaller audit firms lacking the scale and expertise needed for complex, cross‑border assignments, thereby compromising service quality. While some experts support a cooling‑off to preserve independence, others say the three‑year span is excessive and unnecessary. The industry body seeks to omit the provision, citing market realities and the need to balance independence with practical business considerations

Elon Musk’s Fraud Claims Dismissed as OpenAI Trial Looms

A U.S. federal judge has thrown out the fraud and constructive fraud allegations in Elon Musk’s $134 billion lawsuit against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman, clearing the way for the remaining claims of breach of charitable trust, unjust enrichment, and related accusations to go to trial. Jury selection begins Monday in Oakland, with the case structured into a liability phase (decided by a nine‑person jury) and a remedies phase (decided by the judge). Musk contends that OpenAI abandoned its original nonprofit promise, while OpenAI and co‑defendant Microsoft argue the lawsuit is unfounded. The outcome could determine whether OpenAI’s for‑profit restructuring is deemed unlawful and whether damages or corrective actions are imposed

APCO Infratech Negotiates Sale of Z‑Morh Tunnel to Alpha Alternatives for $267 million

JPINFRATEC GAYAHWS ICRA

Lucknow‑based APCO Infratech is discussing selling its Z‑Morh 6.5 km tunnel in Jammu & Kashmir to Mumbai‑based Alpha Alternatives for an enterprise value of $267 million. Built for ₹2,400 crore and inaugurated in 2025, the tunnel, part of a 15‑year DBFOT concession with NHIDCL, generates fixed semi‑annual annuity payments of ₹295 crore. The proposed deal would transfer the SPV’s ownership and O&M responsibilities to Alpha, subject to NHIDCL approval, and reflects the growing trend of monetizing Indian road assets through guaranteed cash‑flow models. The transaction highlights broader infrastructure monetization efforts, with the government having monetized assets worth ₹1.52 trillion and a pipeline of 13,400 km of PPP projects slated for FY26‑FY28. The sector’s M&A activity is rising, driven by policy support, stable cash flows, and a shift toward larger, consolidated players and green infrastructure initiatives

OpenAI CEO Apologizes for Not Reporting Shooter’s Online Activity

Sam Altman posted a letter of condolence after an 18‑year‑old, Jesse Van Rootselaar, used an OpenAI account to plan a mass shooting in Tumbler Ridge, BC, killing eight and injuring 25. The account was banned in June following abuse‑detection flags, but OpenAI decided it did not meet the threshold for a police referral and did not alert the RCMP. Altman apologized to the community, met with local officials, and pledged to work with governments to improve prevention, sparking debate over whether tech firms should have a legal duty to report violent online behavior

Charter Shares Plunge After Weak Q1 Results

PARACABLES DELTACORP

Charter Communications saw its stock drop 23% after reporting a loss of 117,000 residential internet customers and 51,000 cable TV subscribers, missing both customer and earnings expectations. Revenue was roughly on target at $13.6 billion, but EPS fell short of forecasts. The mobile partnership with Verizon added fewer lines than projected, and increased competition, housing market challenges, and shifting consumer habits pressured growth. The company’s recent bundling and streaming strategies failed to sustain gains, and uncertainty surrounds its pending merger with Cox Communications
