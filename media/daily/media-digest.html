Investors Skeptical of Salesforce’s AI‑Driven Growth

TENNIND BANKBARODA EMAMIREAL

Salesforce’s $5 billion stake in Anthropic sparked a brief share bump but has since seen a 5 % decline, reflecting doubts that its AI bets will translate into sustainable revenue. While the company’s core SaaS revenue grew 12 % CAGR to $41.5 bn (FY22‑FY26) and cash flow rose 26 %, investors fear AI could erode the traditional per‑seat model, push pricing toward unpredictable pay‑per‑use, and enable customers to build cheaper, AI‑native solutions. Salesforce has embedded AI across its products, deployed ~300 internal AI agents, and plans $300 m in Anthropic tokens for automation, but has barely hired engineers, focusing hiring on sales tied to AI usage. Strong growth in India—47 % YoY revenue and new AI‑focused offerings—offers a tailwind, and recent acquisitions (Informatica, Slack, Contentful) aim to bolster AI capabilities, yet none have lifted market cap, leaving investors waiting for clear, durable AI‑driven earnings growth

Indian Tech Firms Showcase Global Ambitions at COMPUTEX 2026

BIRLACORPN BEL ARIHANTSUP

Indian electronics maker Sahasra and software provider Zoho displayed their products at Taiwan’s COMPUTEX 2026, seeking overseas customers, partnerships, and joint ventures. Sahasra highlighted its microSD cards, noting strong interest from markets in China, the US, Europe, and the UK, while exploring technology collaborations. Zoho emphasized Taiwan’s growing digital adoption and leveraged India’s reputation for software expertise to build trust. Organizers urged greater Indian participation, citing expanding India‑Taiwan ties in semiconductors, AI, and supply chains. Indian buyers also flocked to the event, eyeing new products for import, while acknowledging India’s developing manufacturing infrastructure. The exhibition attracted over 111,000 visitors from 152 countries, featuring major players like Nvidia, Intel, and Qualcomm

Gulf Air Anticipates Summer Travel Demand Recovery

GULFOILLUB TENNIND

Gulf Air expects travel demand through its hub to rebound after the Iran war disrupted regional passenger flows, with July forward bookings higher week‑on‑week than the previous year. Although load factors are currently 3% below last year, the airline has not reduced fares, which have risen due to higher fuel costs. After a 40‑day suspension of flights from Bahrain, Gulf Air operated from Saudi Arabia and repositioned aircraft. CEO Martin Gauss, who joined in November to revive the loss‑making carrier and launch a premium product, notes that demand into the winter months is at normal levels, while regional rivals like Emirates are still operating below pre‑war capacity
