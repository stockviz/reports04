Markets Shrug Off Geopolitics As AI, Data Centres & Power Grids Fuel A New Growth Cycle: Blackstone

Summary

  • Davos Overview: Stephen Trollsman (Blackstone) says geopolitical headlines dominate attention, but markets are largely unaffected because economic fundamentals, especially in the U.S., are driving growth.

  • Economic Drivers: The U.S. economy is posting solid growth (Q4 +4.3 %). The bulk of this expansion is linked to massive spending by “hyperscalers” on data‑center construction, which in turn is spurring a surge in electricity demand and grid upgrades.

  • Investment Focus:

    • Data Centers: Blackstone is the world’s largest builder and owner of data centers, betting on continued AI‑related infrastructure demand.
    • Power Grids: The U.S. grid, stagnant for two decades, will need a 50 %+ expansion; Blackstone is investing heavily in grid modernization.
    • India: Blackstone’s India platform delivers the highest return rates across the firm. It is expanding across real estate, hospitals, housing, money‑management, and data‑center projects, with a pipeline of new acquisitions.

  • Fundraising Outlook: After a slowdown caused by high interest rates, rates are falling in the U.S. and Europe, re‑opening the private‑equity fundraising cycle. Private credit assets now sit at $500 bn, and Blackstone’s total assets under management are approaching $1.3 tn.

  • Future Themes for India: Continued M&A in high‑growth sectors (healthcare, housing, financial services), scaling data‑center and grid infrastructure, and leveraging India’s stable political environment to generate outsized returns.

Davos WEF 2026 | Agentic AI Will Redefine How Telecom Networks Are Built: Ericsson CTO Erik Ekudden

TV18BRDCST AIRTELPP

Ericsson’s CTO Erik Ekudden explained at Davos 2026 how AI—now embedded in 5G networks—is already delivering better user experiences, lower energy use and faster service rollout. He highlighted that autonomous, AI‑driven networks are deployed in the most advanced markets, and that the ROI of AI is becoming evident through productivity gains in R&D, finance, sales and customer care.

Looking ahead, Ekudden said the next wave will be agentic AI, where multiple AI agents interact in a “intelligent fabric” of traffic, reshaping network design and capacity planning. This will drive the transition from AI‑enhanced 5G to AI‑native 6G, with India playing a pivotal role. India’s massive 5G rollout—projected to reach a billion connections within five years—offers a testing ground for AI‑powered services and network programmability. Ericsson’s extensive R&D footprint in India (24 k+ staff) underpins hardware, ASIC, software and AI development, and supports collaborations with startups, universities and the government’s Bharat 6.0 initiative.

Ekudden also previewed upcoming consumer‑grade AI wearables, especially AI glasses, which will rely on the high‑capacity, low‑latency 5G/6G infrastructure. He stressed the importance of secure, trustworthy networks to enable these applications. Overall, Ericsson sees AI as a catalyst for faster product cycles, new services, and a stronger, AI‑ready telecom ecosystem—starting with India and extending globally.

Suslan 2.0 will evolve from a wind‑focused developer into a full‑stack renewable‑energy player, offering wind, solar and battery‑energy‑storage (BESS) solutions as an integrated, one‑stop shop. Davos WEF 2026 | Cognizant Plots Big Bets With AI, Outcome-Based Pricing And Possible India IPO

SIL

Key Take‑aways from the Davos 2026 interview with Cognizant CEO Ravi Kumar S.

  • Three‑year turnaround: First three years focused on stabilisation; Cognizant now sits in the upper‑quartile of peer performance.

  • Big‑bet agenda (2026‑2029):

    • AI‑driven “software 2.0”: Move from traditional coding to agentic, machine‑assisted development. Goal: >50 % of code generated with AI (currently ~30 %).
    • Outcome‑based pricing: Bundle LLM access, compute, platforms and human talent, selling results rather than effort.
    • Platform expansion: Leverage past platform successes (e.g., Trisodo‑health) to grow in under‑penetrated regions (Europe, APAC) and industry verticals.

  • M&A strategy: Organic growth restored; now looking for selective large‑ticket acquisitions that complement AI platforms and regional expansion. Past deals (e.g., Belcan) cited as proof point.

  • Partnership ecosystem: Alliances with Anthropic, Google, Microsoft to control the full AI value chain from model access to implementation.

  • India secondary listing/IPO: Engaged with Indian regulators for a possible secondary listing; pioneering effort for a U.S.‑listed tech services firm. Timeline uncertain; aiming for 2026 if approvals align.

  • Workforce impact: Emphasis on AI as an amplifier, not a replacer. Research shows 93 % of jobs will have AI exposure by 2026; 30 % of roles will have >50 % AI interaction. Plan to integrate “digital labour” with human talent, driving productivity gains and deflationary growth.

  • Talent metrics: Attrition at record low; customer satisfaction at a high; transparent reporting of machine‑assisted code percentages in earnings.
