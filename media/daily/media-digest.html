Anthropic Halts Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Access After US Export‑Control Order

TCS

Anthropic abruptly disabled its newly released Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models on June 12 following a US government export‑control directive that ordered the company to block all foreign nationals, including non‑US employees, from using the systems. The directive cited a potential jailbreak that could bypass safeguards, though Anthropic argues the issue is minor, already possible with other models, and that the action lacks transparency and due process. The company complied but disputes the decision, warning it could stall future frontier‑model deployments, and apologizes to customers while promising to restore access and provide more technical details

Paramount Secures DOJ Approval for $110 B Warner Bros. Discovery Merger

LGBBROSLTD PARACABLES

The U.S. Department of Justice has cleared Paramount Skydance’s $110 billion acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery, finding no significant competition or consumer harm. Paramount’s stock rose about 3% after the news. The deal, which outbid Netflix with a $31‑per‑share offer, aims to create a massive media conglomerate combining film studios, cable networks, and streaming services (Paramount+ and HBO Max) to better compete with tech giants. However, a $7 million daily “ticking fee” begins on September 30 if the merger isn’t finalized, adding pressure as European and California regulators continue their reviews. The EU will issue its decision by July 14, while California’s Attorney General’s office still investigates the transaction

Faulty Sprinklers and Hydrants Hamper Firefighting at Medline Warehouse in Tracy, CA

SSDL

A 1‑million‑sq‑ft Medline medical‑equipment warehouse in Tracy, California, burned out after a blaze on Thursday. Firefighters found the building’s sprinkler system and on‑site hydrants non‑functional due to a pump delivering little or no water, forcing them to rely on municipal hydrants. The fire engulfed the structure within 40 minutes, spreading embers that sparked grass fires and ignited trailers at a nearby FedEx facility. Although the sprinkler system had passed a test in January, the cause of the failure remains under investigation. No injuries were reported, and Medline activated a contingency plan to reroute supplies. Officials stress that proper maintenance of fire suppression systems and stricter enforcement of fire‑code inspections are essential to prevent similar incidents

Advent International Nears Close of $26 Billion Fund Amid Private‑Equity Exit Headwinds

FEDDERSHOL ADVENTHTL

Boston‑based Advent International is approaching the final close of a $26 billion buyout fund, one of the largest ever raised, after 18 months of fundraising. The close comes despite a broader private‑equity slowdown, where limited partners are more selective and funds face pressure to return capital and find exits. Over‑paying for acquisitions during the Covid‑era boom has created a backlog of deals, leading to valuation disputes and delayed sales. Advent has recently executed major exits, such as the $34 billion sale of TK Elevator to Kone and taking Innio Holding public, showcasing its ability to navigate the challenging environment
