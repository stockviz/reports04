SAEL’s 5 GW Integrated Solar Manufacturing Hub Launched in Jewar

SURANASOL AARON

SAEL Industries, via its subsidiary SAEL Solar P6, inaugurated a 200‑acre integrated solar cell and module plant in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh, with an investment of ₹8,200 crore. The facility will produce 5 GW of solar cells and 5 GW of modules, creating about 5,000 direct and 15,000 indirect jobs, and is part of a broader ₹7,000 crore electronics manufacturing project. The project aims to bolster India’s renewable energy self‑reliance, strengthen the domestic solar supply chain, and position Gautam Buddha Nagar as a major clean‑energy manufacturing hub

Diversifying for Global Growth and Resilience

BAJAJ-AUTO

Chairman Niraj Bajaj’s FY2026 letter outlines a strategy to reduce Bajaj Auto’s cyclical reliance on domestic motorcycles by expanding electric mobility, premium bikes, exports, vehicle financing, and technology. With record revenues (₹58,732 cr standalone, ~₹63,000 cr consolidated) and strong cash reserves (₹18,137 cr), the firm can fund multiple growth engines. Key moves include scaling the Chetak EV line, boosting three‑wheelers and Riki e‑rickshaws, doubling Bajaj Auto Credit’s AUM, growing exports to over 2.25 million units, and leveraging the KTM acquisition for a premium global footprint. Technology upgrades and supply‑chain resilience are prioritized. The letter also admits challenges: a dip in domestic market share (16.6%→15.6%) and heightened competition, prompting a FY2027 focus on the 125 cc+ segment and premiumisation, while navigating geopolitical and commodity headwinds. The overarching goal is a less cyclical, globally integrated Bajaj Auto

TURN World: ITC’s Blueprint for Resilience and Growth

ITC

ITC Chairman Sanjiv Puri warns that turbulence, uncertainty, and rapid change (TURN) will dominate the next decade, making traditional brand‑centric strategies insufficient. In the FY26 report he outlines the ITC Next framework, which integrates digital acceleration, AI‑driven decisions, science‑based innovation, sustainability, agile supply chains, and enterprise synergies across agriculture, manufacturing, research, packaging, distribution, and hospitality. Leveraging decades‑long capabilities, ITC aims to enter higher‑value adjacent markets while reinforcing existing businesses. The strategy treats sustainability as a competitive advantage and aligns with the “Nation First: Sab Saath Badhein” vision to boost domestic manufacturing, farmer partnerships, and inclusive growth. Puri’s message is that future competitive advantage will stem from interconnected systems—technology, supply chains, innovation, and purpose—rather than isolated products or brands. Companies that anticipate disruption and adapt swiftly will thrive in the TURN world

Pir​amal Consumer Healthcare bets on depth, not breadth – premium‑pricing, influencer and quick‑commerce drives to hit $200 mn revenue by 2030

CIPLA PGHL GLENMARK SANOFICONR PPLPHARMA HINDUNILVR

Piramal Consumer Healthcare is tightening its focus to a few “power” brands (Lacto Calamine, CIR, Little’s, etc.), aiming to turn them into mega‑brands and expand into adjacent premium categories. Under CEO Sai Ramana Ponugoti, the unit, which contributed ₹1,274 cr (14% of group revenue) and grew 17% in FY26, cut new launches to 31 (vs 63 in FY23) but boasts an 80% success rate, expecting new products to drive 13‑17% of sales. The strategy leans on premiumisation, influencer marketing (doubling engagements) and quick commerce (over half of its e‑commerce sales), while maintaining a presence in 450k chemist stores. A consolidated women’s line, i‑choose, and price‑cap challenges on the i‑pill are noted. The Indian OTC market is projected to hit ₹98 k cr by 2030, and analysts value Piramal’s consumer health segment at an EV/EBITDA multiple of 13

Asian Paints’ Home‑Decor Push Stalls Amid Stagnant Revenues and Market Competition

HINDWAREAP GROBTEA 5PAISA ASIANPAINT CERA AXISBANK GODREJAGRO GANGESSECU

Asian Paints’ diversification into home décor—via Ess Ess, Sleek Kitchens, The White Teak Company, and Weatherseal—has struggled: Ess Ess and Sleek have flat revenues around ₹350‑₹400 cr over four years, The White Teak’s sales fell to ₹99 cr in FY26, while Weatherseal grew modestly to ₹75 cr. Profitability remains limited, with Sleek posting a loss and Ess Ess just turning a small profit. Analysts cite a crowded market, weak brand differentiation, and execution challenges, noting that home‑decor currently contributes only ~4 % of total sales, far from the 10 % target. Some see upside from the “Beautiful Homes” store concept and recent quarterly gains, but overall the segment remains a small, under‑scaled part of the ₹35,000 cr paints giant
