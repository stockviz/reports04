FSSAI Issues Show‑Cause Notice to The Whole Truth Over “No Added Sugar” Claims Using Dates

METROBRAND AARON PML SANDESH MEDANTA

India’s food regulator has served a show‑cause notice to The Whole Truth (Fitshit Health Solutions) after rival chocolate maker Paul & Mike complained that the brand’s “no added sugar” claim is misleading because its chocolates are sweetened with date powder, which contains sugars. Under FSSAI rules, products cannot claim “no added sugar” if they use sugar‑containing substitutes. The complaint, backed by a Right‑to‑Information petition, led Maharashtra FSSAI to demand clarification. The Whole Truth says it will stop using the claim and is updating labels, but older stock remains on e‑commerce platforms. The dispute highlights broader concerns about clean‑label brands using dates, honey or similar sweeteners while marketing “no added sugar” or “clean” claims, potentially confusing consumers, especially those with diabetes. Industry observers warn the case could set a precedent for how sugar‑related claims are regulated across chocolates, protein bars, snacks and other foods

ALLDIGI

Ashank Desai, founder of Mass Tech, explains that while the Prime Minister’s call for reduced foreign travel and increased remote work aims to conserve foreign exchange and has ancillary benefits for sustainability, a full return to 100% work‑from‑home is impractical. The IT sector proved capable of full remote delivery during COVID, but post‑pandemic client demands, collaboration, culture, and team dynamics require a hybrid model. The broader economic context includes Swadeshi supply‑chain limits and the need for balanced behavioral changes
