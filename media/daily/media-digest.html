India-EU FTA | European Brands Like Skoda, Volkswagen, BMW & Audi Stand To Gain: Goldman Sachs

BMWVENTLTD TATAMOTORS M&M HYUNDAI

The EU‑India FTA will sharply cut import duties on European cars priced above €15,000. Current tariffs of 60‑110 % will fall to 40 % immediately, with a glide path to 10 % for up to 200,000 units annually. This reduces the ex‑showroom price of a €15,000 car to about ₹23.2 lakh. European premium brands (Skoda, VW, Renault, Mercedes, BMW, Audi, etc.) currently hold ~3.7 % of the Indian market; sales of cars above ₹23 lakh accounted for 8.2 % of domestic volumes in FY 26’s first nine months. The tariff cut mainly affects high‑price premium models, benefitting European makers. Domestic rivals with premium SUVs—Hyundai (≈3.3 % impact) and Mahindra & Mahindra (≈2 % impact)—will feel the most pressure, while Tata’s exposure is minimal. Electric vehicles are excluded from tariff reductions for the first five years to protect the Indian EV sector.
