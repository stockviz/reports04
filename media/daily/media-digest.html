Lightweight, Aggressive & Wild: KTM Duke 350 Review

KTM has introduced a 350 cc version of the Duke 390 for the Indian market, dropping the 399 cc LC4C engine to a 350 cc unit that produces about 41.5 PS and 33.5 Nm. Apart from the blacked‑out frame that distinguishes it, the bike retains the same chassis, suspension, electronics (WP adjustable suspension, IMU, ride‑by‑wire, three ride modes, quick‑shifter, traction/ABS), 15 L tank, and 800 mm seat height. The downsized engine is more affordable (≈₹60,000 cheaper) and easier to ride, offering modest fuel economy (~25‑30 km/L), but it lacks the instant punch and high‑rev excitement of the 399 cc model. Overall, the Duke 350 remains a sharp, agile streetfighter with full features, though its performance is a step down, making it a sensible, budget‑friendly alternative for riders who don’t need the full 390’s thrill

Hero MotoCorp Announces Rs 1,500 crore FY27 Capex to Double Scooter Output

HEROMOTOCO

Hero MotoCorp will spend about Rs 1,500 crore in FY27, including over Rs 700 crore for a new global parts centre in South India, to expand scooter capacity. Current production runs at ~60,000 units a month; the goal is to lift this toward 100,000 units by doubling output of popular ICE models like Destini and Xoom and boosting EV capacity by 50% now with further doubling planned later. The company says demand remains strong despite the West Asia conflict, though it will monitor any impact

From Rupee Crash to Oil Shock: Market Turmoil, Policy Responses, and Outlook for Indian Exports

HCL-INSYS TCS

The Indian market suffered a broad sell‑off, with major indices down over 2% and sectoral falls of 6‑8% in IT and banking. The rupee slipped past 96 per dollar and weakened against the yuan, sparking concerns about competitiveness and capital outflows. Panelists debated the limited role of further rate hikes and highlighted possible RBI tools—tightening forex rules, encouraging dollar bonds, faster export repatriation, and higher gold import duties—to stabilize the currency. Despite short‑term pressures, they see long‑term opportunities in engineering‑focused exports, especially in auto components, aerospace, chemicals, and cement, driven by a depreciating rupee and global re‑industrialisation. UCIL to Extract Uranium from Hindustan Copper Tailings in Jharkhand

CONCOR HINDCOPPER

Uranium Corporation of India (UCIL) will set up a recovery plant in Jharkhand to extract uranium from the tailings of state‑owned Hindustan Copper (HC). The two PSUs have agreed to transfer HC’s copper tailings to UCIL, which will process them, extract uranium, and return the treated waste. The move aims to boost domestic uranium supply for nuclear power and defense, reduce import reliance, and turn a waste liability into a resource, though it will require atomic‑energy clearances. Meanwhile, Hindustan Copper posted a 137.3% rise in quarterly profit and plans Rs 7,188.90 crore capex over five years for mine expansion

Power Grid Q4 Net Profit Rises Nearly 10

POWERGRID DELHIVERY

Power Grid Corp reported a 9.8% increase in Q4 consolidated net profit to ₹4,546.33 crore, driven by a massive deferred tax credit of ₹5,179.80 crore versus ₹19.98 crore a year earlier, despite a dip in total income to ₹11,970.69 crore. FY26 net profit grew to ₹15,927.95 crore, and the board proposed a final dividend of ₹1.25 per share (12.5% of paid‑up capital) on top of the interim ₹7.75 per share already paid. The company’s transmission assets total 1,84,960 km of lines, 291 substations, and 6,24,016 MVA capacity, with a system availability of 99.84%. The board also approved merging 28 subsidiaries into two existing ones, complementing a prior amalgamation of 19 subsidiaries, to streamline the SPV structure for better efficiency and governance

Forward-Deployed Engineers: The Hottest AI-Integration Role in Tech

BCG MCLOUD BBOX CHEVIOT

The demand for forward-deployed engineers (FDEs)—engineers embedded with client firms to customize AI solutions—has surged 729% in a year, with postings rising from 643 to 5,330 and salaries topping $200,000. Pioneered by Palantir and now adopted by firms like OpenAI, Anthropic, Stripe, Google Cloud, and top consultancies such as McKinsey and BCG, FDEs blend deep technical skill with consulting acumen. Their rarity and strategic importance in operationalizing AI make them one of the most lucrative and sought‑after tech positions, illustrating a rare job growth story amid broader AI‑related layoffs

Venezuela Releases Draft Oil Law Regulations to Attract Foreign Investment

The Venezuelan government has circulated a 63‑page draft outlining technical, operational, fiscal and control rules for private companies in oil and gas, ending PDVSA’s monopoly on refining, upgrading and trading. The draft, part of a new hydrocarbons law enacted in January, replaces the 1943 oil law and 1969 regulations, and introduces clauses on domestic utilization, unitization, data reversion, greenhouse‑gas effects, and mandatory enhanced recovery. A model contract has also been shared with interested firms. The move follows U.S. sanctions relief and aims to open the sector to foreign investment and integrate Venezuela’s economy into the global financial system
