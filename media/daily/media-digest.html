Tech Giants Face Legal Reckoning Over Addictive Algorithms

A Los Angeles jury found Meta (referred to as “Mera”) and Google negligent for designing platforms that deliberately addict users, awarding $4.2 million and $1.8 billion in damages respectively. Both companies cite Section 230 for protection, but the verdict signals a shift as thousands of similar lawsuits mount nationwide. Governments are responding: the EU’s Digital Services Act, India’s 2026 IT amendment rules (cutting takedown windows to three hours) and the upcoming Digital Personal Data Act (effective 2027) impose stricter duties on platforms, especially for minors. Experts argue that “safe‑harbor” immunity is eroding and that future liability will focus on algorithmic design, prompting a move toward “safety‑by‑design” and new revenue models that prioritize user well‑being over endless engagement. The cases are being likened to the 1990s tobacco litigation, suggesting a possible industry‑wide transformation

Iran‑U.S. Deal Impact on Oil Prices

Dr. Iman Naseri explains that a genuine resolution to the Iran‑U.S. conflict would likely pull Brent crude down from current levels (~$100) toward a fair‑value range of $75‑$85, but only after a month or more for production and shipping to normalize. Without a deal, prices could rebound to $110‑$130 due to supply‑shock fears. OPEC spare capacity, UAE’s potential output increase, and the time needed to lift sanctions on Iran are key variables shaping the market outlook
