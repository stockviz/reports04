PM E‑DRIVE Electric Ambulance Incentive Boosts EV Adoption, Force Motors Set to Lead

TATAMOTORS FORCEMOT M&M

The Ministry of Heavy Industries has launched a dedicated PM E‑DRIVE incentive for electric ambulances, allocating ₹500 crore to subsidise up to 3,811 vehicles in FY27‑28. A typical M2‑class ambulance with a 70 kWh battery (≈₹38 lakh ex‑factory) can now receive up to ₹13.3 lakh, lowering its net cost to ~₹24.7 lakh—near diesel parity. Operating costs drop to ₹2‑3 /km versus ₹9‑11 /km for diesel, with added benefits of lower noise, zero tail‑pipe emissions and better patient comfort. The scheme covers Type B, C and D ambulances, requires domestic production of key EV components (excluding medical equipment), and bans imported battery modules. Force Motors, whose Traveller platform dominates India’s ambulance market, is poised as the biggest beneficiary, followed by Tata Motors and Mahindra & Mahindra. The programme aims to modernise the ambulance fleet, cut healthcare transport emissions, and build a domestic electric‑ambulance manufacturing ecosystem

Accenture’s Guidance Downgrade Sends Ripples Through Indian IT Stocks

WIPRO TCS INFY

Accenture trimmed its FY26 revenue growth outlook to 3‑4% (up to 5% ex‑federal), signaling caution in discretionary tech spend despite steady AI and cybersecurity investments. The downgrade, driven by a $100 M discretionary spend hit and a $400 M revenue impact in the Middle East and AMIA regions, alongside shifts in its operating model—mid‑market expansion, non‑FTE software/data‑center M&A, and a plan to raise long‑term debt—spooked investors. Analysts note the impact is partly Accenture‑specific due to its diversified footprint, but the news has pressured Indian IT firms (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) as their growth outlooks remain modest. Market sentiment is wary, with IT stocks across Asia and Australia also slipping, prompting investors to await clearer, consistent growth momentum in the sector

Bharat Forge’s KSSL partners with AM General to market next‑gen 155 mm mounted artillery systems globally

GODIGIT BHARATFORG KALYANI

KSSL, Bharat Forge’s defence arm, has signed a strategic partnership with US vehicle maker AM General at Eurosatory to develop and export compact, mobile 155 mm artillery platforms featuring patented soft‑recoil technology. The collaboration targets worldwide defence customers and includes a proposal to supply the US Army’s Mobile Tactical Cannon (MTC) program, with a planned 2027 delivery. The system boasts high strategic transportability, a 52‑calibre cannon with 40 km range, on‑board ammunition capacity, and an advanced fire‑control suite, offering reduced recoil, lighter weight and enhanced survivability

Why Matt Orton Is Bullish on India’s Financials, Power & Auto Sectors

ICICIBANK HDFC M&M INFY

Matt Orton argues that while oil prices may ease modestly, the macro environment is clearing away major headwinds, making equities globally more attractive. He downplays the recent Accenture‑driven sell‑off in Indian IT as overblown and suggests investors focus on sectors with stronger fundamentals and longer‑term growth visibility. Specifically, he highlights Indian financials (e.g., ICICI, HDFC) as undervalued with upside loan‑growth potential, hard‑asset plays in power generation (thermal, nuclear, renewables) and DPCs, consumer‑focused companies like Eternl, and auto giant M&M as “screaming buys” at significant discounts. These idiosyncratic stories, he says, are being punished by macro‑risk sentiment rather than any company‑specific flaws, offering solid value and diversification for portfolios

HealthQuad Secures First Close of Fund III to Accelerate New‑Age Healthcare Investments

ALIVUS 5PAISA HALEOSLABS BCG

HealthQuad, backed by Quadria Group, raised 550 crore in the first close of its 1,700 crore Fund III—over one‑third of its target. The early‑growth investor sees a larger, deeper opportunity in healthtech, medtech, biopharma/tech and novel delivery models, driven by rapid tech, digital and AI adoption that could account for ~40% of India’s healthcare spend by 2030. Fund III, managed by HealthQuad Advisors (a Quadria‑owned vehicle), will focus on $30‑50 million tickets in early‑growth companies, increasing allocation to medtech and biopharma/tech while continuing in healthtech and digital care. The fund’s pipeline exceeds 500 companies, many with ₹50‑100 crore revenues. Existing LPs and new institutional, fund‑of‑funds and family office investors participated. HealthQuad’s prior funds have backed firms such as GoApptiv, Qure.ai, Redcliffe Labs, Strand Life Sciences, Medikabazaar, Wysa and Ekincare, delivering 3x returns on the first fund. The first investment from Fund III is in Lifesigns, an AI‑driven remote patient monitoring platform. Overall, HealthQuad’s AUM across three funds now exceeds $400 million, positioning it to tap the projected $600 billion Indian healthcare market, with its target segments growing 16‑40% annually

Blue Sapphire Secures ₹415 cr Private Credit to Buy Out PE Stake in AIMS

ASKAUTOLTD LENSKART 3MINDIA SAPPHIRE BCG KIMS AJANTPHARM MOTILALOFS 360ONE BFINVEST

The promoter of Blue Sapphire Healthcares Pvt Ltd, which runs the Asian Institute of Medical Sciences (AIMS), raised roughly ₹415 crore in private credit from 360 ONE Asset Management to repurchase the 49% stake held by private‑equity investors OrbiMed and British International Investment. The financing, advised by Alvarez & Marsal, will make the Pandey family the sole owners, with a modest amount set aside for growth initiatives. Blue Sapphire reported FY26 revenue of about ₹560 crore and EBITDA of ₹7,580 crore. The deal underscores the increasing use of bespoke private‑credit solutions by Indian promoters to consolidate ownership and fund expansion amid equity‑market uncertainty
