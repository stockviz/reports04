Mark Franklin, deputy head of multi‑asset solutions Asia at Manulife Investments, discussed three main topics:

  1. India‑EU trade deal: He expects a limited U.S. response, noting the Trump administration is preoccupied with its own tariff and industrial agenda. The deal reflects countries’ move toward bilateral agreements to mitigate U.S. trade pressure, but it won’t trigger a major shift in global trade policy.

  2. India’s equity outflows: Foreign investors are chasing stronger cyclical stories in Korea, Taiwan and other EM industrials, while domestic investors favor precious metals, draining equity inflows. Manulife remains cautiously positioned in India— not due to heightened risk but because of opportunity‑cost considerations— awaiting a flow‑ and momentum‑driven inflection point.

  3. U.S. dollar and Fed outlook: A weaker dollar aligns with the Trump administration’s goal of reducing the trade deficit and reviving U.S. manufacturing. Although the dollar has fallen sharply and is technically oversold, further depreciation may be limited.
