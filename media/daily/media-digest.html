Risk‑Based Deposit Insurance Premiums Set to Slash Costs for Indian Banks

BANKBARODA ICICIBANK HDFCBANK CANBK SBIN

RBI’s new draft shifts DICGC from a flat 12 paise per ₹100 deposit to a tiered, risk‑based premium with four risk categories (A‑D) and an additional “vintage” discount of up to 25 % for banks with >25 years’ track record. A‑category banks could see rates as low as 8 paise (33 % discount) plus the vintage cut, yielding total discounts near 45 %. This reduces the deposit‑insurance expense that previously formed 4‑6 % of banks’ operating profit before interest and taxes (OPICS), especially benefiting smaller private‑sector banks (PACs). Analysts project 1.5‑3 % lower OPICS, 1‑2 % higher profit, and up to a 4‑basis‑point rise in ROA, driving a notable rally in PAC bank indices.
