Hardwire Secures $13 Million Series A to Build AI‑Native Edge Chips

AMDIND 5PAISA

Bengaluru‑based Hardwire raised $13 M led by IdeaSpring Capital to develop energy‑efficient, AI‑native chips for “physical AI” applications on the edge. CEO Ramamurti Shiva Kumar highlighted India’s design talent, the shift from cloud‑centric AI to low‑latency edge intelligence, and the company’s focus on IP, talent acquisition, and partnerships for manufacturing. The funds will fuel chip design, R&D, and hiring, with initial product shipments expected by year‑end and revenue growth targeting robotics, industrial automation, warehousing, and healthcare. Hardwire aims to compete with global giants by delivering ultra‑efficient, low‑latency solutions tailored for Indian and global markets

Gulf Oil Lubricants India posts modest profit dip but strong volume growth

HGS GULFOILLUB

Essar Steel Guarantees Sale Faces Prolonged Recovery Challenges

ARIHANTCAP UNIONBANK IDBI PNB 3MINDIA KHAITANLTD ESSARSHPNG SBIN

Lenders, led by SBI, are seeking bidders for personal and corporate guarantees worth ₹13,751.21 crore linked to the former Essar Steel loan. While the consortium hopes to recoup dues by selling these guarantees to ARCs or litigation financiers, experts warn recovery will be arduous due to asset tracing difficulties, overseas holdings, unsecured nature of many guarantees, and low resale values. The reserve price is set at ₹200 crore, with SBI holding the largest claim. Legal opinion suggests assignment of guarantees is permissible under the IBC but seldom used because of bleak recovery prospects. The process is expected to be lengthy, costly, and likely yield only partial repayment

Poly Medicure’s high‑tech pivot and acquisitions boost growth outlook

BCG POLYMED

Indian medtech exporter Poly Medicure, the country’s largest listed medical equipment exporter, raised FY26 revenue 12.3% to ₹1,875.3 crore and expects FY27 growth to jump to ~25% after acquiring Italy’s Citieff and the Netherlands’ PendraCare. The firm is shifting from low‑cost consumables to higher‑value cardiology, orthopaedics, oncology and renal products (₹10,000‑₹50,000+), aiming to out‑compete Chinese rivals in Europe and expand US sales under a prospective tariff cut. Despite a 20% rise in polymer input costs from the West Asia conflict and a 200‑300 bps margin hit, price hikes and a stronger export dollar offset some pressure. Growth is also driven by expanding Indian hospital chains and insurance coverage

PhysicsWallah Scales Back K‑12 Expansion to Prioritize Profitability

ANANDRATHI PAYTM PWL CAREERP

PhysicsWallah is pulling back from its aggressive K‑12 school rollout, halting further capital allocation after investing about ₹100 crore, and focusing on profitability in its core online and offline tutoring businesses. Of its 116 offline centers, 72 are already profitable, with a target for all to be profitable by FY27 and margin improvement from –19% to –10%. Revenue grew 35% YoY to ₹3,900 crore, with a turnaround to a ₹10 crore profit before tax after a loss in FY25, driven by strong online growth (≈51% of revenue). The firm retains a large cash reserve (~₹5,000 crore), remains cautious on new capital spends, and is exploring AI-driven tools and modest education‑financing initiatives, while postponing further K‑12 investments

Green Energy Surge in India: BII Eyes Partnerships and $15 bn Climate Push

NTPC BLUESTARCO ONGC BFINVEST AFCONS AIIL ATHERENERG

British International Investment (BII) CEO Leslie Maasdorp says green energy investments in India will accelerate, driven by energy‑security concerns from the West Asia war. BII aims to keep India at ~25% of its $2.5 bn portfolio and plans to invest £15 bn in climate projects over five years, with £8 bn from its own capital and the rest mobilised from Indian pension, insurance and NIIF funds. The firm seeks partnerships with large domestic investors to co‑finance renewable power, storage, EV ecosystem and infrastructure projects. Recent initiatives include the $300 m “North Star” platform with Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners, targeting solar, wind, hybrid and storage assets that could generate 4 m MWh annually and cut 4 m tonnes of CO₂. BII’s prior investments, such as Ayana and a stake in ReNew’s solar equipment arm, underscore its strategy to catalyse private capital for India’s goal of 500 GW non‑fossil capacity by 2030, with projected $110‑120 bn in solar and wind investment by that date

Activision Blizzard Shareholders Settle $250 Million Claim Over Microsoft Deal

Activision Blizzard shareholders, led by Swedish pension fund Sjunde AP-Fonden, reached a $250 million settlement with the company’s former executives and Microsoft over allegations that the $95‑per‑share acquisition price was driven by CEO Bobby Kotick’s personal incentives. The deal, funded 40% by Microsoft and the rest by directors’ liability insurance, resolves claims that executives breached fiduciary duties, while both sides deny wrongdoing. The settlement awaits approval from the Delaware Court of Chancery

American Airlines Confident in Demand Amid Rising Costs

MCDOWELL-N BCG

American Airlines CEO Robert Isom says travel demand remains strong across domestic, international, and premium segments despite higher jet‑fuel prices and a dip in consumer confidence. The airline expects 2026 profitability similar to 2025, reporting a $237 million adjusted net income for 2025, and plans organic growth with more premium seats and possible asset acquisitions, while rejecting a merger with United. Rising fuel costs pressure lower‑income travelers, but the broader labor market remains stable
