Coal India Absorbs Rising Input Costs to Keep Coal Prices Stable

COALINDIA

Coal India Limited is shouldering the sharp increase in key inputs—ammonium nitrate explosives (up 44%) and industrial diesel (up 54%)—caused by the West Asia conflict, rather than passing them on to consumers. The firm is reimbursing contractors for higher diesel costs and covering the full cost of its 0.9 million tonnes of annual explosives consumption. To further protect downstream users, CIL has lowered reserve prices in selected e‑auctions, increased auction frequency, and offered larger coal volumes, aiming to curb a cascading price impact and maintain affordable coal supplies

Oracle’s AI‑Driven Expansion Triggers Cash Crunch and Massive Layoffs

BCG OFSS

Oracle’s aggressive push into AI infrastructure—spurred by a $300 billion OpenAI deal and massive new data‑center builds—has ballooned capital expenditures from $6.9 billion in FY24 to a projected $50 billion in FY26. The company now carries $105‑124 billion of debt, $248 billion of off‑balance‑sheet data‑center leases, and negative free cash flow of $24.7 billion. To curb the resulting liquidity strain, Oracle is raising its restructuring budget to $2.1 billion and cutting up to 30,000 jobs (≈19% of its workforce), including 10‑12 k roles in India. While OCI revenue is soaring (84% YoY to $4.9 billion) and contracted revenue hit $553 billion, most of it is deferred, margins are lower than peers, and the heavy reliance on a single, unprofitable customer—OpenAI—creates a mismatch between long‑term lease commitments and five‑year contract horizons, amplifying financial risk
