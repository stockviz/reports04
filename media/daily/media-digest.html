AI Disruption and Growth Outlook for Indian IT Services

HCLTECH AMDIND TECHM LTTS TCS COFORGE

Sandeep Agarwal explains that the market is conflating “disruption” with the normal business cycle, leading to misplaced fears that AI will destroy Indian IT. Historically, each disruption (every 5‑7 years) follows a pattern: early losses for incumbents followed by later gains as they adopt the new tech. Current AI‑related revenue contributions (e.g., L&T Tech ~12%, TCS ~8%) reflect a modest but growing share; after accounting for AI‑driven pricing discounts, underlying growth remains around 12‑14% YoY. The sector is at the tail‑end of the deflationary impact of AI, so a rebound in revenue and EBITDA margins is expected from the next quarter onward, with 6‑7% dollar growth and 12‑13% profit‑after‑depreciation (PAD) growth, leading to 45‑70% total returns over three years and 12‑18% EPS CAGR. Talent scarcity and high AI‑skill costs limit speed of adoption, prompting firms to pursue collaborations, alliances, and acquisitions (e.g., Coforge, HCL’s stake in Sarvam AI). While legacy ER&D businesses may face another ~20% correction, the overall IT services index is poised for recovery, with stock upside potentially materialising within 6‑12 months even as full earnings benefits unfold over the next few years

USHAMART
