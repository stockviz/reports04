IKEA India Leverages Food to Boost Store Traffic and Growth

3MINDIA

Food has become a core driver of footfall for IKEA India, accounting for just under 10% of its business and extending customer dwell time by about two hours. The retailer tailors its menu to Indian tastes, offers affordable items (e.g., hot dogs at ₹69, many items under ₹200), and runs family‑focused events like “Happy Wednesdays.” By anchoring visits around meals, IKEA aims to increase repeat visits, improve store productivity, and offset declining furniture sales. The strategy reflects a broader retail shift toward experience‑led destinations, with F&B serving as a funnel that brings customers into stores, encourages browsing, and supports overall revenue growth despite a widening net loss in FY 2024‑25
