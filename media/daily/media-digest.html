Curbs On Chinese Cos To Ease?: Don’t See An Impact On Pricing Even With TBEA’s Entry: IIFL Inst Eq 2026-01-12 10:29

CGPOWER POWERINDIA GEPIL NTPC BHEL JSWCEMENT

The interview with Renu Baid, SVP‑Research at IIFL Institutional Equities, discusses recent developments around Chinese manufacturers in India’s power‑transmission sector. After the 2020 Galwan clash, Chinese firms were barred from government contracts, causing a sell‑off in stocks such as Hitachi, BHEL, and CG Power. However, TBEA—a Chinese‑owned company with a manufacturing base in India since 2014—has recently received approval to bid for high‑voltage reactor projects in the next fiscal year.

Key points:

  • The ban on Chinese firms has not been formally lifted; approvals are case‑by‑case.
  • TBEA’s entry re‑opens government‑sector opportunities for Chinese‑owned manufacturers but does not immediately threaten pricing because overall transformer and reactor capacity (≈50 GW) is already near full utilization.
  • Existing Indian players (Hitachi Energy, Jiv, Varunova, TND, CG Power) are expected to absorb any impact, with valuations already reflecting heightened competition risk.
  • The transformer industry is set to double its capacity in the next 12‑15 months, so price moderation was anticipated regardless of TBEA’s participation.
  • In power‑generation equipment, restrictions remain tighter; state utilities like NTPC are unlikely to source Chinese gear, while private IPPs may still use Chinese vendors for boilers and turbines, though domestic capacity is now constrained.
  • Overall, the market is expected to see limited earnings impact and no sudden price crash, though sentiment may weigh on affected stocks.
