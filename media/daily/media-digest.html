HSBC India Economist on the Rupee, Inflation, and Policy Options

CANHLIFE

Pranjul Bhandari explains that India faces a historic \(65 bn external deficit and a global bond sell‑off, pressuring the rupee, which is trading around ₹96.86/\) and has been depreciating at roughly 80 paise‑₹1 per ten days – a “trickle” depreciation. She advises using FX reserves to smooth the fall, modestly raising oil and diesel pump prices (which previously saved $20 bn), and leveraging recent EU/UK trade deals to attract FDI. More aggressive measures like FCNR‑type quasi‑government bonds or higher gold import duties are seen as risky, costly, or prone to smuggling. Inflation is projected to average 5.6‑6 % this year, driven by energy shocks and a potential super‑El Niño, while growth is expected to slow from 7.5 % to about 6 % and may fall further. Consequently, the RBI may deliver two rate hikes (likely in Dec and Mar) but not more, to balance price stability with growth. Oil price assumptions are $95/barrel this year, $85 next year. Overall, policy space is limited; the priority is an orderly rupee depreciation until the Hormuz Strait reopens, after which a modest 1‑2 % appreciation could follow
