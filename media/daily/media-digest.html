Dividend Stocks in India: Income Tool, Not a Growth Engine

COALINDIA BRITANNIA ITC

Dividend‑paying stocks in India are not inherently safer and can be value traps if yields are high due to deteriorating fundamentals. Historically they have delivered modest capital appreciation, serving better as wealth preservers and sources of steady income rather than drivers of long‑term growth. In a flat or down market they can offer a “safe harbor,” but rising interest rates make them less attractive compared with fixed‑income alternatives. Mutual‑fund dividend payouts are tax‑inefficient; growth or systematic withdrawal plans are preferred. A few companies (e.g., ITC, Britannia) manage both decent yields and growth, but such cases are rare. The prudent approach is to allocate only 10‑15% of a portfolio to dividend stocks for income, keeping the bulk in growth‑oriented assets
