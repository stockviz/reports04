Conglomerates dominate India’s IBC asset acquisitions, driving turnarounds and value capture

JINDALSTEL MSPL ELECTCAST ESSARSHPNG RELIANCE UTTAMSTL JSWSTEEL IOC VEDL ADANIPOWER TATASTEEL BCG JPASSOCIAT

Over the ten‑year span of India’s Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, four major conglomerates—Adani, JSW, Reliance, and Tata—have accounted for nearly a quarter of the total admitted claims (≈13 trillion rupees) despite representing only 28 of 1,376 completed resolutions. Adani led with 13 deals (including six power firms), JSW followed with seven (notably Bhushan Power & Steel), Reliance secured five assets, and Tata bought three. Together they captured a disproportionate share of claim value—JSW ~8%, Adani ~6% (rising to ~10% with Jaiprakash Associates), Reliance ~6%, Tata ~5% of total admitted claims. Their acquisitions often involve large‑ticket claims (>1,000 crore), which represent 86% of total claim value. The groups leverage cross‑sector synergies, deep capital, and operational expertise to turn around distressed assets, achieving cost‑effective capacity expansions (e.g., Adani Power’s 5.5 GW at ~3.2 crore/MW vs. 10‑12 crore/MW for greenfield plants) and profitable exits (e.g., JSW’s sale of half BPSL stake for 37,250 crore). While creditors recovered an average of 31.6% of claims (171.5% of liquidation values), the dominance of these conglomerates underscores the IBC’s role in reallocating stuck assets, enhancing utilization, and preserving jobs
