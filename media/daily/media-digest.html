SpiceJet Seeks Review of Court Order to Deposit ₹144.5 Cr in Maran Arbitration Dispute

SPICEJET

Budget carrier SpiceJet and its MD Ajay Singh have filed a review petition in the Delhi High Court challenging an earlier directive that they deposit ₹144.5 crore within four weeks in the ongoing arbitration with Kalanithi Maran and KAL Airways. Justice Subramonium Prasad, hearing the case, adjourned it to 13 April but warned that filing a review does not excuse compliance with the payment order, which is due by 14 April. Despite claiming to have paid ₹730 crore overall, SpiceJet still owes ₹144.5 crore, a sum that adds to its current liquidity strain caused by flight disruptions linked to the West Asia war. The Supreme Court has declined to intervene, and the airline also faces costs and other legal pressures

Goldman Sachs India Economist Predicts Tightening Amid Oil‑Driven Growth Slowdown

Shantanu Gupta, Chief India Economist at Goldman Sachs, outlines a downgrade of India’s GDP growth to around 6% and a rise in inflation to about 5% due to elevated oil prices. He expects the RBI to keep rates unchanged in the April meeting but to add roughly 50 bps of hikes in the second half of the year as oil‑price pass‑through lifts core inflation. The current account deficit may expand to ~2 % of GDP, prompting a second consecutive balance‑of‑payments deficit. The government is shielding consumers from fuel price spikes, absorbing costs via fiscal and quasi‑fiscal measures, but a prolonged shock could force pass‑through and strain fiscal revenues. RBI liquidity is being managed through domestic injections to offset reserve losses, with overnight rates likely to move closer to the repo rate, while FX intervention is not expected to drive rate hikes
