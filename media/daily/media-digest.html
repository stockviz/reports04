RBI’s New Upper‑Layer NBFC Rules Spotlight Tata Sons’ Unlisted Status

LICHSGFIN NSIL SHRIRAMFIN

India’s central bank has proposed a revised, asset‑based criteria for classifying “upper‑layer” non‑banking financial companies, expanding the list to include NBFCs with assets of ₹1 trillion or more and allowing state‑owned NBFCs to join. The updated FY‑2026 list names Tata Sons as the only unlisted entity among the 15 large NBFCs, raising questions about whether the Tata Group’s holding company still counts as an NBFC after surrendering its licence. Upper‑layer firms are required to list on the equity market, but Tata Sons has not complied and the RBI has not clarified its status. Experts suggest the market’s recent correction may prompt a deadline extension, while the RBI’s move aims to tighten supervision, capture systemic risks, and reduce regulatory arbitrage for large, interconnected NBFCs

U.S. Treasury and Fed Warn Banks About AI‑Driven Cyber Threats

STANLEY BANKBARODA

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Fed Chair Jerome Powell convened a meeting with CEOs of major U.S. banks to highlight cybersecurity risks posed by Anthropic’s newly unveiled Mythos AI model, which can identify and exploit vulnerabilities across operating systems and browsers. Anthropic limited Mythos access to about 40 tech firms and is in talks with U.S. officials about its offensive and defensive capabilities. The briefing aimed to ensure banks are aware of and prepared for these AI‑enabled threats

Boaz Weinstein Secures Victory as Edinburgh Worldwide Investment Trust Backs Saba‑Led Tender Offer

IITL AVONMORE TCNSBRANDS

Edinburgh Worldwide Investment Trust has voted to support a tender offer backed by activist investor Boaz Weinstein’s Saba Capital Management, allowing shareholders to exit at net asset value minus costs now or after a future SpaceX liquidity event. The board’s own proposal, which would have paid about 85% in cash with the remainder deferred, failed to gain enough support. With 36.8% of share capital voting against the board’s plan and 53.8% opposing it overall, shareholders will vote on April 30 to replace the board with Saba’s candidates, likely making Saba the new manager. The chairman expressed disappointment, but the priority remains giving shareholders a meaningful choice
