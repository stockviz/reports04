TVS Supply Chain Targets $2 Billion Revenue Amid Geopolitical Shifts and Operational Turnaround

TVSSCS

TVS Supply Chain Solutions (TVS SCS) is on track to hit its $2 billion revenue goal by 2029, forecasting FY26 revenues of ₹11‑12 trillion (~$1.3 bn) and aiming for a 4% PBT margin in FY27. Growth is driven by a robust pipeline (₹6.3 trillion) and new business wins (₹683 crore) as global supply chains diversify toward India, especially via the US “China+1” shift. The company’s asset‑light model lets it pass higher freight costs to customers, while cost‑reduction programs (e.g., Project One) and a focus on high‑margin Integrated Supply Chain Solutions support margin targets. Short‑term volume boosts from Red Sea disruptions may fade, and currency appreciation limits dollar‑denominated growth. TVS SCS is also expanding into aerospace and defence logistics through an MoU with Italy’s ALA and pursuing small, strategic acquisitions to add sticky clients, but these are not expected to transform the business. Execution on converting pipeline orders, managing external shocks, and disciplined capital allocation will determine whether the firm reaches the $2 billion milestone

From IIT Lab to Niche Dominance: How SEDEMAC Outsmarted Global Tier‑1s

BMWVENTLTD SEDEMAC BAJAJ-AUTO BOSCHLTD CAPTRUST ATHERENERG HONDAPOWER HEROMOTOCO TVSELECT

SEDEMAC Mechatronics, founded by IIT‑Bombay alumni in 2007, built a market‑leading position by targeting overlooked, cost‑sensitive segments rather than chasing the high‑end Tier‑1 playbook. Its breakthrough sensor‑less commutation technology eliminates Hall‑effect sensors, boosting reliability of motor controllers for two‑wheelers, three‑wheelers and generators in harsh Indian conditions. This has given SEDEMAC about 35% of India’s ISG ECU market and a 75‑77% share of domestic genset controllers, with 60% of shipments exported. Riding this niche success, the company is now developing sensor‑less EV motor controllers for electric scooters. Financially, SEDEMAC posted >30% annual revenue growth, EBITDA margins >20% and ROCE ~40%, culminating in a 6,700‑crore‑rupee IPO at a 13.5% premium. Its strategy—solving ruggedness problems that global giants like Bosch, Continental and Valeo ignore—has created new product categories and delivered strong returns without raising fresh capital
