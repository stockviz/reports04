Impact of Rising Crude Prices on India

BPCL ONGC VEDL OIL

Brent crude has surged ~31% in six sessions, reaching about $93/barrel, the highest since Sep 2023, with a YTD gain of 52.3%—the strongest start since 1997.
- India imports ~85% of its oil, so higher crude directly pressures the economy.
- Every $10/barrel rise can widen the current‑account deficit by 0.4‑0.5% of GDP, lift CPI inflation by 30‑50 bps, push WPI up ~80 bps, and trim GDP growth by ~15 bps.
- Oil marketing companies (IOCL, BPCL, HPCL) see their gross marketing margin shrink, reducing earnings by 7‑11% for each ₹0.5/litre margin loss.
- Sectors heavily dependent on fuel—aviation, lubricant makers (Gulf Oil, Castrol), tire/chemical producers, and cement—face margin compression as fuel can form 40‑50% of their total cost.
- Oil exporters (ONGC, Oil India, Hindustan Oil, Vedanta) stand to gain: a $5/barrel rise can boost their EPS by 7‑12%.
- Short‑term price swings have limited lasting impact; sustained high prices over 2‑3 months are needed for significant economic and corporate‑profit effects
