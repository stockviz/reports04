    • Equity brokerage fee cap reduced from 12 bps to 6 bps plus costs, effectively lowering net expense by a few basis points.

Why Is China Emerging As The Biggest Loser In The U.S.–Venezuela Oil War

Trump’s push to secure Venezuelan oil would require U.S. guarantees for companies like Exxon and Chevron, whose past assets were nationalized. Restarting Venezuelan production is technically possible but will take 3‑8 months, so any additional supply won’t hit the market immediately. Consequently, oil prices have remained flat in the short term, suggesting the market is not yet pricing in this geopolitical risk. In the longer run, increased Venezuelan exports could pressure prices, but the primary loser appears to be China, which has invested roughly $60 billion in Venezuelan heavy‑crude projects and now faces reduced access to that supply. OPEC, other importers, and the broader supply chain are less directly affected in the near term.
