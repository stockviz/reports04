Meta Layoffs Continue Despite Judge’s Refusal to Issue Injunction

A U.S. District Judge denied a request by 26 current and former Meta employees to halt layoffs that allegedly used AI tools to discriminate against workers with medical or caregiving absences. The plaintiffs claim Meta’s AI systems—such as “Metamate,” keystroke and screen‑activity scoring, and AI‑based performance reviews—ranked employees and informed layoff decisions, even during protected leave. While the judge found no proof of “irreparable harm” to justify an emergency order, he left open the possibility of revisiting the decision if new evidence about AI usage is presented. The layoffs, affecting about 8,000 staff (≈10% of the workforce), proceed as Meta expands its AI investments, and the plaintiffs’ broader claims will proceed in private arbitration

AI Disruption and the Future of Indian IT Giants

HCL-INSYS TCS

The discussion evaluates whether Indian IT leaders like TCS can navigate the AI wave. Despite a steep stock decline (TCS down 55% YoY, IT index down 40% over two years) and lower valuation multiples, firms are emphasizing AI-driven projects, with TCS reporting $2.6 bn ARR from short‑term AI engagements (≈8.5% of revenue) and committing ₹7 bn to data‑center expansion. Investors might find buying opportunities at current valuations, but the traditional perception of Indian IT as a high‑margin, growth engine may evolve as AI and infrastructure become central to their business models

AIRTELPP TANLA JIOFIN

HDFC Bank Q1FY27 Earnings Call Highlights

HDFCBANK

The bank reported Q1 FY27 results below estimates, with net interest margin hitting a record low of 3.26% and a high CD ratio of 96%. CFO Srinivasan Vedinathan thanked employees, the board, and interim chair Kiki Mistry, and welcomed new chairman Rajeev Kumar, emphasizing stability and growth. Management highlighted strong deposit growth, improved branch productivity, and ongoing investments in distribution, aiming to boost loan advances. They discussed liquidity augmentation via FCNR and swap mechanisms, and detailed plans for FCNRB offerings, including three streams: attractive 6%+ deposits, internal leverage (~9x) and external leverage (9‑19x) to serve NRI customers. Margins were defended as a longer‑term focus, urging investors to look at annual trends rather than quarterly fluctuations, and noting a shift toward a higher retail loan mix (target 60%). Loan growth was robust across mortgages, wheels, personal/business loans, gold and micro‑loans, with gold loans at ~₹25,000 cr and 35% YoY growth, and business banking up ~22% YoY. Corporate loan demand is driven by term and working‑capital needs across sectors like electronics, auto, renewables, and commodities. The CEO’s reappointment remains pending, awaiting the new chairman’s board deliberations. Competition in FCNR deposits and regulatory constraints in Dubai were acknowledged, with the bank pursuing multiple strategies to sustain funding and customer relationships. Overall, management signaled confidence in medium‑ to long‑term efficiencies, technology adoption, and stakeholder returns despite short‑term macro challenges

Skyroot Aerospace’s Historic Vikram‑1 Launch Marks India’s First Private Orbital Success

DIACABS UNIMECH

Skyroot Aerospace CEO Bhavan Kumar Chandana celebrated the successful launch of Vikram‑1, India’s first privately developed orbital rocket, highlighting the company’s rapid rise since its 2018 founding, its youthful 28‑year‑old average team, and innovative low‑cost technologies such as carbon‑composite structures and 3D‑printed engines. The mission delivered multiple payloads—including two satellites, a debris‑capture robotic arm, German separation‑test hardware, micro‑sculptures of Indian scientific legends, and a world‑first green diamond in space—while achieving flawless trajectory and stage separations despite a brief hold‑in‑launch. Chandana emphasized the robust Indian supply‑chain ecosystem, frugal engineering, and the importance of “people engineering” in building a zero‑error culture. Looking ahead, Skyroot aims to scale production to a launch‑per‑month cadence, develop the higher‑capacity Vikram‑2 (targeted for 2027), and focus on reliable commercial services before considering an IPO, all while positioning India among the few nations with private orbital launch capability

KG‑D6 Gas Production Decline and Mitigation Plans

RELIANCE

Natural gas output from Reliance‑BP’s KG‑D6 block fell about 7% YoY to 24.8 mmscmd in Q1 FY27, continuing a natural decline that began mid‑2025. Production peaked at 30.6 mmscmd in Q1 2024 and has dropped 19% since. Reliance (66.7% stake) and BP (33.3%) invested ~$5 bn in the R‑Cluster, Satellite, and MJ projects, which were expected to supply ~15% of India’s gas demand. Gas price fell to $8.89/MMBtu, while condensate price rose to $107.4/bbl. The companies acknowledge the decline, claim it is less severe than expected, and say a mitigation plan is forthcoming, though details and timelines remain undisclosed
