Robust Q4 Outlook for Bank of India: 15‑16% Advance Growth, Strong Pipeline, and Slippage Reduction Targets

BANKINDIA

Bank of India’s MD‑CEO Rajneesh Karnataka highlighted a solid Q4 performance and set ambitious FY‑27 targets: global advances to grow 15‑16% and deposits 13‑14%, while domestic advances aim for 16‑17% and deposits around 14%. The bank’s credit pipeline stands at ₹75,000 cr, with ₹60,000 cr in corporate credit covering new‑gen sectors such as green energy, data centres, EV and PV manufacturing, and ₹20,000 cr in infrastructure projects. Emphasis on higher‑margin MCLR and RAM advances, plus mid‑corporate lending through 19 ECCB branches, is expected to boost net interest margins. Fresh slippages are targeted to fall from ₹5,500 cr in FY‑26 to about ₹4,500 cr, with the slippage ratio reduced to ~0.55% for FY‑27. The bank’s shares rose about 2.5% on the news

Crown Worldwide Boosts Indian Investment to $30 Million, Aiming for ₹500 Crore Revenue by 2030

CROWN

Hong Kong‑based Crown Worldwide Group will double its Indian investment to $30 million over the next two years, allocating $15 million to expand its information‑management and workplace‑services portfolio driven by GCC demand, hybrid‑work trends, and new data‑privacy regulations. The firm is shifting from relocation‑focused logistics to a broader enterprise‑infrastructure platform covering records management, document digitisation, asset management and data‑governance, targeting over ₹500 crore in Indian revenue by 2030 (up from ₹350 crore in FY25) as the market grows at 10‑12 % annually. A new 4.5‑acre facility in Coimbatore with 468,000 SCE capacity (expandable to 720,000 SCE) will support these services, alongside AI‑driven digitisation, sustainability initiatives (20 % electric fleet, 40 % renewable energy), and a presence in 13 Indian cities, positioning Crown as a key partner for physical and digital asset management in a regulated, digitised economy

Prudent Corporate Advisory FY27 Outlook: Growth, Regulation Impact, and Strategic Shifts

INDUSTOWER PRUDENT

Chairman Sanjay Shah discussed the limited impact of recent TER and GST regulatory changes—estimated at a 2‑3 bp hit on existing MF books, with negligible effect on new business. He highlighted consolidation opportunities as smaller, non‑GST players lose 15‑20 % revenue, positioning Prudent to capture market share. The firm plans to add ~30‑32 branches, targeting tier‑2/3 cities, and sees insurance as a major growth driver, already contributing ~14‑15 % of revenue with a strong seasonal boost in Q1. An earlier acquisition of Indus Capital (₹135 cr) was integrated successfully, and while further deals are being evaluated, the process remains cautious. The P2P liquid loan product has been discontinued due to unsustainable RBI regulatory changes. Looking ahead, Shah expects a 10‑12 % net‑sales rise, driven by a healthier mark‑to‑market outlook, and projects MF revenue growth of 20‑22 % and continued strong insurance expansion, though the impact of upcoming IRDA changes remains uncertain

India’s Largest Container Port JNPA Moves Toward IPO

MKPL CELLO BCG ADANIPORTS CONCOR ABMINTLLTD GPPL ATL

The government is preparing an IPO for Jawaharlal Nehru Port Authority (JNPA), India’s biggest container hub handling 54% of national container traffic. State-backed investment banks have been engaged to start regulatory processes, focusing first on compliance and reporting standards before any valuation or pricing discussions. The listing, part of the National Monetisation Pipeline, requires approvals from the ports ministry and the port authority and follows a similar move by Kamarajar Port. JNPA reported a 52% profit rise to ₹2,053 crore on ₹3,262 crore revenue in FY25, with assets of ₹23,149 crore and reserves of ₹14,613 crore, making it attractive to institutional investors. The IPO timeline remains undefined as the process navigates multiple departmental clearances. This would be only the second state‑owned Indian port considered for a public listing, aligning with broader government efforts to monetize infrastructure assets

BigBasket prioritizes profit over market share in quick‑commerce race

SWIGGY PAISALO ETERNAL

Co‑founder Vipul Parekh says BigBasket will accept lower market share to become profitable, shifting focus from rapid expansion to larger, higher‑margin assortments and bigger dark‑store formats. The company’s BB Now service now holds just 7% of the sector’s GMV, trailing Blinkit (46%), Swiggy Instamart (27%) and Zepto (21%). Losses widened to ₹2,006.8 crore in FY 2024‑25, and revenue fell 1.9%. With no external fundraising, Tata Digital will fund internally, while rivals like Amazon and Flipkart ramp up dark‑store networks. Industry observers note the trade‑off between growth and profitability, warning that aggressive discounting may erode margins and customer loyalty. The sector’s growth is moderating after years of rapid expansion

Aramco CEO Warns of Prolonged Oil Market Disruption as Profits Surge

Saudi Aramco chief Amin Nasser cautioned that a prolonged closure of the Strait of Hormuz could keep oil supplies tight and prevent market rebalancing until 2027, despite the company’s 26% profit jump driven by higher crude prices and a strategic pipeline bypass. The war in the Middle East has halted most traffic through Hormuz, pushing Aramco to redirect shipments to Red Sea ports and use stealth tankers. While quarterly sales volumes rose year‑on‑year, they fell quarter‑on‑quarter, and the firm maintained a large dividend despite lower free cash flow. Nasser called for greater industry resilience and investment in alternative energy to mitigate future supply shocks
