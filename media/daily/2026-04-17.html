Vedanta Power Plant Explosion Linked to Fuel Buildup and Boiler Pressure Lapses

NTPC VEDL

A preliminary technical probe into the April 14 blast at Vedanta’s Sakti, Chhattisgarh plant found that excessive fuel accumulation in the boiler furnace caused a pressure surge that forced a steam‑carrying tube to burst, killing 20 workers and injuring 16. The investigation cites lapses in maintenance and negligent operation by Vedanta and its contractor NGSL, establishing prima facie negligence. An FIR has been filed under sections 106, 289 and 3(5) of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, naming up to ten individuals including Vedanta chairman Anil Agarwal, with a special police team overseeing the case

Delhi Court Orders 5‑Day ED Custody for Former Reliance Anil Ambani Executives Over Alleged Loan‑Fund Siphoning

BCG RELCAPITAL RHFL

A Delhi special judge sentenced former Reliance Anil Ambani Group senior officials Amitabh Jhunjhunwala and Amit Bapna to five days of Enforcement Directorate custody in a money‑laundering case tied to a bank loan fraud involving Reliance Home Finance and Reliance Commercial Finance. The court’s 24‑page order says digital evidence, chiefly email trails, shows the duo helped divert loan amounts worth over ₹11,000 crore to shell companies controlled by the group. The ED alleges the funds were quickly disbursed to dummy entities, turning the loan accounts into NPA. Both executives contended that the instructions came from top management (including Anil Ambani), but the court found the emails “crystallise” their role in the siphoning scheme and set a hearing for 20 April 2026

Trump Weighs Michael Murray for DOJ Antitrust Chief

SBGLP AHL DELTACORP TTL LGBBROSLTD PARACABLES TIMETECHNO

The White House is reportedly evaluating former Trump administration deputy assistant attorney general Michael Murray, now a partner at Paul Hastings, to lead the Justice Department’s antitrust division. Murray previously served from 2018‑2020, worked under Deputy Attorney General Rod Rosenstein, and has extensive experience handling major merger reviews, including defending the DOJ’s challenge to AT&T‑Time Warner. If nominated, he would replace Gail Slater, who was ousted after less than a year amid policy disputes, and succeed Acting Assistant Attorney General Omeed Assefi, who plans to depart. The nomination may hinge on the pending appointment of a new attorney general after the recent firing of Pam Bondi. Murray’s supporters cite his familiarity with DOJ operations and a “predictable business climate,” while the agency currently faces a staffing shortage after many senior antitrust litigators left during the transition to a more settlement‑focused approach. The DOJ has yet to file new major merger challenges under the current administration
