Why Bear Markets Excite Investors: Strategies from Nippon India CIO

INDNIPPON

In a 2026 MoneyWise interview, Saleh Shahrajban, President and CIO of Nippon India Mutual Fund, explains why today’s bear market presents rare opportunities for long‑term investors. He notes that recent sell‑offs driven by tariff issues, foreign institutional sell‑downs, and geopolitical shocks have driven valuations to attractive, “margin of safety” levels across high‑quality businesses. The fund is adding to positions in undervalued large‑cap, multicap, and small‑cap stocks, especially financials, private‑sector banks, and select IT services, while maintaining low turnover for core holdings. Shahrajban emphasizes a three‑ to five‑year investment horizon, diversification into global markets for long‑term allocation, and the importance of taking the “right risk” rather than timing markets. He advises investors to stay the course, increase SIP contributions, and accumulate during periods of price weakness, as the fundamentals of Indian companies remain strong despite ongoing macro‑economic uncertainties

Reliance Seeks Approval for Iranian Crude Imports Amid Sanctions Waiver Expiry

RELIANCE

Reliance Industries has asked the Indian government for permission to import Iranian crude on four U.S.-sanctioned tankers, aiming to stay compliant with U.S. and Indian rules as a key sanctions waiver expires on April 19. The move follows increased Russian oil purchases after previous U.S. waivers, but Indian imports from Iran have been nil since 2019 due to sanctions concerns. The shipping ministry cleared the tankers (Kaviz, Lenore, Felicity, Hedy) for potential cargoes, while Reliance also holds a U.S. license for Venezuelan crude and faces reduced Russian oil volumes after EU restrictions

IBM Settles DEI Fraud Probe for $17 Million

IBM agreed to pay $17.1 million to resolve DOJ allegations that it violated the False Claims Act by using discriminatory DEI practices—such as tying bonuses to demographic targets and restricting training programs based on race or sex—in its federal contracts. The company denied wrongdoing but terminated or modified the contested programs and cooperated with the investigation

CFTC Blocks Arizona Criminal Case Against Kalshi

A federal judge issued a restraining order, at the CFTC’s request, temporarily halting Arizona’s 20‑count indictment of prediction‑market platform Kalshi. The CFTC argued that state prosecutions cannot pre‑empt its federal oversight of derivatives and prediction markets. Earlier, a different judge had allowed the state case to proceed, but the new order underscores the agency’s claim that federal regulation supersedes state criminal actions in this arena. The commission is also suing Connecticut and Illinois for similar intrusions on its authority

RBI’s New Upper‑Layer NBFC Rules Spotlight Tata Sons’ Unlisted Status

LICHSGFIN NSIL SHRIRAMFIN

India’s central bank has proposed a revised, asset‑based criteria for classifying “upper‑layer” non‑banking financial companies, expanding the list to include NBFCs with assets of ₹1 trillion or more and allowing state‑owned NBFCs to join. The updated FY‑2026 list names Tata Sons as the only unlisted entity among the 15 large NBFCs, raising questions about whether the Tata Group’s holding company still counts as an NBFC after surrendering its licence. Upper‑layer firms are required to list on the equity market, but Tata Sons has not complied and the RBI has not clarified its status. Experts suggest the market’s recent correction may prompt a deadline extension, while the RBI’s move aims to tighten supervision, capture systemic risks, and reduce regulatory arbitrage for large, interconnected NBFCs

Boaz Weinstein Secures Victory as Edinburgh Worldwide Investment Trust Backs Saba‑Led Tender Offer

IITL AVONMORE TCNSBRANDS

Edinburgh Worldwide Investment Trust has voted to support a tender offer backed by activist investor Boaz Weinstein’s Saba Capital Management, allowing shareholders to exit at net asset value minus costs now or after a future SpaceX liquidity event. The board’s own proposal, which would have paid about 85% in cash with the remainder deferred, failed to gain enough support. With 36.8% of share capital voting against the board’s plan and 53.8% opposing it overall, shareholders will vote on April 30 to replace the board with Saba’s candidates, likely making Saba the new manager. The chairman expressed disappointment, but the priority remains giving shareholders a meaningful choice
