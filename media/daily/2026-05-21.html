Meta Announces 8,000 Layoffs Amid AI Push

Meta laid off about 8,000 employees (≈10% of its global workforce) as it reallocates resources toward artificial intelligence. CEO Mark Zuckerberg sent a memo expressing sadness, acknowledging the weight of the cuts, and assuring staff that further company‑wide layoffs are not expected this year. He emphasized that AI is the most consequential technology of our lifetimes and that Meta aims to help define the next generation, positioning itself as one of the few companies capable of delivering “personal superintelligence.” The layoffs are the largest since the 2022‑23 “Year of Efficiency” campaign, which cut roughly 21,000 jobs. Analysts estimate the cuts will save about $3 billion, a fraction of Meta’s projected AI‑related spend of up to $145 billion this year and hundreds of billions through the decade. The company hopes the restructuring will make it a better place for talented people to have a greater impact

PENINLAND

India’s Microfinance Sector Bounces Back Amid Cautious Outlook

SATIN 5PAISA INDUSINDBK RBLBANK CREDITACC SPANDANA

After nearly two years of stress, India’s microfinance institutions are returning to growth, with improved collection efficiency and lower non‑performing loan ratios thanks to stricter underwriting and self‑regulatory guardrails. Leaders at CreditAccess Grameen, Spandana Sphoorty, and Satin Creditcare note material gains in asset quality and plan disciplined expansion, targeting 25‑30% AUM growth by FY27‑30 while tightening borrower selection (rejecting 60‑65% of applicants). Nonetheless, analysts warn that geopolitical tensions, rising inflation, fuel‑price shocks and lingering delinquencies could temper the rebound. Banks are increasing lending to MFIs, and lenders are diversifying beyond joint‑liability groups into individual, mortgage‑backed, and two‑wheeler loans, though group lending remains core. The sector’s future hinges on stronger governance, digitalisation, and robust capital structures

Marriott Leverages Indian Outbound Travel Surge to Pivot Loyalty Toward Experiences

TENNIND CANHLIFE ABMINTLLTD ADVANIHOTR TAJGVK SWIGGY CHALET INDIGO INDHOTEL

Marriott International is banking on a rapid rise in Indian outbound travel, despite geopolitical and economic headwinds, to drive growth across Southeast and East Asia. The chain reports soaring demand from loyalty‑savvy Indian travelers, who now seek upgrades, unique experiences, and partnerships beyond traditional earn‑and‑burn rewards. Marriott’s 2026 Loyalty Trends Report shows India (and Indonesia) leading in engagement, with 41% prioritizing exclusive experiences and 69% redeeming points on sports, entertainment and concerts. To meet this shift, Marriott has expanded digital marketing, partnered with food‑delivery, fintech and entertainment platforms, and broadened its footprint in India through a mid‑scale alliance with The Fern Hotels, boosting its presence from ~40 to 80‑90 cities and now operating over 210 hotels. The strategy aims to capture both outbound and domestic demand, positioning Marriott Bonvoy as the world’s largest loyalty programme while competing with rivals like IHG, Hyatt and IHCL

SpaceX Announces Record‑Breaking Nasdaq IPO Plans

HEALTHX

SpaceX filed to list Class A shares on Nasdaq under ticker SPCX, targeting a June 12 debut that could raise $50‑$80 billion and value the company at $1.25‑$2 trillion. The 277‑page prospectus shows 2025 revenue of $18.7 billion with a $4.9 billion loss, heavy spending on AI infrastructure ($12.7 billion) and Starlink. AI is now central, with plans for orbital data centres and a proprietary chip fab (“Terafab”). Starlink fuels most revenue, while AI and connectivity together represent a $28.5 trillion addressable market. Elon Musk retains >50% ownership and 85% voting power via a dual‑class structure, with performance‑based share awards tied to valuation milestones and a Mars colony. Goldman Sachs leads underwriting; the offering will be the largest IPO in history, potentially unlocking further mega‑listings
