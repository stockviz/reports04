Air India’s FY26 $3 bn loss dents Tata Sons’ TCS dividend earnings

PAISALO TENNIND TATACAP BCG NSIL BEL TCS

Air India posted an estimated FY26 loss of about $3 bn (S$3.76 bn) driven by foreign‑exchange hits, fuel price spikes and airspace closures, wiping out roughly 21,270 crore of Tata Sons’ stake (the group owns 74.9%). Consequently, Tata Sons’ dividend income from TCS fell 12.1% to 28,292 crore in FY26. The airline’s FY26 revenue was S$10.53 bn and Singapore Airlines, its 25.1% partner, injected S$1.13 bn. Additional pressures include the prolonged Pakistan airspace ban, West Asia conflicts, soaring jet fuel costs, a weakening rupee, and a pending CEO transition. Tata Sons, which relies heavily on dividends (83% from TCS in FY25), is also funding several cash‑intensive ventures such as Tata Digital, battery maker Agratas and semiconductor firm Tata Electronics, further stretching its financial bandwidth

Petrol & Diesel Prices Hiked by Rs 3/L in 4 Metro Cities Amid Middle East Crisis

BPCL

Indian oil marketing companies (IOCL, HPCL, BPCL) raised petrol and diesel prices by Rs 3 per litre—the first hike since April 2022—against a backdrop of rising global crude costs due to the Middle East conflict. The previous price‑rise cycle began after the Ukraine war in March 2022, with OMCs increasing rates by up to Rs 10/L within 16 days, leading to cumulative hikes of 14 times. OMCs face projected losses of ₹1‑1.2 lakh crore in FY 27, prompting the latest increase to improve margins (each ₹0.5/L boost raises margins by ~7 %). The government cut excise duty by ₹10/L on March 27, partially offsetting the rise. Ground reports from metro commuters indicate the hike is straining household budgets, though consumers acknowledge the global market pressures driving the increase

Companies Cite AI Amidst Widespread Layoffs

BCG

A wave of job cuts across tech and other sectors— from Cisco and Block to Dow, Pinterest, and Meta—is being framed as a strategic shift toward artificial intelligence. Executives argue that AI will drive future growth and efficiency, prompting restructuring, reduced headcounts, and reallocation of resources to AI-focused initiatives, even as they invest heavily in the technology. This trend fuels employee anxiety about job security while companies claim the moves will ultimately create new roles and competitive advantage

Milei Approves Incentives for Chinese‑Backed Lithium Expansion in Argentina

BCG

President Javier Milei’s government granted tax breaks and legal guarantees to a joint venture led by China’s Ganfeng Lithium to invest $1.24 billion expanding a Jujuy lithium mine, alongside a $891 million copper project in Mendoza. The moves, part of Milei’s RIGI program to attract large foreign investment and revive hard‑currency inflows, aim to create 8,000 jobs and position Argentina as a key supplier of clean‑energy minerals, despite previous skepticism toward Chinese firms
