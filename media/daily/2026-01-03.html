Limelight Lab Grown Diamonds Adds ₹240 Cr To Its Kitty | CNBC TV18

BROOKS-RE

Limelight Lab‑Grown Diamonds announced a ₹250 crore fundraise to become India’s first fully integrated lab‑grown diamond jewellery brand. Founder‑MD Pooja Madhwan explained that vertical (backward) integration—controlling diamond growth, manufacturing and retail—will secure supply, improve quality, enhance transparency and speed up design innovation. Most of the capital will fund aggressive retail expansion, aiming for 100 stores in 2024 and 200 by 2027, targeting both metro and Tier‑2 cities. The Indian lab‑grown diamond market is projected to grow from a $500 million valuation to $1 billion within three years, with demand rising across all consumer segments, including traditional diamond buyers and new entrants. Integration is seen as essential to maintain market leadership amid rising competition from legacy players entering the lab‑grown space.

Kazam Closes FY25 With ₹40 Cr In Revenue | Startup Street 2026-01-03 11:01

Kazam ended FY25 with ₹40 crore in revenue, driven by its expansion in electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure and the launch of peer-to-peer energy trading. The company enables solar power owners to sell surplus energy directly to EV users, reducing electricity costs compared to utility providers. Pilots are already live in Lucknow and Delhi. Kazam’s device-agnostic, grid-aware platform—termed a “unified energy gateway”—connects chargers, vehicles, and utilities, creating actionable intelligence and enabling interoperability across India’s growing EV ecosystem. Strategic partnerships with OEMs like Maruti Suzuki, Bajaj, Mahindra, and quick-commerce players are critical to scaling infrastructure. Kazam is heavily focused on tier 2 and 3 cities such as Siliguri, Pilibhit, and Bareilly, where direct adoption of EVs from petrol is high, enabling significant cost arbitrage. The company has nearly 8,000–10,000 charge points and expects to reach 12,000–15,000 with upcoming two-wheeler segment expansion. While existing operations are on a profitability path and don’t require new funding, Kazam plans to raise capital for new initiatives like international expansion and scaling its energy trading platform over the next 18–24 months.

  • Recent key events: Heavy cigarette taxes led to ITC hitting a three-year low; VIP’s 32% stake sale to Multiples completed; Devyani and Sapphire’s QSR merger announced.
  • Leadership changes in major FMCG firms: New leaders at HUL (Priya Nair), Nestle (Manish Tiwari), Britannia (Rakshit Jhargan), United Spirits (Praveen, Swameshwar), Titan (Ajay Chawla), Pidilite (Sudhanchu Vats), Avenue Supermarts (Anshula Khanna) – their strategies in 2026 will be critical.
  • Demand recovery triggers: Expected improvement in urban and rural demand driven by last year’s income tax changes, September GST cuts, upcoming pay commission revisions, and government consumption-boosting measures.
  • Event-specific triggers: Reduced competition in paints as disruptor becomes incumbent; fundraising pace of quick-commerce players impacting retail; a wave of 2026 IPOs (Zepto, Boat, Oyo); Titan’s entry into lab-grown diamonds; potential value unlock roadmap for Reliance Retail, possibly including a Reliance Geo listing.

  • Three key themes:
    1. Mass premiumization – growth in India’s middle-income segment.
    2. Bharat-focused businesses – better performance due to low rural penetration vs. saturated metros.
    3. Health and nutrition innovation – rising consumer focus.
  • Risks: Intensifying competition from new-age brands; weaker-than-expected demand recovery; equity overhang from recent IPOs.
  • Outlook: Despite challenges, consumption sector has upside potential in 2026 after a prolonged lull, with leadership transitions and macro tailwinds as key catalysts.
