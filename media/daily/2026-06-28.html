India’s construction boom fuels Quess Corp’s next staffing push

QUESS

Quess Corp, already a leading provider of organised workforce in manufacturing, retail, banking and consumer sectors, is positioning construction as a “hyper‑growth” segment. After entering the market two years ago, the firm now places 3,000‑5,000 workers on projects such as airports, roads, industrial plants and upcoming semiconductor facilities. With India’s infrastructure push and large‑scale manufacturing investments creating demand for compliant, scalable labour, Quess sees an opportunity to replicate its staffing model in a sector that remains largely informal. The company plans to expand its construction footprint while continuing strong demand from banking, retail and consumer businesses as formal employment spreads to smaller cities

ITC Shifts Focus to Digital Rural Services, Life Sciences, and Sustainable Materials

ITC

ITC is pivoting from consumer‑brand growth to three capability‑driven platforms: (1) ITC MAARS, a digital rural services ecosystem linking over 2.6 million farmers with AI advisory, finance, market access and mechanisation, aiming for 10 million users by 2030; (2) a life‑sciences venture leveraging its nicotine expertise, with an integrated pharmaceutical‑grade nicotine plant in Mysuru and expansion into botanical extracts, now EBITDA‑positive; and (3) sustainable‑materials development through its paperboard business, creating recyclable barrier boards and fibre‑based packaging, supported by the planned Century Pulp & Paper acquisition and the formation of ITC Fibre Innovations. These B2B initiatives capitalize on ITC’s long‑standing agriculture, tobacco science and fibre technology capabilities to drive long‑term growth

Raymond’s Aerospace Unit Outpaces Precision Engineering Growth

JKCEMENT RAYMOND

JK Maini Global Aerospace (JKMGAL) is growing at about 25% annually, outpacing its sister precision‑engineering business. The surge is driven by rising global demand for aerospace parts, India’s “China+1” strategy, and a booming aircraft backlog. JKMGAL’s order book will generate over ₹2,350 crore in the next five years, with revenue split across engine (75%), structural (15%) and system parts (5%). While 79% of sales are export‑focused, the company is shifting from a build‑to‑print model to a value‑chain strategy, hiring designers, developing IP, and investing ₹510 crore in a new manufacturing plant in Andhra Pradesh to move into sub‑assemblies and assemblies. Defence activities remain in the prototype stage, focusing on component supply rather than direct contracts

Tesla urges Delhi to revise EV policy incentive cap

Tesla has raised concerns with the Delhi government over the draft EV Policy 2.0, which offers incentives only for electric cars priced at ₹30 lakh or less. Both Tesla’s Model Y (₹50.89 lakh) and Model Y L (₹61.99 lakh) exceed this limit, excluding them from tax and registration fee exemptions and a ₹1 lakh scrapping incentive. The company warns that the price cap will hinder the adoption of high‑tech EVs in the Delhi‑NCR market, where it operates experience and service centers and sold 342 cars in FY‑26. The policy, still under public consultation, aims to grant 100 % road‑tax exemption for sub‑₹30 lakh EVs and a scrapping bonus for eligible buyers, but provides no benefits for pricier models

Diversifying for Global Growth and Resilience

BAJAJ-AUTO

Chairman Niraj Bajaj’s FY2026 letter outlines a strategy to reduce Bajaj Auto’s cyclical reliance on domestic motorcycles by expanding electric mobility, premium bikes, exports, vehicle financing, and technology. With record revenues (₹58,732 cr standalone, ~₹63,000 cr consolidated) and strong cash reserves (₹18,137 cr), the firm can fund multiple growth engines. Key moves include scaling the Chetak EV line, boosting three‑wheelers and Riki e‑rickshaws, doubling Bajaj Auto Credit’s AUM, growing exports to over 2.25 million units, and leveraging the KTM acquisition for a premium global footprint. Technology upgrades and supply‑chain resilience are prioritized. The letter also admits challenges: a dip in domestic market share (16.6%→15.6%) and heightened competition, prompting a FY2027 focus on the 125 cc+ segment and premiumisation, while navigating geopolitical and commodity headwinds. The overarching goal is a less cyclical, globally integrated Bajaj Auto

TURN World: ITC’s Blueprint for Resilience and Growth

ITC

ITC Chairman Sanjiv Puri warns that turbulence, uncertainty, and rapid change (TURN) will dominate the next decade, making traditional brand‑centric strategies insufficient. In the FY26 report he outlines the ITC Next framework, which integrates digital acceleration, AI‑driven decisions, science‑based innovation, sustainability, agile supply chains, and enterprise synergies across agriculture, manufacturing, research, packaging, distribution, and hospitality. Leveraging decades‑long capabilities, ITC aims to enter higher‑value adjacent markets while reinforcing existing businesses. The strategy treats sustainability as a competitive advantage and aligns with the “Nation First: Sab Saath Badhein” vision to boost domestic manufacturing, farmer partnerships, and inclusive growth. Puri’s message is that future competitive advantage will stem from interconnected systems—technology, supply chains, innovation, and purpose—rather than isolated products or brands. Companies that anticipate disruption and adapt swiftly will thrive in the TURN world

HINDWAREAP GROBTEA 5PAISA ASIANPAINT CERA AXISBANK GODREJAGRO GANGESSECU

Asian Paints’ diversification into home décor—via Ess Ess, Sleek Kitchens, The White Teak Company, and Weatherseal—has struggled: Ess Ess and Sleek have flat revenues around ₹350‑₹400 cr over four years, The White Teak’s sales fell to ₹99 cr in FY26, while Weatherseal grew modestly to ₹75 cr.
