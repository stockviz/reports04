5th Circuit Rejects Families’ Bid to Reopen Boeing 737 Max Criminal Case

A three‑judge panel of the 5th U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals unanimously denied dozens of victims’ families’ request to revive a criminal conspiracy charge against Boeing over the 737 Max crashes, finding that the Justice Department did not violate the Crime Victims Rights Act. The families argued they were excluded from negotiations that led to a deal allowing Boeing to avoid prosecution in exchange for $1.1 billion in fines, compensation, and safety reforms. Boeing and prosecutors said most families supported the settlement. The case, stemming from alleged misrepresentations about the aircraft’s flight‑control software that contributed to the 2018 Lion Air and 2019 Ethiopian Airlines crashes that killed 346 people, will remain dismissed

Elon Musk Faces Potential Class‑Action Over Late Twitter Stake Disclosure

A Manhattan federal judge allowed investors alleging Musk delayed disclosure of his 9.2% Twitter stake—missing the SEC’s March 24 deadline by 11 days—to proceed as a class action. The investors claim the delay let Musk profit by over $200 million as they sold shares at depressed prices, relying on his silence and ambiguous tweets. The judge rejected Musk’s argument that reliance couldn’t be proven and noted that class certification is possible despite challenges in measuring damages. The case is distinct from a separate San Francisco lawsuit where a jury found Musk liable for actions to lower the takeover price. Musk plans to appeal, and settlement talks with the SEC over the 5% stake disclosure are ongoing

Tether Dismisses Former HSBC Gold Traders Amid Strategic Shift

BCG CANHLIFE AHL

Tether Holdings has let go of two senior precious‑metals traders, Vincent Domien and Mathew ONeill, who were recruited from HSBC to professionalize its bullion operations. Their departures come months after Tether amassed a record gold hoard—about 140 tons—to back its USDT stablecoin and its XAUT coin. The firm cited a lean‑team approach and ongoing optimization, while noting it is building a “state‑of‑the‑art” gold team. Their exit follows a broader industry hiring spree by trading houses and comes as gold prices slump and Tether prepares its first full audit and a potential $20 billion funding round
