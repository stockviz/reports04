From Minimum Cover to Real Protection: Policybazaar’s Report on India’s Insurance Shift

In 2025, India made significant strides in insurance adoption, driven by government reforms such as GST exemption on health, life, and travel insurance, and the Insurance Amendment Act allowing 100% foreign direct investment. These changes underscored insurance as essential, akin to food and medicine. Consumers responded by opting for higher coverage—health insurance sums rose from ₹14 lakh to nearly ₹20–25 lakh—and multi-year policies, especially five-year plans, gained popularity due to premium stability, discounts, and reduced renewal hassles. Term insurance saw one crore rupees become the standard minimum cover, with increasing uptake of two to three crore policies, fueled by rising awareness and affordability due to an 18% drop in premiums. Looking ahead to 2026, the focus shifts toward personalization, innovation, and prevention. Insurers are offering more tailored products based on location, lifestyle, medical history, and preferences—such as OPD benefits, senior citizen-friendly plans without co-pay, and network hospital choices for cost savings. Unlimited sum insured options emerged, simplifying decision-making. The trend points to more customer-centric, accessible, and comprehensive protection, marking a shift from basic coverage to real financial security.

Buffett emphasizes that success doesn’t depend on market conditions or macroeconomic factors, but on focusing on what you can control: customer experience. Buffett admires their drive, resilience, and potential, believing their best days lie ahead.
