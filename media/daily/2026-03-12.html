Cyberattack Cripples Stryker’s Global Operations

NEWGEN

A pro‑Iran digital activist group, Handala, claimed responsibility for a massive cyberattack that disabled Stryker Corp.’s worldwide Microsoft‑based network, forcing employees to work from home and wiping data on hundreds of thousands of devices. The breach affected over 200,000 systems, erased up to 95% of computers in some offices, and involved the extraction of about 50 TB of data, though the company says there’s no sign of ransomware and the incident appears contained. Stryker, a $25 billion medical‑technology firm with ties to Israel and U.S. defense contracts, saw its shares dip 5.3% following the report. Authorities have not confirmed the group’s Iranian affiliation

Brent Crude Surges Above $100 Amid Geopolitical Tensions and IEA Reserve Release

BANKBARODA

Crude prices have climbed back above $100 per barrel, driven by ongoing Iran‑related supply disruptions, Gulf output cuts of 6‑12%, and attacks on tankers. The IEA approved a historic release of about 400 million barrels (including 172 million from the U.S. and 80 million from Japan), though markets view this as already priced in and a one‑off event. Gasoline and heating oil hit multi‑year highs, while Canada reports record production but limited short‑term capacity to increase output. Forecasts diverge: some analysts expect a surplus and bearish trend, while banks like BofA, JPMorgan, Citi, and Barclays project potential Brent levels ranging from $80‑90 up to $150 by March, depending on further disruptions. The near‑term outlook remains highly volatile

Borosil’s Partial Shutdown Over Gas Shortage

BOROLTD

Chairman Pradeep Kheruka explained that Borosil has halted one of its three glass furnaces in Jaipur due to insufficient LPG and natural‑gas supplies, a resource the glass‑making process cannot curtail. The company is in talks with LPG allocators and major oil companies and expects to receive limited gas soon, allowing reduced‑capacity operation but not a full restart. Kheruka said the government is proactively managing LPG stocks and may source additional supplies, but no firm timeline can be given. He noted that each day of shutdown incurs significant costs, though exact figures were not disclosed. The discussion also covered induction cookers as a potential LPG substitute for households and offices, but Kheruka confirmed that glass furnaces cannot switch to induction technology; only metal melting uses such equipment. He assured that Borosil is complying with regulatory reporting requirements and will update stakeholders as the situation clarifies

ACME Solar Secures 450 MW Battery‑Backed Power Deal with SJVN

ACMESOLAR SJVN

ACME Solar Holdings, via its subsidiary ACME Greentech Seventh Private Ltd., signed two 25‑year PPAs with state‑run SJVN for a total of 450 MW and 1,800 MWh of battery storage.
