Colgate‑Palmolive and Bajaj Finance Lead Nifty‑500 Volume Surge, Mixed Moves

BAJFINANCE COLPAL HAPPSTMNDS

Colgate‑Palmolive recorded its biggest single‑day drop in six years, trading around 16 lakh shares (well above its 20‑day average of 3.5 lakh) with delivery volumes also higher than usual, pushing the stock toward its 52‑week lows. Bajaj Finance was the top Nifty loser, falling sharply for the first time in four months; it saw over a crore shares change hands—far exceeding its 20‑day average of 75 lakh—again with delivery volumes above the norm, and it retreated to budget‑day lows near ₹890. Meanwhile, Happy Minds surged 12 % early in the session before easing to a modest 2.5 % gain, trading about 7 crore shares (well above its 20‑day average of 3 lakh) with a turnover of roughly 85 % of its free‑float market cap. Overall, the Nifty slipped 227 points, reflecting the heavy, divergent activity in these heavily‑traded stocks
