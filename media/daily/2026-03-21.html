When to Sell: Goal‑Based, Tactical and Valuation‑Driven Strategies

The discussion emphasizes that selling is as crucial as buying and should be guided by clear financial goals, asset‑allocation targets, and market valuations. Long‑term investors stay fully invested, but as they near a goal they shift from aggressive equity to conservative debt instruments. Tactical sells occur when individual stocks or funds become overvalued (e.g., high P/E multiples) or when a market segment (small‑cap, mid‑cap) shows froth, prompting profit‑taking and rotation into larger caps or safer assets. A core‑satellite approach is recommended: keep 60‑70 % of equity in a buy‑and‑hold core portfolio, while using the remaining 30 % for tactical calls and rebalancing. Regular rebalancing (annual or quarterly) helps maintain target weightings and prevents drift that can amplify risk or miss opportunities. In downturns, investors may add to equities within a predefined leeway (e.g., 5‑10 % above the target) to capture upside, but should avoid over‑exposure. Fund managers should be evaluated on style consistency and downside protection; under‑performers relative to their stated strategy warrant replacement. Systematic withdrawal or transfer plans should be used three years before a goal to move assets into low‑risk vehicles, ensuring liquidity when needed. Overall, sell decisions are driven by valuation signals, goal timelines, portfolio drift, and tactical opportunities, not emotion
