The AI Layoff Trap: Externalities, Competition, and a Proposed Automation Tax

The CNBC TV18 Global Reset episode discusses the authors’ report on how AI‑driven layoffs can create a demand‑side externality: firms reap wage savings while displaced workers, now poorer consumers, depress overall demand. Because each firm only bears a fraction of the resulting demand loss, competitive pressures push firms to over‑automate, creating a prison‑er‑dilemma where collective restraint would be more profitable. The hosts explore the novelty of AI’s speed and scale, contrast it with historical automation, and examine policy options. While UBI can cushion workers, it doesn’t correct the externality. The authors propose a Pigovian automation tax—levying firms for the demand damage their layoffs cause—to align private incentives with societal welfare, potentially funding retraining and mitigating the “AI layoff trap
