Ola Consumer’s FY25 Results Delayed, Highlighting Heavy Losses and Market Share Decline

OLAELEC AAATECH

Ola Consumer (formerly Ola Cabs) filed its FY25 financials more than six months past the October 30 deadline, citing “administrative” reasons. Revenue fell 41% to ₹1,171 cr and the company recorded a ₹1,974.7 cr loss, of which a ₹1,312 cr loss on Ola Electric’s share value was excluded, leaving a core loss of ₹662.4 cr versus a ₹328.7 cr loss the prior year. Auditor S.R. Batliboi flagged ₹148.4 cr in loans to subsidiaries as potentially prejudicial and noted a ₹2,600 cr total loan write‑off. A new auditor, SN Dhawan & Co., took over from April 2025. The board, comprising six non‑independent members, met only once in FY25, with two members attending all meetings. Ola’s ride‑hailing market share plummeted from ~50% in 2023 to 20‑25% by end‑2025, while Uber held ~45% and Rapido grew to 20%. The delayed filing and financial deterioration raise concerns over governance and compliance

Meesho Accelerates AI Investments to Boost User Growth

MEESHO TCIEXP DELHIVERY

Meesho’s FY26 Q4 results show a 32% YoY rise in transacting users to 264 million and a 43% jump in NMV to ₹11,371 crore, driven by AI tools like the voice‑search agent Vaani and recommendation engine PRISM. The company is focusing on in‑house logistics (Valmo) after third‑party disruptions and plans to continue aggressive spending to capture India’s next wave of online shoppers, especially in smaller cities and vernacular markets

Ola Consumer Eyes IPO Amid Cash Crunch and Slipping Market Share

SPAL AVALON OLAELEC AAATECH

Ola Consumer (formerly Ola Cabs) has approved an IPO, its first public step after a delayed filing, to raise funds as cash reserves shrink to ₹180.3 cr (₹652.8 cr including investments) and operating losses double to ₹662.2 cr in FY25. The company’s net worth fell 57% to ₹1,490 cr, while competitors Uber and Rapido hold stronger cash positions and dominate market share (Uber ~45%, Rapido >20%). With cumulative losses of ₹21,213 cr, dwindling market share (20‑25% vs 40‑45% in 2023), and looming debt obligations, the IPO is seen as crucial to fund driver incentives, promotions, and to stem the decline. Failure to secure an IPO extension could trigger exit rights for CCPS holders

Lakshya 31: L&T’s Ambitious Five‑Year Push to Double Revenue and Diversify into Green Hydrogen, Electronics and Real Estate

LT

Larsen & Toubro’s new five‑year plan, Lakshya 31, targets 12‑15% CAGR revenue growth (up to ₹5.8 trn by FY31) and 10‑12% CAGR order‑inflow growth (up to ₹7.75 trn). The plan allocates ₹42,400 cr across green hydrogen/ammonia (₹15,000 cr), data centres (₹10,000 cr), industrial electronics (₹5,000 cr), semiconductor manufacturing (₹3,000 cr), wind energy, and a listed real‑estate business (₹4,400 cr). L&T aims for 16‑17% RoE, maintaining an asset‑light, technology‑focused approach while exiting low‑return assets. Under Lakshya 26, the company beat revenue (₹2.86 trn vs ₹2.7 trn) and order‑inflow targets (₹4.4 trn vs ₹3.4 trn) but missed the 18% RoE goal, delivering 16.6%. Analysts note potential margin pressure from green‑hydrogen projects and question whether order‑inflow growth in infrastructure is sufficient
