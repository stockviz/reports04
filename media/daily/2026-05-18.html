Meta’s May 20 Layoffs Trigger Employee Panic and Pre‑emptive Job Hunting

ONEPOINT

Meta plans to cut about 8,000 jobs (≈10% of its workforce) on May 20, prompting widespread anxiety among staff who are stockpiling free snacks, drinks and chargers. Adani Group Shifts to Vendor‑Heavy Model and Internal Talent Promotion

ADANIENPP1 BCG ADANIPORTS HYUNDAI

Gautam Adani is overhauling his conglomerate by outsourcing manufacturing, logistics, engineering and other functions to a select group of third‑party vendors, retaining only strategy, R&D, finance and IP. The group is flattening its hierarchy to a three‑layer reporting structure to speed decisions and has instituted a hiring freeze on external senior hires, emphasizing internal promotions via an internal portal, with exceptions approved by Karan Adani. This transformation mirrors Apple’s reliance on contract manufacturers, aims to improve capital efficiency, and supports the group’s massive ₹2 trillion‑plus capex plan, while experts warn of governance, cyber, ESG and talent‑diversity risks inherent in a decentralized vendor ecosystem

LIRR Strike Threatens Commuters as Unions and MTA Clash Over Pay

SAIL BRNL

Federal officials and New York Governor Kathy Hochul are urging the Long Island Rail Road unions and the MTA to resume bargaining to avoid a prolonged strike that has shut down the region’s largest commuter rail system. The dispute centers on workers’ demands for higher wages and better health‑care costs amid rising living expenses, while the MTA warns that meeting those demands could force fare hikes and strain its budget. Both sides face pressure to find a compromise that protects commuters’ access to affordable service and addresses the employees’ cost‑of‑living concerns

Jane Street Doubles Singapore Office Capacity Amid Asian Expansion

BCG

Jane Street Group moved into a 24,000‑sq‑ft office in Singapore’s IOI Central Boulevard Towers, increasing its seating to 250 and adding lease options. The move follows a shift from a co‑working space, aims to tap regional trading opportunities, and coincides with a hiring push for software, cybersecurity, and data‑center talent. Samsung Faces Potential AI‑Boom Strike Over Bonus Disparities

BEL

Over 45,000 Samsung workers are poised to launch an 18‑day strike from May 21, driven by a dispute over bonus allocations between the lucrative memory chip division and the loss‑making logic and foundry units that produce AI chips. The company proposes bonuses for memory staff of up to 607 % of annual salary, while logic employees would receive only 50‑100 %. Unions demand the removal of a 50 % bonus cap and a 15 % share of operating profit for all staff, warning that the gap threatens talent loss, hurts Samsung’s profitability (potential $14‑$21 billion hit), and could disrupt global supply chains, prompting concern from the South Korean government and investors
