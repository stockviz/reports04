US Opposes Adani’s Plan to Shift Cargo Flights to Navi Mumbai Airport

ADANIENPP1

The US Department of Transportation has warned India that Adani Airport Holdings’ directive to move freight carriers, including FedEx, from Mumbai’s main airport to the new Navi Mumbai International Airport may breach the US‑India Air Transport Agreement. The DOT cautioned that forcing American airlines to relocate could trigger adverse measures. Adani’s move, framed as a temporary response to refurbishment work reducing cargo capacity by about 25%, is seen by US officials as an effort to boost traffic at the new airport, raising concerns over the conglomerate’s growing control of India’s aviation infrastructure. FedEx, the sole US cargo airline at Mumbai, could lose prime slot rights. The dispute has diplomatic implications amid broader US‑India relations and ongoing scrutiny of Gautam Adani’s business empire

Racing Driver Jehan Daruvala Swaps Track for Family Business

RELIANCE STERTOOLS SWSOLAR BCG

Indian racer Jehan Daruvala, 27, has left professional motorsport to join his family’s Sterling and Wilson Group, focusing on data‑centre and diesel‑genset projects. After a stint in Formula E and a successful Formula 2 career, he is now gaining hands‑on experience across the group’s operations, shadowing his father and working on sales and project lifecycle tasks. The move reflects both personal choice and the challenges of securing sponsorship for an Indian driver in motorsport. His sister Delna, also a fourth‑generation scion, entered the firm earlier and now heads corporate operations. Sterling and Wilson, founded in 1927 and now a major player in renewable energy and data‑centre construction, recently restructured after a debt crisis and a 40% stake sale to Reliance Industries. The Daruvalas’ entry signals continuity for the family‑owned conglomerate

Brands Pivot to Retention and Brand‑Building as Digital Ad Costs Surge

EIFFL CELLO BCG HTMEDIA

Consumer brands are reworking e‑commerce strategies to counter rising digital ad expenses and diminishing incremental reach. Rather than relying on costly marketplace ads and aggressive discounting, they are allocating spend to owned websites, brand‑building channels (YouTube, Instagram), and customer retention. Companies are testing campaigns, scaling only what works, and emphasizing channel quality, cohort behavior, and long‑term value over pure acquisition metrics. Efficiency is key: acquisition costs have risen 10‑15%, prompting disciplined budget allocation, first‑party data use, AI‑led optimization, and exploration of quick commerce. The shift reflects a broader move from scale‑at‑all‑costs to smarter, execution‑focused growth

Pittsburgh Post‑Gazette Saved by Nonprofit Buyer

MAXHEALTH INFOMEDIA PARACABLES

The Pittsburgh Post‑Gazette, slated to close on May 3, has been purchased by the nonprofit Venetoulis Institute for Local Journalism, which also runs the digital Baltimore Banner. The deal keeps the paper operating, with print editions on Thursdays and Sundays and a website on other days. Financial terms weren’t disclosed. Former executive editor David Shribman joins the institute’s board. The acquisition averts a shutdown that would have left Pittsburgh without a city‑based newspaper, and aims to sustain local journalism through nonprofit support, though questions remain about staffing and investment levels

SumUp Payments Ltd. is preparing for a potential initial public offering, appointing Deutsche Bank, Goldman Sachs, Jefferies and JPMorgan as advisers and favouring London as the primary venue, though Amsterdam, Frankfurt and New York remain options.
