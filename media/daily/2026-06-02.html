India’s Cotton Duty Cut Sparks Export Optimism, but Domestic Shift to Synthetics Persists

GLOBAL

The CNBC TV18 Prime segment discusses the recent removal of the 11% import duty on cotton, highlighting industry leaders’ enthusiasm for the policy change. Prabhu Dhamodaran of the India Tech Sprint News Federation stresses that lower duties will improve raw material competitiveness, stabilize prices, and enable timely imports, potentially boosting cotton garment exports—currently a $20 billion business—especially as FTAs with the EU and UK come into force. He projects a 10‑15% annual growth in apparel exports, aiming toward the government’s $100 billion target by 2030, though achieving this will require significant investment and policy stability. Despite India’s status as a major cotton producer, domestic consumption favors cheaper synthetic blends due to cotton’s premium price, resulting in a roughly 70 % synthetic vs. 30 % cotton split in the local market. The industry hopes the duty relaxation and upcoming trade agreements will double export shares in key markets over the next few years, while acknowledging that price volatility tied to oil and geopolitical tensions remains a risk
