Macquarie, Blackstone and Others Eye Controlling Stake in Welspun New Energy

WELCORP

Global private‑equity firms and strategic investors such as Macquarie, Actis, Blackstone and Sembcorp are preparing bids for a controlling stake in Welspun New Energy, the renewable‑energy arm of the Welspun Group, at a valuation around $250 million—significantly higher than the $100 million valuation reported three months ago. The company, which currently operates a modest 12.7 MW of rooftop and ground‑mount solar assets and is building a pipeline of up to 1 GW of new projects plus green‑hydrogen and pump‑hydro opportunities, seeks to raise capital to achieve its goal of 5 GW of renewable capacity and 2 MTPA of green derivatives by 2030. The sale follows a wave of consolidation in India’s renewable sector, driven by aggressive capacity‑addition targets, strong FDI inflows and the need for long‑duration, low‑cost capital. Large PE and strategic buyers view India as an attractive market for stable cash‑flow platforms and governance upgrades. Binding bids are expected soon, though none of the contacted parties have responded as of press time

Medikabazaar seeks ₹450 crore ($50 million) funding to rebound from 2023‑24 governance scandal

BFINVEST

Medikabazaar, the Indian B2B medical‑supply platform, is raising about ₹450 crore from existing investors (Creaegis, British International Investment, HealthQuad, Ackermans & van Haaren, Rebright Partners) and new private‑equity or individual backers. The round aims to close before FY 2026 ends, with valuation undisclosed (last valued at ~₹700 crore in a 2022 Series D). The company reports FY 2025 revenue of ₹1,600 crore, on track for ₹2,100 crore in FY 2026 and ₹3,000 crore in FY 2027, and has turned EBITDA positive at ₹5 crore versus a ₹150 crore loss in FY 25. After a whistleblower‑triggered forensic audit uncovered inflated sales and other irregularities, founders Vivek Tiwari and Ketan Malkan were ousted, and an indemnity claim of ₹280 crore by Series C investors is expected to be withdrawn with the new raise. New capital will fund digital innovation, deeper category leadership and expansion across India’s fragmented healthcare market, targeting a billion‑dollar scale. The fundraising coincides with strong overall investment activity in India’s healthcare sector
