Macquarie, Blackstone and Others Eye Controlling Stake in Welspun New Energy

WELCORP

Global private‑equity firms and strategic investors such as Macquarie, Actis, Blackstone and Sembcorp are preparing bids for a controlling stake in Welspun New Energy, the renewable‑energy arm of the Welspun Group, at a valuation around $250 million—significantly higher than the $100 million valuation reported three months ago. The company, which currently operates a modest 12.7 MW of rooftop and ground‑mount solar assets and is building a pipeline of up to 1 GW of new projects plus green‑hydrogen and pump‑hydro opportunities, seeks to raise capital to achieve its goal of 5 GW of renewable capacity and 2 MTPA of green derivatives by 2030. The sale follows a wave of consolidation in India’s renewable sector, driven by aggressive capacity‑addition targets, strong FDI inflows and the need for long‑duration, low‑cost capital. Large PE and strategic buyers view India as an attractive market for stable cash‑flow platforms and governance upgrades. Binding bids are expected soon, though none of the contacted parties have responded as of press time

BANKBARODA STANLEY

Medikabazaar seeks ₹450 crore ($50 million) funding to rebound from 2023‑24 governance scandal

BFINVEST

BlueStar’s Data‑Center Outlook: Strong Order Book, Seasonal Challenges, and Growth Prospects

INTERARCH SCHNEIDER BLUESTARCO

Bluestar Managing Director Bitya Gauraj discussed the firm’s performance and outlook. January showed a sharp acceleration in order inflows, signaling early demand recovery despite a muted start after a major primary sale in December. Inventory levels are easing and warmer night temperatures are expected to boost secondary demand. The company’s total electro‑mechanical (MEP) order book stands at around ₹1,500 crore, with the data‑center segment contributing roughly the same amount and representing a market opportunity of ₹3,000‑₹4,000 crore that could double in the near term. Bluestar sees a long‑term CAGR of about 19 % for the sector and expects the summer season to solidify growth, provided hot nights persist for a few weeks. Capacity constraints are being addressed under the PLA scheme, though overall industry capacity (≈25 million units) exceeds the current market size (≈16‑17 million). Competition from global players such as Vertiv and Schneider is acknowledged; Bluestar’s current share in data‑center EPC work is under 10 %, but it is expanding its product range (e.g., chillers, liquid cooling). Pricing pressure is present, with price hikes ranging from 5‑10 % due to commodity costs, which the company hopes to sustain through the summer. Climate variability was noted as a risk factor, emphasizing the need for weather‑proofing both B2C and B2B operations

AI‑Driven Evolution of Advertising: Insights from WPP Media’s Prashant Kumar

NDTV HTMEDIA

In a 2026 interview, Prashant Kumar, CEO of WPP Media South Asia, explains how artificial intelligence is reshaping every stage of the advertising value chain—from consumer insights and activation to real‑time measurement. He stresses that AI is not a finished project but an ongoing, co‑created process that enhances speed, agility, and relevance across fragmented media landscapes. WPP’s internal platforms (WPP Open and proprietary agents) already embed AI to optimise media buying, performance tracking and campaign management, enabling brands to reach the right consumers efficiently. Kumar notes a shift from pure reach to data‑driven personalization and performance marketing, while still recognising the enduring need for long‑term brand building. He highlights tightening data ecosystems, the rise of the “orange economy” (events, music, arts) and the expanding role of digital‑out‑of‑home, influencer, and commerce formats. Trust remains a core brand value, bolstered by authentic, AI‑personalised content. For emerging talent, Kumar advises curiosity, continuous learning, and mastery of prompting and AI tools, alongside traditional creative skills. Looking ahead to 2027, he predicts accelerated digital demand‑generation, deeper ecosystem integration, and a balanced blend of automation and human storytelling as the industry’s headline trend
