One‑Time Gene‑Silencing Injection Could Permanently Lower Cholesterol

Eli Lilly is testing a single‑dose therapy that switches off a liver gene responsible for “bad” cholesterol. In early trials with 35 patients with genetic hypercholesterolemia, the highest dose cut LDL by about 62 % for up to 18 months—better and longer‑lasting than current drugs. The approach targets younger patients with lifelong high cholesterol, aiming to eliminate the need for daily medication. Safety has improved over earlier versions, but the treatment still faces several years of FDA review and will likely carry a high price tag (≈$50 k) if approved

ONGC

Massachusetts Ride‑Hailing Drivers Unionize Amid Pay, Safety, and Automation Concerns

Drivers for Uber and Lyft in Massachusetts have become the first in the nation to certify a union, the App Drivers Union, after a 2024 ballot measure allowed independent‑contractor ride‑hailing workers to collectively bargain. The move, hailed as the largest private‑sector organizing win since 1941, aims to secure better pay, protection from sudden deactivations, and greater job stability for roughly 70,000 state drivers. Organizers view unionization as a way to counter rising costs, opaque algorithmic pay, and the threat of autonomous vehicle expansion. Uber and Lyft have pledged to work with the new union and regulators as broader ride‑hailing reforms are considered
