ONGC Q4 FY26 Profit Surge Amid Higher Crude Prices

ONGC

ONGC posted a 53% YoY rise in consolidated net profit to ₹13,678 cr in Q4 FY26, driven by higher crude prices, while gross revenue reached ₹1.74 lakh cr. Expenses rose to ₹1.61 lakh cr. Standalone net profit grew ~15%, but crude oil output fell to 4.449 mt and gas to 4.78 bcm, impacted by reservoir issues and West Asia pipeline disruptions. The company wrote off ₹4,876.75 cr in exploration wells. Net realisation from nominated fields improved 6% YoY to $78.32/barrel, though annual realisation fell 11% to $68.40/barrel; JV realisation rose 5% to $77.87/barrel but fell 9% annually. The board proposes a 20% final dividend (₹1 per share) and a total FY26 dividend of 265% (₹13.25 per share), including an interim ₹15,411 cr payout. ONGC is accelerating projects worth ₹33,075 cr in Western Offshore, with early success at Mumbai High after onboarding BP, aiming to boost future production

Massachusetts Ride‑Hailing Drivers Unionize Amid Pay, Safety, and Automation Concerns

Drivers for Uber and Lyft in Massachusetts have become the first in the nation to certify a union, the App Drivers Union, after a 2024 ballot measure allowed independent‑contractor ride‑hailing workers to collectively bargain. The move, hailed as the largest private‑sector organizing win since 1941, aims to secure better pay, protection from sudden deactivations, and greater job stability for roughly 70,000 state drivers. Organizers view unionization as a way to counter rising costs, opaque algorithmic pay, and the threat of autonomous vehicle expansion. Uber and Lyft have pledged to work with the new union and regulators as broader ride‑hailing reforms are considered
