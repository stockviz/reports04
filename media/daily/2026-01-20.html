Davos 2026: Manish Kejriwal on the New World Order, AI Boom & Effect On Markets 2026-01-20 07:00

LENSKART VISHAL VIJAYA

Manish Kejriwal emphasized that India’s market is essentially domestic; most of his firm’s investments are in Indian financial‑services and consumer companies whose revenues are 95 % home‑grown (e.g., Lenskart, Vishal Megamart).

He contrasted this with the U.S., where the AI boom—driven by data‑centre and power‑sector spending—is propping up the economy, but warned that the broader U.S. economic health remains weak.

Geopolitically, Kejriwal said the world still relies on multilateral institutions (U.N., Security Council) to curb unilateral actions such as U.S. moves on Greenland or other power plays, and that a “might‑is‑right” mindset threatens global stability.

Regarding India’s future, he noted:

  • India is the world’s fourth‑largest economy and a massive consumer/ talent pool (≈ 1/7 of global population).
  • Global capital is shifting from China toward India, Japan and Australia, creating a diversification tailwind.
  • The domestic market is still at the “tip of the iceberg,” especially in financial services (mortgages, asset management, insurance) where penetration is low but growth potential huge.

Key sectors he sees as “red‑hot”:

  1. Financial services – lending, insurance, asset‑management; a proxy for macro growth (7‑8 % GDP → 15 % FS growth).
  2. Consumer – continued demand across offline/online channels, especially in tier‑2/3 cities.
  3. Tech services/AI infrastructure – investing in “shovels” like Impetus, which prepares data for AI, rather than competing directly with global AI giants.
  4. Healthcare & services – diagnostics, fertility clinics, and other health‑service chains with strong unit‑economics focus.

He stressed the need for strong management teams, profitable scaling, and a balanced offline‑online playbook.

When asked for a line to Trump, Kejriwal’s one‑word answer was: “Take a break.”
