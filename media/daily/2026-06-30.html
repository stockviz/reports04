Delhi’s New EV Policy Targets 30% Fleet Electrification by 2030, Boosting Incentives and Infrastructure

TATAMOTORS M&M

The Delhi cabinet approved an EV policy effective July 1, 2026, allocating up to ₹15,000 crore—₹7,000 crore for purchase incentives and ₹8,000 crore for charging infrastructure and tax breaks. The plan aims for 30% electrification of the city’s vehicle fleet by 2030, bans new petrol/CNG registrations for three‑wheelers from Jan 1 2027 and two‑wheelers from Jan 1 2028, and offers up to ₹50,000 incentive for three‑wheelers, ₹30,000 for two‑wheelers, plus zero road tax/registration for private EVs priced ≤₹30 lakh. Brokers see major beneficiaries as Mahindra, Tata Motors, TVS, Bajaj Auto, Ather Energy, Ola Electric and Sona BLW, while hybrid‑focused OEMs may face headwinds. Execution challenges remain due to Delhi’s borders with Haryana and Uttar Pradesh, and broader impact depends on similar policies in neighboring states

Vedanta Oil’s Ambitious Push to Reverse a Decade‑Long Production Decline

VEDL VOGL VISL VAML VEDPOWER

Vedanta Oil and Gas Ltd (VOGL) plans to double output to over 150,000 boepd by FY29, investing $700 million in FY27 to revive ageing wells and expand exploration. The strategy hinges on scaling alkaline‑surfactant‑polymer (ASP) flooding—projected to lift recovery from 41% to 60%—and new development across six operating units, including deep‑water and coastal assets. Analysts flag the ASP recovery targets as optimistic and warn of execution risks in exploration and cost‑reduction goals. Production has fallen from 211,000 boepd in FY15 to 87,200 boepd in FY26; the company aims to cut unit‑costs to $10‑13 per barrel and boost EBITDA to $961 million by FY29. Share price has slipped since listing, reflecting market scepticism over the ambitious turnaround

CCI closes anti‑competitive practices case against druggists body, pharma firms

SPARC TORNTPHARM PFIZER

The Competition Commission of India dismissed a long‑standing investigation into alleged anti‑competitive conduct by the All India Organisation of Chemists & Druggists (AIOCD) and its affiliates, finding no evidence of mandatory NOCs/LOCs, compulsory PIS charges, margin fixing, or systematic boycotts against companies such as Sun Pharma, Pfizer, GSK and Torrent. The regulator concluded the evidence was insufficient to prove a Section 3(3)(b) agreement, and therefore cleared all parties, including pharma firms and association office‑bearers, of liability

Apple iPhone 18 Pro Supplier List Leaked in Tata Electronics Ransomware Attack

BAJAJINDEF CELLO BEL

A ransomware breach at Indian contract manufacturer Tata Electronics has exposed confidential files on the dark web, revealing detailed supplier mappings for Apple’s upcoming iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max. The leak includes component design documents, photos of grey slab‑shaped handsets undergoing drop tests, and data linking specific chips, batteries, and cameras to their manufacturers—including TSMC, Qualcomm and others. The breach threatens Apple’s tightly guarded supply‑chain relationships, could aid rivals and counterfeiters, and undermines the partnership that underpins Apple’s shift of production to India.
