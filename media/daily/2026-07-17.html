India’s First Hydrogen‑Powered Train Launched by PM Modi

TVVISION

Prime Minister Narendra Modi flagged off a 10‑coach, 2,600‑passenger hydrogen fuel‑cell train running between Jind and Soni Bhatt, covering 356 km at 75 km/h. The indigenously built train uses 1,200 kW fuel‑cell cars (total 2,400 kW), emits only water vapor, and includes advanced leak detection and fire‑safety systems. This makes India the latest member of a select group of nations operating hydrogen locomotives, advancing its rail sector’s net‑zero goals while the PM also announced large infrastructure projects worth over ₹39,500 crore in Haryana, Chandigarh, and Punjab

Adani Defence Systems to Fully Acquire Flight Simulation Firm FSSPL

ADANIENPP1

Adani Defence Systems & Technologies Ltd (ADSTL), a wholly‑owned subsidiary of Adani Enterprises, signed a share purchase agreement to buy the remaining 44.6% stake in Flight Simulation Solutions Pvt Ltd (FSSPL), raising its holding from 55.4% to 100%. The deal will also make ADSTL’s effective stake in FSSPL’s subsidiary Flight Simulation Technique Centre Pvt Ltd (FSTC) rise from 72.8% to full ownership. The cash transaction is based on an enterprise value of ₹820 crore for FSTC and is expected to close within two months. The acquisition aims to broaden ADSTL’s presence in aviation services; FSSPL and its subsidiaries provide integrated pilot‑training services and FSTC is approved by DGCA and EASA. FSSPL reported consolidated revenues of ₹235 crore in FY‑26, compared with ₹240 crore in FY‑25 and ₹215 crore in FY‑24. The filing states the deal is not a related‑party transaction and requires no governmental or regulatory approvals

Major Indian Conglomerates Vie for Odisha’s Karlapat Bauxite Block

FEDDERSHOL ADANIENPP1 COALINDIA VAML HINDALCO RELIANCE NATIONALUM

Billionaires Gautam Adani, Mukesh Ambani, Kumar Mangalam Birla, Naveen Jindal and Anil Agarwal are among the suitors for the Karlapat bauxite block in Odisha, one of India’s largest untapped bauxite reserves (over 200 million tonnes on 3,100 ha). The state put the block up for auction on 29 May along with ten other virgin mineral blocks. Companies that bought tender documents include Adani Enterprises, Reliance Industries, Hindalco, Vedanta Aluminium, Jindal‑backed Powercem and Coal India, signalling intent though bids are not yet confirmed. The intense interest reflects aluminium producers’ need for captive raw material supplies amid capacity expansions and rising demand from infrastructure, renewable energy, EVs and aerospace. Adani plans a $11.5 bn, 2 mtpa aluminium project in Odisha, while Vedanta aims to reach 6 mtpa and Hindalco is adding 360 kpta. Karlapat’s strategic importance and proximity to a wildlife sanctuary have kept it undeveloped, and its reserve price includes a 35 % premium, with expectations of even higher final bids. Odisha has a history of aggressive bauxite auctions, with winning premiums between 72 % and 127 %. The state also offers other blocks such as Nunapaimali and three composite licences for exploration

Pfizer halts Premarin vaginal cream in India amid supply issues, shrinking estrogen therapy options for menopausal women

PFIZER TORNTPHARM

Pfizer has temporarily discontinued Premarin vaginal cream in India due to supply challenges, affecting over 620,000 units sold in the past year and representing 37% of the country’s conjugated estrogen market. The cream treats vaginal dryness and other menopause symptoms, and its absence leaves patients with few alternatives—primarily Evalon, an estriol-based cream. With menopause awareness and treatment demand rising, the shortage highlights limited options in a market projected to grow to $604.6 million by 2033, despite an estimated 400 million Indian women aged 45+ by 2026
