NTSB Calls for Realistic Smoke‑in‑Cockpit Training

ABMINTLLTD

The National Transportation Safety Board urges airlines to adopt realistic smoke‑in‑cockpit simulations for pilots after a Southwest flight crew struggled with severe smoke following a bird strike. Proxy Advisors Recommend Voting Against Exxon’s Texas Redomiciling and Chevron Board Choices

Proxy advisory firms Glass Lewis and ISS have urged shareholders to oppose Exxon Mobil’s plan to move its incorporation from New Jersey to Texas, citing potential restrictions on stockholder rights, and to reject former Utah Governor Jon Huntsman Jr.’s re‑election to Chevron’s board due to conflicts with his other roles. ISS also backs a proposal for Exxon to expand retail voting options, while Glass Lewis supports an independent board chair at Chevron. Both oil majors face heightened scrutiny over climate and human‑rights related shareholder proposals ahead of their May 27 annual meetings
