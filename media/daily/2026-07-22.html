TCS AHL MSPL DELTACORP JSWSTEEL BCG ADANIPORTS

Indian conglomerates such as Adani, JSW Steel and TCS are increasingly securing foreign equity partners before large assets are built, moving away from a debt‑heavy, internal‑cash model. Partnerships with UAE’s IHC, South Korea’s POSCO, US fund TPG and MSC aim to share development risk, bolster balance sheets, bring technology and off‑take links, and improve bankability for capital‑intensive sectors like aluminium, steel, data centres and ports. President Trump has announced a 0% tariff on imported generic medicines for two years starting Aug 1, followed by a 100% tariff for the third year and 200% thereafter. The steep future tariffs could hurt Indian pharma firms reliant on US exports, especially those without US manufacturing (e.g., Alkem, Torrent, Biocon). Industry analysts await clarification and the policy’s implementation, noting the timing aligns with the end of Trump’s term, adding to market uncertainty
