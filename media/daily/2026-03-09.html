Crude Oil Surge’s Ripple Effect on India’s Economy and Key Sectors

CASTROLIND GULFOILLUB ONGC OIL BPCL VEDL

Brent crude has jumped 65% in a week to about $119/barrel, the highest since the Russia‑Ukraine war, pushing YTD gains above 80% – the strongest rise since 1997. With 85% of India’s oil import‑dependent demand, each $10 rise widens the current‑account deficit by 0.4‑0.5% of GDP, adds 30‑50 bps to CPI and ~80 bps to WPI, and could trim GDP growth by ~15 bps.

Sector impacts
- Oil marketing (IOCL, BPCL, HPCL): Higher crude cuts marketing margins (now ~‑20%); a ₹0.5/liter margin change can cut EBITDA by 7‑11%.
- Upstream producers (ONGC, Oil India, Vedanta, HOC): Higher crude lifts earnings – a $5 rise boosts EPS by 7‑12%, provided no windfall tax.
- Aviation & logistics: Fuel/derivative cost ≈40% of input, squeezing margins.
- Lubricants (Gulf Oil, Castrol): ≈50% of raw material cost tied to crude, margin pressure if costs aren’t passed on.
- Tyres, synthetic rubber, carbon black, chemicals: Raw‑material prices linked to crude, threatening profitability.
- Cement: 40‑50% of input basket linked to crude, margin erosion likely.
- Specialty chemicals: Similar exposure to crude derivatives; upside only if windfall tax is avoided.

Overall, the crude price spike threatens inflation, current‑account balance, and growth, while affecting a wide range of sectors beyond traditional oil marketers
