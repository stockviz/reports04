TPG, Sofina and Multiples vie for $80‑100 million stake in Giva as company targets ₹6,000 cr valuation

EDELWEISS BLUESTONE 5PAISA

Private‑equity firms TPG, Sofina and Multiples are in the final stage of negotiations to invest $80‑100 million in jewellery brand Giva, valuing it at about ₹6,000 cr. The round will blend primary and secondary capital, helping Giva fund new stores, inventory and manufacturing, especially its fast‑growing lab‑grown diamond line. This follows a ₹530 cr Series C raise in June that placed the firm at a ₹4,800 cr valuation. Founded in 2019, Giva now operates over 240 stores and plans up to 150 more, focusing on Tier‑II cities, while the Indian lab‑grown diamond market is projected to expand at 15 % CAGR over the next decade. Existing investors may exit or reinvest, and the deal reflects strong demand for stakes in the brand amid a broader shift toward lab‑grown diamonds in India’s organised jewellery sector
