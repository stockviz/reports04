NCLAT Overturns Ligare Aviation Insolvency, Declares Religare Loan a Sham Round‑Trip Transaction

AHL INDIGO RELIGARE CHEVIOT

The National Company Law Appellate Tribunal set aside the NCLT’s admission of a Section‑7 insolvency petition by Religare Enterprises against Ligare Aviation Ltd, ruling that the alleged loan was merely a round‑tripping of funds and not a genuine financial debt. The tribunal held that the MoU for a Rs 5 crore loan at 13% interest was a “sham one‑pager” created to disguise fraudulent money‑layering within the group of companies controlled by the Singh brothers. It found that the Rs 3.6 crore transferred in March 2009 was immediately remitted to another group entity, proving there was no time‑value‑of‑money component required under the IBC. NCLAT faulted the NCLT for not examining the transaction’s nature, rejected reliance on the FIR filed by Religare Finvest, and concluded that no financial debt existed, thus the CIRP could not be lawfully initiated. The order also noted related arbitration awards, asset attachments, and Supreme Court directives, but affirmed that the insolvency proceedings against Ligare Aviation were improper
