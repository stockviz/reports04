SBI Mulls Mortgage‑Backed Securities to Diversify Funding Amid Slower Deposit Growth

BANKINDIA TCNSBRANDS PNB ICRA DAMCAPITAL LICHSGFIN SBIN

State Bank of India is exploring securitization of part of its ~₹10 trillion home‑loan portfolio to create a new liquidity source as deposit growth lags loan expansion. Chairman C.S. Setty highlighted the need to shift from deposit‑funded liabilities to market instruments like bonds and securitized assets. While India’s RMBS market is nascent, recent regulatory steps and the listing of the first mortgage‑backed securities signal growing support. Challenges include modest yields relative to sovereigns, prepayment risk, and limited investor appetite, though high‑quality assets may attract long‑term institutions such as insurers and pension funds. SBI’s move could deepen the market and boost sentiment, though timelines and structuring details remain undefined
