Air India Trims International Network Amid Fuel Surge and Airspace Restrictions

TENNIND

Air India is cutting roughly half its international schedule, dropping from about 2,500 flights a month in April 2025 to an expected 1,200 by summer 2026. The airline has suspended Chicago, paused New York‑Mumbai for the summer, and is consolidating U.S. service on Delhi‑JFK while boosting Mumbai‑New York. Southeast Asian capacity is slashed – Delhi‑Singapore and Mumbai‑Singapore flights are halved, Chennai‑Singapore is suspended, and services to Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur and Hanoi are reduced. Delhi‑Kathmandu flights are cut by 50%, Paris routes are halved, and other European cities are trimmed. Australia’s Delhi‑Melbourne and Delhi‑Sydney frequencies fall to four weekly flights. Record jet‑fuel prices (≈60% of costs) and the Pakistan airspace closure (adding time, cost and a ₹22,000 crore loss) drive the rationalisation, with further cuts possible if pressures persist

How Indian Investors Can Access Global Stocks

KOTAKBANK EDELWEISS INDNIPPON

Indian investors can gain exposure to international markets through three main avenues: 1) International mutual fund FoFs, which are easy to use but face a SEBI‑imposed $7 bn cap that has already been reached, causing many popular funds to halt new inflows. 2) Direct purchase of US equities via the RBI’s Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS), allowing up to $250 k per FY, with no SEBI cap but subject to TCS on remittances above ₹2  lakh. 3) Gift City funds regulated by IFSCA, which bypass the SEBI cap, offer tax efficiency, and require higher minimum investments (≈$5 k). Each route has trade‑offs in cost, control, tax, and accessibility, and investors should treat international exposure as a long‑term (7‑10 year) bet rather than a quick gain
