JBM Auto’s Aggressive EV Push Boosts Revenue but Stresses Cash Flow and Balance Sheet

JBMA

JBM Auto has renamed its OEM division as the EV Business, marking a strategic shift toward electric mobility. FY26 revenue grew 4.1% to ₹6,088 cr, with the EV segment up 16.2% to ₹2,307 cr (38% of total sales). The company now controls about 79% of India’s electric bus market and over 50% of intercity electric luxury coaches. However, trade receivables more than doubled to ₹2,185 cr, operating cash flow fell 59.4% to ₹160 cr, and borrowings rose 61.4% to ₹2,070 cr, raising financing and cash‑conversion concerns. EBITDA improved 24.8% to ₹266.5 cr, but profit attributable to owners fell 24.2% in Q4 FY26. Analysts are split: some see a structural moat and margin upside, while others warn of rising leverage and working‑capital strains. JBM is eyeing a $500 million fundraise to fund an order book of ~10,000 buses, expected to double, making FY27 a critical test of its ability to turn market share into sustainable cash generation

Gold Import Duty Hike: Impact on Consumers, Jewelers, and Markets

KALYANKJIL TITAN SENCO

The Indian government announced a late‑night increase in import duties on gold, silver, platinum and related jewelry components, raising the basic duty to 6% and the cess to 5%, resulting in an effective 15% duty—up from the previous 6‑9%. This follows Prime Minister Modi’s appeal to curb gold purchases for a year to address the current‑account deficit, which is heavily weighted by gold imports (≈90%). The higher duty is expected to reduce gold imports by 5‑10%, push MCX gold prices up (≈₹12 lakh per kg) and increase silver prices (≈₹22,000 per kg). Jewelers may see a short‑term drop in sales volume but could benefit from higher margins and increased gold‑exchange activity, as old gold can be recycled without duty. Stock valuations of major jewelry firms (Titan, Kalyan, Senko) have fallen sharply (10‑15%) over the past two sessions. The duty hike also risks boosting smuggling to lower‑duty markets like Dubai. Overall, consumers will face higher buying costs, the industry gains policy clarity, and investors should watch import volumes, exchange activity, and jewelry stock performance

Aditya Birla Health Leverages Wellness Incentives to Cut Claims Ratios and Boost Growth

ABCAPITAL

The insurer is positioning itself as a health‑behaviour influencer, rewarding policyholders for active, healthy lifestyles through its Active Health app and wellness‑first products. By nudging low‑risk customers to stay healthy and intervening with high‑risk ones, it has reduced loss ratios (94% for moderately active, 75% for highly active versus 100% for inactive) and improved persistence by over 11%. This strategy, coupled with GST removal on policies, has driven a 39% YoY rise in gross written premium to ₹6,855 cr, a market‑share gain to 13.7%, and a 13% rise in net profit to ₹3,764 cr in FY26. Around 40% of policyholders now engage with the insurer on health, and 11% receive rewards for good health, up from 5‑6% two years ago. The wellness model is also being extended to the group‑health segment, which accounts for 45% of business, with flexible, inflation‑proof plans like Activ Yuva offering premium refunds and comprehensive benefits. The company asserts that the investment in wellness incentives will continue to lower claims and sustain profitability

Gold & Silver Imports Face Higher Duty After PM’s Appeal to Defer Purchases

PCJEWELLER TITAN KALYANKJIL

India has doubled customs duties on gold, silver and platinum imports to 10%, raising the effective tax on gold and silver from 6% to 15% (basic duty 10% + cess 5%). The change, effective May 13, also adds ~5% duty on jewelry findings, 5.4% on platinum findings, and 4.4% on catalyst/ash imports containing precious metals. Gold imports in FY 25‑26 rose 24% in value but fell 4% in volume. The hike follows PM Modi’s request that citizens postpone gold buying for a year to protect foreign‑exchange reserves. Higher import costs will raise raw‑material and working‑capital expenses for jewelry makers, squeeze margins, and could temper demand during the festive and wedding season. Stock‑market pressure is expected on jewelry companies such as Titan, Senco, Kalyan, Thangam, and PC Jeweller

The InvIT, launched in July 2024 with a market cap of ~₹2,800 cr, holds six SPVs covering 2,000+ lane‑km of toll and annuity roads across northern and western India.
