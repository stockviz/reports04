Indian Oil’s Q4 Profit Soars 56% Amid Strong Margins and Record Operations

IOC

Indian Oil Corp reported a 56% rise in Q4 net profit to Rs 11,377.51 crore, driven by robust marketing and refining margins before the full impact of the West Asia conflict. FY26 net profit hit a record Rs 36,802.42 crore. The company achieved record crude throughput (75.4 MMT), pipeline throughput (105.3 MMT), and sales volume (104.4 MMT). It added 909 retail outlets, saw lubricants sales jump 15% to 855 kT, petrochemical sales reach 3.22 MMT, and RLNG sales at 5.60 MMT. Despite selling fuel below cost in March to shield the domestic market, the strong operational performance underpinned the profit surge, with the full impact of the conflict expected in the next quarter

Jaguar Land Rover Overhauls Governance to Accelerate Turnaround

TMPV TATAMOTORS PENINLAND NSIL

Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) has reduced its board to three members and created a 13‑member executive committee led by CEO P.B. Balaji to speed up decision‑making. The changes, effective 1 April 2026, aim to cut costs, improve operational agility, and support a $1.7 billion savings plan as JLR faces a steep revenue and profit decline, volume drop, cyber‑attack costs, and higher US tariffs. New appointments include a group chief strategy officer, global chief procurement officer, and chief information & digital officer, while several senior leaders have exited. The restructuring is intended to help JLR meet its EV launch goals and restore profitability for Tata Motors’ passenger‑vehicle unit

Tata Steel opts for solo expansion in India, shunning joint ventures

Tata Steel will increase its domestic capacity to 40 mtpa by acquiring existing JV stakes and operating independently, contrasting with JSW Steel’s JV‑driven expansion to 78 mtpa. TCS Links Pay to Office Attendance Amid Modest Salary Hikes

WIPRO INFY TCS

Tata Consultancy Services has introduced a new compensation structure that ties monthly performance pay to office attendance and deployment metrics, shifting the work‑from‑office component from the quarterly bonus to a monthly pay category. While top performers receive up to a 6% salary increase—below the double‑digit hikes previously hinted—average raises are 4.5‑7% and low‑performers face deductions. The salary structure now separates HRA and conveyance allowances, scrapes several benefits, and places gratuity within the total package, reducing take‑home impact. The changes have left employees confused and less enthusiastic, especially as peers like Infosys and Wipro consider their own raise policies

India’s Top Firms Reinstate Remote Work Amid Fuel‑Saving Call

RPGLIFE 3MINDIA HINDUNILVR ITC LT TATAMOTORS RBA INDUSINDBK TCS

Following Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s appeal to curb fuel consumption, major Indian employers are reviving or expanding work‑from‑home policies. The push began with RPG Group chairman Harsh Goenka’s directive to encourage remote work, EV use, carpooling, and virtual meetings. While many companies are merely restating existing flexible‑work arrangements—such as PwC, Deloitte, and Hindustan Unilever—others like IndusInd Bank and BFSI firms have formalized two‑day remote‑work rosters and invested in digital infrastructure. Sector‑specific firms (e.g., L&T) cannot shift to full WFH, opting instead for EV charging stations and travel‑reduction policies. The move reverses a three‑year trend of returning to office spaces, aligning corporate practices with the government’s fuel‑saving and foreign‑exchange conservation goals

Pentagon Media Escort Policy Sparks New Lawsuit

NIACL

The New York Times has filed a second lawsuit against the Defense Department, challenging a Pentagon rule that requires journalists to be escorted while on the premises. The paper argues the policy infringes on First Amendment rights and aims to shut down independent reporting. The Department defends the rule as a lawful security measure. The case follows an earlier lawsuit that temporarily halted prior restrictions, and the legal battle continues as appeals proceed
