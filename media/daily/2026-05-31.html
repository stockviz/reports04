India’s AI Gap: Market Realities, Global Competition, and Economic Implications

ADANIPOWER RELIANCE AIRTELPP GEPIL AMDIND HDFCBANK LGBBROSLTD HFCL IDEAFORGE POWERINDIA ALLDIGI OFSS NETWEB

The panel debated India’s lag in the AI value chain, noting that while global AI capex is dominated by US giants (Microsoft, Amazon, Google) and hardware by Asian firms (TSMC, Samsung, SK‑Hynix), India has no trillion‑dollar AI players and its listed AI exposure is modest. Foreign investors have withdrawn $24 bn from Indian equities in 2026, shifting capital to AI‑heavy markets like Taiwan and Korea, where a few semiconductor firms now command over $1 tn in foreign holdings. Speakers broke down the AI ecosystem into layers—cloud, accelerators, memory, packaging, networking, data‑center build‑out, and power—highlighting India’s scant presence beyond services and software. They argued that India can succeed as a large AI consumer and a hub for data‑centers, leveraging cheap labor and abundant renewable power, but it lacks core chip and foundational‑model IP, making it a net importer of AI value. The discussion also covered potential risks: a slowdown in US AI capex, rising token costs, and the impact on Indian IT services, where AI could both augment productivity and erode jobs. While some see AI as a growth engine, others caution against over‑hyped FOMO, emphasizing that broader structural issues—such as excess Chinese competition and energy costs—must be addressed before India can fully capitalize on the AI supercycle

Triveni Engineering Q4 Profit Declines 8% to ₹167.45 cr

TRIVENI PATANJALI KALPATPOWR

Triveni Engineering & Industries reported an 8% fall in Q4 net profit to ₹167.45 cr, driven by higher sugarcane costs, while total income dropped to ₹1,842.15 cr. The sugar segment remained profitable, but the Power Transmission Business (PTB) saw reduced turnover and profits due to geopolitical disruptions. PTB will be demerged into Triveni Power Transmission Ltd effective 1 April 2026, with accounting impact in FY27. Despite challenges, the distillery segment helped deliver healthy results. The company operates eight sugar mills, a gear manufacturing plant in Mysuru, a water‑treatment unit in Noida, and six cogeneration plants with 104.5 MW capacity

Pat​anjali Foods Q4 Profit Surges 46% to ₹524 cr

KOTAKBANK PATANJALI

Patanjali Foods Ltd reported a 46% jump in Q4 FY‑26 consolidated net profit to ₹523.97 cr, driven by higher sales of cooking oils and other food items. Total income rose to ₹11,212.17 cr from ₹9,564.47 cr YoY. For the full FY‑26, net profit reached ₹1,814.47 cr on revenue of ₹40,347.78 cr, up from ₹1,300.70 cr profit and ₹33,890.68 cr revenue the previous year. The edible‑oil segment remained the primary revenue source, while the FMCG segment grew 19.95% YoY to ₹11,188.25 cr, representing 27.6% of operating revenue. CEO Sanjeev Asthana highlighted strong domestic demand, resilient rural consumption, and a short‑term off‑take uptick in March, attributing the results to effective strategic initiatives

Blinkit’s Edge Over Amazon in India’s Quick‑Commerce Race

ZOMATO SWIGGY

Deepinder Goyal says Blinkit, now half of India’s quick‑commerce market and profitable, outperforms Amazon because it was built around ultra‑fast delivery rather than a broad catalogue. Blinkit focuses on deepening product assortment (≈80,000 SKUs in NCR) and strengthening customer loyalty, rather than rapid geographic expansion. While Amazon has vast resources, its traditional e‑commerce model struggles to match Blinkit’s speed‑first operating model. Goyal also sees strong growth potential in food delivery and believes Blinkit can sustain its lead against global rivals

Google Engineer Charged with Insider Trading on Polymarket Using Confidential Search Data

A senior Google information security engineer, Michele “Miki” Spagnuolo, allegedly accessed nonpublic internal search trend data to predict Google’s Year in Search rankings and placed $2.7 million in bets on the prediction market Polymarket, winning about $1.2 million. Federal investigators traced the winnings back to him after a less careful cryptocurrency withdrawal linked to his personal ID. He has been charged with fraud and money laundering, placed on administrative leave, and the case raises questions about data access controls for high‑ranking tech employees

SoftBank to Invest Up to €75 Billion in French AI Data Centers

SCHNEIDER DELTACORP

SoftBank Group plans to invest as much as €75 billion ($87 bn) to build AI data centers in Le Bosquel and Dunkirk, with an initial €45 billion for two sites slated to be operational by 2028 and 2031. The move follows a proposal from French President Emmanuel Macron and positions France as a European AI hub, targeting over 5 GW of computing capacity and partnering with Schneider Electric. The investment is set to be announced at the Choose France summit
