Reliance Retail’s Profit Boost Driven by Zero‑Coupon Debt Conversion

RELIANCE METROBRAND RCOM RETAIL

In FY 2025‑26, Reliance Retail Ltd (RRL) posted a profit of ₹13,476 crore, up ₹3,503 crore from the prior year, mainly due to a debt restructuring with its parent, Reliance Retail Ventures Ltd (RRVL). RRVL converted about ₹40,000 crore of interest‑bearing inter‑company loans into zero‑coupon optionally fully convertible debentures (OFCDs), eliminating most of RRL’s finance costs (down from ₹4,725 crore to ₹1,510 crore). While RRVL’s interest income fell correspondingly, the consolidation shifted profit to the operating retail company, improving its financial health ahead of a potential retail listing. Additional restructuring saw long‑term loans of ₹5,656 crore repaid and short‑term loans reduced to ₹11,300 crore, with further OFCD issuances of ₹19,000 crore in mid‑2025. The move, described as a deleveraging exercise, lifted RRL’s profitability and underscored the retail segment’s growing contribution to Reliance Industries’ overall earnings

India’s Protein Powder Price Surge: Causes, Infrastructure Gaps, and Emerging Solutions

PARAGMILK

India’s protein market, worth $1.6 bn and growing 18% annually, is facing a sharp price hike—whey concentrate from ₹700/kg to ₹2,700/kg and isolates nearly quadrupling—driven by three converging forces: a global surge in protein demand, higher freight costs from geopolitical tensions and shipping bottlenecks, and a hard limit on cheese‑derived whey supply. Despite being the world’s largest milk producer (239 m tonnes), India imports over 80% of supplement‑grade whey because its dairy system focuses on liquid milk products, not cheese, leaving domestic whey production minimal. This infrastructure gap forces brands to pass 200‑250% input cost increases onto consumers, though some, like YogaBar, pre‑emptively locked prices, and Parag Milk Foods benefitted from early investment in cheese and whey‑filtration plants. The shortage is spurring a shift toward alternative proteins—fermented yeast, plant blends, and yeast‑based isolates—exemplified by SuperU’s unflavored fermented yeast protein gaining consumer traction. By 2026 the protein ingredients market is projected to exceed ₹33,000 cr, rewarding companies that build processing capacity now, while the broader supply chain remains unready, keeping protein prices on an upward trajectory
