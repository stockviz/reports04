United Breweries CEO Outlook: Weather, Affordability, and Policy Wins Boost Growth and Premium Share

UBL

Vivek Gupta highlighted that United Breweries has recovered about 90 % of its old Telangana dues, but current receivables are still rising, prompting ongoing talks with the state. He attributed the recent slowdown to adverse weather and affordability pressures, noting that recent excise policy changes in key states (Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal, Karnataka, Telangana, Rajasthan, Odisha) have stopped beer tax hikes and increased spirit taxes, which should benefit beer volumes. Gupta expects mid‑single‑digit volume growth in Q4 and a “bumper summer” if weather improves, with premium volumes projected to grow over 20 % driven by Henican Silver, Kingfisher Strong Smooth, and expanded state coverage. India’s Power Surge: Transmission Capex Set to Explode Amid Green Transition

JSWENERGY TATAPOWER ADANIENSOL POWERGRID SURANASOL NTPC NLCINDIA

Rupesh Sankar explains that India’s electricity demand, currently around 240‑245 GW, is projected to hit 330 GW in the next 4‑5 years, driven by higher per‑capita use, industry, data centers, green mobility and hydrogen. To meet this, installed capacity must rise from ~500 GW to ~900 GW, prompting renewables firms like Tata Power, JSW Energy and others to double capacity. While generation and renewable growth are strong, transmission bottlenecks—especially right‑of‑way and compensation issues—will drive massive capital spending in T&D. Lower power prices and declining T&D losses benefit the entire value chain, but merchant generators may see limited price upside. Clear winners are high‑voltage transmission firms (Power Grid Corp, Adani Energy Solutions, NLC, CAC), renewable equipment makers, and integrated solar‑glass‑battery players
