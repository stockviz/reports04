RBI’s New Mis‑Selling Rules: Impact on Banks, Insurers and Consumers

HDFCLIFE SBIN

The Reserve Bank introduced strict guidelines to curb coercive bundling and ensure suitability when banks sell financial products such as insurance, pensions and mutual funds. Experts Dinesh Khara and Nilesh Sathe explained that banks may see a modest dip in fee income—currently 20‑30% of non‑interest earnings comes from third‑party product distribution—but the rules target only improper bundling, not legitimate sales. Larger banks that already practice proper profiling will feel minimal impact, while smaller players could lose bundled revenue. Insurers, especially those reliant on bancassurance like Max Life, may experience an initial slowdown, yet the clear definition of mis‑selling and mandated refunds aim to boost long‑term consumer trust and sustainable distribution. The guidelines, open for public comment until 4 March, become effective from 1 July, giving firms time to adjust processes, risk‑profile assessments and audit mechanisms. Stakeholders also highlighted practical issues such as refund responsibilities during the 30‑day free‑look period and the treatment of mutual‑fund exits. Overall, the reforms are seen as a welcome step toward protecting customers, improving transparency, and strengthening the credibility of banks and insurers alike
