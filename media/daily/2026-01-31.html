Budget for Bharat: What India Really Wants In Budget 2026 2026-01-31 12:55

BCG KPIGREEN DIXON LANDMARK TIGERLOGS RELIANCE ENDURANCE

Key take‑aways from the round‑table on “What Bharat Wants from Budget 2026”

  • Start‑ups & tech founders (Bengaluru) – demand clear, globally‑aligned reforms for ESOPs (single‑tax treatment, longer vesting periods), tax incentives for early‑stage funding, and legal safeguards that keep founders’ equity intact during IPOs and hostile take‑overs.

  • Renewable‑energy sector (Gujarat) – seeks a two‑year implementation roadmap for the nation’s gigawatt‑target, strict enforcement of green‑power purchase obligations for large manufacturers, and assurance that climate‑policy goals won’t be softened.

  • Agriculture & agri‑processing (Madhya Pradesh) – wants stronger export incentives, resolution of tariff barriers, and protection of domestic processors from GM‑import pressures; also highlights labour‑scheme (Jee‑Ram‑Ji) impacts on farm‑labour availability.

  • Textiles & apparel (Coimbatore) – welcomes the recent GST cut on non‑cotton inputs (18 % → 5 %) that lifts margins, but calls for additional export‑support measures and clarity on EU regulatory requirements.

  • Real‑estate & tier‑2 growth (Indore, Chennai, Ahmedabad) – notes rapid price appreciation and rising interest from GCC/IT firms, urging consistent land‑use policies and infrastructure backing.

  • Automobile & auto‑components (Pune, other hubs) – stresses the need for higher R&D incentives (current 2‑3 % of sales far below global peers), a stable PLI framework beyond current cycles, and smoother import‑approval processes for specialised equipment amid supply‑chain disruptions.

  • MSMEs & exporters – request short‑term working‑capital relief, simplified tax‑compliance assistance (no need for large in‑house teams), and faster resolution of tax disputes (target ≤ 12 months).

  • Foreign investors & capital markets – call for restoration of long‑term‑capital‑gains relief, parity between equity and fixed‑income taxation, and a decisive, transparent mechanism for settling disputes such as the Tiger‑Global case.

  • Defence & indigenisation – push for additional PLI schemes to accelerate local defence manufacturing and broader “Make‑in‑India” drives.

  • Overall sentiment – across regions, the consensus is for stable, predictable policy, accelerated dispute resolution, targeted PLI incentives, and continued fiscal discipline that sustains the consumption boost sparked by recent income‑tax and GST cuts.
