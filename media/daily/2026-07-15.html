SpiceJet’s Delays Highlight Operational Struggles

INDIGO SPICEJET

In May, one in seven SpiceJet flights was delayed over two hours, the worst among India’s major airlines and 20× higher than IndiGo’s delay rate. The budget carrier’s market share fell to 3% amid a thin fleet, cash crunch, and regulator scrutiny. While SpiceJet acknowledges on‑time performance can vary, it says safety remains a priority and it aims to improve schedule reliability

Indian IKEA Revamps Strategy for Home‑Living Preferences

BCG NILKAMAL SAMHI DLF DAMCAPITAL URBANCO VIJAYA GODREJAGRO

IKEA is reshaping its India playbook by focusing on living‑room and bedroom furniture, reflecting Indian households’ greater investment in these spaces versus kitchens. The retailer plans to double local sourcing to 50%, expand its workforce to about 5,000 by 2030, and target profitability by FY 2028. While maintaining large destination stores, IKEA is adding smaller city‑mall formats in Delhi, Pune, and other tier‑2 cities to meet demand for convenience. It will invest roughly ₹20,000 crore in 20 stores, mixed‑use Ingka Centres, distribution hubs, digital capabilities, and renewable energy projects. Local sourcing will rise, boosting India’s role in IKEA’s global supply chain, though challenges remain in certified wood and manufacturing standards. The shift aims to capture a home‑furnishing market projected to grow from $17 bn in 2025 to $34 bn by 2034

Grasim’s Evolving Role as Birla’s Flagship Holding and Incubator

HINDALCO ADANIENPP1 BIRLACABLE GRASIM ABLBL ABCAPITAL ULTRACEMCO ATHERENERG IDEA PRUDENT MOTILALOFS RELIANCE GODREJIND ABFRL

Grasim Industries, the Aditya Birla Group’s flagship holding, has become the conglomerate’s primary capital‑allocation and incubation platform, a role inherited after the 2017 merger with Aditya Birla Nuvo. It now backs key businesses such as UltraTech Cement, Aditya Birla Capital, Birla Opus paints, Birla Pivot e‑commerce and Aditya Birla Renewables, which acquired Sprng Energy for $1.8 billion, making it a top renewable player. While its standalone viscose fibre and chemicals units account for only about 20% of its $2.1 trillion market cap, most value comes from stakes in group companies, especially UltraTech. Analysts see upside in its core businesses—improved VSF margins, a ramp‑up in epoxy capacity, and renewable‑powered operations—while noting the need for stronger governance as cash flows increasingly fund new ventures. Grasim’s shares are modestly down on the day but up ~10% year‑to‑date, outperforming the Sensex
