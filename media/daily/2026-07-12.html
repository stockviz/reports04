AI‑Driven DRAM Shortage Sparks Antitrust Lawsuit

A new U.S. class‑action lawsuit accuses Samsung, SK Hynix and Micron—who together control about 90% of the global DRAM market—of deliberately cutting production of standard DRAM while favoring high‑margin AI‑oriented HBM chips since 2022, creating an artificial shortage that pushed DRAM prices up as much as 700% in four years. The complaint claims this strategy inflates costs for consumers and businesses, seeking class‑action status and damages. While the firms deny the allegations and past DRAM price‑fixing cases have been dismissed for lack of evidence, the industry’s history of antitrust violations and the AI boom’s demand for memory make the case noteworthy. The outcome remains uncertain pending court proceedings

Supreme Court to Hear SEBI’s Challenge to SAT Relief for SICCL Managers

ZSARACOM

The Supreme Court will hear SEBI’s plea on July 13 challenging a Securities Appellate Tribunal decision that granted relief to four managers and the company secretary of Sahara India Commercial Corporation Ltd (SICCL). The SAT had earlier upheld SEBI’s action against SICCL for issuing Optionally Fully Convertible Debentures (OFCDs) that amounted to a public offer, mobilising about Rs 14,106 crore from 1.98 crore investors between 1998‑2008. While the tribunal dismissed appeals by the company and its directors, it allowed the managers’ appeal, deeming them employees not liable for the company’s actions. SEBI contests this relief, arguing the managers remain responsible under powers of attorney granted by the directors. The Supreme Court has also listed all pending Sahara matters, including investor refunds, for hearing

AI‑Driven Memory Crunch: RAM Prices Set to Stay High Until 2028

RUBICON

Tarun Pathak of Counterpoint Research explains that the surge in AI workloads, especially large language models, has dramatically increased demand for RAM in AI servers, now accounting for over half of global memory allocation. This shift has slashed supply for consumer devices, cutting smartphone memory share from 40% to 20% while raising RAM’s cost share in smartphones from 12% to 45%, making it the most expensive component. As a result, RAM prices are expected to remain elevated, with further hikes likely, and the market won’t see relief until around 2028, when supply catches up. OEMs are focusing on protecting margins and navigating reduced volumes amid this prolonged pricing pressure

Nissan Tekton First Look: Design, Features, and Powertrain Overview

The Nissan Tekton, a C‑segment SUV priced between ₹10.49 L and ₹18.59 L, borrows the Duster’s platform but gets a distinct, square‑ish front inspired by the Patrol, full‑width LED lighting, and rugged SUV styling. It offers 12 variants with two petrol engines – a 1.0 L (100 PS, 166 Nm) paired only with a six‑speed manual, and a 1.3 L turbo (163 PS, 280 Nm) available with a six‑speed DCT or manual. Ground clearance is 212 mm, boot capacity up to 700 L (likely measured to roof), and 18‑inch chunky wheels. Inside, the cabin mirrors the Duster’s layout but adds purple accents, premium soft‑touch surfaces, powered/ventilated seats, dual screens with Google‑based navigation, ADAS, ambient lighting, and a dash‑cam. Standard features include a 360° camera, powered tailgate, hydraulic gas strut, and multiple charging options. The Tekton targets the Indian CSUV market, promises E20‑compatible fuel, and positions itself as a more premium, tech‑rich alternative in its segment
