Ola Electric’s Leadership Flip‑Flops Spark Investor Doubts

OLAELEC

Founder Bhavish Aggarwal’s inconsistent statements on gigafactory capacity, sales break‑even targets, revenue guidance, and distribution strategy have heightened concerns about Ola Electric’s future. Since FY2025, the company has repeatedly revised its gigafactory expansion plans—from a 5 GWh cap to a 20 GWh target, then back to a modest 6 GWh—without clarifying fund allocation. Its store network shrank from 4,000 to 700 outlets amid falling scooter sales, while break‑even volume estimates dropped from 50,000 to 15,000 units per month. Sales have plunged 57 % year‑on‑year, and the firm’s market value has halved, with analysts cutting buy calls and citing cash constraints. Potential upside may hinge on a strategic stake sale in its battery business, but overall investor confidence remains low

Underweight IT Sector Amid Uncertain AI‑Driven Growth, Says BOB Capital’s Girish Pai

TCS INFY SRPL-RE FSL

In a CNBC TV18 interview, BOB Capital Markets analyst Girish Pai explains why his firm remains underweight on Indian IT stocks despite attractive valuations. He notes that Infosys’s and TCS’s investor‑day messages highlight significant AI opportunities, but the sector faces a “deployment gap” where enterprise adoption lags technology advances. Pai points to the uncertainty around cash‑flow and earnings trajectories, the unclear timing of massive job displacement versus creation, and the difficulty of scaling AI productivity gains at the enterprise level. While 45 % of revenues now stem from AI, ROI concerns and high implementation costs keep adoption modest. Consequently, BOB Capital maintains hold calls on tier‑one names (Infosys, TCS, Tech Mahindra) and a buy on Firstsource, but overall recommends underweighting the sector until the AI disruption’s impact on earnings becomes clearer

SBI plans to boost its stake in Investec joint venture to gain majority control

CRISIL ARIHANTCAP SBIN 3MINDIA DHUNINV IIFLCAPS AXISBANK

State Bank of India is evaluating raising its share in the investment‑banking JV with Investec India from about 20% to 51%, aiming for greater control and RBI‑approved supervision amid a boom in India’s equity capital markets. The move follows a prior failed attempt to increase to 40% that was rejected by the RBI. The JV, Investec Capital Services (India) Pvt Ltd, is currently owned 20% by SBI Caps (SBI’s subsidiary) and 80% by Investec India Holdco. The partnership has delivered strong growth, with FY25 revenue of ₹453 cr and profit of ₹119 cr, and is rated AAA by CRISIL. SBI’s articles of association allow it to purchase additional shares from Investec within a stipulated period, and the bank intends to seek RBI approval before formally proposing the stake increase. No formal application has been made yet

AI vs Copyright: Should Generative Content Seek Consent

The panel debated whether AI models must obtain permission before training on copyrighted works. Disney’s lawsuit against ByteDance highlighted Hollywood’s push for consent and compensation, while tech firms argue for innovation freedom. Indian stakeholders discussed the impact on Bollywood and the creator economy, weighing protection of IP against potential stifling of startups. Experts noted the lack of clear legislation, the role of fair‑use, and the need for new business models and licensing frameworks. Consensus emerged that consent will likely become mandatory, but implementation must balance creators’ rights with rapid AI development, possibly through sector‑specific licensing and revenue‑sharing schemes that allow India to shape its own regulatory approach
