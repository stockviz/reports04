Reliance SEZ Export Duty Uncertainty Amid India’s Fuel Tax Overhaul

RELIANCE

India has reintroduced export duties of ₹21.50 per litre on diesel and ₹29.50 per litre on aviation turbine fuel while cutting excise duty on petrol and diesel by ₹10 per litre. The key question is whether Reliance’s SEZ refinery exports will retain the exemption they enjoyed under the 2022 windfall‑tax regime. If exempt, Reliance’s margins stay insulated; if not, diesel and ATF export margins could be sharply compressed. Analysts note that OMCs benefit from the excise cut, reducing marketing losses and raising their breakeven crude price to about $80 a barrel. Export duties translate to roughly $36 per barrel for diesel and $50 per barrel for jet fuel, yet strong product cracks still allow export margins of $15‑25 per barrel. The fiscal impact hinges on the SEZ exemption, with potential revenue loss ranging from ₹80,000 crore to ₹1.5 lakh crore annually. Clarity on the SEZ treatment will be a near‑term catalyst for Reliance’s earnings outlook and government revenue forecasts

Modi to Inaugurate Kaynes Semicon OSAT Facility in Sanand

KAYNES CGPOWER IDEAFORGE

Prime Minister Narendra Modi will open Kaynes Semicons’ 3,300‑crore outsourced semiconductor assembly and test (OSAT) plant in Sanand, Gujarat, on March 31. War in West Asia Sends Gold, Oil and Silver Markets Into Turbulence

ANMOL ATHERENERG

The broadcast examined how the Iran‑Israel conflict has rattled commodities. Gold remains erratic, hovering around $4,400/oz with investors wary of holding positions; Dubai jewellers report minimal retail buying but a modest return of local wholesale demand and a willingness to buy at $4,000/oz. Oil has surged roughly 50% in 2026, with Brent breaching $110 as the Strait of Hormuz stays “very restricted” – only about 130 tankers have crossed since late February, far below pre‑war volumes, prompting fears of a continued supply pinch and price scenarios ranging from $80 to $150 per barrel. Analysts attribute price swings to sentiment and political messaging rather than fundamentals. Silver has slumped about 52% from its peak, trading near ₹2,300‑₹2,500 per kg, with bullion demand down to 10‑15% of normal and jewelry sales also weak, leading to a bleak outlook for the quarter. Overall, the conflict has created a highly volatile, sentiment‑driven environment across precious metals and energy markets

Vedanta’s April‑2026 Demerger Plan

VEDL

Vedanta founder Anil Agarwal confirmed the conglomerate will split into five listed entities—aluminium, zinc, oil & gas, steel and power—by early April, aiming to boost growth and shareholder value. The demerger, expected to double the combined market cap from $27 bn to around $54 bn, will leave the five firms with about $7 bn of debt, while Agarwal’s private holding will retain roughly half the shares in each. The move follows the resolution of a government legal challenge and forms part of a broader strategy to cut debt, diversify financing (including a planned ₹2,575 cr NCD issue), and expand production, especially in oil and gas
