E20 Fuel: Slight Mileage Loss, Faster Acceleration – Expert Overview

KIRLOSBROS

The executive explains that E20 (20% ethanol, 80% petrol) reduces mileage by about 4‑5% due to ethanol’s lower energy density, but improves acceleration and torque because ethanol has a higher octane rating. All vehicles sold after April 2023 in India are E20‑compatible, and older models can also run on it. E20 is the national standard; higher blends like E85 or E100 require flex‑fuel vehicles, which many countries (e.g., Brazil) use to raise overall ethanol usage. The shift to ethanol cuts crude oil imports, supports farmers, and aligns with global practices, but consumer awareness and clear communication from manufacturers, oil companies, and the government are needed to address misinformation and build confidence. Scientific studies and certified testing back the safety and benefits of E20

Bank of Baroda Settles NMC Health Litigation for $600 Million

MEDANTA BANKBARODA

Bank of Baroda has agreed to pay $600 million (≈5,700 crore) to settle the Abu Dhabi Global Market court case brought by the administrators of the collapsed UAE healthcare group NMC Health. The settlement ends the bank’s involvement in one of the largest overseas litigations faced by an Indian state‑run bank, despite earlier claims in its FY26 report that it had a strong chance of a favorable outcome and had not set aside provisions. The case arose from NMC’s 2020 collapse, which uncovered over $4 billion of undisclosed debt. Administrators alleged that senior BoB officials colluded with former NMC executives, including founder B.R. Shetty, in fraudulently facilitating loans and transactions. Shetty’s counsel asserts his signatures were forged and that the bank was complicit, though these allegations remain unproven in court. The out‑of‑court settlement caps BoB’s liability at $600 million, discontinues related proceedings in Abu Dhabi and England, and directs the funds to NMC’s estate for creditor distribution. The bank maintains it settled without admitting wrongdoing, citing the desire to avoid prolonged litigation, uncertainty, and legal costs

Women’s Growing Role in Mutual Funds: Challenges and Opportunities

BLAL TVVISION

The interview highlights that women now represent about 25% of mutual fund investors in India, with faster growth in tier‑2/3 cities and among those under 35. Most women prefer regular (advisor‑managed) plans over direct ones, favor equity‑oriented funds (especially hybrids and passive indexes), while older women lean toward gold and fixed deposits.
