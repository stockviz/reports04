Women’s Growing Role in Mutual Funds: Challenges and Opportunities

BLAL TVVISION

The interview highlights that women now represent about 25% of mutual fund investors in India, with faster growth in tier‑2/3 cities and among those under 35. Most women prefer regular (advisor‑managed) plans over direct ones, favor equity‑oriented funds (especially hybrids and passive indexes), while older women lean toward gold and fixed deposits. Barriers include lower disposable income, gender pay gaps, lack of confidence due to jargon, and patriarchal decision‑making norms. Recommendations focus on tailored financial‑literacy programs, more women distributors, relatable goal‑based communication, and cultural shifts to empower women to invest independently

Rapido founders booked for operating illegal bike‑taxi service in Maharashtra

DHANI

The founders of Rapido—Rishikesh S R, Pavan Guntupalli and Aravind Sanka—were booked at Nagpur’s Sitabuldi police station after the regional transport office’s special drive seized a private two‑wheeler used for a Rapido ride, finding it operated without the required licence or government approval for petrol‑powered bike taxis. A complaint alleges the company encouraged unlicensed commercial use of private bikes, violating transport rules, endangering passengers and causing revenue loss. The case has been filed under sections of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, Motor Vehicles Act and the IT Act, with further investigation ongoing

Capgemini clarifies external daycare operator amid Bengaluru child abuse probe

Capgemini confirmed that the day‑care centre at its Bengaluru campus is run by third‑party provider Little Scholars. Following media reports of alleged physical and mental abuse of children—including videos of mistreatment—the company temporarily closed the facility on July 1, is cooperating with police, and is offering support to affected families through helplines, counseling, and flexible work‑from‑home options. The Bengaluru Police have registered an FIR against five caregivers, seized CCTV footage, and formed a special investigative team to examine compliance across all daycare centres. Capgemini emphasized its due‑diligence processes for daycare providers and its commitment to employee welfare

Bank of Baroda Settles NMC Health Litigation for $600 Million

MEDANTA BANKBARODA

Bank of Baroda has agreed to pay $600 million (≈5,700 crore) to settle the Abu Dhabi Global Market court case brought by the administrators of the collapsed UAE healthcare group NMC Health. The settlement ends the bank’s involvement in one of the largest overseas litigations faced by an Indian state‑run bank, despite earlier claims in its FY26 report that it had a strong chance of a favorable outcome and had not set aside provisions. The case arose from NMC’s 2020 collapse, which uncovered over $4 billion of undisclosed debt. Administrators alleged that senior BoB officials colluded with former NMC executives, including founder B.R. Shetty, in fraudulently facilitating loans and transactions. Shetty’s counsel asserts his signatures were forged and that the bank was complicit, though these allegations remain unproven in court. The out‑of‑court settlement caps BoB’s liability at $600 million, discontinues related proceedings in Abu Dhabi and England, and directs the funds to NMC’s estate for creditor distribution. The bank maintains it settled without admitting wrongdoing, citing the desire to avoid prolonged litigation, uncertainty, and legal costs
