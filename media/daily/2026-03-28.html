Porter’s Journey: Scaling a Hyper‑Local Logistics Platform from 30 to 7 Lakh Daily Orders

ZOMATO M&M SWIGGY

Three IIT‑Kharagpur alumni founded Porter in 2014, inspired by Uber’s capacity‑utilisation model but focused on on‑demand intra‑city freight for SMEs, which comprise 80 % of the market. Starting with a bootstrapped call‑center and tower‑based tracking, they built an app to digitise driver availability, enforce a carrot‑and‑stick system, and improve reliability and pricing. Early funding came within six months (K Capital, Sequoia, Mahindra) after numerous pitches; subsequent rounds were harder. By solving fragmented, under‑utilised logistics, Porter now handles ~7 lakh daily transactions, serving SMEs who need fast, affordable delivery comparable to consumer‑grade services. They manage driver quality, customer complaints, and continuous product iteration, leveraging data and AI for allocation and efficiency. The company aims to expand to 70‑80 Indian cities, explore inter‑city services, and maintain a moat through scale, cost efficiency, and user obsession, while remaining cautious about profit focus and potential competition

West Asia Conflict Drives Indian Rupee to Record Lows, Near ₹95 per

The rupee plunged to a new record near ₹95 per dollar as Middle‑East tensions spiked oil prices, squeezing a dollar‑scarce Indian economy. Despite steady bond yields, foreign‑institutional investors dumped $12 bn in the month—the largest outflow ever—forcing the RBI to intervene, mainly via forward‑market dollar sales. With limited export earnings, dwindling reserves, and rising current‑account deficits, analysts warn the rupee’s slide could persist if the oil shock endures, echoing past balance‑of‑payments crises
