RBI’s New Capital Market Exposure Rules Tighten Prop Trading, Minimal Impact on Client‑Facing Brokers

HDFC ISEC HDFCBANK MOTILALOFS

RBI’s latest circular introduces stricter collateral requirements for stock brokers, mandating 100 % secured funding for proprietary (prop) trading—50 % cash and 50 % cash equivalents—while client‑related margin guarantees remain unchanged. The rules clarify eligible collateral, haircuts, and introduce tighter monitoring and margin‑call provisions. Prop brokers, who account for roughly 40 % of F&O volume, face higher borrowing costs and reduced intraday limits, potentially shrinking F&O activity by 15‑20 % from April 1, 2026. Large, client‑focused broker‑dealers (e.g., HDFC, Angel, ISEC) are expected to see negligible impact, whereas smaller “mom‑and‑pop” prop houses will feel the brunt. The circular also opens bank funding for margin‑trade facilities (MTF) and non‑MTF trade receivables, offering new lending avenues for brokers. Overall, the measures aim to curb speculative derivative trading while providing clearer collateral guidelines, with limited effect on mainstream brokerage operations
