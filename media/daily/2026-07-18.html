IFSCA Tightens GIFT City Credit Rating Rules, Introducing Issuer Fact‑Check and Enhanced Record‑Keeping

SPAL AGL

The International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA) has amended its Master Circular for Credit Rating Agencies operating in GIFT City. Effective immediately, agencies must give issuers a chance to review and correct any factual errors, omissions or misperceptions that could materially affect a rating before it is published, while preserving the agency’s analytical independence. The rule does not apply to unsolicited or private ratings. Additional changes include stricter record‑keeping requirements—maintaining detailed, reconstructible documentation of rating decisions and their rationale—and an expanded definition of credit ratings to cover issuers, credit‑quality and financial‑strength ratings. Private and unsolicited ratings are exempt from mandatory public disclosure. The amendments aim to align GIFT City practices with global standards and IOSCO guidelines

Rocket Flexibility: Intentional Design for Performance and Control

UNIMECH

The Vikram‑1 launch has been delayed, prompting a push to inspire young viewers to become future scientists whose names might one day grace Indian rockets. Modern launch vehicles, though appearing rigid, are deliberately designed with built‑in flexure so they can bend under aerodynamic loads, thrust, wind gusts, and maneuvering forces. This flexibility reduces weight and improves efficiency, but it introduces complex challenges: onboard sensors must separate genuine attitude changes from structural bending, and guidance‑control systems must account for predicted bending modes and natural frequencies to avoid unnecessary corrections
