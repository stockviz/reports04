Vedanta Accelerates Bokaro Steel Capacity Expansion

VOGL VEDPOWER VAML VEDL NSLNISP JINDALSTEL VISL

Vedanta Iron & Steel plans to double its Bokaro plant’s steelmaking capacity from 1.5 mtpa to 3 mtpa by the end of 2026, a year earlier than originally slated, and is already outlining a further boost to 5 mtpa by FY29. After acquiring ESL Steel in 2018, the expansion faced regulatory delays, but final clearances are expected within six months. The company will fund the growth through internal cash flow and market debt, with minimal reliance on Vedanta Ltd’s legacy debt. The move aligns with Vedanta’s post‑demerger strategy to focus on higher‑margin steel products and target an EBITDA of $526 million by FY29

Indian Firms Tie AI Adoption to Leader Appraisals

AXISBANK CHEVIOT RELIANCE SASKEN 3MINDIA

Companies across sectors—from banking giants like Axis Bank to consulting firms such as PwC India and conglomerates like Reliance—are now evaluating senior leaders on AI implementation as part of performance reviews. Approximately 5‑15% of senior‑leadership goals are being linked to AI projects, with expectations that leaders devote around 20% of their time to AI initiatives and demonstrate measurable outcomes such as revenue growth, cost savings, risk mitigation, or productivity gains. The move follows substantial investments in AI upskilling and the development of in‑house AI platforms. While initially limited to top management, analysts predict the KPI could extend to middle and lower tiers. Compensation structures remain largely fixed, but a portion of bonuses is tied to business performance, now increasingly influenced by AI‑driven results. This shift aims to embed an “AI‑native” culture, ensuring AI’s benefits are quantified and integrated into core business processes

Pressure on Indian OMCs to cut fuel prices as Nayara slashes rates

BPCL ATHERENERG HINDPETRO IOC JIOFIN

Private refiner Nayara Energy reduced petrol by ₹5/litre and diesel by ₹3/litre, prompting calls for state‑run oil marketing companies (IOCL, BPCL, HPCL) to follow suit. Global crude prices have fallen to pre‑war levels (Brent ≈ $72/barrel, WTI ≈ $69), yet OMCs’ retail prices remain high, reflecting inventory from earlier higher‑priced imports and a 50‑60‑day supply chain lag. Experts suggest OMCs can absorb lower rates now, while the government cites past excise cuts and modest real‑price changes. Meanwhile, commercial LPG and jet fuel prices have been lowered, but diesel and petrol remain unchanged at most OMC pumps

TVS’s Costly Norton Gamble Aims for Long‑Term Premium Gains

HYUNDAI BAJAJ-AUTO GRINDWELL NUVAMA TVSMOTOR

TVS Motor has logged nearly ₹2,000 crore of post‑tax losses on its Norton acquisition (2020‑26), about one‑fifth of its six‑year profit, with FY26 alone seeing a ₹723 crore loss despite a July global relaunch from its Hosur plant. The group is investing heavily in design, engineering (Solihull hub, Italy centre) and new models, targeting 200 dealer touch‑points across seven countries and 20,000 units annually over time. TVS hopes to emulate Bajaj’s turnaround of KTM, using Norton to boost brand perception and feed technology into its own lineup. Analysts view the premium push as a decade‑long play that could lift TVS’s domestic share from 19.5% (FY26) to 20.5% (FY28) and drive 13% revenue and 18% earnings CAGR, despite near‑term cost pressures
