Power Demand Outlook and Investment Themes in India’s Power Sector

NTPC JSWENERGY ADANIPOWER CLEANMAX RELIANCE

Nikhil, senior analyst at Bernstein, explains that India’s power demand typically grows at about 6% medium‑term, tracking real GDP but subject to weather cycles. After a weak 18‑month period, demand rebounded sharply in FY24 due to a low base, warm weather and low rainfall, and strong growth is expected in the second half of the year and into FY26. The spot market shows extreme diurnal price swings: solar surplus drives prices near zero in the afternoon, while evening shortages push prices to the regulated ceiling of ₹10/kWh, creating opportunities for battery storage, pump‑storage and non‑solar generation. Bernstein favours non‑solar assets—thermal, nuclear, and battery/storage—over pure‑play solar, citing margin compression and upcoming solar capacity oversupply. Preferred equity picks include NTPC and JSW Energy for their diversified thermal, nuclear and renewable portfolios; power financiers are seen as cheap but riskier. Solar developers such as Adani Green, WAAREE and Premier are underweight until margins stabilise and industry consolidation occurs. Clean‑Power‑Non‑Interruptible (CNI) contracts show strong demand from data centres and corporates, but regulatory changes could erode profitability. Overall, the firm sees a structural, not cyclical, growth story for India’s power sector, with investment focus on assets that serve evening peak demand and provide firm capacity
