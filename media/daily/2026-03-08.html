Euler Motors urges relaxation of auto PLI criteria for EV start‑ups

EICHERMOT

Euler Motors CEO Saurav Kumar argues that India’s auto PLI scheme, which requires a minimum global revenue of ₹10,000 crore and fixed‑asset investment of ₹3,000 crore, excludes promising EV start‑ups like his firm. Despite producing electric cargo trucks and three‑wheelers and having invested ₹1,500 crore (with plans for an additional ₹500‑1,000 crore), Euler Motors cannot qualify. Kumar proposes expanding the investment metric beyond fixed assets to overall capital deployment and lowering revenue thresholds, noting the company’s creation of around 2,000 jobs and its role in advancing green mobility. He acknowledges the original intent to favor large OEMs but stresses the need for inclusive incentives to support emerging EV innovators

Jaquar Group Targets Rapid Growth in Lighting Business

BCG TRANSRAILL

Jaquar aims to boost its lighting revenue to ₹1,600‑₹1,700 cr within three years, up from an expected ₹700 cr this fiscal year, while lighting currently accounts for 8‑10% of its total turnover. The plan includes a ₹100 cr investment in a new Bhiwadi plant (potentially rising by ₹50‑60 cr), expansion of outdoor, landscape and architectural lighting, manufacturing of lighting poles, and collaborations with designers. Exports, presently 15‑16% of lighting sales to the Middle East and Africa, are slated to rise to ~20%, supported by a new “Jaquar World” showroom in Dubai. The group’s overall target is ₹8,300 cr (≈$1 bn) revenue by FY26

Cipla USA Recalls Over 400 Cartons of Nilotinib Capsules Due to Failed Specifications

CIPLA

Cipla USA, Inc., a New Jersey-based subsidiary of Indian drugmaker Cipla, has voluntarily issued a Class III recall of 400+ cartons of generic Nilotinib (150 mg and 200 mg) after detecting tablet/capsule specification failures. The recall, initiated on February 18, poses minimal health risk. Nilotinib is used to inhibit abnormal proteins that drive cancer cell growth. This incident highlights the significant role of Indian pharmaceutical firms, which supply a large share of US prescriptions, with India hosting the most USFDA‑compliant drug plants outside the United States

Editor’s Roundtable Recap: Market Gains, IT Sell‑off, Cement Outlook & Sandeep Tandon’s Sector Views

INFY BEML MINDTREE

The show opened with a strong market week: Nifty up ~3.5%, Sensex up ~3%, mid‑caps and small‑caps posting 3‑4% gains, while the IT index fell about 7% amid a global tech pull‑back. Panelists discussed the impact of the late‑night budget, the EU‑India trade deal, and RBI policy, noting that the pace of news kept markets buoyant. Sandeep Tandon highlighted India’s relative outperformance versus a weakening global equity landscape, especially in Korea, China and Taiwan, and stressed a constructive stance on a relative‑basis view. He warned of a possible deeper correction in US tech stocks but remains cautiously optimistic about Indian IT, viewing the sector as undervalued and awaiting a buying opportunity after further pull‑backs.

Sector allocations: Tandon’s fund has increased exposure to private‑sector banks, insurance, sunrise industries, large‑cap quality names, and asset‑management firms, while trimming VSU banks. Defense is deemed bullish long‑term but currently overvalued, so he prefers a wait‑and‑watch approach.

Cement: The panel noted improving price trends, solid demand (high‑single‑digit to low‑double‑digit volume growth) and cost‑saving measures, suggesting a strong Q4 earnings outlook and potential upside for selective cement stocks.

Precious metals: Gold and silver are expected to be highly volatile, behaving like crypto. Tandon has reduced exposure to both but plans to rebuild positions when data become more constructive, emphasizing disciplined, strategic allocation.

Overall, the week’s market rally was driven by policy optimism and earnings, but the IT sell‑off and global tech turbulence remain key risks, with opportunities seen in banks, insurance, cement and a cautious re‑entry into IT and precious metals
