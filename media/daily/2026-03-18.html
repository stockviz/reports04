Meta challenges CCPA’s Rs 10 lakh penalty over walkie‑talkie listings on Facebook Marketplace

MEESHO TANLA

Meta filed a petition in the Delhi High Court contesting a Central Consumer Protection Authority order that fined it Rs 10 lakh for allegedly allowing unauthorised walkie‑talkie sales on Facebook Marketplace. Meta’s counsel argued that Facebook is merely a notice‑board, not an e‑commerce platform, and that it does not facilitate transactions or charge commissions, thus outside CCPA’s jurisdiction. The court scheduled a hearing for March 25, asking Meta to explain the jurisdictional claim and why the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission cannot hear the matter. The petition contends the CCPA exceeded its powers, misapplied e‑commerce rules, and violated natural‑justice principles, warning that such an interpretation could burden ordinary individuals selling personal items. The CCPA’s earlier order had identified over 16,970 non‑compliant walkie‑talkie listings across multiple platforms, directed Meta to block such listings, and mandated periodic self‑audits and public disclosure of compliance certificates

KKR backs PMI Electro’s full‑stack e‑mobility push with $310 million

OLECTRA TATAMOTORS ASHOKLEY OLAELEC

KKR is investing up to $310 million in PMI Electro Mobility, acquiring a majority stake in its fleet‑management platform Allfleet and a minority stake in its manufacturing arm. The deal marks KKR’s first Indian Climate Transition investment and transforms PMI from a pure electric‑bus maker into a full‑stack operator covering manufacturing, fleet ownership and long‑term services. Capital will fund expansion of Allfleet’s portfolio to over 5,000 buses under Gross Cost Contracts, creating predictable revenue streams. PMI, the biggest winner of the PM E‑DRIVE tender with 5,210 buses, already operates 3,000 buses and has an order book of 8,200+. Revenue is projected to rise from roughly ₹580‑600 cr in FY25 to ₹1,200‑1,500 cr in FY26 and exceed ₹2,800 cr later, supported by a new 33,000‑unit plant in Rajasthan. The company’s localisation strategy gives it cost advantages over larger rivals, reshaping the competitive landscape as India targets 50,000 electric buses by 2030

Maruti Suzuki’s SUV‑Centric Expansion and Capacity Boost

MARUTI

Maruti Suzuki plans to launch seven new SUVs over the next 5‑6 years and raise its production capacity to about 4 million units to meet rising domestic demand and export growth. The strategy combines a revival in entry‑level car sales—driven by GST‑induced price cuts—and a shift toward SUVs, now 54.7% of India’s passenger‑vehicle market. The company will deepen rural penetration, expand its dealer network (including compact NEXA formats), and maintain a multi‑fuel mix of CNG, hybrids, flex‑fuel and EVs. Exports are set to rise sharply, supporting the capacity build‑out, as Maruti targets a 56 million‑unit Indian market by 2030 and positions India as a global hub for Suzuki
