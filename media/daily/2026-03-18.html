Income Tax 2026: New Reporting Thresholds and Simplified Compliance

The NDTV Profit segment breaks down the key provisions of the new Income Tax Act effective 1 April 2026. Major changes include raising PAN‑reporting thresholds (cash/withdrawals to ₹10 lakh, immovable property to ₹20 lakh, vehicle purchases over ₹5 lakh, hotel/restaurant bills over ₹1 lakh, and broader insurance and credit‑card reporting). Credit‑card spend of ₹10 lakh (or cash withdrawals over ₹1 lakh) will now be auto‑reported, with mandatory PAN for card applications and stricter scrutiny of business‑related card use. ITR filing deadlines are extended (ITR‑3/4 to 31 August, revised returns to 31 March). Tax rates are adjusted: TCS on alcohol rises to 2 %, a flat 2 % TCS on overseas education, medical and digital services, and higher STT on futures (0.05 %) and options (0.15 %). The government emphasizes reliance on real‑time digital data from banks, brokers, insurers, and foreign entities to simplify compliance, treat taxpayers as partners, and boost collections through “nudge” mechanisms (e.g., HRA declarations and political‑donation disclosures). The overall thrust is to reduce paperwork for low‑value transactions while tightening oversight on high‑value, high‑risk activities
