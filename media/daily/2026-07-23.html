Debt Fund Strategy in a Rising Rate Environment: Focus on Accrual and Short‑Medium Duration

In a volatile fixed‑income market driven by higher oil prices, a weakening rupee and rising yields, Devam Shah (Access Mutual Fund) advises investors to move away from long‑duration bonds. He expects the RBI to stay on hold for now and only gradually hike rates later in the year, which would hurt long‑duration assets. The preferred approach is an “accrual” bias—investing in high‑yielding, short‑to‑medium‑term securities (2‑5 year segment). AAA bonds in this bucket offer ~7.5% yields, while AA/Single‑A provide 8–9%. Credit spreads have widened, presenting attractive opportunities, but the overall theme remains lower duration and higher carry. Hybrid fund managers are similarly cutting duration (from ~8‑9 years to ~2.5 years) and emphasizing accrual assets to provide stability amid equity weakness. The sweet spot across the yield curve is therefore the 2‑5 year range, with a focus on quality credit and reduced interest‑rate exposure
