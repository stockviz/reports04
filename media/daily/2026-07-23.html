India’s First Strategic Gas Reserve Planned Near Western Production Assets

ONGC

ONGC aims to create a strategic natural gas reserve in western India, using depleted gas wells to store gas and cushion supply disruptions from the West Asia war. The move follows stalled government plans for underground salt cavern storage and complements ONGC’s recent 1.75 mt crude oil reserve project, addressing India’s reliance on imports for about 55% of its gas needs

BusinessNext Secures $40 Million Series B from ServiceNow to Accelerate Global Expansion

BusinessNext raised $40 million in a Series B round led by ServiceNow Ventures, valuing the Noida‑based autonomous banking platform at $700 million and giving ServiceNow a ~5% stake. The investment grants rapid market access across Southeast Asia, New Zealand and Australia via ServiceNow’s extensive go‑to‑market network. The funding comes as banks intensify AI adoption, and BusinessNext’s AI‑driven solutions for lending, customer service and compliance are gaining traction with major Indian banks and international clients, contributing to FY25 revenue of ₹312 crore and a modest profit. The partnership also positions the firm for future growth opportunities, including a potential IPO

Transfer of Ratan Tata’s Tata Sons shares to his foundations delayed amid regulator probe

NSIL

Executors of Ratan Tata’s will cannot transfer 3,368 Tata Sons shares to the Ratan Tata Endowment Foundation and Trust because a 2026 complaint alleges a 1989 transfer of 833 shares was improper. The Maharashtra charity commissioner is investigating, though Tata Trusts claim the transfer was legal. Until the commissioner rules, the share transfer remains stalled, while other assets from Tata’s estate have been allocated

CEO Pay Caps Under Scrutiny as IT Firms Seek Higher Share‑Based Remuneration Limits

COFORGE SONATSOFTW MPHASIS PERSISTENT

Indian IT services companies Mphasis and Persistent Systems have asked shareholders to raise the statutory ceiling on CEO remuneration from 5% to higher percentages (7% for Mphasis, 21% for Persistent) to accommodate large stock‑option and RSU payouts. The moves reflect CEOs cashing in accumulated share awards, prompting concerns from investors and proxy advisers about excessive pay relative to net‑profit growth and peer performance. While promoters back the resolutions, several institutional investors and advisory firms have recommended voting against them, citing lack of transparency, disproportionate pay, and the need for caps on incentive compensation. The debate highlights the tension between rewarding executives with equity and maintaining shareholder‑approved remuneration limits

EU Clears Paramount‑Warner $81 B Merger with Distribution Conditions

PARACABLES LGBBROSLTD CARBORUNIV

The European Commission approved Paramount’s $81 billion acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery, finding sufficient competition in most media markets but imposing safeguards on theatrical film distribution. Paramount must exit its United International Pictures partnership in the European Economic Area within 13 months and avoid new deals with Universal for ten years, while shifting Warner film distribution to its existing pipeline. The EU will monitor compliance. The deal still faces U.S. challenges: a federal judge ordered a pause pending a preliminary injunction hearing after a lawsuit by California and 11 other states alleging reduced competition, with a hearing set for Aug. 3. The U.S. Justice Department, however, opposes blocking the merger. Paramount has secured clearances in several other countries and must meet a Sept. 30 deadline or pay daily fees, while also addressing concerns about financing from Gulf sovereign funds

Paramount‑Skydance Wins EU Clearance for $110 bn Warner Bros Deal Amid US and UK Challenges

PARACABLES LGBBROSLTD CARBORUNIV

The European Commission approved Paramount Skydance’s $110 bn acquisition of Warner Bros Discovery after the company pledged to end its United International Pictures joint venture with Universal and to refrain from any film‑distribution partnership with Universal in Europe for ten years, ensuring the merged studios’ films won’t be distributed jointly with Disney or Universal. In the United States, a court‑ordered pause—prompted by a coalition of states—has stalled the deal despite DOJ clearance, triggering a “ticking fee” of $0.25 per share (about $7 million per day) if the merger isn’t completed by September 30. The transaction also faces a Writers Guild lawsuit alleging threats to writers’ livelihoods and potential UK intervention over possible effects on news, children’s television, and streaming services

Batista Brothers Secure Stake in Venezuelan Oil JV Amid Sanctions Easing

BLUECOAST BNALTD DENORA ONGC DELTACORP 5PAISA

Brazilian billionaire brothers Joesley and Wesley Batista, through their firm Fluxus Oil, Gas & Energy, have acquired A&B Oil and Gas, which holds a 49% stake in the Petrolera Roraima joint venture with Venezuela’s PDVSA. The deal, part of a wave of new investments ahead of a July 28 regulatory deadline, aims to raise output at the Orinoco Heavy Oil Belt project to 120,000 barrels per day within five years. The transaction complies with U.S. OFAC licenses, reflecting Washington’s push to revive Venezuelan oil under the Trump administration. The Batistas, long linked to Venezuela via meat supply contracts, are eyeing further oil, mining and infrastructure opportunities despite recent earthquakes
