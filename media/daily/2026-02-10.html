Tata Steel Pursues New Iron‑Ore Sources as Legacy Mines Expire

TATASTEEL

Tata Steel will lose its captive mining leases from 2030, prompting a hunt for fresh iron‑ore supplies. The firm is expanding domestically with a 50.01% stake in Thriveni Pellets and a JV with Lloyd Metals to explore mining in Maharashtra’s Gadchiroli region. Internationally, Tata Steel Minerals Canada’s Labrador mine (3 mtpa, expandable to 10 mtpa) offers high‑grade ore (>67% Fe, <1% alumina) that can be blended with Indian ore to boost blast‑furnace efficiency. CEO T.V. Narendran stresses testing this option now to decide on future Canadian imports, especially as Indian ore costs could rise to 130‑140% of global prices. Meanwhile, the UK arm continues to miss its EBITDA breakeven target due to low steel prices and limited policy support, though a shift to electric‑arc furnaces may improve costs long‑term. In FY26 Q3, Tata Steel posted 57,002 crore rupees revenue, a 6% YoY rise, with a consolidated net profit of 2,689 crore rupees; European earnings varied, with the Netherlands showing lower EBITDA and the UK narrowing losses. The company expects modest price improvements in the March quarter and is counting on policy backing to stabilize its UK operations.

JK Lakshmi Cement Accelerates Capacity Expansion to Safeguard Regional Market Share

JKLAKSHMI

JK Lakshmi Cement, a top‑10 Indian cement maker, announced a ₹3,000 crore expansion plan to add 4.6 mt of capacity (raising total to 22.6 mt by FY28, with a longer‑term target of 30 mt) focused on its core North, West and East Indian markets. Operating at 73 % utilisation (above the 70 % industry average) and hitting 90‑95 % during peak months, the company seeks to prevent capacity constraints from eroding its market share as larger peers also ramp up capacity. Funding will be a 70:30 debt‑to‑internal accrual mix, keeping net debt‑to‑EBITDA below 1. JK Lakshmi also aims to boost premium cement sales, targeting a rise from 13‑14 % to ~16 % of turnover, and expects double‑digit volume growth in FY26, outpacing the industry’s 7‑8 % growth forecast. The strategy emphasizes defending and modestly expanding regional dominance rather than pursuing a pan‑India footprint.

India’s FMCG Titans Accelerate D2C Acquisitions

FCONSUMER MARICO EMAMILTD ITC

Indian FMCG giants are rapidly buying digital‑first (D2C) brands to tap premium, niche categories and boost growth. Over the past five years, 60% of FMCG deals have targeted personal care and 40% foods & beverages, with D2C firms capturing about 40% of market share. Major moves include Marico’s stakes in Cosmic Wellness, Zia Maze, Beardo, Just Herbs, True Elements and Plix, pushing its combined sales past ₹1,000 cr; HUL’s acquisition of Minimalist and Xavier, both projected to hit ₹1,000 cr; ITC’s purchase of Yoga Bar, Mother’s Pledge and 24 Mantra, aiming for ₹1,100 cr ARR; and others like Emami’s Mann & Co and Go Search’s Moshta. Drivers are portfolio diversification, premium‑category entry, faster online growth, and attractive valuations that enhance margins without straining financials. The strategy positions traditional FMCG players to dominate the emerging D2C space.
