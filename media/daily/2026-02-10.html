US Tariff Relief for Bangladesh and Its Implications for Indian Textile Exporters

PGIL

The United States reduced Bangladesh’s reciprocal tariffs from 37% to 19% and granted duty‑free access for apparel that incorporates a specified share of U.S.‑produced raw materials. Pallab Banerjee of Pearl Global explains that while Bangladesh already benefits from a strong textile ecosystem, the new agreement mainly helps products that contain a high percentage (around 70%) of U.S. cotton or yarn, a segment that is currently small due to cost considerations. Most of Bangladesh’s cotton yarn still comes from India, so the immediate cost advantage of zero tariffs is uncertain. For Indian exporters without Bangladesh operations, the impact is expected to be modest; the competitive edge for Bangladesh may increase slightly, especially as U.S. buyers may request “U.S.‑origin” inputs, but overall market dynamics—including EU trade rules and Bangladesh’s impending loss of LDC status—remain complex. India could consider similar arrangements, but the overall shift is unlikely to dramatically reshape the Indian textile export landscape in the short term
