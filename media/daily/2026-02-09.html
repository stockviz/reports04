Upstox Shifts Focus Beyond Brokerage Amid Regulatory Clampdown

5PAISA HGM GROWW KOTAKBANK

Upstox, backed by Tiger Global, is seeking licences to offer non‑bank lending, asset management and insurance as SEBI’s tighter derivatives rules and higher transaction taxes curb retail trading volumes. After flat growth in FY25, the broker aims to double profits in FY26‑27 by targeting high‑value, active traders, boosting ARPU by over 40% and maintaining ~90% retention. EBITDA is set to rise ~120% and PAT could more than double the FY25 level of ₹215 cr. The firm is investing in algo‑trading infrastructure, expanding commodities turnover, margin‑trade facilities, and SIP volumes, while planning to diversify into full‑stack financial services without raising new equity. A public listing remains under consideration, but Upstox prefers staying private to experiment freely.

India‑US Interim Trade Deal: Progress, H‑1B Outlook & Next Steps

Minister Piyush Goyal highlighted that the Section 232 probe on pharma is ongoing, with assurances of zero‑duty tariffs for generic medicines and ingredients. He said the next phase involves finalising legal paperwork and further negotiations to ease tariffs and boost bilateral trade. Regarding H‑1B restrictions, Goyal noted Indian firms aren’t worried, expecting continued access and even potential benefits as fringe applications drop. He emphasized the rise of Global Capability Centers (≈1,800 in India) which keep talent and earnings domestic. No concessions for US e‑commerce inventory storage were discussed. On non‑tariff barriers, both sides aim to address issues like GPU and technology import restrictions as part of a broader trade agreement. Goyal declined to set a deadline for the full Bilateral Trade Agreement but affirmed strong political and people‑to‑people ties and a desire to conclude it swiftly. He praised US Ambassador Sergio Gore for his helpful role in the negotiations, while keeping detailed behind‑the‑scenes discussions confidential.

Hudco Targets $2 bn Overseas Funding to Boost Urban Infrastructure

HUDCO

India’s state‑run non‑bank lender HUDCO plans to raise $1 bn each from the World Bank/ADB and Yen‑denominated loans to finance its Urban Invest Window, which will support urban local bodies in tier‑2/3 cities with project structuring, municipal bond advisory, and capital mobilization. The move, part of a cost‑optimisation strategy and the government’s city‑focused growth agenda, aims to double HUDCO’s loan book to ₹3 trn by 2030 and strengthen institutional capacity of urban bodies through consultancy and broader investor participation.
