Gold Loans Surge Past Vehicle Loans: Drivers, Risks, and Market Outlook

MUTHOOTFIN AVAILFC

Gold financing has become the fastest‑growing asset class, overtaking vehicle loans as gold loan volumes now account for about 31% of total loan issuance. The boom is fueled by stable gold prices, strong first‑quarter demand, a shift toward securitisation (both pass‑through certificates and direct assignments), and robust investor appetite for low‑risk, high‑return paper. Leading players—Manappuram Finance, Muthoot Finance and IFL Finance—control the bulk of the market (80‑93% of each firm’s AUM) with valuations ranging from 1.75× (Manappuram) to lower multiples for Muthoot and IFL. Key tailwinds include liquid gold collateral, low delinquency rates, and RBI‑capped LTV ratios (generally 65‑70%). Risks involve tighter RBI gold‑lending norms, potential operational/fraud issues, and gold‑price volatility, though a 25‑30% price drop—required to trigger major losses—has been rare historically. The surge also reflects borrowers shifting from stressed unsecured/micro‑finance channels to secured gold loans. Overall, the sector enjoys structural support but must monitor regulatory and operational risks
