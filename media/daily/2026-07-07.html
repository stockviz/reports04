Adani’s Week‑Long $10 Billion Capital Raise Fuels $100 Billion 2026‑2030 Expansion Plan

ADANIENSOL BCG ADANIPORTS ADANIENPP1 BFINVEST

In just seven days the Adani Group secured nearly $10 bn of fresh capital through a mix of equity placements, stake sales and strategic partner commitments, including a $3.2 bn qualified institutional placement by Adani Enterprises, a $1.1 bn QIP by Adani Energy Solutions, a $539 m sale of a 49% stake in its Vizhinjam port to MSC, and a $11.5 bn 50:50 JV with UAE’s IHC for an aluminium plant in Odisha. Skyroot Aerospace’s Quest to Commercialize Indian Space Launches

AWFIS UNIMECH SOLARINDS BROOKS-RE

Skyroot Aerospace, founded by ex‑ISRO engineers Pawan Kumar Chandana and Naga Bharath Daka in 2018, is poised to become India’s first private company to launch a commercial satellite with the upcoming test flight of its orbital rocket Vikram 1 (≈300 kg to LEO). The firm’s USP is building production‑scale infrastructure in parallel with rocket development, enabling a target cadence of one launch per month and a rapid path to scaling. Leveraging a largely domestic supply chain (≈400 Indian suppliers) and a deep talent pool—augmented by returning expatriates and ISRO hires—Skyroot aims at the global launch market, where demand outstrips supply. The company has already demonstrated sub‑orbital capability (2022) and generated ~₹100 crore in pre‑contract revenue. It plans a family of rockets (Vikram 1 for 200‑300 kg payloads, Vikram 2 for ~1 ton) and will eventually pursue reusable larger vehicles. While policy reforms (IN‑SPACe) and ISRO facilities have been enabling, Skyroot seeks more anchor‑customer government contracts to accelerate growth. In the next five years, it targets regular launches, a launch‑a‑month cadence, and a leading role in the expanding global space market
