AI Disruption Decoded: Sanjeev Bikhchandani on Jobs, IT Services, Productivity & India’s AI Future

In a candid interview, Sanjeev Bikhchandani, founder and vice‑chairman of InfoEdge, emphasizes that AI will bring both disruption and opportunity. While some roles—especially routine coding and support tasks—may be automated, new jobs will emerge, shifting the workforce toward deployment, oversight, and AI‑enhanced services. Legacy IT contracts and large‑scale systems mean transition will be gradual, with short‑term churn but long‑term productivity gains. He underscores that regulation typically lags behind technology but eventually catches up, citing India’s 1990s satellite‑TV rollout as an analogy. Bikhchandani shares internal examples where AI bots expanded outreach to previously unserved customers and enabled low‑cost content creation, illustrating AI’s ability to unlock new business avenues rather than merely replace staff. Regarding large Indian IT firms, he believes they will adapt, possibly sacrificing short‑term profitability while reorienting to AI‑driven models. Finally, he notes India’s AI advantage lies in focusing on local needs—such as Indic‑language platforms like Gyan.ai—leveraging domestic capital and partnerships, rather than trying to compete directly with US or Chinese LLM developers
