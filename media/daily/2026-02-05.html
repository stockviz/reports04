Union Budget 2026 | STT On Futures & Options Hiked: How Will It Impact Arbitrage Funds and SIFs?

TVVISION

Key Takeaways from the MF Corner discussion on Union Budget 2026

  • STT Increase on Futures & Options
    • STT on futures rose from ~2 paisa to ~5 paisa (≈3 bps per contract).
    • Annualised cost rise ≈ 36 bps; with a 70 % arbitrage component, this trims fund returns by ≈ 21 bps.
    • Adding portfolio churn (≈10 %) pushes the total hit to 30‑35 bps per year for arbitrage funds.
  • Arbitrage vs. Liquid Funds After the Hike
    • Assuming pre‑tax returns of 7 % for both, the post‑tax edge of arbitrage over liquid funds fell from ~1.23 % to ≈ 0.9‑1.0 % (still in favour of arbitrage).
    • Despite the drag, arbitrage remains the better short‑term option for investors in higher tax brackets.
  • What to Do When a Short‑Term Holding Ends?
    • If the horizon extends beyond a year, shift to hybrid funds based on the new time frame:
      • 1‑3 yr → Equity Savings or Conservative Hybrid.
      • 3‑5 yr → Balanced Advantage, Multi‑Asset, or Aggressive Hybrid.
    • Staying in debt‑only funds is unlikely to beat arbitrage returns at present; long‑end bonds also look unattractive.
  • Savings‑Bank Deposits vs. Debt‑Fund Alternatives
    • Bank savings/current accounts earn 4‑6 % fully taxable.
    • For low‑tax brackets, short‑term debt instruments (overnight, liquid, ultra‑short, money‑market, low‑duration, short‑term funds) are preferable.
    • For higher tax brackets, arbitrage funds become the more tax‑efficient choice for horizons > 1 month.
  • Budget Change: No Interest Set‑off Against Dividends
    • Interest expense can no longer be offset against dividend income from mutual‑fund holdings.
    • The move curtails the “dividend‑stripping” arbitrage strategy where investors borrowed cheap funds, bought high‑dividend stocks, and netted the dividend after tax.
    • Intended to reduce speculative borrowing, improve market stability, and close a loophole used by a niche group of traders.
  • Overall Outlook
    • The STT hike dents arbitrage fund returns but does not erase their advantage over liquid funds.
    • Investors should reassess the appropriate fund bucket as their investment horizon evolves, favouring hybrids for medium‑term goals and arbitrage for tax‑efficient short‑term parking.
