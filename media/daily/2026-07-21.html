Transition VC raises second energy fund targeting ₹1,500 cr

DHANUKA

Bengaluru‑based Transition VC is launching a second venture fund of ₹1,500 cr (~$155 m), nearly double its first ₹800 cr fund that closed in Dec 2025. Co‑founder Raiyaan Shingati says the aim is to replicate the first fund’s success with more capital and a larger support team. The first close is expected in FY27’s December quarter, aligning with growing institutional interest in India’s capital‑intensive energy‑hardware sector as the country pushes its energy‑transition goals

Vijay Singh’s Reappointment to Tata Trusts in Question Amid Regulatory and Internal Disputes

NSIL TVSMOTOR BCG TITAN

Vijay Singh’s second term on the board of Sir Ratan Tata Trust (SRTT) ends on 14 August, but a Maharashtra charity commissioner order blocks SRTT meetings, preventing a reappointment resolution. New state rules cap permanent trustees at one‑fourth of the board and require unanimous approval for appointments, jeopardising Singh’s chances. Even if the order is lifted, his recent shift to support a Tata Sons listing—contrary to the Trusts’ earlier stance—makes reappointment unlikely. Singh also faces possible opposition for a December reappointment to Sir Dorabji Tata Trust (SDTT). The disputes reflect broader governance tensions within the Tata Trusts, including past removals, disagreements over board composition, and differing views on Tata Sons’ future

Judge Issues TRO Blocking $81 Billion Paramount‑Warner Merger Over Antitrust Concerns

LGBBROSLTD PARACABLES

A U.S. district judge granted a 14‑day temporary restraining order, later possibly extendable to 28 days, halting the proposed $81 billion (≈$111 billion with debt) merger of Paramount Global and Warner Bros. Discovery. The order follows a lawsuit by a coalition of 12 states, led by California, alleging the deal would reduce competition, limit consumer choices, increase market concentration, and lead to higher prices and less innovation in film, TV, and streaming. A hearing on a preliminary injunction is set for August 3. Paramount argues the merger would enhance competitiveness against global rivals and has already secured approvals in other jurisdictions. The companies face additional challenges, including from the Writers Guild of America, and risk paying a $7 million‑per‑day “ticking fee” if the deal isn’t closed by September 30
