India Remains a Neutral Bet as North Asia Outperforms

Rajiv Batra of JPMorgan explained that recent 15‑20% corrections in Korea and Taiwan were driven by geopolitical risk (US‑Iran tensions), concerns over excess AI compute capacity, and a shift toward open‑source models rather than fundamentals. He sees these dips as buying opportunities but warns they stem from derating, not earnings weakness. India is expected to deliver positive absolute returns, yet its outperformance is unlikely to sustain; earnings growth is projected at 10‑11% for FY26 (down from earlier 15‑16% expectations) and could rise if commodity prices stay stable and monetary policy remains accommodative. He notes that foreign inflows into emerging markets have fallen, with large‑cap foreign holdings in India declining, while mid‑ and small‑cap foreign participation is rising, mitigating pressure on the domestic market. Overall, India is viewed as a neutral, selective play amid broader regional dynamics
