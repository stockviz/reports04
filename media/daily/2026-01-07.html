RBI Proposes To Cap Dividend Payout At 75% Of Net Profit For Eligible Banks | CNBC TV18

SBIN

The RBI’s draft norms propose capping banks’ dividend payouts at 75% of net profit (80% for regional rural and local area banks), up from the current 40% cap. The limit applies to equity share dividends, including interim payouts, but excludes perpetual non‑cumulative preference shares. Dividend calculations will use profit after deducting net NPS as of 31 March, and extraordinary profits cannot be used. Boards must consider NPA levels, auditor remarks, capital buffers, and growth plans before declaring dividends, ensuring capital remains above regulatory minima. Foreign bank branches meeting eligibility can remit net profits without prior RBI approval, but any excess remittance identified post‑audit must be returned. The draft is open for comments until early February and would take effect from FY 27.
