US Naval Blockade Destroys Iranian Fastboats and Pressures Tehran’s Economy

TVVISION

The U.S. has deployed a large naval and air force to protect commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz, destroying six‑seven Iranian fast‑attack boats that attempted to swarm U.S. vessels. The operation, framed as defensive, is part of a broader economic pressure campaign—codenamed “Operation Epic Fury”—that combines a naval blockade costing Iran up to $500 million daily with intensified sanctions aimed at crippling its oil revenue and financial networks. Iran’s inflation is reported at 70 %, trade is down 90 %, and the U.S. warns of secondary sanctions for entities aiding Iran. The stated goal is to reopen the straits for all traffic without tolls or mines, urging Iran to negotiate a peaceful settlement

Samsung Joins TSMC in $1 Trillion Club Amid AI Chip Boom

BEL BAJAJINDEF

Samsung Electronics’ market value surpassed $1 trillion, driven by soaring demand for AI‑focused chips and record semiconductor profits that jumped 48‑fold in the March quarter. The rally makes Samsung the second Asian firm after TSMC to hit the milestone, while peers SK Hynix and TSMC also reached record highs. Analysts see the AI‑driven memory surge as structural, expecting continued price rises for NAND and DRAM through 2027. However, Samsung’s mobile and display units face margin pressure, and labor tensions have sparked threats of an 18‑day strike. Despite these challenges, sell‑side forecasts predict a roughly 30 % stock gain over the next year, with the shares trading at just 5.3 × forward earnings. The gains have boosted the Korean Kospi and broader Asian markets, highlighting Asia’s pivotal role in the global AI ecosystem

NLC India partners with France’s EDF for nuclear projects

NTPC NLCINDIA

NLC India has signed an agreement with French state utility EDF to explore nuclear power development, focusing on European Pressurised Reactors (EPR) and small modular reactors, as India aims for 100 GW of nuclear capacity by 2047. This follows a similar EDF deal by NTPC. Additionally, NLC will abandon its costly lignite‑to‑methanol project, a ₹4,400 crore plan to produce 400,000 tonnes of methanol annually

Coal India’s Outlook Amid India’s Energy Transition

COALINDIA

Coal India dominates over 80% of domestic coal production, benefitting from government licenses and long‑term power supply contracts. While India’s aggressive shift to renewable energy and net‑zero goals pose long‑term demand challenges, the company is boosting coal output (targeting 1.23 bn t by FY35) and investing in infrastructure, coal washeries, and solar capacity (209 MW). Strong Q4 FY26 results (₹46,490 cr revenue, ₹10,791 cr profit) and a 5.25 ₹ dividend underline its financial health. Diversification into renewables and critical minerals aims to mitigate transition risks, but investors must weigh fundamentals, governance, and valuation before deciding on the stock’s potential to reach ₹600

Man Charged with $450 Million Fraudulent Loan Scheme Targeting Mexican Billionaire

BCG

Vladimir Sklarov, using aliases and the Astor family name, created a fake company called Astor Asset Group to lure Mexican magnate Ricardo Salinas Pliego into a $115 million loan secured by $450 million of his company’s stock. Sklarov sold the pledged shares, kept most of the proceeds, and later sent false notices of default. He was arrested in Chicago after a New York grand jury indictment revealed the scheme, which involved other conspirators using prestigious-sounding aliases. The indictment highlights the use of false prestige to deceive a high‑profile investor

Xiaomi’s Fall from India’s Smartphone Leaderboard

BEL

Xiaomi’s once‑dominant position in India collapsed from a 2021 peak of 40 million shipments to just 13.7 million in 2025, slipping out of the top‑five brands. The decline was driven by a 2022 government crackdown hurting brand perception, leadership exits in 2023‑24, and failure to keep pace with the market’s premium shift. Rivals—Vivo, Oppo, Realme and Apple—gained market share by expanding offline retail and premium offerings, while Xiaomi remained online‑focused and budget‑centric. Revenue fell from ₹39,099 crore in FY22 to ₹23,977 crore in FY25, with net profit dropping over ₹15,000 crore. Margins stayed thin (~1%) and local manufacturing lagged behind competitors. Xiaomi has appointed a new COO and is pivoting toward a value‑driven, broader ecosystem strategy, aiming to stabilize its 9% market share and shift away from volume‑only growth

Google DeepMind UK staff seek union recognition over Pentagon AI contracts

Workers at Google DeepMind’s London office, backed by the Communication Workers Union and Unite, have voted 98% in favor of unionising to demand an end to the company’s AI work for the US Department of Defense and the Israeli military, reinstatement of the 2018 “no‑weapons” AI pledge, an independent ethics board, and the right to refuse objectionable projects. The move follows backlash to a deal allowing Gemini models to be used in classified military networks, reviving the 2018 Project Maven protest legacy amid broader industry concerns over AI in warfare

Freshworks Announces 11% Workforce Reduction Amid AI‑Driven Restructuring

Freshworks will cut about 500 jobs, roughly 11% of its staff, as AI reshapes its product development and automates routine tasks. CEO Dennis Woodside says over half of the company’s code is now generated by AI, and savings from layoffs, merged sales teams, and reduced management layers will be reinvested in its Employee Experience division. The cuts come as the software sector faces AI‑related cost pressures and competitive threats, with Freshworks forecasting Q2 revenue of $232‑$235 million, slightly above analysts’ estimates

AI Disruption Causes Market Turbulence for Thomson Reuters

Thomson Reuters posted strong Q1 results, beating earnings and revenue expectations, and highlighted growth in its AI legal tool CoCounsel. However, shares fell after Anthropic unveiled broader AI agents, reviving investor concerns that AI could erode the firm’s market share. The stock’s volatility reflects ongoing skepticism about AI’s impact on traditional data and software providers, despite the company’s confidence in customer retention and upcoming AI product launches
