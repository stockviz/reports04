AI Cost Overrun Shows Governance Gaps in Enterprise Adoption

BMWVENTLTD PAISALO

An enterprise’s unrestricted access to Anthropic’s Claude AI led to a $500 million monthly bill after employees used high‑cost workflows—long‑context prompts, coding agents, and autonomous pipelines—without spending caps or real‑time monitoring. Similar spikes have hit Microsoft, Uber, and Amazon, highlighting missing governance like usage dashboards, alerts, and role‑based controls. Experts say firms often automate low‑value tasks, “tokenmaxxing” AI usage, and fail to align AI spend with revenue‑generating outcomes, leading to budget overruns and even layoffs. The incident underscores the need for built‑in cost controls, clearer ROI focus, and stronger procurement scrutiny to avoid reputational damage and ensure sustainable AI deployment

FTC Launches Antitrust Probe into Fertilizer Prices

ABMINTLLTD TCNSBRANDS AHL

FTC Chairman Andrew Ferguson announced a comprehensive antitrust investigation into the sharp rise in U.S. fertilizer costs, which have become the biggest input expense for farmers since 2020. The probe follows geopolitical disruptions that boosted prices and profit margins for firms such as CF Industries and Nutrien, while others like Mosaic faced higher input costs. The FTC has not named any specific companies, but the Justice Department is also pursuing criminal and civil inquiries into alleged price‑fixing by major producers including Nutrien, Mosaic, CF Industries, Koch and Yara. The investigations aim to determine whether anticompetitive behavior is driving the soaring prices affecting farmers nationwide
