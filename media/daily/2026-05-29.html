Beyond the Carton: Tetra Pak’s India Expansion

Over four decades, Tetra Pak has grown from a carton‑centric brand to a full‑service food‑processing partner in India. Its 45‑acre Chakan facility—India’s largest outside Sweden—hosts packaging material production, equipment manufacturing, a straw factory, a renovation centre, a recipe lab and global back‑office functions, employing about 1,500 people. Localisation is key: 85 % of Indian equipment is made at Chakan, with 75 % of components sourced from Indian suppliers, and the plant exported 400+ machines to 56 countries last year. The company tailors support: large brands get dedicated account managers and on‑site engineering, while small and emerging businesses receive formulation help, market research and co‑packing via a network of 450 machines. Emphasising food‑waste reduction, Tetra Pak highlights aseptic packaging and UHT processing that extend product shelf life without preservatives. Sustainability has been a long‑term focus, with roughly ₹10 million invested in recycling infrastructure ahead of mandatory EPR rules, creating collection points and converting used cartons into roof tiles, school furniture, etc. The expansion reflects a shift from merely supplying cartons to delivering end‑to‑end production, automation, digital solutions, and a robust recycling ecosystem

BPCL RELIANCE

The Supreme Court set aside SEBI’s ₹447.27 crore disgorgement order against Reliance Industries in the 2007 Reliance Petroleum derivatives dispute, reversing a 2020 SAT ruling that had upheld fraud findings.
