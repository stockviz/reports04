Beyond the Carton: Tetra Pak’s India Expansion

Its 45‑acre Chakan facility—India’s largest outside Sweden—hosts packaging material production, equipment manufacturing, a straw factory, a renovation centre, a recipe lab and global back‑office functions, employing about 1,500 people. Localisation is key: 85 % of Indian equipment is made at Chakan, with 75 % of components sourced from Indian suppliers, and the plant exported 400+ machines to 56 countries last year. Emphasising food‑waste reduction, Tetra Pak highlights aseptic packaging and UHT processing that extend product shelf life without preservatives. Sustainability has been a long‑term focus, with roughly ₹10 million invested in recycling infrastructure ahead of mandatory EPR rules, creating collection points and converting used cartons into roof tiles, school furniture, etc. The expansion reflects a shift from merely supplying cartons to delivering end‑to‑end production, automation, digital solutions, and a robust recycling ecosystem

Supreme Court Overturns SEBI’s Disgorgement Order in Reliance Petroleum Derivatives Case

BPCL RELIANCE

The Supreme Court set aside SEBI’s ₹447.27 crore disgorgement order against Reliance Industries in the 2007 Reliance Petroleum derivatives dispute, reversing a 2020 SAT ruling that had upheld fraud findings. While the court dismissed the fraud allegations under the PFUTP Regulations, it upheld SEBI’s findings of violations of the 2001 derivatives position‑limit and disclosure rules. Reliance is to be refunded the ₹250 crore deposited in the Investor Protection Fund. The decision follows a prior Supreme Court judgment that had also quashed penalties on Mukesh Ambani related to the same episode. The ruling may influence perceptions of regulatory enforcement and investor confidence in India’s capital markets
