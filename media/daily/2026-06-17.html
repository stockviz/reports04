Yum! Brands Sells Pizza Hut for $2.7 Billion in Global Restructuring

AHL 5PAISA METROBRAND

Yum! Brands announced the sale of Pizza Hut for a total enterprise value of $2.7 billion—$1.5 billion to LongRange Capital for operations outside mainland China and $1.2 billion to Yum China for Pizza Hut China. The deals, part of a strategic portfolio reshaping, are expected to net about $2.3 billion after taxes and fees and close in Q3 2026. Yum! will retain its technology platform and provide transitional support, and will use proceeds to invest in the business, fund a $4 billion share repurchase, and return capital to shareholders. After the sale, Yum! will no longer report Pizza Hut results

India’s Insurance Landscape Amid War‑Driven Inflation and Supply‑Chain Strains

Aon’s Joe Peiser warns that the West Asia conflict is pushing up inflation and disrupting supply chains, heightening risk values for Indian insurers. While India remains under‑insured, new policies such as 100% FDI allowance aim to boost capacity. Global insurance markets are soft, with corrections expected to last 1–1.5 years, but cyber, climate, and US liability risks persist. Companies seek solutions for inflation‑linked risk, supply‑chain resilience, and data‑centre coverage, where capacity is rapidly expanding but still limited. Government‑backed schemes (e.g., maritime pool, parametric disaster covers) are positive steps, yet private insurers remain the primary providers. Shipping claims are rising but manageable; war‑related political violence, credit risk, and data‑centre energy demands are other key concerns. Integrated risk modelling, early engineering controls, and resilient supply‑chain design are advised to secure underwriting for large‑scale projects

Law Firms Expand Footprint in GIFT City as IFSC Hits $100 Billion Milestone

JPASSOCIAT SBIN ADANIPORTS KHAITANLTD 3MINDIA CENTRALBK

Leading Indian law firms—including Khaitan & Co., SNG & Partners, Trilegal, Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas, Economic Laws Practice and Dhaval Vussonji & Associates—are opening or expanding offices in the International Financial Services Centre (IFSC) at GIFT City. The hub now holds over 1,034 entities, 38 banks and 229 funds, with assets surpassing $100 bn. Firms cite the new IFSC regulatory framework, growth in investment funds, aircraft leasing, insurance, fintech, and multinational treasury centres, and anticipate future work in data centres, AI, climate finance, digital assets and foreign university campuses. Offices operate with lean, specialised teams, reflecting the early‑stage market but offering a competitive edge for firms that invested early in IFSC expertise

Adani Group’s Workforce Overhaul Aimed at Youth, Diversity and AI‑Driven Efficiency

BCG ATGL ADANIPORTS ADANIPOWER ADANIGREEN

The conglomerate is launching a three‑pronged transformation to create a younger, more diverse and leaner workforce across its 115,000 employees. Chairman Gautam Adani is pushing outsourcing, son Karan Adani is driving youth and gender diversity, and CFO Jugeshinder Singh is implementing an AI‑led “cobotic” model that automates routine tasks and introduces score‑based performance management. The plan includes compressing middle management, fast‑tracking high‑potential talent, and increasing women’s representation (currently ~5%). AI will handle transactional work, while humans focus on strategic roles with AI assistance. The shift also involves moving many roles to external vendors, as seen in a sharp workforce reduction at Adani Green Energy. While the initiatives promise rapid career growth for some, they raise concerns over job security, vendor‑based employment, and transition challenges such as recent strikes and salary‑formula changes. The outcomes could set a benchmark for large Indian firms navigating AI and workforce modernization

US Commerce Dept. Orders Anthropic to Halt Export of Advanced AI Models

Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick warned Anthropic that its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models cannot be accessed by foreign nationals without a U.S. export‑control license, threatening civil and criminal penalties. Anthropic disabled the models, met with officials, and argued the action is disproportionate, while the government cites national‑security concerns over potential jailbreaks and misuse
